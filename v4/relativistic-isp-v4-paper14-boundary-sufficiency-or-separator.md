## 1. Boundary Conditional Variance

### Definition 1.1: Boundary Predictor

For a tested readout \(G\), define its boundary predictor by

$$
\widehat Y^{\partial}_{a,G}
:=
\mathbb E_a^{act}
\left(
Y^{phys}_{a,G}
\mid
{\mathcal B}^{\partial}_a
\right).
$$

Since all spaces are finite, this is the ordinary conditional average on

$$
\partial_a^{-1}(b).
$$

### Definition 1.2: Boundary Conditional Variance

Define

$$
{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G)
:=
\mathbb E_a^{act}
\left[
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}-\widehat Y^{\partial}_{a,G}\|^2
\right].
$$

The aggregate boundary conditional variance is

$$
\overline{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a
:=
\max_{G\in{\mathcal G}^{phys}_a}
{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G).
$$

This is the finite scalar deciding whether boundary data are sufficient for
the tested physical readout law.

### Lemma 1.3: Same-Boundary Two-Copy Identity

Let \((Q,Q')\) be conditionally independent draws from the same actual law
given the same boundary record.  Equivalently,

$$
\mathbb P_a^{\partial,2}(dq,dq')
:=
\sum_{b\in{\mathsf BdDef}_a}
\mathbb P_a^{act}(\partial_a=b)\,
\mathbb P_a^{act}(dq\mid \partial_a=b)\,
\mathbb P_a^{act}(dq'\mid \partial_a=b).
$$

Then for every tested readout \(G\),

$$
\mathbb E_a^{\partial,2}
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2
=
2{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G).
$$

Proof.

Condition on the boundary atom \(b\).  The two variables
\(Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)\) and \(Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\) are independent draws from
the same finite conditional distribution.  Expanding the squared norm gives
twice the conditional variance on that atom.  Averaging over \(b\) proves the
identity.  `square`

### Lemma 2.2: Exact Finite Doob-Dynkin

Let \(X:\Omega\to E\) and \(Y:\Omega\to F\) be maps on a finite set.  Then
\(Y\) is \(\sigma(X)\)-measurable if and only if there exists

$$
\Phi:E\to F
$$

$$
Y=\Phi\circ X.
$$

Proof.

On a finite set, \(\sigma(X)\)-measurability is exactly constancy of \(Y\)
on fibers of \(X\).  Define \(\Phi(x)\) to be this common value on
\(X^{-1}(x)\).  `square`

### Theorem 2.3: Exact Boundary Sufficiency Is Exact Boundary Factorization

   \Phi_{a,G}:{\mathsf BdDef}_a\to{\mathcal O}_a

Proof.

Variance zero means the random variable agrees almost surely with its

the equivalence between measurability and factorization through

## 3. Feynman Side: Same-Boundary Separator

### Definition 3.1: Same-Boundary Readout Separator

A same-boundary readout separator at scale \(c>0\) is a boundary value
\(b\), two actual records

$$
q_0,q_1\in\partial_a^{-1}(b),
$$

and a tested readout \(G\) such that

$$
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(q_0)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(q_1)\|\ge c.
$$

It has actual mass \(\rho>0\) if, under the same-boundary two-copy law,

$$
\mathbb P_a^{\partial,2}
\left(
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2\ge c^2
\right)
\ge\rho.
$$

### Theorem 3.2: Positive Boundary Variance Gives A Separator

Assume that, for some tested \(G\),

$$
{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G)\ge v>0,
$$

and that the pairwise readout diameter is bounded:

$$
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(q')\|^2\le K
$$

whenever \(\partial_a(q)=\partial_a(q')\).  Then there is a same-boundary
readout separator with

$$
c^2=v,
\qquad
\rho\ge {v\over K}.
$$

Proof.

By Lemma 1.3,

$$
\mathbb E_a^{\partial,2}
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2
=
2{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G)
\ge
2v.
$$

If the event

$$
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2\ge v
$$

had probability \(<v/K\), the expectation would be
\(<v+K(v/K)=2v\), up to the usual harmless strict/limit adjustment.  Hence
the event has mass at least \(v/K\).  Any pair in the event is a
same-boundary separator.  `square`

### Definition 3.3: Pair-Switch Visibility

The same-boundary separator is pair-switch-visible if there exist
\(\sigma>0\) and an error \(r_a\) such that, under the same-boundary
two-copy diagnostic,

$$
S_a(Q)+S_a(Q')
\ge
\sigma
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2
-r_a(Q,Q'),
$$

with

$$
\mathbb E_a^{\partial,2}r_a^+\to0.
$$

This is the correct finite bridge from a two-record separator to the
one-record switch detector, because

$$
\mathbb E_a^{\partial,2}[S_a(Q)+S_a(Q')]
=
2\mathbb E_a^{act}S_a.
$$

### Theorem 3.4: Separator Plus Pair-Switch Visibility Gives A Switch Floor

Assume:

1. for some tested \(G\),

   $$
   {\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G)\ge v>0;
   $$

2. pair-switch visibility holds for that \(G\).

Then

$$
\liminf_a\mathbb E_a^{act}S_a
\ge
\sigma v.
$$

Proof.

By Lemma 1.3,

$$
\mathbb E_a^{\partial,2}
\|Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q)-Y^{phys}_{a,G}(Q')\|^2
=
2{\mathsf Var}^{\partial}_a(G)
\ge2v.
$$

Integrating pair-switch visibility gives

$$
2\mathbb E_a^{act}S_a
\ge
2\sigma v-o(1).
$$

Divide by \(2\) and take the lower limit.  `square`

### Theorem 5.5: Minimal Boundary-Center Theorem

The record \(C_a^{read}\) is support-minimal among all boundary refinements
that are sufficient for the tested readout battery.  Precisely:

1. \(C_a^{read}\) is sufficient over the boundary;
2. if \(C_a\) is any sufficient boundary refinement, then

   $$
   B_a^{read}\preceq_{act}(\partial_a,C_a).
   $$

Proof.

Sufficiency is immediate: each \(Y^{phys}_{a,G}\) is a coordinate projection
of \(Z_a\), hence a function of \(B_a^{read}\).

Now let \(C_a\) be any sufficient boundary refinement.  By Definition 5.4,
each \(Y^{phys}_{a,G}\) is a function of \((\partial_a,C_a)\) on

\((\partial_a,C_a)\), and so

$$
B_a^{read}=(\partial_a,Z_a)
$$

is also a function of \((\partial_a,C_a)\) on actual support.  This is
exactly

$$
B_a^{read}\preceq_{act}(\partial_a,C_a).
$$

`square`

### Corollary 5.6: Boundary Sufficiency Versus Nontrivial Center
