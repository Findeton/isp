# Note (C2): why discharging Paper VII's covariance premise is deferred — the point-locality leg is BHS-blocked

**Author:** Felix Robles Elvira (ORCID: 0009-0009-2017-4394; independent researcher)

**Status:** internal program note, version 2026-06-15. Not a paper. It records why the attempt to make Paper VII's covariance result *unconditional* is premature, decomposes the premise into three legs, and assigns each its honest status. Receipt `v7/code/c2_bhs_collar_nogo.py`.

## The premise, and why it was attractive to attack

Paper VII established a *conditional* positive: the decoherence layer of the record click-law inherits the 2025 all-orders microcausality result **if and only if** the holonomy diamond coarse-grains, under an emergent Lorentz action, to a **point-local self-adjoint Lorentz-scalar seal density** (collar → 0). Discharging that one premise would upgrade Paper VII from conditional to unconditional — the single highest-value move available on the covariance frontier. This note records the result of attempting it: the premise factors into three legs, one of which genuinely reduces, while the other two are the field's open emergence-of-Lorentz core, one of them blocked by the corpus's *own* discreteness result. So the attempt is deferred, and Paper VII stays conditional.

## The three legs

The premise "the diamond coarse-grains to a point-local Lorentz-scalar seal density" is not one statement but three:

- **(P-Lor)** the causal **order** / the `σ`-arrow descends Lorentz-invariantly;
- **(P-point)** the diamond's finite-valency **collar** coarse-grains to a point (collar → 0);
- **(P-scalar)** the seal is a **scalar** under the emergent Lorentz action.

### (P-Lor) — reduces [a real, small win]

The causal order is Lorentz-invariant on owned machinery. Receipt `c2` confirms, on one finite `1+1` Minkowski sample, that the interval `s² = −t² + x²` is boost-invariant to machine precision and that the causal order — the past and future light-cone sets of a point — is **invariant** under a boost (rapidity `0.9`). This receipt is an illustration of the order-level invariance, not a proof of the full distributional Bombelli–Henson–Sorkin theorem. The theorem supplies the stronger statement that a Poisson sprinkling breaks no Lorentz symmetry. The reduction claimed here is only the causal-order leg: the `σ`-arrow lives in the frame-invariant causal order.

### (P-point) — finite-valency collar blocked by the BHS no-go

This is the decisive obstruction for the attempted route. Point-locality of the seal was being attacked through a finite-valency collar coarse-grained to a point in a Lorentz-invariant way. But a Poisson sprinkling admits **no Lorentz-invariant finite-valency graph** (Bombelli–Henson–Sorkin): any rule that picks a finite number of "nearest" neighbours is frame-dependent, because "nearest" needs a frame-dependent distance. Receipt `c2` exhibits this directly: the `k = 5` nearest neighbours of a point in the rest frame overlap the boosted-frame nearest neighbours in only **2 of 5** — the collar pick *moves* under the same boost that leaves the causal order fixed.

So the finite collar cannot coarse-grain to a point Lorentz-invariantly from discreteness alone; collar → 0 does **not** commute with the emergent Lorentz action for a frame-fixed sharp collar. This does **not** rule out every route to emergent point-locality or effective locality. Covariant infinite-valency/order-theoretic smearings, causal-set d'Alembertian-style layer kernels, terminal actions, or a matter sector that supplies an emergent Lorentz group acting on seal operators remain logically possible. The no-go here is narrower and cleaner: a finite-valency collar alone cannot supply the point-local premise.

### (P-scalar) — the open emergence core

Whether the seal transforms as a scalar under the *emergent* Lorentz action is, like (P-point), an emergence statement, not an algebraic fact (the seal is a scalar under the substrate's internal automorphisms, which is established; the emergent-Lorentz scalar property is not). Asserting it would be circular — it presupposes the emergent Lorentz group acting on operators that (P-point) shows discreteness does not supply.

## Verdict and disposition

Discharging Paper VII's premise is **premature**: of its three legs, one reduces at the causal-order level, and two remain part of the field's open emergence-of-Lorentz core, with the finite-valency collar version of (P-point) actively **blocked** by the BHS no-finite-valency result. The honest deliverable is therefore this **premise-sharpening note**, not a paper: it pins the premise's structure, isolates exactly which leg reduces and which route is blocked, and confirms that Paper VII's "conditional positive" grade is correct and not improvable by a finite collar from discreteness alone. The covariance result stays conditional; routes to unconditionality may run through an unbuilt matter sector, covariant nonlocal/order kernels, or another emergent Lorentz mechanism, but not through a frame-fixed finite-neighbour collar.

This note does not claim to discharge the premise, nor to prove it undischargeable (only that *discreteness alone* cannot supply point-locality); it records the attempt's honest outcome. Receipt `c2` exits cleanly (the order boost-invariant, the collar frame-dependent, the contrast decisive).
