# Note (C2): why discharging Paper VII's covariance premise is deferred — the point-locality leg is BHS-blocked

**Author:** Felix Robles Elvira (ORCID: 0009-0009-2017-4394; independent researcher)

**Status:** internal program note, version 2026-06-15. Not a paper. It records why the attempt to make Paper VII's covariance result *unconditional* is premature, decomposes the premise into three legs, and assigns each its honest status. Receipt `v7/code/c2_bhs_collar_nogo.py`.

## The premise, and why it was attractive to attack

Paper VII established a *conditional* positive: the decoherence layer of the record click-law inherits the 2025 all-orders microcausality result **if and only if** the holonomy diamond coarse-grains, under an emergent Lorentz action, to a **point-local self-adjoint Lorentz-scalar seal density** (collar → 0). Discharging that one premise would upgrade Paper VII from conditional to unconditional — the single highest-value move available on the covariance frontier. This note records the result of attempting it: the premise factors into three legs, one of which genuinely reduces, while the other two are the field's open emergence-of-Lorentz core, one of them blocked by the corpus's *own* discreteness result. So the attempt is deferred, and Paper VII stays conditional.

## The three legs

The premise "the diamond coarse-grains to a point-local Lorentz-scalar seal density" is not one statement but three:

- **(P-Lor)** the causal **order** / the `σ`-arrow descends Lorentz-invariantly;
- **(P-point)** the diamond's finite-valency **collar** coarse-grains to a point (collar → 0);
- **(P-scalar)** the seal is a **scalar** under the emergent Lorentz action.

### (P-Lor) — reduces [a real, small win]

The causal order is Lorentz-invariant on owned machinery. Receipt `c2` confirms, on a finite Poisson sprinkling in `1+1` Minkowski, that the interval `s² = −t² + x²` is boost-invariant to machine precision and that the causal order — the past and future light-cone sets of a point — is **invariant** under a boost (rapidity `0.9`). This is the Bombelli–Henson–Sorkin statement that a Poisson sprinkling breaks no Lorentz symmetry, and it is exactly the discreteness-covariance result the corpus already owns: the `σ`-arrow lives in the frame-invariant causal order. This leg is a genuine reduction, not a restatement — the *order* descends Lorentz-invariantly.

### (P-point) — blocked by the corpus's own BHS no-go

This is the decisive obstruction. Point-locality of the seal would require coarse-graining the diamond's finite-valency collar to a point in a Lorentz-invariant way. But a Poisson sprinkling admits **no Lorentz-invariant finite-valency graph** (Bombelli–Henson–Sorkin): any rule that picks a finite number of "nearest" neighbours is frame-dependent, because "nearest" needs a frame-dependent distance. Receipt `c2` exhibits this directly: the `k = 5` nearest neighbours of a point in the rest frame overlap the boosted-frame nearest neighbours in only **2 of 5** — the collar pick *moves* under the same boost that leaves the causal order fixed. So the finite collar cannot coarse-grain to a point Lorentz-invariantly from discreteness alone; collar → 0 does **not** commute with the emergent Lorentz action for a frame-fixed sharp collar. Point-locality is recoverable only by importing structure the records do not yet carry (a matter sector that supplies an emergent Lorentz group acting on the seal operators) — which is the same emergence-of-Lorentz core the whole dynamical-collapse field leaves open.

### (P-scalar) — the open emergence core

Whether the seal transforms as a scalar under the *emergent* Lorentz action is, like (P-point), an emergence statement, not an algebraic fact (the seal is a scalar under the substrate's internal automorphisms, which is established; the emergent-Lorentz scalar property is not). Asserting it would be circular — it presupposes the emergent Lorentz group acting on operators that (P-point) shows discreteness does not supply.

## Verdict and disposition

Discharging Paper VII's premise is **premature**: of its three legs, one reduces on owned machinery (the causal order is Lorentz-invariant), and two are the field's open emergence-of-Lorentz core, with (P-point) actively **blocked** by the corpus's own BHS no-finite-valency result. The honest deliverable is therefore this **premise-sharpening note**, not a paper: it pins the premise's structure, isolates exactly which leg reduces and which is walled, and confirms that Paper VII's "conditional positive" grade is correct and not improvable by discreteness alone. The covariance result stays conditional; the genuine route to unconditionality runs through the unbuilt matter sector (an emergent Lorentz action on the seal), not through the substrate's discreteness.

This note does not claim to discharge the premise, nor to prove it undischargeable (only that *discreteness alone* cannot supply point-locality); it records the attempt's honest outcome. Receipt `c2` exits cleanly (the order boost-invariant, the collar frame-dependent, the contrast decisive).
