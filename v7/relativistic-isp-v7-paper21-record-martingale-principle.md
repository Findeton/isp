## Scope -- four guards, stated first

1. **This is a change of primitive statement, not a new numerical prediction.** The constant `kappa` is still the one free scale/calibration of Paper I. The martingale formulation does not fix `kappa`, the sparse spacing `d`, the inter-seal coherent profile, the tensor product, the canonical mode, or manifoldlikeness.

2. **The martingale statement is relative to the actual record filtration.** The filtration is the completed, right-continuous natural filtration generated by the committed sealed ledger, not an arbitrary coarse-graining and not an omniscient hidden-variable filtration. Hidden deterministic machines may carry more information underneath; the law says their **record projection** has the regime-appropriate compensator `A` (dense: `A_chi=kappa chi`; sparse lattice: `A_n=sum_{j<n}pi_j`).

3. **In the dense simple natural-record regime this is not weaker than Poisson.** Under the usual Watanabe-style hypotheses stated in Section 3, `N_chi - kappa chi` being a martingale is the standard constant-compensator characterization of a homogeneous Poisson process in `chi`. The gain is not that Poisson disappears there; the gain is that the fundamental statement is now compensator/ledger consistency rather than primitive stochastic ontology.

4. **For sparse and multi-channel records the statement is intentionally less totalizing.** Paper I forces the sparse first-survival skeleton, not a full iid counting process. On a sparse seal lattice the compensator is the predictable lattice compensator; the iid Bernoulli compensator is an extra stationary-renewal realization. For multiple unresolved channels, the total martingale fixes only the total predictable click drift unless additional channel martingales are postulated.

## Abstract

Paper I stated the dense click-law as `S(chi)=exp(-kappa chi)`, equivalently "clicks are Poisson in content." Paper XX then showed that the same record-facing law can be realized by deterministic hidden machines, so primitive stochasticity is not the right ontological commitment. This paper replaces the ontology-sounding slogan with the record-intrinsic compensator statement:

$$
N-A\quad\text{is the record martingale}.
$$

Here `N` is the committed sealed count and `A` is its predictable compensator in the committed-record filtration. In the dense simple regime `A_chi=kappa chi`, so the useful dense slogan remains:

$$
N_\chi-\kappa\chi\quad\text{is the record martingale}.
$$

In plain terms: after subtracting the lawful evidence-to-click allowance, the record has no predictable surplus or deficit visible from its own past. The click law is a **fair-ledger condition**.

Technically, the dense statement says that the predictable compensator of the sealed-counting process in the committed-record filtration is

$$
A_\chi=\kappa\chi.
$$

By Doob-Meyer uniqueness, this is a sharp law-level object: after the process and record filtration are fixed, the predictable drift is unique if it exists. In the dense simple natural-record regime, the constant compensator is equivalent to the older Poisson/exponential law: after any sealed stopping record, the next waiting content has survival `exp(-kappa u)`, hence increments are Poisson in `chi`. In the sparse regime, the same idea becomes the lattice martingale `N_n-A_n`, where `A_n=sum_{j<n}E[Y_j|F_j^rec]`; the special stationary iid Bernoulli realization has `A_n=n p_d` and `S(nd)=(1-p_d)^n`. Choosing `p_d=1-exp(-kappa d)` samples the dense exponential on the chosen lattice, but does not fix `d` and is not forced by the sparse skeleton alone.

The new constraint is on persistent hidden-rate mixtures. A static hidden sector mixture with rates `kappa_alpha` may have the right average rate at `chi=0`, but it does **not** satisfy the dense record martingale unless all active `kappa_alpha` are equal almost surely. No-click records update the posterior sector weights, producing the hazard

$$
h(\chi)=\sum_\alpha p_\alpha(\chi)\kappa_\alpha,
$$

and

$$
\frac{dh}{d\chi}=-\operatorname{Var}(\kappa_\alpha\mid \text{no click to }\chi)\le 0.
$$

Thus any nondegenerate **persistent static** hidden-rate heterogeneity of this form creates record-visible aging and violates the constant compensator. This does not rule out refreshed sectors, mark-only hidden structure, deterministic scheduling, or any hidden mechanism whose conditional projected intensity remains flat. Deterministic machines, cellular automata, and hidden tapes are allowed only if their record projection has the regime-appropriate predictable projection. This is stronger and cleaner than "use a Poisson process": it says exactly what the records must not be able to forecast.

The conceptual payoff is a string-theory-like consistency move, but not a string-theory claim: the click law can be read as **record compensator consistency**. The "anomaly" language is only an analogy for predictable click drift beyond the allowed compensator. Stochastic Poisson clocks, deterministic Bernoulli suspensions, and sparse Bernoulli renewal skeletons are realizations of the same general record-martingale condition in their proper regimes.

## 1. The slogan shift

The old slogan:

`clicks are Poisson in chi`.

The dense-regime slogan is:

$$
N_\chi-\kappa\chi\quad \text{is the record martingale}.
$$

The general foundational statement is slightly more careful:

$$
N-A\quad \text{is the record martingale}.
$$

The first sentence sounds like an ontology: nature samples a Poisson process. The martingale statement is a ledger statement: the record subtracts its predictable compensator `A`, and what remains has no record-visible trend. In the dense simple regime, `A_chi=kappa chi`, so the dense slogan is exactly `N_chi-kappa chi`.

This distinction matters because Paper XX showed that the same exponential evidence clock can be represented by a deterministic measure-preserving hidden system. Once the hidden tape is fixed, nothing stochastic happens pathwise. Yet the record-facing law is still exact because the invariant measure and record partition project to the exponential clock. So the stochastic process is not necessarily fundamental. The record compensator is.

Let `F_chi^rec` denote the committed-record filtration: everything the actual sealed ledger has committed up to accumulated content `chi`, completed and made right-continuous. Let `N_chi` be the sealed-counting process, assumed integrable and adapted. The record martingale principle is:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N_\chi-A_\chi\mid \mathcal{F}_\psi^{\mathrm{rec}}]
=N_\psi-A_\psi,\qquad 0\le \psi\le \chi.
$$

In the dense simple case, this specializes to:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N_\chi-\kappa\chi\mid \mathcal{F}_\psi^{\mathrm{rec}}]
=N_\psi-\kappa\psi.
$$

Equivalently, the dense records see no predictable increment except `kappa dchi`:

$$
\mathbb{E}[dN_\chi\mid \mathcal{F}_{\chi-}^{\mathrm{rec}}]=\kappa\,d\chi.
$$

That second line is only shorthand; the exact object is the predictable compensator.

## 2. The compensator is the law-level object

Let the seal crossings in evidence content be `chi_1, chi_2, ...`, and define the counting random measure

$$
\xi(d\chi)=\sum_k\delta_{\chi_k}(d\chi),
$$

with `N_chi=xi((0,chi])`. For deterministic hidden machines this is still a random measure after pushing the invariant hidden measure `mu` through the record partition; pathwise it may be a deterministic set of crossings for each hidden state.

For a submartingale or counting process with the usual regularity hypotheses, the Doob-Meyer theorem splits the process into

$$
N_\chi=M_\chi+A_\chi,
$$

where `M_chi` is a martingale and `A_chi` is predictable, increasing, and unique up to indistinguishability. Equivalently, `A` is the dual predictable projection of `xi` onto `F^rec`: for every bounded record-predictable test process `H`,

$$
\mathbb{E}\!\left[\int H\,d\xi\right]
=\mathbb{E}\!\left[\int H\,dA\right].
$$

In a counting process, `A_chi` is the predictable click ledger: the part of the count that the record can already account for from its past. This definition also blocks filtration-shopping. The filtration is the actual committed sealed ledger of the proposed theory. If a supposedly hidden phase is itself committed as a record, it belongs in `F^rec`; if a coarse partition hides a real committed record, it is not the record law of that theory.

Paper I forced the dense survival shape by refinement multiplicativity. The martingale principle says the same thing one level earlier, but with regime separation:

- general record principle: `N-A` is a martingale; [PRINCIPLE]
- dense simple regime: `A_chi = kappa chi`; [EQUIVALENCE]
- sparse lattice: `A_n=sum_{j<n}pi_j`, `pi_j=E[Y_j|F_j^rec]`; [PRINCIPLE]
- stationary iid Bernoulli sparse realization: `A_n=n p_d`. [REALIZATION]

This is not a statement about a hidden clock in the substrate. It is a statement about the **dual predictable projection** of whatever hidden clicking mechanism exists onto the committed-record filtration. A deterministic hidden machine may have pathwise scheduled crossings, a cellular automaton may have a huge hidden tape, and a stochastic model may have an ordinary rate. The record law only sees the predictable projection. In the dense simple case the allowed projection is `kappa dchi`; in the sparse case it is the appropriate lattice compensator.

That gives a precise nontechnical interpretation:

> evidence is the charge, clicks are the expense, and the compensator `A` is the only expense the ledger may predict. In the dense simple case, `A_chi=kappa chi`. Any systematic surplus or deficit after subtracting `A` is a record anomaly in the loose analogy sense.

The word "martingale" therefore does not mean "randomness is fundamental." It means "there is no record-extractable drift left."

## 3. Dense simple records: equivalence with Poisson in `chi`

Under the dense-regime assumptions of Paper I, the martingale formulation recovers the old exponential law. The precise Watanabe-style theorem needs the following hypotheses:

- `N_0=0`;
- `N_chi` is a simple cadlag non-explosive counting process;
- `E[N_chi]<infinity` on bounded content horizons;
- `F_chi^rec` is the usual completed, right-continuous natural filtration of the committed record chain;
- no simultaneous dense clicks;
- the compensator is deterministic and continuous, `A_chi=kappa chi`;
- the seal channel is scalar and stationary, as in Paper I.

Then

$$
N_\chi-\kappa\chi\quad\text{is a martingale}
$$

is the constant-compensator characterization of a homogeneous Poisson process in `chi`. [EQUIVALENCE, under the listed hypotheses]

Sketch. After any record stopping content `tau`, condition on the current record. On the event that no new click has occurred for an additional content length `u`, the compensator of the next click is still `kappa du`. Hence the conditional survival `S_tau(u)` satisfies

$$
\frac{dS_\tau}{du}=-\kappa S_\tau,\qquad S_\tau(0)=1,
$$

so

$$
S_\tau(u)=e^{-\kappa u},
$$

independent of the previous record. The waiting contents are therefore memoryless and identically distributed, and the count increments are Poisson with mean `kappa Delta chi`. This is the same dense click-law as Paper I:

$$
S(\chi)=\Pr(N_\chi=0)=e^{-\kappa\chi}.
$$

The receipt `p21_record_martingale.py` checks the conditional martingale identity directly at `dps = 140`:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N_t-\kappa t\mid N_s=n]=n-\kappa s,
$$

and also the compensated variance

$$
\mathbb{E}[(M_t-M_s)^2\mid \mathcal{F}_s]=\kappa(t-s).
$$

For `kappa=0.73`, `s=1.25`, `t=4.75`, the maximum conditional mean gap over `n=0,...,4` is `2.62e-141`.

Outside this scope, `N_chi-kappa chi` is only a compensator statement; it should not be inflated into a Poisson-process ontology. So the older slogan remains true in its proper regime. It is just not the most primitive way to say the law.

## 4. Sparse records: the lattice martingale

In a genuinely sparse/indivisible regime, content does not admit arbitrary refinement seals. Paper I already says the forced object is only the skeleton

$$
S(nd)=S(d)^n,
$$

with spacing `d` and inter-seal coherent profile free.

The martingale version is the discrete compensator. At lattice opportunity `j`, let `Y_j` be the click indicator and

$$
N_n=\sum_{j<n}Y_j.
$$

The general sparse compensator is

$$
A_n=\sum_{j<n}\pi_j,\qquad \pi_j=\mathbb{E}[Y_j\mid \mathcal{F}_j^{\mathrm{rec}}].
$$

Then

$$
N_n-A_n
$$

is the lattice record martingale. This is the sparse counterpart of `N_chi-A_chi`, not automatically `N_n-np_d`.

Paper I's forced sparse statement is only the first-survival skeleton

$$
S(nd)=S(d)^n.
$$

That skeleton alone does not determine the full post-click counting process. For example, one may choose the first waiting time to be geometric and then make the next post-click opportunity deterministic; the first-survival skeleton remains geometric, but `N_n-np_d` is not a martingale. So the iid Bernoulli count process is an additional stationary-renewal realization, not the forced content of Paper I.

In the stationary iid Bernoulli realization, if the conditional click probability at every opportunity is a constant

$$
\pi_j=p_d=1-q_d,
$$

then

$$
N_n-np_d
$$

is the lattice record martingale, and the no-click skeleton is

$$
S(nd)=q_d^n.
$$

If one additionally chooses the dense-compatible sampling

$$
q_d=e^{-\kappa d},\qquad p_d=1-e^{-\kappa d},
$$

then

$$
S(nd)=e^{-\kappa nd}.
$$

This is not a derivation of `d`, and it is not forced by the sparse skeleton alone. It is the Bernoulli-renewal realization of the sparse skeleton once a lattice/mode spacing and stationary iid opportunity law have been chosen. The receipt verifies that special realization for `d=0.0625`, `kappa=0.73`, with residuals below `1e-120`.

The important distinction:

- dense regime: `A_chi=kappa chi` for all `chi`;
- sparse regime: `A_n=sum_{j<n}E[Y_j|F_j^rec]` on the seal opportunity lattice;
- stationary iid Bernoulli sparse realization: `A_n=n p_d`;
- inter-seal profile: still open.

## 5. Persistent hidden-rate mixtures: the no-aging theorem

The martingale wording exposes a constraint that the Poisson slogan hides.

Suppose there are persistent hidden sectors `alpha`, each with its own static homogeneous dense click rate `kappa_alpha`, and the record initially sees only a prior `p_alpha`. This is a specific model class: a static mixture of Poisson rates. It is not a no-go for refreshed hidden sectors, mark-only hidden structure, deterministic schedules whose projected compensator stays flat, or hidden variables in general.

For a no-click history, the survival is

$$
S(\chi)=\sum_\alpha p_\alpha e^{-\kappa_\alpha\chi}.
$$

The record hazard after no clicks to content `chi` is not the initial average. It is the posterior average

$$
h(\chi)=\sum_\alpha p_\alpha(\chi)\kappa_\alpha,
$$

where

$$
p_\alpha(\chi)=\frac{p_\alpha e^{-\kappa_\alpha\chi}}{S(\chi)}.
$$

Different hidden rates are learned by silence: high-rate sectors are progressively ruled out by no-click records. Differentiating gives the exact identity

$$
\frac{dh}{d\chi}=-\operatorname{Var}(\kappa_\alpha\mid \text{no click to }\chi)\le 0.
$$

[DERIVED]

Therefore the hazard is constant in `chi` iff the posterior variance is zero for all `chi`, i.e. iff all active sectors have the same `kappa_alpha`. [NO-GO]

This is a real restriction on hidden variables and deterministic machines:

> a hidden sector may be unobserved, but it may not create record-inferable hazard aging.

Having the right **average** rate at `chi=0` is not enough. The dense record martingale requires the right **conditional projected rate**

$$
\mathbb{E}[\lambda_{\mathrm{hid}}(\chi)\mid \mathcal{F}_{\chi-}^{\mathrm{rec}}]=\kappa
$$

after every record history.

For this static-rate mixture the posterior after a history with `m` clicks by content `chi`, ignoring the common ordered click-time factor, is

$$
p_\alpha(\mathrm{history})\propto p_\alpha \kappa_\alpha^m e^{-\kappa_\alpha\chi}.
$$

The next no-click stretch again evolves by the same formula, with

$$
\frac{dh}{d\chi}
=-\operatorname{Var}(\kappa_\alpha\mid \mathrm{history\ and\ no\ click\ through\ }\chi).
$$

Thus the only way a persistent static rate mixture has a flat projected hazard for every history is the degenerate single-rate case.

The receipt uses a two-sector example with rates `0.20` and `2.10`, weights chosen so the initial mean is exactly `kappa=0.73`. It verifies:

- mean rate equals `0.73` to `1.31e-141`;
- the survival differs from `exp(-0.73 chi)` by `0.255...`;
- the hazard drops by `0.5299...` after no-click evidence;
- `dh/dchi + Var(kappa | no click)=0` to `4.10e-143`;
- survival multiplicativity fails by `0.09678...`.

This is the clean hidden-variable constraint in its proper scope. Deterministic ontology is admissible; persistent hidden-rate heterogeneity visible through the record filtration is not.

## 6. Deterministic machines: the projected compensator

Paper XX built a deterministic Bernoulli suspension whose record projection realizes the dense exponential law. In the martingale language, the condition on such machines is:

$$
\xi^{p,\mathrm{rec}}(d\chi)=A(d\chi),
$$

where `xi(dchi)=sum_k delta_{chi_k}(dchi)` is the seal-crossing random measure after evidence-time reparametrization, and `xi^{p,rec}` is its dual predictable projection onto the committed-record filtration. In the dense simple regime this reads

$$
\xi^{p,\mathrm{rec}}(d\chi)=\kappa\,d\chi.
$$

That is the deterministic version of the record law. It does not require the hidden dynamics to be stochastic, but it does require the invariant measure and record partition to project to the allowed predictable drift.

For a sealed record machine

$$
M=(X,\Sigma,\mu,T,\Pi_{\mathrm{rec}},E,\rho),
$$

the requirements sharpen to:

1. `mu` is invariant under the deterministic update/flow;
2. `E` is the orthogonal conditional-expectation seal onto the committed-record sigma-algebra;
3. `chi` is an additive nonnegative evidence cocycle;
4. the projected click measure has compensator `A` in the committed-record filtration, dense case `A_chi=kappa chi`;
5. finite-valued observed laws still satisfy the finite positive-cone criterion;
6. Bell-correlated joint readouts still fail local factorization under measurement independence, as in Paper XX.

The `p21` receipt now checks the Paper XX suspension at the count-law level: integrating the renewal law of iid exponential roof intervals gives

$$
\Pr(N_t=n)=e^{-\kappa t}\frac{(\kappa t)^n}{n!}
$$

for `n=0,...,10`, maximum gap `1.64e-142`. This is the count-process pushforward behind the projected dense compensator; Paper XX's survival check was the zero-count member of the same law.

The finite automaton obstruction from Paper XX becomes sharper in this language, but the scope must stay exact. A closed finite deterministic automaton with finite state set, deterministic update, stationary initial measure, no external input tape, and no non-atomic hidden reservoir cannot realize an exact unbounded dense compensator `A_chi=kappa chi`. For a first hit of a target set, the support is bounded before repeat plus a possible atom at infinity; for repeated intervals, closed finite orbits are eventually periodic. Finite machines can approximate finite horizons, and finite families may approach a limit, but an exact dense clock requires leaving the closed finite-automaton class by adding an infinite reservoir, non-atomic measure, external tape, continuum/large-state limit, or explicit stochastic coarse-graining.

So the record martingale does not say "deterministic machines are impossible." It says exact deterministic machines need enough hidden reservoir and the correct record partition to keep the projected compensator flat.

## 7. Multi-channel counts and entanglement guardrail

In the dense simple regime, the scalar martingale fixes the total predictable count drift:

$$
N_\chi-\kappa\chi.
$$

If the record count decomposes into channels,

$$
N_\chi=\sum_i N_\chi^i,
$$

then the dense scalar principle fixes only

$$
\sum_i\lambda_i(\chi)=\kappa
$$

at the predictable level. It does **not** by itself fix each channel law, each mark distribution, or the joint entangling complement. A stronger marked principle would have to state additional martingales such as

$$
N_\chi^i-\int_0^\chi \lambda_i(u)\,du.
$$

In the general compensator language, this is just `N-A` for the total count; channel laws require their own marked compensators. This matters because Paper I's multiplicativity is sequential, not parallel. The record martingale is a law for predictable drift along a committed chain. It does not imply independence of parallel chains, and it does not close the Bell/joint click-law problem. The entanglement guardrail remains:

- operational no-signaling must hold;
- Bell-local deterministic response functions remain forbidden under measurement independence;
- the joint contextual/nonfactorized readout remains inside the `Qtilde` envelope;
- scalar count compensation does not determine the entangling content `chi_AB`.

Thus the martingale principle constrains the click count without collapsing the open joint-record structure.

## 8. Compensator consistency and the anomaly analogy

The useful analogy to famous string-theory moves is not "this is AdS/CFT" and not "this is literally a worldsheet law." The analogy is the form of reasoning:

> impose a consistency condition on the observable/encoded side, and allow only hidden/bulk stories whose projection realizes it.

In string language, one often gets spacetime restrictions by demanding that a lower-dimensional consistency condition have no anomaly. Here the record analogy, in the dense simple case, is:

$$
\mathbb{E}[dN_\chi\mid \mathcal{F}_{\chi-}^{\mathrm{rec}}]-\kappa\,d\chi=0.
$$

The "anomaly" is any record-predictable excess or deficit of clicks after the lawful evidence charge. More generally it is the excess drift after subtracting `A`.

This remains an analogy, not a literal string/gauge anomaly calculation. The mathematical content is compensator consistency plus uniqueness: after the process and record filtration are fixed, if a compensator exists it is unique up to indistinguishability. Therefore in the dense simple regime `kappa chi` is not just a convenient parametrization; it is the entire predictable scalar drift if the principle holds.

The analogy should still be kept scoped. It does not derive spacetime, does not derive `G`, does not solve the tensor/mode walls, and does not prove that a hidden substrate exists. It supplies a cleaner consistency target for any substrate:

`project to the record martingale, or fail as a click-law substrate`.

## 9. What this constrains

The martingale principle constrains the click law in four precise ways.

1. **It identifies the predictable scalar drift as the law-level object.** The general law is `N-A`. In the dense simple regime, `A_chi=kappa chi`, not an arbitrary increasing function. Any dense `A(chi)` with nonconstant derivative is record-visible aging.

2. **It rules out nondegenerate persistent static hidden-rate mixtures.** Hidden variables may exist, but in the static-rate mixture model, if sectors change the click rate and records can update their posterior from click/no-click histories, the hazard drifts by `-Var(kappa_alpha | record)`.

3. **It recovers exponential/Poisson only under the dense simple natural-record assumptions.** This keeps Paper I intact while preventing overclaim in sparse, marked, hidden, or enlarged-filtration settings.

4. **It separates count law from channel law.** Total count compensation does not fix sparse spacing, inter-seal coherence, mark distribution, entanglement, tensor product, or mode selection.

The most compact statement:

> the click-law is the unique predictable evidence-to-count ledger; Poisson is its dense simple natural-filtration realization.

## 10. What remains open

1. **Origin of `kappa`. [OPEN]** The compensator formulation still has the one free scale/calibration. It does not evade the Paper I/Paper XVII scale wall.

2. **Why this martingale principle? [OPEN]** The paper proposes the record martingale as the cleaner fundamental statement. It is motivated by refinement consistency, deterministic-machine neutrality, and compensator uniqueness. It is not yet derived from an even deeper axiom.

3. **Sparse spacing and profile. [OPEN]** The sparse compensator gives a lattice martingale once the opportunity process is specified. Paper I itself forces only the first-survival skeleton. Neither statement chooses `d` or the inter-seal coherent profile.

4. **Joint marked laws. [OPEN]** The scalar martingale does not determine the Bell-safe joint readout or `chi_AB`.

5. **Invariant measure origin. [OPEN]** Deterministic realizations still require an invariant hidden measure. The martingale principle constrains its projection; it does not derive the measure.

6. **Manifoldlikeness and covariance. [OPEN]** A process can have the correct record compensator and still fail the C2/Paper XI covariance and manifoldlikeness gates.

## 12. Conclusion

The sentence "clicks are Poisson in `chi`" is correct in the dense simple regime, but it is not the best foundational sentence. It sounds like a primitive stochastic mechanism. The better general sentence is:

$$
N-A\quad\text{is the record martingale}.
$$

That says the records have a fair evidence ledger: `A` is the unique predictable click charge, and the residual count has no record-visible drift. In the dense natural-record regime, `A_chi=kappa chi`, so the dense slogan becomes `N_chi-kappa chi` and is equivalent to the Poisson/exponential law. In the sparse regime, the compensator is the predictable lattice compensator; the iid Bernoulli lattice martingale is a stationary renewal realization, not forced by the first-survival skeleton alone. In deterministic hidden machines it becomes a projected-compensator condition. In persistent static hidden-rate mixtures it forbids record-inferable rate heterogeneity, because silence itself updates the posterior hazard.

So the martingale principle does not replace Paper I. It refactors it into the ontology-neutral form the later papers were asking for:

> the law is not "nature samples a Poisson clock"; the law is "records admit no predictable click anomaly beyond the compensator `A`" -- and in the dense simple regime, `A=kappa chi`.

That is the new value.
