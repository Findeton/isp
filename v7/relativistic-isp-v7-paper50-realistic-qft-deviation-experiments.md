# Relativistic ISP v7 - Paper L

# Realistic Experiments For Record-Law Deviations From QFT

## 0. Purpose

This paper asks whether one can realistically probe record-law behavior outside
ordinary QFT predictions without using black holes, singularities, or the big
bang.

The answer is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{yes, but only as anomaly searches in regimes where QFT-readiness gates
are stressed, not as arbitrary direct manipulation of records.}
}
$$

The guiding rule is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{a plausible experiment must target a finite gate failure:
Born/amplitude, source, locality, manifoldness, tail, or typed-net closure.}
}
$$

## 1. What Would Count As A Record-Law Deviation?

Let `p` be the controlled experimental record panel.  Ordinary QFT predicts:

$$
\boxed{
\Pr_{\rm QFT}(Y=y\mid p).
}
$$

The record-history law predicts:

$$
\boxed{
\Pr_{\rm rec}(Y=y\mid p)
=
\frac{\sum_{H\in\Gamma_B(p),Y(H)=y}h_B^\star(H)}
{\sum_{H\in\Gamma_B(p)}h_B^\star(H)}.
}
$$

A candidate deviation is:

$$
\boxed{
\Delta_Y
=
\Pr_{\rm rec}(Y=y\mid p)-\Pr_{\rm QFT}(Y=y\mid p)
}
$$

above calibrated environmental and instrumental error.

The record-law signal is not "anything weird."  It must survive the ordinary
QFT model, detector model, environment model, and boundary/source model.

## 2. Gate Taxonomy

The relevant finite gates are:

1. **Born/amplitude gate:** probabilities are projections of positive histories
   or licensed pre-commitment amplitudes.
2. **History-tail gate:** older or outside records do not alter the panel above
   tolerance.
3. **Manifold/continuum gate:** the local record packet admits a stable
   Lorentzian continuum readout.
4. **Typed-source gate:** matter/source residues are printed as stable typed
   sectors.
5. **Locality/net gate:** separated finite regions commute or factor as QFT
   expects after typed shared carriers are accounted for.
6. **Ward/gauge gate:** finite charge/source balances close.
7. **Amplitude/decoherence gate:** persistent unprinted interference forces a
   decoherence-functional layer.

Every experiment below targets one or more of these gates.

## 3. Tier Ranking

The most realistic experiments are not those with largest energies.  They are
those where:

1. the QFT prediction is extremely clean;
2. environmental decoherence can be ledgered;
3. the experiment contains many controllable histories or paths;
4. the output is an interference, entanglement, or locality null test;
5. the anomaly has a record-law scaling, not just a hardware scaling.

The best targets are therefore:

$$
\boxed{
\text{multipath interference, macroscopic superposition, tabletop gravity,
precision Lorentz/locality tests, and process-memory tomography.}
}
$$

## 4. Experiment Class A: Higher-Order Interference

Born's rule forbids irreducible third-order interference for single-particle
linear evolution.

For three paths `A,B,C`, define:

$$
\boxed{
I_3
=
P_{ABC}
-P_{AB}-P_{AC}-P_{BC}
+P_A+P_B+P_C.
}
$$

Standard quantum mechanics gives:

$$
\boxed{
I_3=0
}
$$

under the correct linear single-particle assumptions.

### Record-Law Interest

Persistent nonzero `I_3` after all known nonlinear, detector, and path
reconstruction corrections would indicate a failure of the usual
amplitude/Born gate:

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal QDef_B^{amp}>\epsilon.
}
$$

### Realistic Platforms

1. photonic triple-slit or multiport interferometers;
2. single-spin qutrit experiments;
3. atom/BEC multipath interferometry;
4. many-particle interference, where generalized Sorkin parameters may amplify
   sensitivity.

### Main Warning

Nonlinear evolution can generate apparent higher-order interference while
remaining ordinary quantum physics.  Therefore the clean target is not merely:

$$
\boxed{
I_3\ne0.
}
$$

It is:

$$
\boxed{
I_3\ne0
\quad
\text{after the nonlinear QFT/control model explains all ordinary
contributions.}
}
$$

## 5. Experiment Class B: Many-Path And Many-Particle Born Tests

Many-particle interference gives a richer hierarchy of forbidden higher-order
terms.  If `M` particles are used, quantum theory predicts vanishing of high
enough interference order.

The record-law reason to care is that a hidden record-history projection could
produce tiny residuals that scale with:

1. number of paths;
2. number of particles;
3. path-history overlap;
4. detector record compression.

The target observable is a scaling law:

$$
\boxed{
I_{r}^{(M)}
\sim
\text{record-boundary residual}
}
$$

not a single anomaly.

## 6. Experiment Class C: Macroscopic Superposition And Collapse-Like Residuals

Macroscopic superposition experiments stress the transition between:

$$
\boxed{
\text{positive committed histories}
}
$$

and:

$$
\boxed{
\text{pre-commitment amplitude/decoherence layer}.
}
$$

Collapse models are not SHARD, but they are experimentally useful foils
because they predict mass/size-dependent deviations from ordinary unitary
quantum mechanics.

### Realistic Platforms

1. levitated nanospheres;
2. optomechanical resonators;
3. superconducting mechanical systems;
4. matter-wave interferometry with high-mass particles;
5. space-based long free-fall interferometry such as MAQRO-like concepts.

### Record-Law Signature

A SHARD-like residual would be:

$$
\boxed{
\Gamma_{\rm extra}
\ne
\Gamma_{\rm env}
}
$$

with a scaling tied to:

1. record complexity;
2. boundary incompleteness;
3. history depth;
4. detector commitment structure;
5. typed source closure;

rather than only mass or separation.

### Why This Is Realistic

These experiments are already designed to separate ordinary environmental
decoherence from possible collapse-like deviations.  The record-law use is to
reinterpret any unexplained residual as a candidate history-tail or
commitment-gate defect.

## 7. Experiment Class D: BMV-Type Tabletop Gravity Entanglement

BMV experiments ask whether gravity can mediate entanglement between two
masses placed in spatial superposition.

The ordinary claim:

$$
\boxed{
\text{if gravity mediates entanglement, the mediator has nonclassical
features.}
}
$$

### Record-Law Interest

This probes the typed-source and geometry/source gates:

$$
\boxed{
\operatorname{SrcDef}_B,\quad
\mathcal B_B^{Ein},\quad
\mathcal QDef_B^{loc}.
}
$$

If the gravitational interaction produces entanglement exactly as expected,
the finite typed source/net picture gains support.

If it produces anomalous dephasing, anomalous phase, or no entanglement where
ordinary quantum gravity-inspired modeling expects it, the anomaly targets the
source/geometry closure layer.

### Realistic Status

The experiment is extremely hard but much more realistic than Planck-energy or
black-hole probes.  It uses mesoscopic masses, long coherence, and precise
control of electromagnetic backgrounds.

## 8. Experiment Class E: Lorentz/CPT/Clock-Network Tests

If the manifold/continuum gate is slightly imperfect, the cleanest laboratory
signature may be tiny anisotropic or frame-dependent residue.

Precision clocks, Penning traps, atom interferometers, spin precession
systems, neutrinos, and photons already constrain Lorentz/CPT violation in the
Standard-Model Extension framework.

### Record-Law Mapping

The relevant defects are:

$$
\boxed{
D^{caus},\quad D^{sig},\quad D^{proj},\quad \mathcal QDef^{loc}.
}
$$

The SHARD-specific search would not merely fit SME coefficients.  It would ask
whether any residual correlates with:

1. record-boundary configuration;
2. entanglement/context of the measurement;
3. detector commitment history;
4. gravitational/source environment;
5. scale-window transitions.

## 9. Experiment Class F: Process-Memory Tomography

Ordinary effective QFT in a well-isolated lab often behaves Markovian at the
chosen coarse-graining.  SHARD is explicitly history-first and non-Markovian.

Thus one realistic class of tests is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{look for controlled non-Markov memory not accounted for by ordinary
environment models.}
}
$$

Platforms:

1. superconducting qubits;
2. trapped ions;
3. photonic quantum processors;
4. spin defects in diamond;
5. optomechanical sensors.

The target is not "non-Markovianity exists"; ordinary open quantum systems are
often non-Markovian.  The target is a residual memory functional that survives
full environment ledgering and scales with record-boundary changes.

## 10. Experiment Class G: Detector-Commitment Dependence

The record framework cares about commitment: when a record becomes an
indivisible event for the bounded panel.

A possible experiment compares setups with the same QFT amplitudes but
different detector record architectures:

1. threshold detector versus number-resolving detector;
2. immediate amplification versus reversible premeasurement;
3. local readout versus delayed entangled readout;
4. redundant environmental records versus isolated readout.

### Signature

Standard QFT plus measurement theory should predict the same operational
statistics after all detector details are modeled.

A record-law deviation would be a remaining dependence on commitment
architecture:

$$
\boxed{
\Delta_Y
=
F(\text{commitment structure})
}
$$

after ordinary detector response corrections.

## 11. Experiment Class H: Entanglement With Engineered Boundary Complexity

If "entanglement is shared record structure," then a practical stress test is
not merely long-distance entanglement.  It is entanglement under controlled
boundary complexity.

Vary:

1. shielding and isolation layers;
2. redundant environmental records;
3. detector causal ordering;
4. number of intermediate reversible couplers;
5. spatial separation while keeping field-theory channels fixed.

Look for:

$$
\boxed{
\text{an unexplained dependence on boundary/history complexity rather than
Hamiltonian coupling alone.}
}
$$

## 12. Experiment Class I: Vacuum And Casimir-Like Record Boundaries

Casimir and dynamical-Casimir systems are ordinary QFT arenas, not automatic
beyond-QFT probes.  But they strongly stress boundary conditions.

Record-law relevance:

$$
\boxed{
\text{boundary work and source completion are central objects.}
}
$$

Possible target:

1. compare dynamically switched boundary conditions with different record
   commitment architectures;
2. test for residual noise or correlation not captured by QFT boundary models;
3. use superconducting circuits where boundaries can be modulated precisely.

This is lower priority than Born, macro-superposition, BMV, and Lorentz tests,
because ordinary QFT boundary modeling is powerful.

## 13. Experiment Class J: Quantum Simulation Of Nonmanifold Record Graphs

One can engineer artificial record graphs in quantum simulators:

1. trapped-ion graph states;
2. superconducting qubit hypergraphs;
3. photonic cluster states;
4. programmable tensor-network devices.

These do not test whether spacetime is manifoldlike directly.  They test the
finite algebra:

$$
\boxed{
\text{how typed nets fail when the underlying record graph is intentionally
nonmanifold.}
}
$$

This can calibrate the manifold/QFT-readiness defects in a controlled
laboratory setting.

Do not claim:

}
$$

Do claim:

$$
\boxed{
\text{we can stress the effective QFT gates and search for above-tolerance
residuals.}
}
$$

Do not claim:

$$
\boxed{
\text{any anomaly is SHARD.}
}
$$

Do require:

$$
\boxed{
\text{the anomaly must survive ordinary QFT, detector, environment, and
boundary-source modeling.}
}
$$

## 15. Experimental Priority List

### Priority 1: Multipath Born/Sorkin Tests

Best for the amplitude/Born gate.  Use atoms/BEC or many-particle interference
if possible, because optical nonlinearities can fake higher-order
interference.

### Priority 2: Levitated Macro-Superposition

Best for commitment/decoherence/history-tail gates.  Requires brutal
environment accounting.

### Priority 3: BMV Gravity Entanglement

Best for typed source/gravity mediator closure.  Hard but conceptually direct.

### Priority 4: Lorentz/CPT Clock Networks

Best for continuum/cone/signature defects.  Already highly constrained, but
record-boundary correlations could be a new analysis axis.

### Priority 5: Process-Memory Tomography

Best for non-Markovian history tails.  Must beat ordinary open-system memory
models.

### Priority 6: Detector-Commitment Architecture

Best for the distinctive record idea.  Hard because measurement theory already
absorbs many detector changes.

## 17. What Would Be Most SHARD-Like?

The most SHARD-like anomaly would not be a random small offset.  It would obey
three patterns:

1. it is invisible in simple microscopic systems;
2. it grows with record/boundary/history complexity;
3. it appears at commitment, projection, or source-closure bottlenecks.

Symbolically:

$$
\boxed{
\Delta_Y
\propto
\operatorname{BoundaryWork}
+\operatorname{HistoryTail}
+\operatorname{CommitmentDefect}
}
$$

and not merely:

$$
\boxed{
\Delta_Y\propto E
}

## 18. Hostile Review

### Review 1: "These are all already QFT experiments."

Mostly accepted.  The experiments are QFT experiments until they show a
controlled residual.  That is exactly why they are realistic.

### Review 2: "Higher-order interference can come from nonlinear optics."

Accepted.  Nonlinear loopholes must be modeled first.  Atom/BEC and
many-particle protocols are attractive because they can vary the source of
nonlinearity.

### Review 3: "Collapse-like residuals are not record-law residuals."

Accepted.  Collapse models are foils.  A record-law residual needs different
scaling: boundary/history/commitment dependence, not only mass localization.

### Review 4: "BMV is quantum gravity, not QFT deviation."

Accepted.  It tests the source/geometry interface, which is exactly where QFT
on a fixed background may be insufficient.

### Review 5: "Lorentz tests are already extremely constrained."

Accepted.  The new angle is conditional correlations with record-boundary and
commitment structure, not generic SME coefficient fishing.

### Review 6: "Process memory is ordinary open quantum systems."

Accepted.  The target is residual memory after the ordinary environment ledger
is closed.

## 19. Campaign Result

The realistic experimental program is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{do not try to break QFT directly;}
\quad
\text{stress the finite gates where QFT-readiness is licensed.}
}
$$

The cleanest near-term targets are:

$$
\boxed{
\text{Born/Sorkin interference, macroscopic superposition, BMV gravity
entanglement, Lorentz/CPT precision tests, and process-memory tomography.}
}
$$

The strongest SHARD-specific design principle is:

$$
\boxed{
\text{vary record-boundary and commitment architecture while holding the
ordinary QFT Hamiltonian model fixed.}
}
$$

If no residual appears, QFT-readiness is reinforced.

If a residual appears and survives ordinary modeling, it becomes a candidate
record-law gate failure.
