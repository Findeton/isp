## Abstract

Paper IV established that the joint record click-law on two entangled chains is a *forced skeleton with a free complement*: the multiplicative survival shape `exp(−κ·σ_AB)` transfers verbatim to the joint scalar, while the entangling content `χ_AB` — the part of the joint law not fixed by the two marginals — is a free Tier-A input, constrained only by no-signaling and Tsirelson. This paper asks whether a *transverse* self-consistency principle, the parallel-chain analog of the one-diamond KL = Fisher balance that fixes the single-diamond constants, could promote `χ_AB` from free to forced. The answer is a sharp no-go with a real positive corollary. **(i) [NO-GO]** Any self-consistency condition the record substrate can express is a positive-semidefiniteness constraint on the shared moment matrix of the two chains — the two chains meet only through the joint moment `⟨A_x B_y⟩` (commutation *on the state*), never through a global Hilbert tensor product. Under the substrate-modeling premise that a record interface commits a single joint outcome pair, furnishing only degree-`(1,1)` cross moments, the feasible set of such a condition is the almost-quantum set `Q̃ = Q^{1+AB}`, which strictly contains the quantum set `Q`. This is **[NECESSARY]** — it reproduces the entire Paper IV envelope (no-signaling, the Tsirelson ceiling `CHSH ≤ 2√2`, and exclusion of the PR-box) — but **not sufficient**: `Q̃` overshoots `Q` (e.g. on the `I3322` facet, which needs a higher relaxation level). So `χ_AB` is free within `Q̃`. The supraquantum gap is contingent on that premise (a substrate that committed higher joint moments would reach a higher relaxation level, shrinking toward `Q`), but the headline no-go is *not*: the records furnish no global composite state space at all, so at **every** finite moment level the **global tensor product** is missing (commuting-on-the-state is not tensor-factorization — Tsirelson's problem), and `χ_AB` is free strictly above `Q` regardless. **(ii)** The missing structure has one source with two faces. The records furnish only the shared moment `⟨A_x B_y⟩` and **no composite state space**, so they lack both the **global tensor product** that would close `Q̃ ∖ Q` (the moment-form gap) and **local tomography** `K_AB = K_A·K_B` — the complex-over-real selector — that would cut a tensor-product theory down to `Q` (the state-space-form gap; a real-vector rebit theory *has* a tensor product yet fails local tomography). The record seal is provably blind to both: the orthogonal-projection / `q = 2` screen that fixes the single-chain law holds identically over a real and a complex Hilbert space, and a rebit theory is the explicit foil — same marginals, same Tsirelson bound, different `χ_AB`. **(iii) [INHERITED]** The same self-consistency that fails to pin `χ_AB` succeeds in *bounding* it: the moment-positivity that defines the no-go arena (`Γ ⪰ 0`) caps `CHSH` at exactly `2√2` — so the Tsirelson ceiling is a *theorem* of record self-consistency, machine-verified, upgrading Paper IV's externally-*respected* bound to an inherited one. The records additionally own the static information-theoretic core of Information Causality (a non-negative, no-signaling-consistent, data-processing-monotone, chain-rule mutual information on the record KL functional), an independent witness that the bound is Tsirelson's — one operational lever (the random-access-code protocol, not forced by the seal-law) short of full Information-Causality-as-theorem. The structure is the program's recurring shape: self-consistency fixes the *envelope* and never the *last record-blind distinguishing input* — here the composite state space, as elsewhere the absolute scale.

## 1. Introduction: the transverse question

The single-chain record click-law is forced. Paper I derived its survival shape `S(χ) = exp(−κχ)` uniquely in the divisible regime (Cauchy plus refinement-multiplicativity) and a rigid power-law skeleton `S(nd) = S(d)^n` in the indivisible one, with one free scale `κ` and a free inter-seal coherence profile. The single-diamond *constants* are pinned by a self-consistency principle: paper4's balance of accumulated KL content against Fisher capacity, `C(η) = J(η)`, with a unique crossing `η_A = 1.0903…`. That principle is the template for everything the records fix without external input.

Paper IV extended the click-law to two entangled chains. The joint survival law splits exactly,
`σ_AB = σ_A + σ_B + I_σ`,
over the product of the two commit-orders; the multiplicative shape is forced on the joint scalar, but the cross term — the entangling content `χ_AB` — is *not* fixed by the two marginals. It is a free Tier-A input, two-sided in sign, constrained only by no-signaling and by the Tsirelson ceiling `CHSH ≤ 2√2` (with the PR-box excluded). Paper IV took those constraints as given; the question this paper settles is whether they are *all* the constraints — whether a **transverse self-consistency principle**, the parallel-chain analog of the one-diamond `C = J` balance, could force `χ_AB` the way `C = J` forces `η_A`.

This is the last open lever of the joint law. If such a principle existed and forced `χ_AB`, the records would carry a derived, non-classical correlation content — the strongest possible positive result. We show instead that no principle the record substrate can host can do better than the *almost-quantum* boundary, and we identify, by name, the one input that is missing. The discipline throughout is pre-geometric: every quantity is a record-internal probability, KL number, or moment of the transition law; no spacetime, Hilbert tensor product, or metric is used as input — the absence of the tensor product is precisely the result.

## 2. The transverse arena: what a record self-consistency principle can be

A self-consistency principle is a closure condition on the records' own correlations. The single-chain principle `C = J` is a balance between two functionals of the *same* one-diamond law. Its transverse analog must be a balance condition on the *joint* correlations of two chains `A` and `B`, each carrying its own commit-order, that share entangled content.

The decisive structural fact is how the two chains are coupled. In the record substrate the two chains are not glued into a global state space; they meet only where their measurements meet — through the joint moments `⟨A_x B_y⟩` of the bipartite correlation. A self-consistency condition is therefore a positivity requirement on the matrix of these moments: the Gram/moment matrix `Γ` whose entries are `⟨A_x⟩`, `⟨B_y⟩`, `⟨A_x B_y⟩`, and the within-chain moments, must be positive-semidefinite (it is a matrix of inner products of the substrate's own statistical observables). The coupling between the chains is the algebraic relation `B_y A_x = A_x B_y` *on the state* — the joint moment is symmetric — and **not** a tensor factorization `H_A ⊗ H_B`. There is no global product space to factor through; there is only the shared moment and its symmetry.

This is exactly the data that defines a moment-matrix relaxation of the quantum correlation problem at the level **`1 + AB`**: the identity, the single operators `A_x`, `B_y`, and their products `A_x B_y`, with PSD-ness of `Γ` and the on-state commutation as the only constraints. Receipt `t1` builds `Γ` for the CHSH scenario and confirms the load-bearing point: the chains meet through `⟨A_x B_y⟩ = E_xy` with `B_y A_x = A_x B_y` on the state, *not* through a tensor product. Any self-consistency principle the substrate can express is a constraint of this form — a balance or extremum over the PSD cone of such moment matrices.

Two claims must be separated here. That a record self-consistency principle is a *moment-positivity* condition (it lives on the Gram matrix of the substrate's own observables) is the robust structural claim — call it **(C-form)**. That this condition sits at level `1 + AB` and no higher — call it **(C-level)** — is a **substrate-modeling premise**: a record interface commits a single joint outcome pair `(a, b)`, furnishing only the degree-`(1,1)` cross moment `⟨A_x B_y⟩` (and the within-chain moments), not a higher cross-party word such as `⟨A_x A_{x'} B_y⟩` that would bind two successive non-commuting `A`-seals to a `B`-seal and reach a higher relaxation level. Paper IV's joint click-law supplies exactly the pairwise commit `⟨A_x B_y⟩`, which fixes the level at `1 + AB`; we flag `(C-level)` as that premise, not a theorem. The qualitative no-go does not depend on it — see §3.

## 3. The no-go: self-consistency lands on the almost-quantum set [NO-GO]

The feasible set of correlations admitted by PSD-ness of the `1 + AB` moment matrix has a name. By the definition of Navascués, Guryanova, Hoban and Acín (2015), the set of bipartite correlations realizable by a moment matrix at level `1 + AB` — positive-semidefinite, with on-state commutation but **no Hilbert tensor factorization** — is the **almost-quantum set `Q̃ = Q^{1+AB}`**. Under premise `(C-level)` every self-consistency principle of the kind §2 permits therefore selects a subset of `Q̃`.

**The qualitative no-go is level-independent. [NO-GO]** The conclusion that `χ_AB` is free *strictly above* `Q` survives whether or not `(C-level)` holds. At *every* finite NPA level (each an outer relaxation containing the commuting-operator set), and in the commuting-operator limit of the hierarchy, the records still furnish no global Hilbert tensor factorization: commutation on the state is strictly weaker than a tensor product — the negative resolution of Tsirelson's problem, the commuting-operator and tensor-product correlation sets are not equal (Slofstra 2019; Ji–Natarajan–Vidick–Wright–Yuen 2020). So the record substrate's self-consistency admits a set that contains `Q` and is *not* cut down to `Q` at any level the records can reach; the entangling content `χ_AB` is free strictly above the quantum set regardless of `(C-level)`. What `(C-level)` controls is only *how far* above `Q` — `Q̃` itself if the level is `1 + AB`, a tighter supraquantum set if the records committed higher moments — never whether the gap exists. None of these sets can be shrunk below the supraquantum boundary without importing a constraint that is not a moment-positivity condition; the rest of this section works at the `1 + AB` level fixed by `(C-level)`, where the feasible set is exactly `Q̃`.

**The envelope falls out — `Q̃` is necessary. [NECESSARY]** The almost-quantum boundary reproduces the entire Paper IV envelope, with nothing imported. Receipt `t1` sweeps the isotropic CHSH correlator and solves the SDP for the largest admissible value:

- the moment-matrix self-consistency **caps** `CHSH` at the Tsirelson value: NPA level-1 maximum `2.8284271250` against `2√2 = 2.8284271247`, gap `|max − 2√2| = 2.78 × 10⁻¹⁰`;
- the **PR-box** `(CHSH = 4)` is **excluded** — the correlator `E = (1, 1, 1, −1)` is infeasible in the `1 + AB` moment matrix;
- the **Tsirelson point** `(+,+,+,−)/√2` (`CHSH = 2√2`) is feasible/optimal, and a point just past it (`CHSH = 2.9084`) is infeasible — a sharp boundary.

So no-signaling (the marginals decouple in `Γ`), the Tsirelson ceiling, and PR-box exclusion are not extra postulates; they are entailments of moment positivity. A quantum (tensor-product) realization gives a moment matrix of exactly this form, so `Q ⊆ Q̃`: any transverse self-consistency principle that the records can host **necessarily** admits at least the quantum envelope.

**`Q̃` is not sufficient — it strictly overshoots `Q`. [NO-GO]** The containment is strict: `Q̃ ⊋ Q`. The CHSH facet does not witness the gap (there `Q̃` and `Q` coincide at `2√2`), but higher Bell functionals do — the `I3322` inequality separates them, and certifying the quantum boundary there requires a moment-matrix relaxation of level `≥ 4`, which the substrate's `1 + AB` data cannot express. A principle confined to `1 + AB` moment positivity therefore admits supraquantum correlations in `Q̃ ∖ Q`; it cannot single out `Q`, let alone a single `χ_AB` within it. The gap `Q̃ ∖ Q` is **permanent** for any record-internal self-consistency principle: it is not a matter of finding the right balance condition, because every balance condition expressible on the shared moment matrix lives at or below the `1 + AB` level by construction.

**Verdict.** A transverse self-consistency principle is *necessary-not-sufficient*: it reproduces the Paper IV envelope but leaves `χ_AB` free across the whole almost-quantum set. `χ_AB` is **not** forced. The parallel-chain analog of `C = J` does not exist as a content-pinning principle — only as an envelope-pinning one.

## 4. The gap has one source: no composite state space [FORCED]

The supraquantum gap is not a vague deficiency; it traces to a single missing structure — the records furnish no composite state space at all — which shows up as two distinct, named missing inputs. The first cuts `Q̃` down to `Q`: it is the **global tensor product**, the composite factorization that the commuting-on-the-state moment data lack (§3). The second is what would then single complex quantum theory out from its real-vector twin within the tensor-product world: **local tomography** `K_AB = K_A·K_B`. These are not the same axiom — a real-vector (rebit) theory *has* a tensor product yet *fails* local tomography — but they have the same origin: a substrate with no composite state space supplies neither. Receipt `t2` makes the second quantitative through the information-dimension count `K` (the number of real parameters fixing a state), with local tomography the identity `K_AB = K_A·K_B`:

- complex quantum theory satisfies it: `K_qubit = 4`, `K_2qubit = 16 = 4·4` — **local tomography holds**;
- real-vector (rebit) theory does **not**: `K_rebit = 3`, but `K_2rebit = 10 ≠ 9 = 3·3` — a limited-holism deficit `K_AB − K_A·K_B = 1`, joint parameters invisible to local measurements.

So local tomography is the input that, *given* a tensor product, would draw the complex-vs-real boundary; the tensor product is the input that, *given* the moment data, would draw the `Q̃`-vs-`Q` boundary. The record seal is **blind to both**. The single-chain forcing that fixes the seal — orthogonal projection `E = E² = E*` and the `q = 2` Banach–Lamperti norm screen — holds *identically* in a real and a complex Hilbert space. Receipt `t2` verifies the field-blindness directly: the real (rebit) seal satisfies `E = E²` and `E = E*` exactly, the `ℓ²` norm and projection structure are unchanged, and the single-chain marginal data together with no-signaling do not pin `χ_AB`. A real-vector theory is the explicit foil: same marginals, same Tsirelson bound `2√2` to full precision, but a different entangling content `χ_AB`. Because the seal cannot tell the real theory from the complex one — and the moment data cannot tell a commuting-operator model from a tensor-product one — no principle built on the seal can supply either input, and none that fails to see the joint composite structure can pin `χ_AB`.

The two faces of the no-go — "lands on `Q̃`" (§3, the missing tensor product in moment-matrix form) and "blind to local tomography" (§4, the missing complex-over-real selector in state-space form) — are one obstruction: the records carry every constraint that lives on the shared moment matrix and none that requires a composite state space.

## 5. The positive corollary: Tsirelson is inherited [INHERITED]

The no-go has a genuine positive complement. Although the substrate cannot *pin* `χ_AB`, it does *bound* it — and the bound is Tsirelson's, inherited from the records' own self-consistency rather than imported.

**The bound is a theorem of moment-positivity.** The same condition that defines the no-go arena already supplies the ceiling. The moment matrix `Γ` is a Gram matrix of the substrate's own observables, so `Γ ⪰ 0` is forced; and §3's SDP shows `Γ ⪰ 0` caps `CHSH` at exactly `2√2` (receipt `t1`: maximum `2.8284271250` against `2√2 = 2.8284271247`, with a point just past the bound infeasible). The Tsirelson ceiling is therefore a **theorem of record self-consistency**, machine-verified — not an external postulate. This is the new content over Paper IV: there the `CHSH ≤ 2√2` ceiling and PR-box exclusion were *respected* as an external quantum bound; here they are *inherited* from `Γ ⪰ 0`, the records' own moment-positivity. What stays free is `χ_AB` *within* the ceiling; what is now sourced is the ceiling itself.

**An independent witness: the records own IC's static core.** The substrate also carries the static information-theoretic core of Information Causality, a second and independent reason the bound is Tsirelson's. In the Pawlowski–Paterek–Kaszlikowski–Scarani–Winter–Żukowski derivation, `CHSH ≤ 2√2` follows from a mutual-information functional with four properties — non-negativity, consistency (vanishing exactly on product distributions), a data-processing inequality, and a chain rule — and receipt `t3` verifies the record KL mutual information has all four (KL non-negativity, `I(A:B) = 0` iff factorized, DPI, chain-rule residual `3.63 × 10⁻¹²²`). The IC facet `V(E) = E² − ½ ≤ 0` then lands at the Tsirelson point and nowhere else (`t3`: `V < 0` sub-Tsirelson, `V = −9.7 × 10⁻¹²²` i.e. `0` exactly at `E = 1/√2`, `V > 0` super-Tsirelson, PR-box maximally violating). So the records own the static substrate of the IC route to the same bound.

**What it does not do.** Neither route pins `χ_AB`: Tsirelson's ceiling is field-blind — the real-vector foil saturates it too (§4) — so inheriting the bound is fully consistent with `χ_AB` remaining free across `Q̃`. And the IC route specifically is one lever short of full Information-Causality-as-theorem: the static functional core is present and necessary, but the *operational* random-access-code protocol that turns it into the dynamical IC statement is not itself forced by the seal-law (receipt `t3`: the static substrate is present, the operational protocol is not entailed). The honest scope is therefore "Tsirelson inherited as a bound — as a theorem via moment-positivity, and additionally witnessed by the static IC core" — a real positive result — not "Information Causality derived in full."

This is the program's distinctive posture. The entangling content `χ_AB` is free up to `Q̃`, permanently.
