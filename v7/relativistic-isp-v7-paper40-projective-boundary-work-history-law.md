## 12. Conditional Existence and Uniqueness Theorem

### Theorem 1: Positive History Weight From Convex Boundary Work

Assume:

1. `\mathcal U_{B,k}^{adm}` is a nonempty closed convex subset of a finite
   dimensional log-weight space after normalization;
2. `\mathsf{BW}_{B,k}+\Lambda_B+X_B+\mathsf{ISP}_{B,k}` is proper,
   lower-semicontinuous, record-covariant, and convex;
3. the sum is strictly convex modulo same-actual zero-residue equivalence;
4. the commitment term is finite on the admissible interior and coercive at
   the boundary;
5. all terms are nonreconstructive: none uses raw presentation multiplicity
   except through committed record projections.

Then a positive minimizer `h^*_{B,k}` exists and is unique modulo same-actual
zero residue and normalization.

**Proof.**

After normalization, the admissible log-weight domain is finite dimensional and
closed.  Coercivity prevents minimizing sequences from escaping to the boundary
or to infinity.  Lower semicontinuity gives existence by the direct method of
the calculus of variations.  Strict convexity on the quotient gives uniqueness
on quotient classes.  Because all terms are record-covariant, the minimizer is
record-covariant.  Positivity follows from the log-weight representation and
the commitment barrier. `\square`

### Theorem 2: Projective Compatibility Under Exact Marginal Penalty

Assume Theorem 1 at every depth and suppose the projective penalty is exact:

$$
\mathsf{Projective}_{B,k}(h)=0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\bar h_{B,k}
=
(\pi_{k+1\to k})_\#\bar h_{B,k+1}.
$$

Assume also that the boundary-work and drift terms are compatible with
pushforward:

$$
\mathcal F_{B,k}((\pi_{k+1\to k})_\#h)
\le
\mathcal F_{B,k+1}(h)+\varepsilon_{k}.
$$

Then the minimizers satisfy:

$$
\boxed{
\Pi_{B,k}(h^*)\le C\,\varepsilon_k
}
$$

for a stability constant `C` determined by the convexity modulus.  In the exact
case `\varepsilon_k=0`, the minimizers are projectively consistent.

**Proof Sketch.**

Strong convexity converts action excess into distance from the unique
minimizer.  Pushforward compatibility says the pushed-forward finer minimizer
is an admissible near-minimizer at the coarser depth.  Therefore its total
variation distance from the coarser minimizer is bounded by the action excess.
Since the projective penalty is exactly the TV mismatch, the stated bound
follows. `\square`

**Proof.**

### 18.3 Axioms For Primitive Center Work

Let:

$$

$$

be the local work for replacing actual center mass `\mu` by predicted center
mass `\nu`.

1. **Zero match.**

   $$
   W(\mu,\nu)=0
   \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
   \mu=\nu.
   $$

2. **No-silent positivity.**

   $$
   W(\mu,\nu)>0
   $$

   whenever committed residue is unmatched or overpredicted.

3. **Additivity over independent channels.**

   $$
   W((\mu_1,\mu_2),(\nu_1,\nu_2))
   =
   W(\mu_1,\nu_1)+W(\mu_2,\nu_2).
   $$

4. **Coarse-graining monotonicity.**

   Merging channels cannot increase the apparent work:

   $$
   W(\mu_1+\mu_2,\nu_1+\nu_2)
   \le
   W((\mu_1,\mu_2),(\nu_1,\nu_2)).
   $$

5. **Local Taylor calibration.**

   For small mismatch:

   $$
   W(\mu,\mu+\delta)
   =
   \frac{\delta^2}{2\mu}
   +O(\delta^3)
   $$

   after choosing the channel unit.

6. **Projective chain rule.**

   Refining a channel into subchannels decomposes work into coarse work plus
   conditional refinement work.

These axioms are exactly the finite no-silent requirements.  They do not
assume spacetime or manifoldlikeness.
