## 2. The seal in the indivisible-stochastic family [OWNED framing · IMPORT correspondence]

**The adjacent program.** Barandes (arXiv:2507.21192) reconstructs a quantum system as an **indivisible (non-Markovian) stochastic process** in configuration space: the stochastic-quantum correspondence makes the Hilbert space and the wavefunction *secondary* tools, derived from a division-event structure on a stochastic trajectory rather than postulated. "Indivisible" is the load-bearing word: the process cannot be subdivided into a Markov chain of intermediate transition matrices; only at *division events* does the system admit a configuration-space description with no interference.

**What the correspondence leaves open, and what the seal supplies.** The correspondence is, deliberately, a *kinematic* dictionary — it characterises which stochastic processes are quantum, not what *physically distinguishes* a division event from a non-division stretch. That is exactly the slot the SHARD seal occupies. The record click-law forces a multiplicative **survival skeleton** along the refinement axis,
```
    S(χ) = exp(−κ χ),    S(n·d) = S(d)ⁿ  (dense / sparse limits, Paper I §3.2),
```
i.e. survival is *multiplicative over stretches of one chain*. A refinement that does not seal carries `S = 1`; a sealed one decrements `S` multiplicatively in the accumulated content `χ`. This is a **physical mechanism** for indivisibility-versus-division read natively off the records: the seal *is* the event that breaks divisibility, and its rate is the forced `κ`. Paper I §3.2 and the cocycle check (`p6_transverse_nogo.py` Part 6: associator `≡ 1` sympy-exact; grouped survival grouping-independent to gap `< 10⁻¹³⁰` over `2200` random assignments) make precise that this multiplicative law composes consistently — `H²` trivial, no forced phase obstruction.

**Honest owned-vs-imported.** The indivisible / non-Markovian *process viewpoint* and the stochastic-quantum *correspondence* are Barandes's; SHARD does not claim them and does not re-derive the correspondence. What SHARD owns is the **framing** that the forced multiplicative survival is a candidate physical mechanism *for* sealing — kin in spirit (multiplicativity along refinement is a sequential-axis statement, exactly where indivisibility lives) but a **separate construction**. We also flag the boundary of the analogy precisely: Doukas (arXiv:2602.22095) is a *divisibility / emergence* result — it lifts a stochastic kernel to `B(H)` and shows Chapman–Kolmogorov divisibility of the lifted family is the decisive extra constraint (a CK-consistent CPTP family admits a Lindblad form; the off-diagonal phase is a *compressed carrier of history* not fixed by the transition kernels) — **not** a unistochastic-from-first-principles theorem. The seal-as-mechanism reading is consistent with all three (the survival skeleton is precisely a non-Markovian, history-carrying datum), but inherits none of their theorems. A direct probe of whether the seal forces the *matrix-level* transition structure settles it negatively (receipts `p18_seal_divisibility.py`, `f6_unistochastic_record_blind_probe.py`): the `q = 2` single-channel screen (Paper I §3.2) collapses the Chapman–Kolmogorov matrix to the scalar survival, so the seal forces only the scalar `S = exp(−κχ)` — **not** the matrix-level CK-divisibility that is Doukas's decisive constraint, and **not** the doubly-stochastic or unistochastic class (Barandes's assumed envelope). Two consequences are stated plainly. First, the unitary phase that would promote a doubly-stochastic kernel to a unique unitary (unistochastic) preimage is the *same* complex-over-real / `\ker R` bit that fixes `χ_AB` (a real orthogonal and a phase-dressed complex unitary give identical record moments `|U_{ij}|²` to `< 10⁻¹²¹` yet differ by phase — e.g. opposite Jarlskog sign `±0.0521`) — not a new wall. Second, the seal forces a forward-stochastic kernel but **not** double-stochasticity: an explicit sealed semigroup carries a non-uniform stationary law, so Barandes's doubly-stochastic envelope is only partly motivated by the seal — the divisibility half is not even forced (only the scalar survival is), and the unbiased-prior half is a further import. **The seal motivates the survival, never the kernel.**

A structural caution that controls the next two sections: the survival multiplicativity that the seal supplies is a property of the **sequential / refinement** axis (stretches of one chain). It is *orthogonal* to factorisation of the joint outcome law across **parallel** chains. The transverse envelope of §3 lives on that second axis; the seal mechanism does not by itself force any transverse correlation (this is the v7-paper1-companion OI/PI-consistency result: the forced multiplicative skeleton is silent on outcome-independence).

## 4. The 2026 real-quantum reopening and the corrected status of the tensor-product bit [CONTESTED · IMPORT]

**The locus.** The single bit SHARD cannot supply from inside — the one in `\ker R`, the deficit `K_{AB} − K_A K_B` — *is* the complex-over-real / local-tomography / composition-rule choice. Until 2026 the natural reading was that an experiment fixes it from *outside* (Renou et al., Nature **600**, 625 (2021), read as ruling out real QM; Chen et al., PRL **128**, 040403 (2022); Li et al. 2022), precisely as a measurement fixes `G`. That reading is now contested.

**The live 2026 controversy (balanced, side-neutral).** Three papers reopen the question by changing the **composition rule** — the precise locus SHARD names as the un-forceable bit:

- **Hoffreumon–Woods (arXiv:2603.19208)**, *Quantum theory based on real numbers cannot be experimentally falsified*: real quantum theory with the **Kronecker composition rule**, under *operational* (not product-state) independence, reproduces **all finite-network correlations** of quantum theory — empirically indistinguishable absent a quantum-theory violation.
- **Maioli–Curado–Gazeau (arXiv:2604.19482)**, *Quantum mechanics over real numbers fully reproduces standard quantum theory*: a real Kähler-space framework with a **symplectic composition rule** (replacing Kronecker) reproduces all of QM and reaches the maximal `CHSH3 = 6√2` with real variables.
- **Moradi Kalarde–Xu–Renou (Comment, arXiv:2604.07425)**: Renou et al. respond that the Hoffreumon–Woods postulate **fails in Fermionic Information Theory** and is not a general physical postulate.

**SHARD takes no side.** It is *not* claimed that real QM is correct, that Renou et al. are wrong, or that the tensor product is "really" real. Renou et al. 2021 **stands in its own framing** (its independence postulate, in which real QM is excluded). The only SHARD claim is structural and side-neutral: *whichever way the community resolves the debate, the resolution is being decided by a choice of postulate / convention* — operational versus product-state independence; whether local tomography is a law of nature — **not** by a record-internal derivation.

**The vindication argument (vindication, not proof).** SHARD's no-go always held that the tensor product / `χ_AB` is a field-blind import sitting in `\ker R`, weight-`0` — un-forceable from *inside* the records (Papers XII/XIII, three independent proofs). The natural worry was: "surely an experiment forces it from *outside*, so 'records can't' is a mere bookkeeping gap." The 2026 controversy undercuts exactly that worry. If the complex-over-real bit is a composition-rule **convention** that may be experimentally **unfixable** — as Hoffreumon–Woods and Maioli et al. argue, and as the live Renou exchange leaves genuinely open — then "the records cannot supply it" is not a parochial limitation of SHARD but a symptom of a deeper fact: **neither the records nor a clean experiment may carry the bit.** That is the strongest available corroboration of the SHARD reading that this is *the* un-forceable field-blind import. SHARD did not predict the controversy; but the controversy is exactly what "un-forceable from inside" looks like when viewed from the outside. Framed as **vindication, never proof**; the physics question remains open.
