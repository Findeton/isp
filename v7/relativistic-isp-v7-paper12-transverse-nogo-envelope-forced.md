## 1. The question, and the split

Paper I forced the *shape* of single-chain sealing — refinement-multiplicativity `S(χ₁+χ₂) = S(χ₁) S(χ₂)` gives the unique `S(χ) = exp(−κχ)`, with `p = 1` the only refinement-consistent exponent. Paper II pinned the diamond *constants* by a one-diamond self-consistency, the **KL = Fisher capacity crossing**: the accumulated KL content `C(η)` rises, the contrast's instantaneous Fisher capacity `J(η)` falls, they meet once, and the meeting fixes the pure number `W_*` (§2). That crossing is the engine of every *forced number* the single-chain theory owns.

Paper IV carried the construction to *parallel* chains and proved the joint click-law is **forced-skeleton, free-complement**: the exponential survival shape transfers verbatim to the joint scalar argument (the refinement step is content-blind), but the *additivity* of the joint content is not forced — the sequential refinement telescopes exactly while leaving the mutual term `χ_AB = I_σ` untouched, with `S_AB/(S_A S_B) = exp(−κ χ_AB)`. So entanglement is permitted, never forced, never forbidden; `χ_AB` is a free Tier-A input, two-sided in sign, a continuum at fixed marginals, constrained only by no-signaling and the almost-quantum envelope. Paper IV left exactly one question open, and named it as the natural next step:

> *Could a **transverse** self-consistency principle — a parallel-chain analog of the one-diamond KL = Fisher capacity crossing that fixed the single-chain constants — convert `χ_AB` from free to forced?*

This paper answers it. The answer is a single split, and the two halves point opposite ways:

- **[NO-GO] The content is not forced.** No record-internal transverse self-consistency principle pins `χ_AB`. Two independent, mutually reinforcing **weight-0** mechanisms (M1 capacity blindness §3–4; M2 field blindness §5), and the two structural hopes that remained — a non-moment holonomy resource (§6) and the weaker target of forcing only the sign or only the magnitude (§7) — both die.
- **[CONSTRAINED] The envelope *is* forced — from inside.** The same self-consistency that fails to fill `χ_AB` succeeds in deriving the *boundary* it lives within: moment-positivity `Γ ⪰ 0`, which holds because `Γ` is the Gram matrix of the records' own observables, entails the whole almost-quantum envelope (§8). This is the constructive upgrade over Paper IV, which *respected* the envelope as an external bound; here the records *own* it.

The contrast with the program's scale no-go (Paper 57: the records cannot fix Newton's `G`, equivalently the seal-spacing length `l_step`) is sharp and worth stating up front, because it is what makes this a *new kind* of no-go. The scale no-go is a **grading-homomorphism** obstruction: an absolute length is weight `+1`, and a weight-`+1` object can never be a weight-`0` record functional — a degree count. Here `χ_AB = I_σ` is weight-`0` (a pure KL number, gauge-invariant under record-length rescaling), so the grading argument *does not apply*. The obstruction is instead an **invariance / capacity-degeneracy** obstruction at weight-`0`: `χ_AB` is the right weight to be forced, but the records' self-consistency is constant along the orbit (M2) and its capacity leg is flat in the entangling direction (M1). Different mechanism, same shape of conclusion — the records carry everything relative but are barred from one more SHARD-specific last input, here the composite *tensor product* rather than the absolute *length*.

## 2. The single-chain template, and what a transverse analog must replicate [FORCED, reproduced]

The arena is one mode, pre-geometric (Papers I–II). A contrast `q ∈ {−1, +1}` carries the exponential/parity family `P_η(q) = e^{ηq} / (2 cosh η)` over the eventless count-dual base `μ_D = (½, ½)`; `η` is the accumulated tilt — a record-internal clock, weight-`0`, *not* a length. - **KL content** (the odometer reading, accumulated distinguishability):
  `C(η) = D(P_η ‖ μ_D) = η tanh η − log cosh η`, with `C′(η) = η sech²η > 0` (sympy-exact) — **strictly rising**, `0 → log 2`.
- **Fisher capacity** (the single contrast's instantaneous coherent capacity):
  `J(η) = ψ″(η) = Var_{P_η}(q) = sech²η`, with `J′(η) = −2 tanh η sech²η < 0` (sympy-exact) — **strictly falling**, `1 → 0`.

**LAW-A, the forcing.** Demand `C(η) = J(η)` — the accrued content equals the contrast's own capacity to carry it. Because `C` rises monotonically from `0` and `J` falls monotonically from `1`, they cross *exactly once*, at `η_*` (`= η_A` of Paper II) `= 1.090344354879492196…`, giving `θ_* = tanh η_* = 0.797003794162878272…` and

  `W_* = J(η_*) = 1 − θ_*² = 0.364784952089976362…`.

What makes this a **forcing** rather than a coincidental crossing is the max-min identity `W_* = max_η min(C(η), J(η))`: on `[0, η_*]` the pointwise minimum is the rising `C`, on `[η_*, ∞)` it is the falling `J`, so the minimum *peaks* exactly at the crossing. `W_*` is the largest content a single diamond can simultaneously accumulate *and* hold within its own capacity — a genuine saturation extremum. The reduction to a *scalar* `min(C, J)` (rather than a vector Pareto frontier) is itself forced by the `q = 2` single-channel screen of Paper I: one off-diagonal contrast ⇒ one scalar capacity channel. (`η_*` is, by construction, the root of `C − J`; the value `W_*` and the sympy-exact sign opposition `C′ > 0`, `J′ < 0` are the load-bearing content, not any numerical "two-formula agreement," which is a root-finder residual.)

The forcing is LAW-A's; the seal fires inside it. **The checklist a transverse analog must satisfy to *force* `χ_AB`** is therefore exact, and §§3–7 test it against it: (i) two record-internal scalar functionals of one shared transverse parameter; (ii) *proved* monotone opposition (one strictly rising, one strictly falling); (iii) a supply-vs-capacity reading at the right weight (`χ_AB` is weight-`0`, good); (iv) a *scalar* capacity (else only a Pareto constraint); (v) a crossing at an *intrinsic* parameter value, not one drifting with the marginals; (vi) the max-min attained analytically; (vii) **no imported quantum axiom** — no tensor product, local tomography, Tsirelson bound, or Born rule on a composite Hilbert space, since Paper VI proved the seal is blind to exactly that composite structure. ## 3. M1 — capacity blindness: the faithful transverse capacity is marginal-only [NO-GO]

The literal analog Paper IV names is the **parallel-diamond capacity crossing**. Two parallel chains commit contrasts `a, b ∈ {−1, +1}`; the joint record law is the maximum-entropy family on the sufficient statistics `(a, b, ab)`,

  `P(a, b) ∝ exp(η_A a + η_B b + λ ab)`,

with marginal tilts `(η_A, η_B)` the per-chain content knobs and `λ` the entangling parameter that, *at fixed marginals*, controls `χ_AB = I_σ`. The faithful capacity leg is what the single-chain crossing used: the marginal-tilt diagonal Fisher, `Var(a) = 1 − ⟨a⟩²` (and `Var(b)`), exactly as `J(η) = Var_{P_η}(q)`. To test a forcing of `χ_AB`, the marginals *must* be held fixed (otherwise "capacity" merely tracks the marginals — the circularity of §3.2); so one re-solves `(η_A, η_B)` as functions of `λ` to pin `⟨a⟩ = ⟨b⟩ = m`.

**The capacity leg is exactly blind to the entangling parameter.** `Var(a) = 1 − m²` is a function of the marginals alone, so at fixed `m` it is *constant in `λ`*: across a sweep `λ ∈ [−0.6, 0.6]` the spread of `Var(a)` is `1.3 × 10⁻¹⁴¹` (dps 140 — one unit in the last place), and the central-difference `dJ_marg/dλ` sits at the round-off floor `~10⁻¹¹²`. The capacity crossing therefore re-fixes only the *marginals* (recovering the per-chain `η_*`) and says nothing whatever about the entangling content. Checklist item (ii) fails on the capacity side: there is no falling leg in `λ`.

**There is also no single rising leg.** The joint KL content against the eventless base, `C_AB(λ) = D(P_λ ‖ μ_D)`, is **U-shaped** at fixed marginals — minimised at the product `λ = 0` and rising on *both* signs of `λ` (at `m = ½`: centre `0.26162407`, wings `0.37125` at `λ = +0.6` and `0.31128` at `λ = −0.6`). This reflects `χ_AB = I_σ` being signed/two-sided: departure from independence in *either* direction raises the joint content. So `max_λ min(C_AB, J)` is not even well-posed as one crossing — the rising-vs-falling opposition that did the single-chain work is absent on both legs.

### 3.2 Any forced-looking balance is circular and marginal-dependent [NO-GO]

One can still *write down* a balance — e.g. `C_AB(λ) = Cov(a, b)` — and solve it. It does not force an intrinsic number: the root **moves with the marginals**, `λ_*(m = 0.3) = 0.1155`, `λ_*(m = ½) = 0.6189`, and *non-robust* (no genuine crossing) at `m = 0.7`. This is the diagnostic signature of a non-forcing balance (compare the single-chain `W_*`, which is marginal-independent). The structural reason is M2's preview: at fixed marginals the only remaining coordinate is the single cross-moment `E_ab`, and the only Fisher entry that responds to `λ` is `Cov(a, b) = E_ab − ⟨a⟩⟨b⟩` — the entangling content *itself*. Balancing the content against the entangling content is circular, not a content-vs-capacity opposition.

## 4. M1 is robust to the definition of "capacity" [NO-GO]

"Capacity" carries a definitional choice, so the no-go must survive replacing the faithful Fisher leg. It does. Every defensible record-internal alternative was tested at fixed marginals (dps 140, sympy-exact signs):

- **Entangling-parameter Fisher `J_λ = Var(ab) = 1 − E_ab²`.** Genuinely `λ`-dependent (it beats blindness), but sympy-exact `sign(dJ_λ/dλ) = −sign(E_ab)`: it *rises* while `E_ab < 0`, peaks at `E_ab = 0`, *falls* while `E_ab > 0` — a **hump**, not a monotone-falling capacity; and its turning point `E_ab = 0` is marginal-dependent (`λ* ≈ −0.112` at `m = 0.3`, pushed to the `λ → −∞` boundary for `m ≥ ½`). Items (ii) and (v) fail.
- **The `A|B`-cut capacity** — both the record-rate mutual information `I(A:B)` and the Blahut–Arimoto Shannon channel capacity `C_chan(b → a)` across the *parallel* cut (not the sequential refinement that telescopes blind). Both are **U-shaped**, minimised at `λ = 0`, rising on both signs — not a falling capacity; the channel reading additionally re-optimises the input, moving the marginals. Item (ii) fails.
- **The nearest-product KL `E_cl`** (calibrated `E_cl(Bell) = ln 2`). U-shaped, minimum `0` at `λ = 0`, sign-blind — no single rising arm. Item (ii) fails.

The only monotone-opposed pair one can manufacture is the tautology `E_ab` (rising) vs `1 − E_ab²` (falling); its crossing pins the golden *moment* `E_ab^* = (√5 − 1)/2 = 0.6180339887…`, but at a **marginal-dependent** `λ` (`λ* = 0.685 / 0.602 / 0.377` at `m = 0.3 / ½ / 0.7`, spread `0.31`) — it pins a moment, never an intrinsic `λ`, and the moment it pins is a property of the chosen functional, not of the records. This makes explicit the criterion that separates the genuine `W_*` from such an artifact: the forcing pair must be the **KL content and the Fisher capacity of one canonically-normalised exponential family** — `C(η) = η ψ′ − ψ` and `J(η) = ψ″`, both derived from the *same* log-partition `ψ = log cosh η`, with the `q = 2` screen fixing the units so that **no free relative scale is available**. The golden crossing fails this: it is a bare equality `f = g` between two functionals carrying an arbitrary relative dial `c` (`1 − E_ab² → c(1 − E_ab²)`), and under that free dial its root `E_ab^*` moves (`0.414 / 0.618 / 0.781` at `c = 0.5 / 1 / 2`), so the "forced" value is an artifact of `c`. The single-chain pair carries no such dial — `C` and `J` are the content and curvature of one family — which is what makes `W_*` canonical. (This is *not* an invariance of `W_*` under independent rescaling of `C` and `J`: the crossing `η_*` does move under such a rescaling; the point is that the records make no such free rescaling available.)

**The unified reason** (anticipating M2): at fixed marginals the level-`(1+AB)` moment data leaves only the single cross-moment `E_ab` as a free coordinate. Any record-internal capacity is therefore one of three things — marginal-only (blind), an *even* function of the signed correlation about independence (U-shape / hump — a magnitude-of-departure, sign-blind), or `E_ab` itself (tautological). None is the strictly-monotone-opposed pair, with a marginal-independent crossing, that forced `W_*`. (Two classes are honestly unexhausted but are *not* record-internal Tier-A principles: capacities defined via a *preferred tensor split* import the very `χ_AB` they would force, and off-state / higher-moment algebras escalate to a different arena — §9.)

## 5. M2 — field blindness: the moment algebra is an orbit invariant [NO-GO]

The second mechanism is operator-algebraic and is the one Paper VI forces as the *arena*. On the records the two chains meet **only at shared cross-moments**: a record interface commits single joint outcome pairs `(a, b)` under abstract Tier-A setting labels `(x, y)`, furnishing the degree-`(1,1)` data — marginals `⟨A_x⟩, ⟨B_y⟩` and cross-moments `⟨A_x B_y⟩ = E_xy` — coupled by the on-state commutation `B_y A_x = A_x B_y`, *not* a Hilbert tensor factorisation. So every record-internal transverse principle is a **moment functional** — that the records' self-consistency is a moment-positivity condition on the Gram matrix of their own observables is the robust structural claim; the *level* (that a record interface commits a single joint outcome pair, and no higher word) is a flagged substrate-modeling premise rather than a theorem (§9). Concretely, the principle is a functional of the level-`(1+AB)` moment algebra

  `M = ⟨{1, A_x, B_y, A_x B_y}⟩_ψ`.

**`M` is an invariant of the field-reduction `R`** (qubit-pair → rebit-pair, complex → real). At the Tsirelson correlators `E = (s, s, s, −s)`, `s = 1/√2`, the joint law `p(a, b | x, y) = (1 + ab E_xy)/4` is realised by *both* a qubit singlet and a rebit Bell pair, and `R` preserves every element of `M`: identical marginals `(½, ½)`, identical cross-moments (`CHSH = 2√2` to `< 10⁻¹¹⁸`), identical orthogonal-projection seal `E = E² = E*` (`|E² − E| ~ 6.6 × 10⁻¹⁴²` over *both* `ℝ` and `ℂ`), identical `q = 2` norm, identical Tsirelson point, and — the decisive check — **identical sealing scalar**: `I_σ(rebit) − I_σ(qubit) = 0` exactly (both `= 0.27665…`). Yet `R` *changes* the local-tomography count: the information-dimension counts are `K_single = 3` (real) / `4` (complex) and `K_AB = 10` (two-rebit) / `16` (two-qubit), so the local-tomography deficit `K_AB − K_A·K_B` is `+1` over `ℝ` (local tomography *fails*: `10 ≠ 9`) and `0` over `ℂ` (`16 = 16`).

The one bit that distinguishes a unique *complex* `χ_AB` — the one that would carry the feasible set from the almost-quantum `Q̃` to the quantum `Q` — is exactly this deficit, and it lies in the **kernel** of the records' factorisation through `M`: every record-internal principle takes the same value on the whole `R`-orbit, while the selecting bit varies across it. This is the invariance/orbit obstruction at weight-`0` promised in §1: `χ_AB` is the right weight, but the field-distinguishing content is constant along the orbit, unreachable not by weight but by symmetry. M1 (capacity blindness) is the same blindness seen information-geometrically — the faithful capacity is a function of the `R`-invariant marginals, exactly flat in the entangling direction; two faces of one wall.

## 6. The retained-holonomy phase is a moment functional or pure gauge [NO-GO, escape closed]

The no-go rests on the substrate-modeling premise that every transverse principle the records host is a functional of `M`. The one resource that could break it — a record-internal datum *beyond* the committed degree-`(1,1)` moments — had a named suspect: the **retained-holonomy phase** the corpus flagged as "entering composition without proven content." Closing it requires showing no resource is simultaneously (a) forced by the sealing self-consistency, (b) not a functional of `p(a, b | x, y)`, and (c) field-distinguishing (changing under `R`, hence able to reach the selecting bit). It is not.

Construct three realisations of the Tsirelson point: a qubit singlet in the `X`–`Z` plane (`R1`), a qubit carrying a genuine `Y`/phase component (`R2`), and a rebit Bell pair on `ℝ² ⊗ ℝ²` (`R3`); all realise the identical correlator table (gap `0`). Then:

- **(a → not forced).** A `Y`-phase *can* sit behind fixed `E_xy` (`R2` reproduces `R1`'s correlators with `Y`-carrying observables, gap `1.3 × 10⁻¹⁴¹`), but it is pure **local gauge**: `R1` and `R2` have an identical full moment table over all generators (gap `2.2 × 10⁻¹⁴¹`), and the gauge-invariant composite seal-loop Bargmann invariant `z` is identical for the two (`|z_{R1} − z_{R2}| = 7.4 × 10⁻¹⁴³`). The forced self-consistency is moment-positivity `Γ ⪰ 0`, a functional of moment *values* that are identical across the realisations, so it cannot prefer a phase-carrying seal; and the forced `q = 2` orthogonal-projection calculus (`E = E² = E*`, `q² = 1`) is real-closed — it holds over `ℝ` with no imaginary unit, so the seal never manufactures a phase. Not forced.
- **(b → a moment functional).** On the records' shared (real-direction) seals, the composite seal-loop invariant *is* the degree-`≤ 3` moment polynomial, `z = (1 + Σ⟨O_k⟩ + Σ⟨O_i O_j⟩ + ⟨O_1 O_2 O_3⟩)/8` — an algebraic identity for any joint state. It is verified **non-vacuously over a scan of generic loops and generic joint states** where `|z|` reaches `0.73` (genuinely `O(1)`, not the degenerate Tsirelson point where `|z| ≈ 0`): `z` reconstructed from the state matches the moment polynomial to `1.3 × 10⁻¹⁴¹`. So it adds nothing beyond `M`.
- **(c → not field-distinguishing).** For every shared `X`–`Z` loop the holonomy is identical on qubit and rebit, with `Im(z) = 0` on both fields (every such joint seal observable is a real matrix, so every Bargmann invariant is real — a phase in `{0, π}`, a sign already fixed by the real `E_xy`). The *only* genuinely complex value `γ ∈ (0, π)` needs an out-of-plane `Y`-seal: a single-party Pancharatnam loop gives `γ_Y = π/3 = 1.0472`, and the positive control `Tr(P_X P_Y P_Z) = 0.25 + 0.25 j` (`Im = 0.25`, the octant solid angle) confirms the probe is genuinely *sensitive* to complex phase — so "`Im = 0` on shared seals" is a real result, not a blind probe. But a `Y`-seal is not hostable by the field-reduced rebit substrate, is not forced by self-consistency, and is removed by a local unitary that fixes all of `M`.

None of (a), (b), (c) holds simultaneously, so the field-blind invariance of `M` survives the holonomy escape, and the flagged "phase content" is resolved: it is exactly the moment polynomial.

## 7. Neither the sign nor the magnitude is forceable [NO-GO, weaker hopes closed]

Even granting that the full `χ_AB` cannot be pinned, two weaker positives might survive — forcing its **sign**, or forcing a non-negative **magnitude**. Both fail.
