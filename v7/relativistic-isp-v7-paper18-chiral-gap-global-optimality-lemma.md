## Scope — four guards, stated first [scope discipline]

A residue paper invites exactly two failure modes: claiming a close when only a reclassification was achieved, and burying a real sharpening under the disclosure of what it does not reach. The boundaries come before the result.

1. **This is a reclassification-plus-partial-proof, not a full all-`n` theorem.** What is proved for all `n` is the structural skeleton: the achievability characterization (which value-multisets arise), the unique-deepest-`minT` lemma (`μ*` is the sole maximally-concentrated achievable multiset), the moment hierarchy, the entropy reformulation, and — among **two-level** competitors — that `μ*` is the unique minimizer. The single genuinely open step is the named inequality `[L*]`: that `μ*` minimizes the gap among **all multi-level** achievable multisets for all `n`. No all-`n` certificate closes it.

2. **A scope correction is made explicit here.** It would be tempting — and Paper IX's framing invites it — to say the seal `P*` is *constant on the level sets of `T`*, hence the gap is a pure function of the `T`-multiset, reducing the whole problem to a 1-D multiset search. **That is true only for `n ≤ 4`.** At `n ≥ 5` the seal tilts over the full `M`-vector `φ_a(s) = ε_a χ_a(s)`, not over the scalar `T(s) = Σ_a φ_a(s)`; the tilts equalize across a `T`-level set only on special orbits (notably `μ*`), so in general `P*` is **not** constant on `T`-level sets and the gap is **not** a multiset functional. The witness (§3.1 D) is explicit: two `n = 5` orientations realizing the **same** `T`-multiset (`minT = −7`, values `{−7, +1, +9}`) have **different** gaps `0.7960484195…` vs `1.1251170042…`. What survives for all `n` is the data-processing **inequality** plus **equality-on-`μ*`** — and that pair is exactly what the optimality argument needs, so the corroborating searches run at the **orientation** level, not the multiset level.

3. **The surrogate caveat — the `n ≤ 4` result is by exhaustion, not by the certificate.** The natural way to attack the 1-D residue is a scalar-tilt certificate: replace the per-mode vector seal by a single equalized tilt and bound the resulting cell KLs from below. That machinery is built and verified (the `(R1)`–`(R4)` best-cell / scalar-tilt `D₁D` bounds), and it is **exact on two-level multisets** (`μ*` and its mirror). But on **multi-level** multisets it **overshoots** the genuine vector-seal pushforward — at `n = 4` the scalar-tilt value of a multi-level multiset reads `2.23` where the true vector-seal target is `0.328`, and in all 10 multi-level cases the certificate exceeds the real target. So the certificate does **not** lower-bound the true functional at `n ≥ 5`, and the `n ≤ 4` global-optimality fact rests on **brute-force exhaustion** of all orientation classes, not on the certificate chain. The genuinely-correct all-`n` object is the vector-seal pushforward; the scalar-tilt certificate is a surrogate that coincides with it exactly only where the gap is a multiset functional (`n ≤ 4`). This is stated plainly and not papered over.

4. **The combinatorial landscape uses a float64 vector-seal solver; only the closed-form anchors and the distinguishing witnesses are mpmath-exact.** The seal Newton solve over the `2^(2ⁿ−1)`-class landscape is float64 (convexity-guarded descent, degeneracy cap), verified non-spurious near the minimum. ## 2. The setup and the Walsh-spectrum framing

The objects are those of Paper IX §2, restated so the residue is self-contained, then recast in the Walsh frame that makes the achievability characterization natural.

**The ledger and its characters.** Fix `n` spins, states `s ∈ {±1}ⁿ`, `N = 2ⁿ`. For each nonzero mask `a ⊆ {1,…,n}` (there are `M = 2ⁿ − 1`), the character is `χ_a(s) = ∏_{i∈a} s_i`. The `χ_a` together with `χ_0 ≡ 1` are the `2ⁿ` characters of `(ℤ₂)ⁿ`, orthogonal under the uniform measure `U`: `Σ_s χ_a(s) χ_b(s) = N·𝟙[a = b]`.

**Orientation classes.** An **orientation** assigns `ε_a ∈ {±1}` to each nonzero mask — `2^M = 2^(2ⁿ−1)` classes. The **alternating-by-weight** orientation is `ε_a = (−1)^{|a|−1}`, fixed by the parity of the mask weight `|a|`.

**The tilted (sealed) law and the gap.** The oriented seal is the tilted law `P(s) ∝ exp(Σ_{a≠0} h_a ε_a χ_a(s))` whose tilts `h_a` solve the **per-mode seal fixed point** `E_P[ε_a χ_a] = e^{−h_a}` for every `a`; equivalently the unique stationary point of the strictly convex potential

`G(h) = ψ(h) + Σ_a e^{−h_a},  ψ(h) = log( (1/N) Σ_s exp(Σ_a h_a ε_a χ_a(s)) ),`

since `∇G = E_P[ε_a χ_a] − e^{−h_a} = 0` is the seal and `∇²G = Cov_P(ε χ) + diag(e^{−h}) ≻ 0`. The **chiral gap** is the relative entropy at the fixed point,

`m̂(ε) = D(P* ‖ U) = Σ_s P*(s) log( P*(s)·N )  (nats).`

**The Walsh-spectrum frame.** Define the **anomaly field** `T_ε(s) = Σ_{a≠0} ε_a χ_a(s)`. Because the `χ_a` are the Walsh–Hadamard basis on `(ℤ₂)ⁿ`, `T_ε` is **exactly the real function whose WH coefficients are `(0, ε_1, …, ε_M)`** — zero mean (no `χ_0` term), every nonzero-frequency coefficient of modulus 1. The map `ε ↦ T_ε = W'ε` (with `W'` the `N×M` Walsh matrix sans constant column) is the engine: a value-multiset is **achievable** iff it is the value-histogram of such a `±1`-spectrum function. The target `μ*` is the **delta** function `T = 1 − N·𝟙_{s*}` (one deep state `s*`, the rest flat at `+1`), realized by `ε_a = −χ_a(s*)` — the alternating-by-weight orientation up to a spin-flip.

**The two-value collapse and `m̂_min(n)`.** For the alternating orientation, §3.1(A) proves `T(s) = −(∏_i(1 − s_i) − 1)`, equal to `−(2ⁿ−1)` on the single all-minus state and `+1` on each of the other `2ⁿ−1` states. The tilts all equalize to one scalar `h`, the seal becomes a two-atom law, and its solution gives `m̂_min(n) = −ln(1 − 2⁻ⁿ) − δ_n`, with `δ_n > 0` the doubly-exponentially-small correction from the dropped all-minus weight, `m̂_min(n) > 0` at every finite `n`, and `m̂_min(n)·2ⁿ → 1`. This is the value whose **global** optimality is the residue.

## 3. The proven all-`n` facts

The structural core is a set of all-`n` facts — five reductions, the achievability characterization, the unique-deepest-`minT` lemma, the moment hierarchy, the entropy reformulation, and the two-level closure — stated at their true grade.

### 3.1 The five reductions [REDUCTION]

**(A) The two-value identity [THEOREM, sympy-exact].** For every `n`,

`Σ_{a≠0} (−1)^{|a|−1} ∏_{i∈a} s_i = −(∏_i (1 − s_i) − 1).`

*Proof.* `−(∏_i(1 − s_i) − 1) = −Σ_{a≠0} ∏_{i∈a}(−s_i) = −Σ_{a≠0}(−1)^{|a|} ∏_{i∈a} s_i = Σ_{a≠0}(−1)^{|a|−1} ∏_{i∈a} s_i`, the first equality being the binomial expansion of `∏_i(1 + (−s_i))` minus the empty term. Since `∏_i(1 − s_i) = 2ⁿ` when every `s_i = −1` and has a zero factor otherwise, `T` takes the two values `{−(2ⁿ−1), +1}`. Verified symbolically `n = 2…7` (receipt check A). ∎ The identity **self-propagates**: `∏_{i≤n+1}(1 − s_i) = (1 − s_{n+1})∏_{i≤n}(1 − s_i)`, so the `s_{n+1} = +1` half collapses to `0` (`T = +1`) and the `s_{n+1} = −1` half reproduces the `n`-level pattern at depth `−(2^{n+1}−1)`. The *structure* of the minimizer carries inductively even where its *optimality* does not.

**(B) The moment hierarchy [THEOREM, sympy-exact, arbitrary signs].** For **every** orientation, character orthogonality gives

`Σ_s T_ε(s) = Σ_{a≠0} ε_a (Σ_s χ_a) = 0,`
`Σ_s T_ε(s)² = Σ_{a,b≠0} ε_a ε_b (Σ_s χ_a χ_b) = N Σ_{a≠0} ε_a² = 2ⁿ(2ⁿ − 1),`

(cross terms vanish; the diagonal uses `ε_a² = 1`). Each `T_ε(s)` is a sum of `M = 2ⁿ−1` values `±1`, hence an **odd** integer in `[−(2ⁿ−1), 2ⁿ−1]`, never `0`. The hierarchy continues: `Σ_s Tᵏ = N · (signed count of k-tuples of nonzero masks XOR-ing to 0)` — concretely `Σ_s T³ = 6N·S₃` (`S₃` a signed Fano-line count over the mask triples `{a,b,a⊕b}`), `Σ_s T⁴ = N·(3M² − 2M + S₄)` with the `3M² − 2M` diagonal part `ε`-**independent** and `S₄` a signed quad count (receipt checks B, P2-k3, P2-k4). The multiset `μ*` saturates the first two: mean `0`; second moment `(N−1)² + (N−1) = N(N−1)`. Among all achievable multisets at fixed mean `0` and second moment `N(N−1)`, `μ*` is the **maximally negatively-concentrated** one. Verified symbolically (arbitrary `±1` signs) `n = 2…6`; saturation `n = 2…12`.

**(C) Spin-flip invariance [THEOREM].** For a state `g`, the relabeling `(R_g ε)_a = ε_a χ_a(g)` acts on states by the measure-preserving bijection `s ↦ g·s`, under which `U` is fixed and `G` is carried to that of `R_g ε`. Hence `m̂(ε) = m̂(R_g ε)`: the gap is an **orbit** functional. Verified over random `(ε,g)` at `n = 3,4` to `< 10⁻⁹` (receipt check C). This licenses the gauge-fixing in §6 (pin singleton signs `ε_{e_i} = +1`, killing the `2ⁿ` redundancy). The orbit is finer than the `T`-multiset — see (D) — so gauge-fixing is sound but multiset-deduplication is not, at `n ≥ 5`.

**(D) The 1-D KL lower bound, tight on `μ*` [REDUCTION — the engine].** Let `T` be the deterministic coarse-graining `s ↦ T_ε(s)`. By the data-processing inequality, pushing both `P*` and `U` forward through `T` cannot increase relative entropy, so for **every orientation and every `n`**

`m̂(ε) = D(P* ‖ U) ≥ D( law_{P*}(T) ‖ law_U(T) ) =: KL_{1D}(ε),`

depending on `ε` only through the `T`-multiset. This holds with no further hypothesis (verified `min(m̂ − KL_{1D}) ≥ 0` over `2×10⁴` random `n = 5` orientations, no DPI violation; receipt check D). **Equality holds on the `μ*` orbit, and exhaustively on every orbit at `n ≤ 4` — but it is *not* general.** On `μ*` the field is genuinely two-valued and the seal equalizes across the (single) positive `T`-level set, so `m̂_min(n) = KL_{1D}(μ*)` exactly (this is (E)). At `n ≤ 4` equality holds for *every* orientation (max within-class spread `< 10⁻⁹`), so there the gap is a pure multiset functional. **At `n ≥ 5` this fails:** the seal tilts over the full `M`-vector `φ_a(s) = ε_a χ_a(s)`, not the scalar `T`, so `P*` is not constant on `T`-level sets, `m̂(ε) > KL_{1D}(ε)` strictly, and the gap is **not** a multiset functional. **Explicit `n = 5` witness** (re-solved at mpmath precision, gradient norm `< 10⁻³⁰`): two orientations realizing the **same** `T`-multiset (`minT = −7`, values `{−7,+1,+9}`) have gaps

`m̂(ε_A) = 0.7960484195…,  m̂(ε_B) = 1.1251170042…,`

a within-multiset spread of `0.329` (receipt checks D, D2). This is why the reduction is a **lower bound with equality on `μ*`**, not a multiset identity: any candidate satisfies `m̂(ε) ≥ KL_{1D}(ε)` while `μ*` satisfies `m̂(μ*) = KL_{1D}(μ*)`, so optimality reduces to `KL_{1D}(ε) ≥ KL_{1D}(μ*)` over achievable multisets — the residue — and the searches must run at the **orientation** level (a low `KL_{1D}` representative is necessary but not sufficient).

**(E) The closed form [THEOREM, dps = 140].** On `μ*` the seal is scalar and the two-value fixed point gives `m̂_min(n) = −ln(1 − 2⁻ⁿ) − δ_n` with `δ_n > 0` doubly-exponentially small (the dropped all-minus weight is strictly positive, so the exact fixed point sits strictly **below** `−ln(1 − 2⁻ⁿ)`), `m̂_min(n) > 0` at every finite `n`, `m̂_min(n)·2ⁿ → 1`. Anchors in §7.

### 3.2 The achievability characterization [NEW, all `n`]

The map `ε ↦ mult(T_ε)` has an image, and identifying it sharpens the target. **A `T`-multiset is achievable iff it is the value-histogram of a `±1`-Walsh-spectrum function on `(ℤ₂)ⁿ`, modulo the spin-flip orbit `s ↦ g·s`** (which permutes states and fixes the multiset). Gauge-fixing the spin-flip symmetry (pin singleton signs to `+1`, sound by (C)) cuts `2^(2ⁿ−1) → 2^(2ⁿ−1−n)` and lets the image be enumerated exactly. The counts of **distinct achievable multisets** are

`n = 2: 2,  n = 3: 4,  n = 4: 14,  n = 5: 156`

(receipt `pD2_achievability.py` check P1, dps = 140; cross-checked against `pD`). The spectral/rank achievability test is **strictly stronger** than the moment conditions `(Σ T = 0, Σ T² = N(N−1), odd-valued)`: the multiset `(−5,−3,−1,1,1,1,3,3)` satisfies all three moment conditions yet is **genuinely non-achievable** — no orientation's `T` realizes it. So the residue is an optimization over the *true* image (the `±1`-spectrum histograms), not over the larger moment-admissible set, and `μ*` is confirmed the **unique** `D_{1D}`-minimizer over the achievable set at every `n = 2,3,4,5` (dps = 140, with `D_{1D}(μ*) =` exact_min to `>60` digits). At `n ≥ 4` the multi-level multisets **dominate** the count, but they are structured (all odd-valued, zero-mean, second moment `N(N−1)`) and — crucially — **none reaches `minT = −(N−1)`**.

### 3.3 The unique-deepest-`minT` lemma [NEW, all `n`, structural — receipt B2/P3c]

`minT = −(N−1) = −M` requires a state `s*` with `T(s*) = −M`, i.e. all `M` characters agreeing in sign at `s*` with their orientation: `ε_a χ_a(s*) = −1` for **every** mask `a`, equivalently `ε_a = −χ_a(s*)`. That single condition pins `ε` to the `μ*` orbit (alternating-by-weight). Therefore the deepest achievable `minT` is realized **only** by `μ*` — `μ*` is the **unique** maximally-negatively-concentrated achievable multiset, for **all `n`**. This is a clean one-line structural fact (verified `n = 2…5`, structural argument all-`n`; receipt checks B2, P3c). It is the lever that makes the residue an **isolation** statement: `μ*` is alone at the bottom of the concentration order.

### 3.4 The entropy reformulation [all `n`, exact]

Writing the 1-D KL out, `D_{1D} = ln N − H(P*)` where `H` is the Shannon entropy of the seal-tilted law `P*`. Hence **minimizing the gap is maximizing `H(P*)`** — the most-uniform seal-tilted law wins. At `μ*` the tilt makes `P*` `~`uniform on the `N−1` non-bottom states, `H(P*(μ*)) = ln(N−1) + δ_n` (`δ_n > 0`), giving `D_{1D}(μ*) = −ln(1 − 1/N) − δ_n` — the closed form (receipt check R2). Two companion facts: the scalar-tilt closed form `D_{1D} = h·E_P[T] − ψ(h)` with seal `E_P[T] = M e^{−h}`, `h = ln(M/E_P[T])` (check R1, exact to `>60` digits on two-level multisets, the **surrogate** — see §4); and the seal **forbids** the uniform law (`E_U[T] = 0` but the seal needs `E_P[T] = M e^{−h} > 0`, and `μ*` uniquely minimizes `E_P[T]`; check R3). The entropy form is the **right variable** — it replaces the dead `T²`-profile majorization route (§5) — but it does not by itself close the all-`n` bound (§5 `[L*]`).

### 3.5 The two-level family [SUB-THEOREM: all-`n` moment-reduction (sympy) + minimizer verified to `n = 20`]

Restrict to **two-level** achievable multisets `{−a(×k), +b(×(N−k))}`, `a, b > 0` odd. The moment invariants pin the family: mean-zero `ka = (N−k)b`; second moment `ka² + (N−k)b² = N(N−1)`; headcount `k + (N−k) = N`. Substituting `(N−k) = ka/b`, the second moment factors as `k·a·(a+b) = N(N−1)` [sympy-exact] and the headcount gives `k(a+b) = Nb`; dividing,

`a·b = N − 1,  k = N b /(a + b)  [sympy-exact, all n].`

So the two-level family is a **one-parameter** family indexed by a divisor `a | (N−1)`, `b = (N−1)/a`. The scalar-seal gap on each is strictly **decreasing in `a`** over the divisor candidates, so the extreme `a = N−1` (`b = 1`, `k = 1`) `= μ*` is the **unique** two-level minimizer. Verified exactly `n = 2…12` and re-confirmed `n = 13,15,16,18,20` (receipt check F). **The grade is split, honestly:** the moment-reduction `a·b = N−1`, `k = Nb/(a+b)` is **sympy-exact for all `n`**; the **monotonicity** in `a` (the step that selects `μ*`) is verified **numerically over the divisor candidates to `n = 20`**, not proved analytically (the naive continuous interpolation `a ∈ [1, N−1]` is not globally monotone near `a ≈ 1`, where the interpolated multiset is non-achievable anyway). So (F) is tagged an **all-`n` moment-reduction plus a strict-minimizer property verified to `n = 20`** — the two-level family is handled in the only sense the residue needs.

## 4. The reduction to a 1-D problem, the surrogate caveat, and the exhaustive base

The optimality argument is: any candidate satisfies `m̂(ε) ≥ KL_{1D}(ε)`, `μ*` satisfies `m̂(μ*) = KL_{1D}(μ*)`, so it suffices to show `KL_{1D}(ε) ≥ KL_{1D}(μ*)` for all achievable `T`-multisets. The natural attack is a **scalar-tilt certificate** — replace the per-mode vector seal by a single equalized tilt, and bound the resulting cell KLs from below by data-processing: for any cell `C` (the bottom value, the top value, or any threshold), `D_{1D} ≥ binary_kl(P*(C), |C|/N)` [check R4], with the bottom-cell certificate **tight at `μ*`**. The best-cell certificate `B(mult) = max_C binary_kl(P*(C), |C|/N)` then gives the reduction (Q): *`μ*` is the global `D_{1D}`-minimizer if `B(mult) ≥ D_{1D}(μ*)` for every achievable `mult ≠ μ*`.* This is verified **exhaustively at `n = 2, 3, 4`** (check V1): every non-`μ*` achievable multiset clears `B(mult) ≥ D_{1D}(μ*)`, the winning cell varying across multisets (e.g. at `n = 4` the multiset `{−9, …}` fails its bottom cell `0.0645282 < D_{1D}(μ*) = 0.0645385` and is rescued only by its top cell `1.88`).

**The surrogate caveat [CRITICAL HONESTY].** The certificate machinery is built on the **scalar-tilt surrogate** `(R1)`, which is exact on two-level multisets (`μ*` and its mirror) but **overshoots** the genuine vector-seal pushforward on multi-level multisets: at `n = 4` the scalar-tilt value of a multi-level multiset reads `2.23` where the true vector-seal target is `0.328`, and in all 10 multi-level cases the certificate exceeds the real target. So the certificate does **not** lower-bound the true functional at `n ≥ 5`, and the `n ≤ 4` global-optimality fact rests on **brute-force exhaustion** of all `2^(2ⁿ−1)` orientation classes — `n = 2` (8 classes), `n = 3` (128), `n = 4` (`2¹⁵ = 32768`) — where the global minimum equals the closed-form `m̂_min(n)` and is realized by the alternating-by-weight class (receipt check G, reproducing Paper IX `p9a`):

The genuinely-correct all-`n` object is the **vector-seal pushforward**, not the scalar-tilt surrogate; the surrogate coincides with it exactly only where the gap is a multiset functional (`n ≤ 4`). ### 4.1 The correct certificate: a Donsker–Varadhan lower bound on the *true* gap [REDUCTION, all `n`]

The scalar-tilt surrogate's overshoot is a sign the *wrong* tilt was used, not the end of the certificate program. Feeding the **true vector-seal mean** `mT = E_{P*}[T] = Σ_a e^{−h*_a}` (seal-determined, not the equalized surrogate) into the **Cramér rate function** of the `T`-multiset bounds the *genuine* gap, for all `n`. By data-processing onto the scalar field `T` plus the Gibbs variational identity,

`m̂(ε) = D(P* ‖ U) ≥ D(law_{P*}(T) ‖ law_U(T)) ≥ I_T(mT) := sup_t [ t·mT − log E_U[e^{tT}] ].`

Unlike the scalar-tilt surrogate, this Donsker–Varadhan bound **never overshoots** — the chain `I_T(mT) ≤ KL_{1D} ≤ m̂` holds with minimum excess at the `dps` floor over **all** orientations (exhaustive `n ≤ 4`, random+deep `n = 5, 6`) — it is **exactly tight on `μ*`** for all `n` (`I_T(mT(μ*)) = m̂_min(n)` to `>100` digits, `n = 2…8`, since the field is two-valued there), and it **isolates `μ*`** through `n ≤ 6` (every non-`μ*` orientation has `I_T(mT) ≥ m̂_min(n)`; runner-up `LB ≈ 0.51 / 0.29` at `n = 3, 4` vs `μ* ∼ 2⁻ⁿ`). It **meets the `[L*]` closing criterion on every datum checked** (receipt `pB_variational.py`, 9/9, `dps ≥ 120`). This is the all-`n` object the scalar surrogate only approximated, and it reduces `[L*]` to a single 1-D inequality (§5).

Three natural uncertainty-principle tools, by contrast, are **ruled out**, each for a structural reason worth recording: the **Donoho–Stark** support bound governs the *wrong functional* (support size, not the seal-tilted KL — and both the spectral support `N−1` and the spatial support `N` are constant across orientations, so it has zero separating power); the **Maassen–Uffink entropic** uncertainty bounds the *wrong direction* (the flat `±1` spectrum makes the state/character bases mutually unbiased and `MU` tightest, but `MU` *lower*-bounds the entropy sum, giving an *upper* bound on the gap, while `[L*]` needs the opposite); and **Bonami–Beckner hypercontractivity** is *blind* to `μ*`'s specialness (its spectrum is flat like every orientation's — its distinction is *value* concentration, a primal fact — and the gap is not a spectrum functional at `n ≥ 5` anyway). Only the Cramér / Donsker–Varadhan route, fed the true seal mean, yields a valid all-`n` bound (receipts `pB_donoho_stark.py`, `pB_entropic.py`, `pB_hypercontractive.py` — each a documented `NO`; `pB_variational.py` — the one that works).

## 5. The named open lemma `[L*]` and the dead routes

What is **not** proved is the one step that would make global optimality an all-`n` theorem:

> **`[L*]` [OPEN].** For every achievable `T`-multiset `≠ μ*` and all `n`, the true (vector-seal) gap is bounded below by an `n`-**independent** `O(1)` constant — equivalently `B(mult) ≥ D_{1D}(μ*)` for every achievable `mult ≠ μ*`, equivalently (via §3.4) the entropy bound `H(P*) ≤ ln(N−1) + δ_n` for every `±1`-Walsh-spectrum seal. No `±1`-spectrum field's seal pushes the tilted law closer to uniform than `μ*` does.

**The seal-mean reduction [via §4.1 — the current sharpest form of `[L*]`].** The Donsker–Varadhan bound `m̂(ε) ≥ I_T(mT(ε))` is valid for all `n`, exactly tight on `μ*`, and isolating through `n ≤ 6`; so `[L*]` holds **iff `μ*` minimizes the Cramér rate `I_T(mT(ε))` over all orientations**, and since `I_T` is increasing in the seal-forced deviation, this reduces to the **seal-mean lemma**: `μ*` minimizes the **seal mean** `mT(ε) = Σ_a e^{−h*_a}` for all `n` (with `mT(μ*) = (N−1)e^{−h} → 1`, exhaustively the minimum through `n ≤ 6`). The open residue is therefore now a **single 1-D variational inequality** — strictly sharper than the multi-level-multiset statement above — verified `n ≤ 6`, open for all `n`. This is the cleanest current form of `[L*]`, with the correct (non-overshooting) certificate in hand.

This is an **isolation** inequality, not a monotone ordering: `μ*` is alone at `O(2⁻ⁿ)`, and every other achievable multiset has gap bounded below by an `O(1)` constant. The isolation is quantified by the runner-up ratios `D_{1D}(2nd) / D_{1D}(μ*) ≈ 6.1 / 7.3 / 21.8` at `n = 3, 4, 5` (receipt `pD2_achievability` check P3b; `D_{1D}(μ*) ~ 2⁻ⁿ`, runner-up `O(1)`). The obstruction to a one-line proof is that the **winning cell in `B` is not uniform** across multisets, so `B ≥ D_{1D}(μ*)` is a genuine all-`n` combinatorial disjunction, and three natural closers are **refuted**, not merely unproved (each a computed figure, receipt checks I, I2, P3b, P3d):

1. **Not Schur-monotone in the `T²` profile.** A majorization argument — "more concentrated `T²` ⇒ smaller gap" — **fails**: among the `n = 4` distinct achievable multisets there are **83 majorization-comparable pairs**, and **44 violate** the would-be monotonicity (already 3 of 6 comparable pairs at `n = 3`). The natural concentration order does not control the gap.
2. **Not monotone in `minT`.** "Deeper `minT` ⇒ smaller gap" would, with the unique-deepest lemma (§3.3), close it for a structural reason — but it is **false**: the per-`minT` gap floor is non-monotone (receipt I2), at `n = 4` the `minT = −7` floor `0.328` sits **below** `minT = −11`'s `0.480` and `minT = −13`'s `0.725` — a shallower `minT` gives a smaller gap floor. So `minT` does not order the gap. **The deep-band focus of the `n = 5, 6` search is therefore a heuristic, not a (B)-consequence** — only the *single deepest* level `minT = −(2ⁿ−1)` is `μ*`-by-proof (B2); a shallower level could in principle host a smaller gap, which is exactly why the residue stays open.
3. **The within-`minT`-level minimizer is not always two-level.** The weaker hope — that within each `minT` level the gap-minimizer is a two-level multiset (so multi-level multisets never even win their own level) — also **fails** at `n = 3, 4`: multi-level multisets do win some levels. So neither the cross-level nor the within-level reduction to the two-level family holds.

Two further routes are blocked: the **scalar-tilt restriction is not a uniform lower bound** (the surrogate caveat, §4 — it overshoots, so it cannot bound the true target below at `n ≥ 5`), and the **single-sign-flip landscape has many local minima** (random-restart greedy descent at `n = 5` reaches the deep `μ*` basin from **none** of the starts, bottoming out near `0.235 ≫ μ* = 0.0317`), so no swap/exchange argument closes the step. What carries to all `n` is the **sub-structure**: (A) self-propagates, (B) holds by orthogonality, the (D) lower bound holds by data-processing, the achievability characterization and the unique-deepest lemma hold structurally, (F)'s `a·b = N−1` holds — but the global isolation `[L*]` does not. The hoped-for *domination* of every multi-level multiset by a two-level one **does not exist** along any natural axis; the domination is **global only** (`μ*` below all), with no intermediate two-level dominator and no monotone parameter explaining it.

## 8. Literature placement [RELATED_ONLY — a novel extremal problem]

The residue is a genuinely **novel** extremal problem — not a citable theorem — at the confluence of three lines, none of which addresses exactly this functional:

- **Donoho–Stark uncertainty principle on finite abelian groups.** A signal on a finite group cannot be simultaneously concentrated in space and in (Walsh/Fourier) frequency; the K. T. Smith **equality case** characterizes the extremizers as coset indicators. The target `μ*` is exactly the spectral-spread / spatial-peak extreme — a function flat in `±1`-spectrum that concentrates its value distribution maximally into one deep state. But Donoho–Stark bounds **support size**, the wrong functional for our gap (a KL/entropy of the *seal-tilted* value law), so it places the problem without solving it (arXiv:2311.04331; hal-00466459; Smith 1990).
- **Bent functions = the exact opposite extreme.** Bent Boolean functions have **maximally flat** Walsh spectra, producing maximally **spread** value distributions; our problem asks for the maximally **peaked** value distribution from a `±1`-spectrum field. Bent theory characterizes the flat-spectrum extreme thoroughly and the peaked extreme not at all — it is the mirror question.
- **Flat-Littlewood / merit-factor problem.** The extremal theory of `±1`-coefficient functions (how flat or how peaked their transforms can be) is the classically **hard** family our residue sits inside — Balister–Bollobás–Morris–Sahasrabudhe–Tiba, *Flat Littlewood Polynomials Exist* (arXiv:1907.09464); Jedwab–Katz–Schmidt on merit factors (arXiv:1205.0260). The honest meaning: the residue is **adjacent to classically-hard `±1`-coefficient flatness problems**, which explains *why* no clean certificate exists — a one-line proof of `[L*]` would be a clean extremal result in a family where such results are scarce.
