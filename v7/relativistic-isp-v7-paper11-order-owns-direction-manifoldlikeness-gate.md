## 2. Order → the conformal structure and directions [THEOREM / DEMONSTRATED]

The conformal half of the metric is owned by the order, in principle, as a theorem the records may invoke. The **Malament–HKMM theorem** (Hawking–King–McCarthy 1976; Malament 1977): for a manifold-like (past- and future-distinguishing) spacetime, the causal order determines the metric *up to a local conformal factor* — i.e. the null cones and all directions, everything scale-free. Its discrete companion is the **Myrheim–Meyer** dimension estimator: the *ordering fraction* (the fraction of pairs that are causally related) is a function `f(d)` of the dimension alone, `f(d) = Γ(d+1)Γ(d/2) / [2 Γ(3d/2)]` (giving `f(2)=1/2`, `f(3)=8/35`, `f(4)=1/10`), with no length scale in it.

Receipt `r1` demonstrates the whole conformal datum coming out of the pure record order (9/9 checks):

- **Dimension from the order.** A `1+1` Alexandrov order has ordering fraction `0.4963` against the exact `f(2) = 1/2`, giving Myrheim–Meyer dimension `2.01`; a `2+1` order gives `0.2259` against `f(3) = 8/35 = 0.228571`, dimension `3.01`. The cone-opening / dimension — a conformal datum — is read off the order sign alone, no `s²` magnitude.
- **The null cone from the links.** The order's *links* (covering relations = nearest causal neighbours) concentrate on the null directions as density grows: the median link slope `|dx/dt|` rises toward the null value `1` (`0.83` at `N = 400`, `0.93` at `N = 3000`). The light cone is the continuum limit of the order's link skeleton — Malament's conformal shadow, discretely.
- **Boost- and scale-invariance.** A boost is an order automorphism: the order matrix and the link skeleton are bit-identical under rapidity `0.8`. And a global dilation `x → λx` (`λ = 0.137, 7.3`) leaves the *entire order and every order-derived direction unchanged* — the conformal/direction structure is scale-free, exactly as Malament's conformal class is.

**This delivers Paper X's direction.** Paper X needs a unit propagation vector `n̂` and scopes it as "a unit vector in the emergent metric continuum … not the absolute-scale wall: a direction is scale-free." Receipt `r1` confirms precisely that scale-freedom: the order-only content is that the order *and its link/null skeleton are bit-identical under any dilation* (the conformal class is invariant), and the Malament–HKMM theorem says that class *is* the directions; a representative link direction, read from the held-out coordinates purely to *score* this, is correspondingly dilation-invariant (drift `0.0` under `λ = 7.3`) while its *length* scales by `λ` (`2.70 → 19.70`). So the propagation direction is an order-determined, scale-free member of the conformal class — **owned in principle by the record causal order, and not blocked by the `l_step` no-go**. This reclassifies Paper X's first missing input from "premature/unbuilt" to *order-determined, gated only by manifoldlikeness* (§4). It delivers the direction, not Paper X's second missing input — the canonical `+/×` mode frame, which remains the mode-canonicalization fifth wall (Papers VIII–IX).

The grade is **[THEOREM]** for the order → conformal map (Malament; Myrheim–Meyer, with the exact `f(d)` machine-cross-checked) and **[DEMONSTRATED]** for its exhibition on the record order — both *conditional on manifoldlikeness* (§4), which is the hypothesis of Malament's theorem.

## 3. Number → the volume measure, up to `l_step` [NO-GO]

The volume half is owned too — up to the one scale the records can never supply. The seal **count** is an unbiased volume counter: by the Myrheim–Meyer cardinality relation `E[N] = V / l_step^d`, the number of seals in a region estimates its volume in units of the fundamental volume element `l_step^d`. Receipt `r2` confirms it (6/6): the count is unbiased (`|⟨N⟩ − E[N]|/E[N] = 0.5%`) and Poisson (`Var[N]/E[N] = 0.97`), recovering the volume to `~1/√N`.

But it is the volume in `l_step^d` units, and the absolute scale is walled. Under a relabelling `l_step → μ·l_step`, the **count is invariant** (a weight-0 integer, `49` whatever `μ`) while the **physical volume scales as `μ^d`** (`49 → 196 → 534` for `l_step = 1, 2, 3.3`). So the weight ledger reads: volume-in-`l_step^d`-units (`= N`) is weight `0` and **owned**; absolute physical volume (`= N · l_step^d`) is weight `+d` (here `+2`, the 2d demonstration) and **walled**; volume *ratios* are weight `0` and owned exactly (count-ratio `0.355` against the true `0.36`, error `1.5%`). This is the program's `l_step` grading no-go (Papers III, 57) **recurring** — the *same* wall behind the spacing `d`, Newton's `G`, the `c_m` hierarchy, and the absolute metric (Paper IV's walled half) — now on the volume / conformal-factor axis, refining Paper IV's walled metric into the actual volume measure.

The one external input that closes it is **`G`**. Inputting the Newton constant fixes `l_step` (in natural units `G ∼ l_step²`), hence the absolute volume normalization — exactly the constant the antichain-deformation algebra of v6 paper 1 "leaves undetermined." So *number → volume* is the continuum-side face of v6 paper 1's "general relativity up to `G` and `Λ`": with `G` supplied, the conformal factor (the local scale) is pinned, and tier II is closed. The records own the volume *measure*; `G` is the one number that turns it into absolute volume.

## 4. Manifoldlikeness — the gate [WALLED, field-shared]

Tiers I and II are theorems **only for manifold-like causal sets** — those faithfully embeddable in a Lorentzian manifold. This is the hypothesis of Malament's theorem and the regime in which the Myrheim–Meyer estimators are valid. The bare Lorentz-invariant record order does *not* supply manifoldlikeness, and the obstruction is sharp and field-wide.

**Non-manifold orders dominate.** The **Kleitman–Rothschild theorem** (1975): the number of partial orders on `n` elements grows as `2^{n²/4 + o(n²)}`, and almost all of them are 3-layer, height-2 "KR orders." Receipt `r3` makes the count concrete: at `n = 10⁴`, the dominant family carries `~2.5 × 10⁷` bits against `~5 × 10⁵` bits of manifold-like embedding data — the non-manifold orders overwhelm the manifold-like ones by the square of the size.

**And a single estimator is fooled by them.** Receipt `r3` exhibits the trap (18/18, exact `f(1)=1`, `f(2)=1/2`, `f(3)=8/35`, `f(4)=1/10`): a genuine sprinkling recovers its embedding dimension (`2d → 1.991`, `3d → 3.001`), but a KR 3-layer order returns a spurious near-integer Myrheim–Meyer "dimension" `d_MM = 2.38` — a manifold-looking number from a non-manifold order. It is unmasked only by a *second*, height-based estimator: the KR order's longest chain is `3` (height 2, for any `N`), against a `2d` sprinkling's `46`. Certifying manifoldlikeness needs *agreeing* estimators, and the dominant family is exactly the one on which they disagree.

**The click-law offers no proven lever — and this is already on record.** Whether the record dynamics — the click-law / sequential-refinement sealing — *produces* manifold-like orders rather than the dominant KR junk is the causal-set field's central open problem (the entropy / dominance problem), shared by every causal-set and emergent-gravity programme. SHARD inherits it; it does not solve it. The first recorded symptom is v6 paper 1's own result: its hypersurface-deformation route exhibited "only the locality skeleton and a **non-discriminating finite-sprinkling drift**" — the record dynamics, run on a test order, did not discriminate the manifold-like structure. That non-discrimination is read here as a symptom of the manifoldlikeness gate — consistent with it, though v6 paper 1 did not name the gate as such. A further, separate conjecture sits above this even for manifold-like orders: the **causal-set Hauptvermutung** — that a causal set faithfully embeds into *at most one* manifold up to approximate isometry — is open (proved only in restricted cases), so order fixes a *unique* conformal manifold only modulo it.

So manifoldlikeness is **[WALLED, field-shared]**: an open problem of the whole field, the unstated floor under tiers I–II and under Paper VII's emergent-Lorentz premise, and the corpus had never named it (it has zero occurrences across the v7 papers before this one). It is categorically distinct from the `l_step` scale wall: `l_step` is SHARD's *own* no-go (a proved scale impossibility, closed by inputting `G`); manifoldlikeness is the *field's* open emergence problem (not proved either way, not closeable by inputting a constant).
