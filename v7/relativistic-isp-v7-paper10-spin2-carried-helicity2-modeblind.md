The headline: **spin-2 structure carried, Weinberg coupling owned, helicity-2 polarization content record-blind — the graviton's spin is priced, and its one genuinely missing piece is the program's known walls.**

## 1. Introduction: what "spin-2" actually asks

Paper 57 priced gravity's *equation of state* and proved its *scale* unfixable. It left a sharper question only partly answered: does the record structure carry the graviton's **spin** — its rank-2, two-polarization, helicity-`±2` character — or is that, like the scale, record-blind? The corpus's static pricing (paper 52) found four of the nine stress components universal (`T_{00}` and `T_{0i}`, the energy and momentum densities, at `1/4ν`) and the spatial stress collapsing under refinement, and attributed the missing propagating content variously to dimensionality, lattice shape, or holography. This paper replaces those guesses with a clean three-layer pricing.

The decomposition is the point: "spin-2" is not one claim but three. There is the **tensor structure** of the source (is the radiating multipole a traceless symmetric rank-2 quadrupole?); the **coupling** (does a conserved symmetric current force a helicity-2 field, by Weinberg's theorem?); and the **polarization content** (does the records fix the two physical propagating helicities?). Pricing each separately shows exactly where SHARD is strong (the first two) and exactly what it cannot supply (the third) — and that the third is not new. The discipline is pre-geometric: the source multipoles and the conservation law are record-internal stress statistics; where a "metric direction" or "polarization basis" appears, it is named as the walled input the records lack, never used as a derivation input.

## 2. (Q-tensor) the spin-2 tensor structure is carried [OWNED]

The record matter stress carries the graviton's tensor fingerprint outright, reproducing paper 55-A on a self-contained binary source. Receipt `p10` takes two equal record masses on a circular orbit and computes the mass multipoles symbolically:

- the **mass dipole** `Σ m x` vanishes (the symmetric binary's centre of mass) — so dipole radiation is forbidden, as the conservation hierarchy demands (the monopole is the conserved mass, the dipole the conserved momentum; neither radiates);
- the **quadrupole** `Q_{ij} = Σ m (3 x_i x_j − r²δ_{ij})` is **traceless** (`tr Q = 0`, sympy-exact) and nonzero, with `Q_{xx}(t) = 1 + 3\cos(2t)` — it oscillates at **twice** the orbital frequency, period `π`, the hallmark of quadrupole (not dipole) radiation;
- the radiating content is **pure `l ≥ 2`**: `Q` is symmetric (no `l = 1` vector part) and traceless (no `l = 0` monopole part).

These are the exact kinematic selection rules of a spin-2 source — quadrupole-leading, dipole-forbidden, period-`π`, `l ≥ 2`-only. They are properties of the conserved-stress **index structure** and the **conservation hierarchy** (the monopole and dipole are conserved charges that cannot radiate), witnessed on — but not dependent on — the concrete circular embedding: the binary on its orbit is an illustrative Tier-B slice, reproducing paper 55-A, not the source of the selection rules. The rank-2 symmetric-traceless tensor structure is **owned**; the numeric multipole demo is a confirmatory check in an assumed Euclidean 3-geometry.

## 3. (Q-coupling) the Weinberg lever: a conserved symmetric current forces helicity-2 [LEVER]

The second layer is the coupling, and here SHARD owns precisely the input the relevant external theorem needs. (§2 and this section exhibit two illustrative slices of the *same* record matter stress: the binary slice carries the multipole selection rules, the massless-field slice carries the conserved-symmetric algebra — neither toy model is claimed to carry both at once.) Weinberg's soft-graviton uniqueness theorem states that a massless **helicity-2** field, to have a Lorentz-invariant `S`-matrix, *must* couple universally to a **conserved symmetric rank-2 current** — the stress tensor (this is the field-theoretic origin of the equivalence principle). SHARD owns that current. Receipt `p10` verifies, for a massless record field `φ` (a plane wave with `ω² = |k|²`), that the record stress `T_{μν} = ∂_μφ ∂_νφ − ½ η_{μν}(∂φ)²` is **symmetric** and **conserved** (`∂_μ T^{μν} = 0`, sympy-exact on-shell). So the record matter sector supplies exactly the source Weinberg's theorem couples a graviton to.

Two honesty points fix the grade at **[LEVER]**, not a derivation of the graviton. First, the theorem itself is external: SHARD supplies the conserved symmetric current; "a massless helicity-2 field must couple to it" is Weinberg's result, borrowed, not re-derived. Second, the **Weinberg–Witten** no-go (a theory with a Lorentz-covariant conserved stress tensor admits no massless particles of spin `> 1`) does *not* block this — the record stress is an *emergent internal modular current*, not the fundamental Poincaré charge, so the emergent graviton evades Weinberg–Witten exactly as every emergent-gravity program does. The records own the soft-graviton source and respect the no-go; they do not, and this paper does not claim to, construct the helicity-2 quantum.

## 4. (Q-polarization) the two helicities are record-blind — the metric and mode walls [BLIND / WALL]

The third layer is where the records stop, and the stopping point is exactly the program's known walls. The propagating graviton has **two** physical polarizations — the helicity-`±2` transverse-traceless `+` and `×` modes. Obtaining them from the source requires the **transverse-traceless (TT) projection**, which reduces the five-component symmetric-traceless source tensor to two independent components — and that projection needs two inputs the records lack. Receipt `p10` makes the counting explicit: a symmetric traceless `3`-tensor (5 degrees of freedom), TT-projected along a direction `n̂`, leaves exactly **2** independent components (`Stt_xx = −Stt_yy` and `Stt_xy`, transverse-traceless to machine precision). The two missing inputs are:

- a propagation **direction** `n̂` — a unit vector in the **emergent metric continuum**. This is *not* the absolute-scale wall: a direction is scale-free. It is the continuous Lorentzian metric structure (null cones, directions) that the records do not yet build — Paper IV owns the connectivity graph and its hop-metric *up to one scale*, and places the absolute metric as the `l_step` no-go for the scale and *premature* for the continuous structure a propagation direction needs;
- a canonical **mode frame** — *which two* of the five source components are the physical `+, ×` modes. Given a metric this `+/×` labelling is pure gauge (the physical helicity-2 magnitude and eigenvalues are basis-independent); it becomes a genuine *second* missing input only because the records lack the full continuum that would render it gauge — and selecting a canonical frame among a degenerate set is exactly **mode-canonicalization**, the fifth wall (Papers VIII–IX).

So physically this is largely **one** missing structure — the emergent metric continuum, with a canonical mode frame on top — seen through two of the program's walls. The missing second helicity is **record-blind for the same reasons everything absolute is**, and it is **not a new gravitational obstruction**. This re-attributes the corpus's earlier accounts of the missing propagating content — `3+1`-dimensionality, non-square lattice, holography — to their actual source: the two polarizations are walled by the unbuilt metric continuum and the mode frame, not dimensionally accidental.

## 5. What this prices, and what it does not

The three-layer pricing sharpens Paper 57's "spin-2-blind, partly priced" into a clean ledger. The graviton's **tensor structure** is owned (§2); its **coupling** is owned-input-to-an-external-lever (§3); only its **propagating polarization content** is blind, and that blindness is the metric and mode walls (§4). In the corpus's component count, this aligns by *sector*, not by an identity of objects: the four universal static charges (`T_{00}, T_{0i}`) are the conserved energy-momentum that §3's Weinberg source rests on; the five-component traceless spatial stress that paper 52 found blind as a *static* modular charge is the **source** whose TT projection (5 → 2 along a direction) yields §4's propagating polarizations — the same traceless spin-2 sector seen once as a static charge and once as a propagating source, not the same count. (Paper 55-B separately found the *dynamical* traceless modular channel spin-2-active, consistent with the structure being carried while the two physical modes are not selected.)

The honest scope: this is a **pricing**, largely confirmatory of owned results, with two genuinely new moves — naming the Weinberg lever as the coupling layer, and re-attributing the missing second helicity from geometric guesses to the established metric and mode walls. It does not construct the graviton, derive the equivalence principle (it borrows Weinberg's theorem), or fix the polarizations. The verdict mirrors the program's shape one more time: the records carry the *structure* of gravity's spin, and withhold exactly the *absolute* content — here the propagating two-polarization mode, which is the scale and the mode walls again.
