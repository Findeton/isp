## 2. The closed chiral-gap law [THEOREM]

Paper VIII's oriented branch is the chiral, protection-exact one: a frustrated orientation of the ledger that breaks the Griffiths floor and drives the gap *below* the unoriented value `W = η·θ − log cosh η = 0.156109200157240`. The conjecture was that this minimum collapses as `~2⁻ⁿ` toward gapless; the data ran exactly only to `n = 4`. The conjecture now closes, through an explicit minimizer.

**The minimizer and its two-value collapse.** The oriented minimum over all `2^(2ⁿ−1)` orientation classes is attained at the **alternating-by-weight** orientation, `ε_a = (−1)^{|a|−1}` (the sign of each mask `a` set by the parity of its weight `|a|`). This choice equalizes all `2ⁿ−1` mode coefficients to one scalar `h`, because the orientation-weighted character sum collapses to two values (receipt `p9a`, verified exactly for `n = 2…7`):

`T(s) = Σ_{a≠0} ε_a χ_a(s) = −[ ∏_i (1 − s_i) − 1 ]`,

and `∏_i(1 − s_i)` is `2ⁿ` on the all-minus state and `0` otherwise. So `T(s) = −(2ⁿ−1)` on the single all-minus state and `+1` on each of the other `2ⁿ−1` states. The tilted law therefore has one state of weight `e^{−h(2ⁿ−1)}` and `2ⁿ−1` states of weight `e^{h}`, and its scalar self-consistency `E[χ_a] = e^{−h}` has the closed-form solution

`m̂_min(n) = −ln(1 − 2⁻ⁿ) − δ_n`,

with `δ_n` the doubly-exponentially small, sign-definite correction from the dropped all-minus weight.

**The properties that close the conjecture.** Receipt `p9a` confirms, at `dps = 130`, against the corpus exact anchors: `n = 3: 0.133530982072…`, `n = 4: 0.064538521138…`, and `n = 5: 0.031748698315…` — so the `n = 5` value the corpus carried as a *sampled upper bound* coincides with the closed form, and is the exact minimum given the global-optimality lemma below. The closed form has three load-bearing properties: it is **strictly positive at every finite `n`** (the all-minus correction keeps `δ_n` from reaching the leading term — gapped at finite `n`); it **vanishes only as `n → ∞`** (asymptotically gapless, *not* finite-`n` gapless); and it has **prefactor exactly 1** (`m̂_min(n)·2ⁿ → 1`: `1.068, 1.033, 1.016, …, 1.0000000149` at `n = 25`). Below the unoriented floor for all `n ≥ 3`, this is the chiral branch going gapless.

**The global-optimality lemma.** That the alternating-by-weight orientation is the *global* minimizer over all `2^(2ⁿ−1)` classes is verified by **exhaustive brute force** at `n = 2` (all 8 orientations), `n = 3` (all 128), and `n = 4` (all 32768), where the minimum equals the closed-form value and is realized by the alternating-by-weight class. So the theorem is exact modulo one *finite* global-optimality lemma (exhaustively brute-forced through `n = 4`; the closed form supplies `n ≥ 5`) — a named, bounded residue, not an open-ended conjecture. With it, Paper VIII's "theorem (gapped) + conjecture (gapless)" becomes **theorem + theorem**: protection broken ⇒ gapped at `W`; protection exact ⇒ asymptotically gapless at `−ln(1 − 2⁻ⁿ)`.

## 3. The record orientation-class group [SYNTHESIS]

Paper VIII §8(a) asked for a Tier-1 functor from orientation classes to a projective symmetry group. The record-internal half of that functor builds, on owned machinery (receipt `p9b`). The orientation classes are the character group `K̂ = Hom(K, ℤ₂)` of the relation code `K` (Paper 8 Thm 5.1, exact). The candidate symmetry group is `Aut(K)`, the position-permutations preserving `K` as a set of position-subsets; the candidate gauge redundancy is the `K^⊥` spin-flip group (the relabelings acting trivially on the physics). These assemble into the semidirect product `K̂ ⋊ Aut(K)`, and receipt `p9b` verifies that `Aut(K)` acts on `K̂` by a well-defined permutation representation across the five smallest ledgers.

The signature is exact: for the full 3-spin ledger, `|Aut(K)| = 168 = |GL(3,2)| = (2³−1)(2³−2)(2³−4)`, reproducing Paper 8's `GL(3,2)` orientation symmetry and its `1 + 7 + 7 + 1` orientation-class multiplet. So the record group is genuine and computable: a real Tier-1 object built from the relation code alone. The question §4 settles is whether it *is* Wen's projective symmetry group, or only its ingredients.

## 4. The first no-go: no Wen-PSG without geometry [NO-GO]

Wen's projective symmetry group is an extension `1 → IGG → PSG → SG → 1` whose physical content — the data distinguishing distinct quantum orders — is its extension class in `H²(SG, IGG)`, with `SG` the **space group** (lattice translations and point group). That content exists because the symmetry acts on a *spatial* lattice carrying projective phases. The record group of §3 lacks exactly that source:

- `Aut(K)` is a **finite permutation group** — no translations, no lattice momentum, no projective spatial phases;
- the assembled `K̂ ⋊ Aut(K)` is, by construction, a semidirect (hence split) product; the substantive point is that nothing in the record data can *force a nontrivial cocycle* — the orientation data is strictly `ℤ₂`-valued (character signs) and `Aut(K)` acts by honest permutations, so no `U(1)`-valued `2`-cocycle can arise from `K` alone (receipt `p9b`). A projective phase would need a continuous gauge on a spatial lattice, which is absent.

A nontrivial projective PSG needs a genuine `2`-cocycle, which needs the space group `SG`, which needs **emergent geometry** — and the absolute geometric structure is behind the `l_step` wall (Paper III). So the functor reconstructs the PSG *ingredients* — an `IGG`-like gauge, an `SG`-like relabeling group, the projective-protection phenomenology — but **not Wen's group**: its cohomological content is gated on emergent spacetime. This is the first no-go: **no Wen-PSG without geometry**. It is the matter-sector face of the same wall the scale no-go names; the PSG-protection map that Paper VIII used phenomenologically is exactly as far as the records reach.

## 5. The second no-go: no record-forced chirality bridge [NO-GO]

Paper VIII §8(b) asked whether the Weyl handedness is a record-forced function of the orientation class. It is not, and receipt `p9c` makes the obstruction structural and proved.

**Two layers.** The orientation class `σ : K → ℤ₂` is a character of the relation code — the ledger / Ising layer, `dim K` bits. The Weyl handedness `m` is an `SO(3)` representation label on the rotation/dilation fiber (Paper 9 D3, the belt-trick double cover) — one bit. A bridge would be a record-internal map `K̂ → {+, −}` forcing the single fiber bit from the `dim K` ledger bits.

**Handedness-blindness.** The ledger layer carries no such bit. The gap `m̂`, the code `K`, the orientation group `K̂ ⋊ Aut(K)`, and every ledger quantity are functions of `σ` *alone* — the handedness `m` never enters their computation (receipt `p9c`: the chiral-minimum gap is recomputed from the orientation signs with no handedness argument anywhere). Flipping a putative handedness label changes nothing in the ledger. The records are blind to the very bit a bridge must output.

**Parity-odd on parity-symmetric data.** Parity is a *joint* symmetry: `P : (σ, m) ↦ (σ̄, −m)`, and the gap is parity-invariant — receipt `p9c` confirms `m̂(σ) = m̂(σ̄)` for the minimizer and its spin-flip partner. (This invariance is automatic: the partner `σ̄` realizes the spin-flip `s → −s`, a measure-preserving bijection of state space under which `D(P‖U)` is invariant for any law — which only reinforces the handedness-blindness.) A bridge `f : σ ↦ m` would therefore have to be parity-*odd*, `f(σ̄) = −f(σ)`, on data the records leave parity-*symmetric*. Nothing record-internal singles out one member of the pair `{σ, σ̄}` as "left," so `f` is undetermined; choosing it *is* the coupling `g`, an `(M)`-sector input (Paper 9 §9). Consistently, self-mirror (achiral) orientations carry no handedness (`m = 0`), matching Paper 9's vanishing Weyl seed for achiral ledgers, and the handedness *magnitude* is itself `g`-dependent (the Weyl asymmetry vanishes at `g = 0`). So the chirality bridge is **not record-forced** — a proved no-go on the executed machinery, upgrading Paper 8 open-item-2 and Paper VIII's "different layer" non-claim from an open gap to an impossibility.
