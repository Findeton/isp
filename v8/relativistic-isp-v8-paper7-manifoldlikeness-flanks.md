## Abstract

Paper 4 §5 classified manifoldlikeness as the **field-shared open gate**: Kleitman–Rothschild (KR) layered orders dominate uniform counting (`2^{⌊n²/4⌋}`), fool the Myrheim–Meyer estimator (`d_MM = 2.38`, manifold-plausible), the interference-phase probe returned a designed artifact/null, and "whether the click-law dynamics suppresses them" has no corpus lever. This paper works the three *flanking* moves that are available without attacking the gate head-on, and grades each honestly. **(I) The concordance certificate [DEFINITIONAL + DEMONSTRATED].** Paper 4's "certification needs agreeing estimators" is made operational: a four-axis battery — Myrheim–Meyer inversion, height band, midpoint-scaling dimension, interval-abundance profile distance — with all bands *control-calibrated* on reference sprinklings under the held-out-coordinates methodology. On the populations: both positive controls certify at their true dimensions and at no other; the paper-4 KR trap is reproduced (`d_MM = 2.375`) and rejected; a **designed adversary** (a KR block density-tuned to `d_MM = 2.017` plus a disjoint chain tuned into the defender's own height band, `H = 39`) fools axes 1 *and* 2 simultaneously and is caught by axes 3/4 (abundance distance `0.43` against a `0.03` threshold). Limits stated: passing is finite-`N` evidence, never sufficiency, and the existence of an adversary against *any* finite battery is left open, not denied. **(II) The two-dimensional regime [THEOREM ×2 + IMPORT + DEMONSTRATED].** In `1+1` dimensions the gate's *embeddability* half becomes tractable and decidable: a finite order embeds in the causal order of `M²` **iff** its Dushnik–Miller order dimension is `≤ 2` (proof in-paper; the lightcone-coordinates correspondence is checked bit-identically, and a realizer extracted from a transitive orientation reproduces the order exactly); the test is polynomial-time while `dim ≤ 3` is NP-complete (Yannakakis — **[IMPORT]**), so the 2D gate is exactly the regime where certification is *algorithmically* meaningful. Faithfulness is then a **discrepancy statement, quantitatively**: for a point set in `[0,1]²` with dominance order, `|r − 1/2| ≤ 12·(N/(N−1))·D*_N + 1/(2(N−1))` (proof in-paper; `≤ 14 D*_N + 1/(N−1)` for `N ≥ 7`), so low-discrepancy embeddings *force* the Myrheim–Meyer estimator to read `2` — verified with the star discrepancy computed exactly, and the bound becomes discriminating as `N` grows (`0.045` at `N = 2048`, below the adversarial cluster's deviation `0.246` measured at `N = 96`). **(III) The measures the records do not supply [THEOREM + DEMONSTRATED + SCOUT].** The frontier question "does the click-law give any lever on KR suppression" is sharpened from an observation to a theorem for the natural candidate class: **no record-native counting measure and no extensive action suppresses the layered bulk.** With `m_U` (unlabeled), `m_L` (labeled, `n!/|Aut|`), `m_H` (commit histories: `m_L · e(P)`, one microstate per odometer history — the linear-extension count), and any action with per-seal-bounded cost `|S| ≤ c·n`, the weighted mass ratio obeys `W(2-layer bulk)/W(dim ≤ 2) ≥ 2^{⌊n²/4⌋}/[(n!)³ e^{2βcn}] → ∞`, with **self-contained counting** (free-bipartite lower bound; realizer-pair upper bound; `e(P) ≤ n!`) — no KR asymptotics imported. The exact census (all posets to `n = 7`, `A000112`/`A001035`/`A006455`-cross-checked) shows the direction at enumerable `n`: the history measure *anti-selects* — it **boosts** the KR-type share (`0.240 → 0.342` at `n = 7`) and lowers the geometric share — and the 2D Benincasa–Dowker action pulls the *wrong way* for a positive Boltzmann weight (the bulk has *lower* mean action; gap `−2.76 → −3.72` from `n = 6` to `7`) **[SCOUT]**. Consequence, stated as the sharpened frontier: any positive-weight suppression mechanism over a counting base measure must carry **super-extensive action gaps, `Ω(n²)`** — the bar the field's complex-phase mechanism would have to meet on magnitude grounds alone, structurally consistent with pE's finding that positive weights cannot produce the *cancellation* mechanism either. The gate stays **[OPEN]**; the flanks now carry a certificate, a decidable regime, and a theorem-grade negative that closes the cheap exits.

## 2. Flank I — the estimator-concordance certificate [DEFINITIONAL + DEMONSTRATED]

### 2.1 The certificate

> **Definition (the four-axis certificate `C(d)`).** Fix a candidate order on `N` elements and a dimension hypothesis `d ∈ {2, 3}`. Compute, from the order alone: **(1)** `d_MM`, the Myrheim–Meyer inversion of the ordering fraction through the exact `f(d) = Γ(d+1)Γ(d/2)/(2Γ(3d/2))`; **(2)** the height `H` (longest chain); **(3)** `d_mid`, the midpoint-scaling dimension (the largest order-interval `I(x,y)`, halved at the element `m` maximizing `min(|I(x,m)|, |I(m,y)|)`; `d_mid = log₂(|I(x,y)|/|I_half|)`); **(4)** the interval-abundance profile `(N₀ … N₅)/pairs` and its total-variation distance to the reference profile. **PASS(d)** := all four axes land inside the *operative* reference bands for the *same* `d`. The operative rule, in full: the raw bands are min/max envelopes of 8 reference diamond sprinklings at the same `N` (held-out-coordinates methodology: coordinates generate controls and score answers; every candidate is analyzed order-only); axes 1–2 use the envelope widened by half-width `×1.5` with an absolute half-width floor `0.15`; axis 3 uses `×1.8` with floor `0.4`, computes `d_mid = log₂((|I(x,y)|+2)/(|I_half|+2))` (inclusive-endpoint offset), and an undefined `d_mid` (largest interval too thin to halve) **fails the axis**; axis 4 uses threshold `3·max(tv_max, 0.01)` where `tv_max` is the in-family reference spread — at `d = 2` the `0.01` floor dominates the calibrated `0.0046`, so that threshold is floor-set, not purely control-calibrated (disclosed). **[DEFINITIONAL]**

The axes are chosen for *independent failure modes*: the ordering fraction is a global pair statistic (foolable by density tuning); the height is an extremal chain statistic (foolable by grafting); the midpoint scaling probes interval *geometry*; the abundance profile probes interval *statistics* at all small sizes. Estimator provenance: Myrheim/Meyer for axis 1 (exact fractions `f(2) = 1/2`, `f(3) = 8/35` re-verified to `10⁻⁴⁰`); the causal-set estimator family reviewed in Surya's Living Review for axes 2–4 **[IMPORT]**; the 2D height law `H ≈ 2√N` (Vershik–Kerov / Logan–Shepp **[IMPORT]**) is quoted as commentary only — the certificate uses control bands, not the constant (measured ratio `0.890` at `N = 480`).

At `N = 480` (receipt `g1`, 10/10): raw envelopes `d = 2`: `d_MM ∈ (1.955, 2.06)`, `H ∈ (36, 42)`, in-family abundance TV ≤ `0.0046`; `d = 3`: `d_MM ∈ (2.861, 3.087)`, `H ∈ (14, 15)`; **operative bands** (the rule of §2.1) `d = 2`: `d_MM ∈ (1.857, 2.157)`, `H ∈ (34.5, 43.5)`, TV ≤ `0.0300`; `d = 3`: `d_MM ∈ (2.805, 3.143)`, `H ∈ (13.75, 15.25)`, TV ≤ `0.0465`. (The fresh 3D control's `d_MM = 2.847` sits below the raw envelope and inside the operative band — the widening is what absorbs single-draw fluctuation, and is why it is part of the definition.)

### 2.3 Limits [OPEN, stated]

Passing `C(d)` is finite-`N` *evidence* of manifold-plausibility, never sufficiency: the Hauptvermutung (embedding uniqueness) and the faithfulness question remain open even for orders that pass, and the existence of a designed adversary defeating any *fixed* finite battery is left open — the demonstration shows this battery catching this adversary class, nothing stronger. What the certificate adds over paper 4 is not a theorem but an *instrument*: a defined, calibrated, adversarially-probed pass/fail object that any future dynamics claim ("the click-law produces manifold-like orders") would have to clear, at scale, on order data alone.

## 3. Flank II — the two-dimensional regime [THEOREM + IMPORT + DEMONSTRATED]

In `1+1` dimensions the gate splits cleanly into an embeddability half that is **decidable** and a faithfulness half that is **quantitative**. Everything in this section is elementary; the value is that it is exact, and that it identifies 2D as the regime where "manifoldlike?" is not just estimable but *checkable*.

### 3.1 Embeddability is order dimension ≤ 2 [THEOREM]

> **Theorem 3.1 (classical; proved here for self-containment).** A finite strict order `P` embeds order-isomorphically into `(M², ≼_causal)` (distinct points, `J⁺`-causal precedence) **iff** its Dushnik–Miller order dimension is at most 2.

*Proof.* In lightcone coordinates `u = t + x`, `v = t − x`, causal (`J⁺`) precedence of distinct points of `M²` is coordinatewise dominance with ties allowed on one coordinate: `p ≼ q` iff `u_p ≤ u_q` and `v_p ≤ v_q` (and `p ≠ q`); the interior (chronological, `I⁺`) relation is the strict-dominance sub-relation. (⇐) If `dim P ≤ 2` there is a realizer `{L₁, L₂}` (two linear extensions with `P = L₁ ∩ L₂`); map each element to `(rank_{L₁}, rank_{L₂}) ∈ ℝ²`. Then `x <_P y` iff `x` precedes `y` in both linear orders iff the image of `x` is strictly dominated by the image of `y` — an embedding realizing `P` even as the chronological order, a fortiori the causal one. (⇒) Given a causal embedding, define `L₁` = the lexicographic order by `(u, v)` and `L₂` = the lexicographic order by `(v, u)`. Both are linear extensions of the embedded order (a causal relation never decreases either coordinate), and their intersection is exactly it: `x` precedes `y` in both lexicographic orders iff `u_x ≤ u_y` and `v_x ≤ v_y` with not both equal — no perturbation, and null (one-coordinate-tie) relations are handled exactly. Hence `dim P ≤ 2`. ∎

Provenance: the dominance characterization of dimension-2 orders is Dushnik–Miller (1941); the lightcone reading is folklore in causal-set theory; the lexicographic-realizer proof is included so the paper is self-contained. The receipt demonstrates the *strict* (chronological) version — for sprinkled points coordinate ties have probability zero, so the two readings coincide almost surely, and the `N = 300` check is on generic points.

The correspondence is demonstrated bit-identically (receipt `g2`: a 300-point diamond sprinkling's causal order equals the dominance order of its lightcone coordinates on all `N²` pairs), and the constructive half is demonstrated end-to-end: from a transitive orientation of the incomparability graph (the Dushnik–Miller/Golumbic criterion, the same oracle as receipt `pE`, revalidated on `S₃`/chain/antichain/N-poset), a realizer is extracted and its integer-grid embedding reproduces the order exactly. On populations at `n = 24`: every 2D sprinkling order passes `dim ≤ 2` (theorem instance; 12/12 trials); 3D sprinkling and KR orders fail in some trials (failures observed in both populations — the receipt requires existence, not universality).

### 3.2 The gate is decidable exactly in 2D [IMPORT]

Testing `dim ≤ 2` is polynomial-time (transitive orientability; modular-decomposition algorithms make it `O(n²)` — Golumbic; McConnell–Spinrad), while testing `dim ≤ 3` is **NP-complete** (Yannakakis 1982). So `1+1` dimensions is the one dimension where an exact efficient decision procedure for the embeddability half is *known*: for `d ≥ 3`, Minkowski-causal embeddability is no longer equivalent to order dimension `≤ d` (the cone is a quadrant only at `d = 2`), and its recognition complexity is not settled by the cited import — the natural dimension-theoretic proxy, `dim ≤ 3`, is NP-complete. This sharpens what "restricted regime" means: 2D claims about the record order are falsifiable by algorithm, and the paper 4 `GEOMETRIC` class (which `pE` flagged as generous) is now placed exactly — it is the embeddability class, and what it generously omits is precisely the faithfulness half, quantified next.

### 3.3 Faithfulness is a discrepancy statement [THEOREM]

> **Theorem 3.2.** Let `Q` be `N ≥ 2` points in `[0,1]²` (distinct coordinates) with the dominance order, `r` its ordering fraction (`2·#comparable pairs / N(N−1)`), and `D*_N` its star discrepancy. Then
> `|r − 1/2| ≤ 12·(N/(N−1))·D*_N + 1/(2(N−1))`;
> in particular `|r − 1/2| ≤ 14·D*_N + 1/(N−1)` for `N ≥ 7`.

*Proof.* Write `g(u,v) = (1−u)(1−v)` and let `μ_N` be the empirical measure. For a point `p`, the elements dominating `p` occupy the corner box `B_p = (u_p, 1] × (v_p, 1]`, and `#\{q ≻ p\} = N·μ_N(B_p)` (the point `p` itself is not in the open box, so no self-count correction). By inclusion–exclusion, `B_p = [0,1]² − [0,u_p]×[0,1] − [0,1]×[0,v_p] + [0,u_p]×[0,v_p]`: the full square carries no error and each of the three remaining boxes is anchored at the origin, so `|μ_N(B_p) − λ(B_p)| ≤ 3D*_N` with `λ(B_p) = g(u_p, v_p)`. Summing over `p`: the comparable-pair count `K = Σ_p #\{q ≻ p\}` satisfies `K = N²·E_{μ_N}[g] ± 3N²D*_N`. By Koksma–Hlawka, `|E_{μ_N}[g] − ∫g| ≤ V_{HK}(g)·D*_N ≤ 3D*_N` (mixed-derivative mass `∫∫|∂²g| = 1` plus the two one-dimensional face variations `≤ 1` each — the crude bound suffices), and `∫g = 1/4`. Hence `K = N²(1/4 ± 6D*_N)`, and `r = 2K/(N(N−1)) = (N/(N−1))·(1/2 ± 12 D*_N)`, giving the display; for `N ≥ 7`, `12·N/(N−1) ≤ 14` and `1/(2(N−1)) ≤ 1/(N−1)`. ∎

Since the Myrheim–Meyer inversion is smooth at `r = 1/2` with `f(2) = 1/2`, low-discrepancy embeddings *force* `d_MM → 2` at rate `D*_N` **[DERIVED]**. Receipt `g2` verifies the bound with the star discrepancy computed **exactly** (critical-grid maximization, both closure sides) on three families at `N = 96` — Halton(2,3) (`D* = 0.046`, `|r−1/2| = 0.0013`), iid uniform (`0.088`, `0.032`), an adversarial cluster (`0.497`, `0.246`) — the bound holding on all three and ordering them; and the scaling run shows the bound becoming *discriminating*: `14D* + 1/(N−1)` for Halton falls `0.661 → 0.133 → 0.045` at `N = 96/512/2048`, below the cluster's deviation. Honest scope: the discrepancy is a property of a *chosen* embedding (a realizer choice); the theorem quantifies "this embedding is faithful", and realizer-dependence is declared — a canonical-realizer optimization is left **[OPEN]**.

### 3.4 What flank II buys

The 2D regime now has: an exact embeddability criterion (Theorem 3.1), an efficient decision procedure (§3.2), and a quantitative faithfulness metric with an estimator-forcing bound (Theorem 3.2). A future claim "the record dynamics produces 2D-manifoldlike orders in the dense regime" is therefore *fully falsifiable*: test `dim ≤ 2` exactly, extract a realizer, measure its discrepancy. No analogous package is *known* for `d ≥ 3` (and §3.2's import makes the natural dimension-theoretic route to one NP-complete).

## 4. Flank III — the measures the records do not supply [THEOREM + DEMONSTRATED + SCOUT]

Paper 4 §5's frontier question — does the forced click-law give any lever on KR suppression? — had the honest answer "nothing in the corpus suggests a lever". This section sharpens that in both directions: the natural candidates are *proved* insufficient, and the exact census shows the strongest natural candidate pointing the *wrong way*.

### 4.1 The three record-native counting measures [DEFINITIONAL]

On `n`-element orders: `m_U(P) = 1` per isomorphism class (the unlabeled uniform of `pE`); `m_L(P) = n!/|Aut P|` (labeled uniform); and the **commit-history measure** `m_H(P) = m_L(P)·e(P)`, where `e(P)` is the number of linear extensions — motivated by paper 1's odometer: each commit history (a linear extension of the record order) is one microstate, so `m_H` counts (labeling, history) pairs. `m_H` is a *counting* definition, not a derivation from the click dynamics — tagged **[DEFINITIONAL]**, with the honest note that a genuinely dynamical measure (e.g. click-law-weighted sequential growth in the Rideout–Sorkin arena) is a different, unbuilt object.

### 4.2 The extensive-action no-go [THEOREM]

> **Theorem 4.1.** Let `W(P) = m(P)·e^{−βS(P)}` with `m ∈ {m_U, m_L, m_H}`, `β ≥ 0`, and `S` any **extensive** action: `|S(P)| ≤ c·n` for all `n`-element orders. Let `B_n` be the two-layer orders (height ≤ 2) and `G_n` the `dim ≤ 2` class. Then
> `W(B_n) / W(G_n) ≥ 2^{⌊n²/4⌋} / [(n!)³ e^{2βcn}] → ∞`.
> In particular no such weight suppresses the layered bulk relative to the geometric class, under any of the three measures, for any `β` and `c`.

*Proof.* Lower bound: on the fixed balanced split `{1,…,⌊n/2⌋} ∪ rest`, every one of the `2^{⌊n/2⌋·⌈n/2⌉} = 2^{⌊n²/4⌋}` relation patterns is a valid labeled two-layer order (transitivity across two layers is vacuous), each with `m_U`-mass at least `1/n!` per class-count accounting (a class has at most `n!` labelings), `m_L`-mass `≥ 1` per labeled order, `m_H`-mass `≥ m_L`-mass (since `e(P) ≥ 1`), and weight factor `≥ e^{−βcn}`. Upper bound: every `dim ≤ 2` order is the intersection of an ordered realizer pair of linear orders, so there are at most `(n!)²` labeled orders in `G_n`; each carries `m_L ≤ n!` per unlabeled accounting or `m_L`-mass `≤ (n!)²` total, `e(P) ≤ n!` (so `m_H`-mass `≤ (n!)³`), and weight factor `≤ e^{+βcn}`. Per measure: `m_L` gives numerator `≥ 2^{⌊n²/4⌋} e^{−βcn}` and denominator `≤ (n!)² e^{+βcn}`; `m_U` costs one extra `n!` in the numerator (a class has at most `n!` labelings); `m_H` costs one extra `n!` in the denominator (`e(P) ≤ n!`) while its numerator only grows (`e(P) ≥ 1`). The uniform bound across the three is therefore `2^{⌊n²/4⌋}/[(n!)³ e^{2βcn}]`, and `(n²/4)·log 2` dominates `3·log n! + 2βcn = O(n log n)`. ∎

Two remarks make this the right statement. First, height-2 orders fail *any* fixed-dimension faithfulness certificate at large `n` (the height axis alone: a faithful `d`-dimensional order's height grows without bound — `H ~ N^{1/d}`, the sprinkling height law, an **[IMPORT]** distinct from the KR asymptotics — while a two-layer order has `H = 2`), so `B_n` is unambiguously non-manifoldlike bulk — no KR asymptotics **[IMPORT]** is needed anywhere (the classical `2^{⌊n²/4⌋}` lower-bound construction is the two-layer family itself). Second, the theorem covers the odometer: the history factor `e(P)` is at most `n!`, i.e. **history-counting is an `O(n log n)`-scale reweighting** (`log e(P) ≤ n log n` — a log factor above extensive, still negligible against an `n²`-scale entropy). The sharpened frontier is a bar, not a hope:

> **Corollary 4.2 [DERIVED].** Any positive-weight mechanism *over a record-native counting base measure* (`m_U`, `m_L`, or `m_H`) that suppresses the layered bulk must carry action gaps `Ω(n²)` — super-extensive in the seal count. Per-seal-bounded costs (the click-law's per-seal quantities — the `σ`, `χ` increments of paper 1 — are of this type) are structurally insufficient. Non-counting *dynamical* measures are outside the theorem's scope, as §4.2's closing sentence states.

This composes with `pE`'s scoping rather than repeating it: `pE` showed positive real weights cannot produce the *cancellation mechanism* (interference); Theorem 4.1 shows extensive positive weights cannot even produce *suppression by magnitude*. What survives for a dynamics-side resolution of the gate is exactly: super-extensive positive actions, complex weights, or measures outside the counting class (genuinely dynamical growth measures).

### 4.3 The exact census at enumerable `n` [DEMONSTRATED]

Anchors: `e(chain) = 1`, `e(antichain) = n!`, `e(N-poset) = 5`. The shares:

### 4.4 The super-extensive candidate, scouted [SCOUT]

The one in-corpus object with `n²`-capable scale is the Benincasa–Dowker action (its abundance terms count pairs). The census records its class separation: mean `S^{(2)}` over the bulk minus the geometric class is `−2.76` at `n = 6` and `−3.72` at `n = 7` — **negative and growing in magnitude**: the bulk has *lower* mean action, so a positive Boltzmann weight `e^{−βS}` *enhances* the bulk at enumerable `n` (consistent with `pE`'s check-8 reading that `e^{−βS}` favors low-action, antichain-like orders). Grade: **[SCOUT]** — enumerable `n` cannot exhibit asymptotics (`pE`'s scoping holds here too), and no trend claim is made beyond the recorded values; but the sign is a warning that the natural "Boltzmann-suppression with the BD action" hope fails in the accessible regime in the *direction*, not just the magnitude, and Corollary 4.2's `Ω(n²)` bar applies to whatever replaces it.

## 5. Honest scope, and what would change the gate's status

The gate is exactly as open after this paper as before it: no claim here produces manifold-like orders from the click-law, and none is graded higher than its content. What has changed: (i) any future production claim now has a defined instrument to clear (§2) and, in 2D, an exact decision procedure plus a quantitative faithfulness metric to clear (§3); (ii) the search space for a dynamics-side mechanism is provably narrowed — counting measures and extensive actions are out (§4), leaving super-extensive actions, complex weights (paper 4 §5's cross-connection to the un-forceable composition bit stands), or non-counting dynamical measures; (iii) the 2D regime is identified as the honest first battlefield: decidable, quantitative, and the only dimension where embeddability certification can be efficient.
