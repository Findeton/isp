# Design note 5: the dense-vs-sparse decider — formalizing paper 1 §7's deferred computation

**Status:** design + derivation document, 2026-07-02. **[Executed same-date: receipt `f6` green 11/11 first run — outcomes in `LEDGER.md` #34; this note is left as pre-registered.]** Written **before** receipt `f6` runs; the criterion, the exemplar constructions, the predicted clarifying negative, and the check list are fixed here. This is the wall-deciding item of the post-`j4` plan: the content-degeneracy wall stands only on the conjunction *(sparse realization) ∧ (exact determinism) ∧ (menu as computed)*, and this note attacks the first conjunct's decidability.

## 1. What paper 1 actually defers, and the known hazard

Paper 1 §7 (placement item): *"whether gravitational sealing is sparse (genuinely indivisible) or dense (classical shadow). Sparse-vs-dense is a decidable computation on the decoherence functional `D` — whether `D` carries off-diagonal support between commitments."* The hazard, already proved in the corpus: paper X shows structural indivisibility is **operationally undecidable from the reduced channel**, and v6 paper 56 shows state-independent sealing is always CP-divisible. So whatever the decidable criterion is, it cannot live in reduced dynamics at the commit grain — and (this note's predicted clarifying negative) it cannot be the *raw magnitude* of `D`'s off-diagonals either, because partial decoherence leaves nonzero off-diagonals in both regimes. The pre-registered claim: the correct formalization is **record-witnessed support** — whether the between-commit coherence loss is held by a *committed* record (dense) or by an *uncommitted* collar degree of freedom (sparse), with three equivalent faces: the committed-overlap staircase, the recoverable slack, and the echo floor.

## 2. The matched-profile exemplar pair (the decidability exhibit)

Both exemplars are pure-dephasing record processes on a system qubit with the **identical** single-time coherence profile `G(t) = s^t` and identical at-commit data — built so that everything the click law and single-time tomography can see is the same — while the regime differs:

- **Dense exemplar (classical shadow):** `k` micro-records per unit interval, each step committing contraction `s^{1/k}`. The committed-record overlap advances *continuously*: `G_committed(t) = s^t` at every `t`. No collar; nothing recoverable; every infinitesimal loss is a fact.
- **Sparse exemplar (genuinely indivisible):** one uncommitted **collar** qubit accumulates the intra-interval loss unitarily (conditional rotation with `θ(t) = arccos(s^{t−t_n})`, so the *reduced* profile matches `s^t` exactly); at each commit the collar state is **swapped into a fresh record and the collar resets** — the loss committed so far becomes a fact only at seal events. The committed overlap is a **staircase**: flat at `s^{n−1}` between commits, dropping to `s^n` at commit `n`.

Both are closed-form products of single-qubit overlaps — every check below is computable at `dps = 40`, no simulation noise anywhere except the one disclosed float64 measurement (check 5).

**One structural subtlety, stated in advance:** in the sparse exemplar the *next* seal commits the collar's accumulated path-dependence — mid-interval alternatives are unrecorded only *until the next commit*. That is what "indivisible" means operationally: the interval is one undivided step, and the recoverability window closes when it commits. All recovery/echo statements below act mid-interval, before the closing seal.

## 3. The criterion, three faces, and the predicted negative

**Ω (the decider):** *does the committed-record overlap advance strictly between commitments?* Dense: yes (by `1 − s^{1/k}` per micro-step). Sparse: no (staircase, advance exactly `0`). Equivalent faces: **(R)** the recoverable slack — the optimal system+collar operation mid-interval restores coherence from `s^t` up to the committed floor `s^{n−1}` in sparse (an explicit collar-undo unitary achieves it exactly), and to nothing above `s^t` in dense (there is no collar; committed records are untouchable — data processing); **(E)** the **echo floor**: paper 10's echo protocol measures exactly `R` — the floor equals the committed staircase in sparse and the full profile in dense. **The regime question is therefore lab-facing, and the corpus already owns the instrument (paper 10 §2's echo line).**

**Predicted clarifying negative (pre-registered):** the naive reading of §7's phrase — "off-diagonal magnitude of `D` between commitments" — does **not** decide: mid-interval interference visibility equals the same `s^t` in both exemplars by construction, and two-path `D` entries are nonzero in both. What *does* differ two-time-wise is the mismatch-segment environment overlap (`s^{Δt}` dense vs `cos(Δθ)` sparse) — i.e., only *active multi-time* probing separates, which is paper X's undecidability boundary drawn precisely: passive records and single-time tomography blind; echo-class protocols decisive.

**The supply bridge (the `j4` hook):** the odometer's per-event increment is the committed-overlap drop at the event. Sparse: exactly `−ln s` per commit — the *atomic* increments of route A/C. Dense with evidence-threshold readouts: the drop accumulated since the last readout — *continuous*, exponential-law increments: route B. So **conjunct 1 of the wall's standing conjunction formally equals "the realized regime is sparse"** — in-model, a theorem-shaped statement.

**The determinism leg (feeding design note 6):** the arena's content atom is exact only at the exactly count-dual base; the skewed-base fixed point (`p2c`'s own exhibit, extended) gives the sensitivity `dC/dp` at `p = 1/2`. Exploratory value (to be receipt-fixed): `−0.713` — **first order**, so relative content spread `w ≈ 4.6·δp`: base-configuration inhomogeneity at the `10⁻³` level already produces the `j4`-measured dispersion-passing spread. The exact-determinism conjunct is thereby priced as *count-dual exactness to ~10⁻³ web-wide*.

## 4. What `f6` checks (pre-registered)

(1) profile identity `G(t) = s^t` both exemplars, all sampled `t`, `< 10⁻³⁰`; (2) at-commit blindness — committed overlaps and reduced states at commit times identical across exemplars; (3) **Ω separates**: between-commit committed-overlap advance `= 0` (sparse) vs `> 0` per micro-step (dense); (4) **R/E**: the explicit collar-undo restores sparse to the committed floor exactly; no dense operation exceeds the profile; the echo-floor identity stated against paper 10; (5) the supply bridge: sparse increments exactly atomic; dense readout increments continuous (KS vs exponential, float64 measurement, disclosed); (6) the two-time separator and the clarifying negative: single-slot visibility identical; mismatch-segment overlaps differ (`s^{Δt}` vs `cos Δθ`, values printed); (7) the base-skew sensitivity `dC/dp` and the `w ≈ 4.6·δp` map at `dps = 40`; (8) scope: which regime is *realized* is **not** decided here — it is the placement-sector open, now equipped with a formal, model-decidable, experiment-facing criterion. Constants: `s = 0.7`, three commits, `k = 4` micro-steps per interval, mid-interval sample grid `t ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 0.75}` per interval.
