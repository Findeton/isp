## 1. The reframe

The wall (paper 12 §5, and the order-theory literature): above `d = 2`, Minkowski dimension detaches from order dimension, finite realizers die on the round cone, and recognition inherits believed-hardness from the sphere-order family. Every direct attack — including the field's estimator batteries and embedding procedures — either yields necessary conditions only, or procedures without theorems (Johnston's SVD embeddings produce good `d = 2, 3, 4` coordinates and certify nothing). The reframe: **give up symmetric decision; build the certificate.** A witness structure `W` for "P is `d`-volume-faithful with defect ε" such that (T1) *verifying* `W` is polynomial and yields a constructive embedding with a defect bound, and (T2) every faithful order *has* a `W` (the true geometric structure). Finding `W` is then honest heuristic territory — worst-case hard (as it must be), average-case open, and *any* success is a proof, not a score. This paper pilots the full stack in 2+1D, where the celestial sphere is a circle and every component can be validated against held-out geometry.

## 2. The tomography engine [THEOREM + DEMONSTRATED]

**Theorem 2.1 (shadows).** For `P` the order of points in `M^{1+d−1}`: for every spatial unit vector `m`, the projection `x ↦ (t, m·x⃗)` maps `P` to a 2D dominance order that *coarsens* `P` (`|Δ(m·x⃗)| ≤ |Δx⃗| < Δt`), whose null coordinates are the null-coordinate functionals `u_{±m} = t ∓ m·x⃗`; and `P` is exactly the intersection of its shadows over the *continuum* celestial sphere (per pair, the sup is achieved at `m = Δx⃗/|Δx⃗|`). ∎ *(Receipt: coarsening exact in every tested shadow; the intersection identity's finite face measured — a K-direction grid intersection converges to `P` from above, extra relations `32/11/1` of `17,291` at `K = 16/32/64`.)*

**The target law.** A faithful diamond sprinkling's shadow is *not* uniform-2D: its density is the chord law `ρ(t, s) ∝ √((1−|t|)² − s²)` (verified: TV `0.023` on a central grid against a 200k-projection). The paper-12 certifier generalizes in one move: score the shadow's points against the **target copula** (quantile-transformed chord law) instead of Lebesgue — a weighted `D*`, floor-calibrated with same-law subsamples. Measured: true shadows sit near the two-sample floor (`0.074` vs floor `0.053` at `N = 400` — `1.40×`, within the `1.5×` gate; the floor's subsamples are drawn from the reference itself, slightly deflating it, disclosed); random-extension garbage fails at `0.498`; and the honest fine print is printed rather than hidden — the chord copula's uniform-defect is real but small (`0.040` asymptotically), so at `N = 400` the *unweighted* paper-12 test barely tells shadow from uniform (`0.047` vs `0.030`): the weighting earns its keep in defect attribution and at scale, not as a finite-`N` dramatic kill.

**The harmonic condition and T1's constructive core.** For true Minkowski coordinates, `u_θ(x) = t − x cos θ − y sin θ` — *exactly* a degree-≤1 Fourier polynomial in `θ`. Certificate condition: the quantile-gauged profiles (each `u_θ` recovered intrinsically from ranks through the target marginal — the weighted analog of paper 12's volume gauge) are degree-≤1 concentrated; the Fourier coefficients `(a₀, −a₁, −b₁)` *are* `(t, x, y)`. Measured: RMS residual `0.020`; reconstruction correlation `0.9994/0.9994/0.9994`; the rebuilt point set agrees with the original order on `99.67%` of pairs and matches box counts to a mean absolute difference of `2.1` elements — faithfulness transfers to the reconstruction without any closed-form volume formula. This is the pilot form of **T1** (certificate ⇒ constructive embedding + defect bound); the journal-grade quantitative statement (defect propagation constants, the `S²` version) is the named next stage, and **T2** (faithful ⇒ certificate exists) is witnessed here by construction — the true null orders are the certificate.

## 3. The celestial circle, the plateau, and the honest search face

**The celestial sphere from order data.** Pairwise Kendall (inversion) distances between the 16 null-direction extensions, classical MDS: a circle of radius CV `0.009` with cyclic adjacency `16/16` — **S¹ recovered from inversion counts, given the family**. (The `S²` version in 3+1 is the same computation with more directions; nothing in it used `d = 2`-specific structure.)

**The plateau — a redesign forced by measurement.** The draft's warm-start test presumed a local score gradient toward the true extensions; the measurement says otherwise: 400 adjacent-swap corruptions leave the grid score *at the floor* (`0.0296 → 0.0290`) at Kendall-to-true `0.006` *(the draft quoted a `0.023` excursion here — a stale number from the deleted basin design, and provably impossible for 400 swaps at `N = 256` (max `0.0123`); round-1 referee)*. The certifier has a **plateau** of certificate-grade extension pairs. The right robustness question — since T1 reconstructs from *any* certified family — is whether plateau members reconstruct: corrupting **all 16** direction-extensions (400 swaps each) still yields reconstruction at `> 0.999` per axis. Certificate-grade families are interchangeable; the certificate's tolerance is a feature aligned with its semantics, not a looseness.

**The cold start, measured.** Random extension pairs annealed for 12k local proposals stall at `6.0×` the floor. The hardness face is exactly where the reframe put it: verification easy, search hard-from-far. Named open: better search (Johnston-SVD seeding is the obvious next move — his procedure as the finder, this certificate as the prover), non-local moves, and the record-native seed (real webs carry a distinguished extension — the commit order — for free).

## 4. The decoration probe — Attack 2's gate [DEMONSTRATED]

The principle under test (before any record-native construction): *does order-only cross-chain coupling carry transverse geometry at all?* On faithful 2+1D input (`N = 600`): greedy longest-chain extraction (`20` chains, lengths `8–15`, covering `198/600` elements — the uncovered two-thirds are unused, and greedy longest chains preferentially sample the diamond's deep interior; disclosed), coupling `w(c, c′)` = cross-comparability fraction, MDS on `1 − w`. Measured: `w` tracks the held-out transverse distance at Spearman `0.978`; the recovered plane matches held-out chain positions at **Procrustes `0.993`**; shuffling the coupling entries collapses recovery to `0.19` — the geometry lives in `w`, not the pipeline. **The decoration route's gate passes.** What remains — and it is the entire physical content — is the record-native identification: is the corpus's `χ_AB`/collar-sharing structure a `w` of this kind? That is **[POSITED]** territory (paper 2 §4.7's unbuilt locus, paper 6's bridge-style posits), now with a validated geometric target to aim at: the identification succeeds iff the record coupling reproduces this probe's behavior.

## 5. The cage upgrades — Attack 3 [DEMONSTRATED]

**Paper 13's named target, answered — with an honest split verdict.** The abundance term `S_ab = Σ (f0_I − F0(k))²` (interior link fractions against the calibrated, cleanly decreasing `F0(k)` curve) **prices the dense-compromise attractor statically**: the regenerated `r ≈ 0.76` class carries `2.7×` the sprinkling abundance-misfit. But the **drift retest** (same kernel, budget, scale, `S_r + S_link + 4·S_ab`) lands at `r = 0.78` again: **pricing without steering** — at this λ and scale the local dynamics finds the same compromise anyway. Both outcomes were declared admissible upfront; this is the measured one, and it sharpens the dynamics program again: the attractor is not an action-*value* problem but a *landscape/kernel* problem (the price exists; the local moves never collect it) — the next lever is the move set or the annealing schedule, not another term.

**A new cage axis.** The two-point interval statistic `C2 = corr(|I(x,y)|, |I(y,z)|)` over composable pairs — beyond the field's one-point abundances — is computable order-only on all populations (estimator conventions: one random `z` per sampled pair, ≤ 4,000 pairs, legs `|I| ≥ 4`; KR at `p = 0.5`) and separates the dense class (`−0.356`) from sprinklings (`−0.213`); lattice sits at `−0.218` (near-sprinkling on this axis — consistent with paper 12's split); KR yields no qualifying composable pairs (`nan`, disclosed — the axis presupposes interval depth KR lacks).
