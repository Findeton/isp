## 1. What the corpus actually pins about supply

Paper 13 swept *generic* supply families ("physically natural": renewal, heterogeneous rates, wandering, Lévy). None were derived from paper 1. Reading paper 1 §4.2–4.4 as a generative process, the supply decomposes into three factors, each with a definite epistemic tag:

**F1 — the commit axis: a homogeneous race [DERIVED + one posit].** The firing law (§4.3) puts each seal on a random evidence clock `S(I) = e^{−I}`, `I ~ Exp(1)`, with the rate **fixed at λ = 1 by exact self-accounting** (`−log S(I) = I`; commitment ratio `r = 1`; v6 paper 4 §71's renewal analysis closed the shape drift at Poisson). So the corpus's own law leaves **no room for per-chain commit-rate heterogeneity**: in evidence time every lineage fires at rate 1. Under the one added posit — independence of the lineages' evidence clocks (a refinement of paper 13's posit 1: it *specifies* the global sequencing distribution instead of merely assuming one exists) — the commit interleaving is a homogeneous race: the next committer is uniform on the live fleet. Notably, receipt `j1` already implemented exactly this (`chain = rng.integers(0, M, N)`), so **the b-axis was never the obstruction** — every j1 family already had the derived commit axis.

**F2 — the increments: deterministic per mode, atomic menu [DERIVED values; carrier OPEN].** Per mode the committed content is deterministic (`Var[content | mode] = 0`, §4.3): all χ-randomness is mode-randomness. The admissible mode menu the corpus has actually computed (`p2b`/`p2c`, `C(η) = η·tanh η − log cosh η`):

| mode | root | content atom |
|---|---|---|
| 1-character parity | `η_B = 0.609377863436…` (`tanh η = e^{−η}`) | `C = 0.156109200157…` |
| 3-character coupled `{x, y, xy}` | `h_* = 0.495053…` | `C = 0.109004004450…` |
| 7-character (full `{±1}³`) | `η = 0.3680` | `C = 0.063388010127…` |

So the *derived* increment law is supported on (at present) **three atoms** with irrational pairwise ratios. What the corpus does **not** pin is the **mode carrier** — whether a lineage *is* a ledger type (mode fixed per lineage), the committing ledger is drawn per event from the web's state (commit-local), or something between (Markov with mixing time τ). Paper 1 names mode-dependence "the one live non-canonicity" (§4.4's trichotomy prices it); the fork is therefore pre-registered three ways, none privileged.

**F3 — the lineage structure: births reset the odometer [DEFINITIONAL reading + one fork].** The odometer is *per chain by definition* — `χ` is the accumulated `σ` of the chain's **own** seals (paper 1 §3; additivity is [DEFINITIONAL] because a sum telescopes). A lineage born partway through the process (at a branch of the web DAG) has no prior seals, so under the plain reading **its odometer starts fresh** — lineage birth is a `χ`-reset. The corpus is silent on the web's branching statistics (paper 1's "not delivered" list: seal placement, the joint law across parallel chains; `f3c`'s parallel/tensor cocycle is about additivity across factors, not placement), so the mean lineage length `L` (own-commits between birth and death) is **unpinned — the single new physical quantity this note names**, sitting in the same [PHYSICAL]-input family as paper 1 §4.4's spacing. One honest fork rides on the chain-decomposition ambiguity at a branch: the continuation child could *inherit* the parent's accumulated `χ` rather than reset. Both readings are pre-registered (reset / inherit); they make opposite predictions (§3), which is exactly what makes the fork valuable.

## 2. The re-analysis of paper 13's negative (why the sweep failed)

With F1 already in place in `j1`, family A (homogeneous, iid exponential increments, uniform race) was *the exchangeable-increment process* — and it failed at `6.2×` with strong clock correlation. Therefore **increment exchangeability was never the missing ingredient**. The deeper obstruction layer, visible only from the dynamics view: in every j1 family, all `M` lineages are **born at `b = 0` with `χ = 0` and live forever**. Every odometer then rises together — the fleet's `χ`-ensemble co-grows with `b` — and Spearman(b, χ) is large *even for perfectly exchangeable increments*. A faithful configuration, by contrast, realizes the two-clock frame with per-seal supply values that revisit low `χ` at late `b` (paper 13's positive control is the singleton-chain limit: fresh `χ` per seal). The obstruction thus has two layers: **(O1) common-birth co-growth** (kills A; increment statistics irrelevant) and **(O2) rate/mode persistence** (kills B/C on top, as slope classes). Paper 13 measured their sum; the decomposition is new here.

**What kills O1 is lineage turnover with fresh odometers** — births scattered through the commit range put low-`χ` seals at every `b`-epoch, making the `χ`-ensemble `b`-stationary. And by F3, that is not a mechanism to be invented: it is what the odometer's own definition does on a branching web. The disjoint-persistent-fleet model was the pathology, not the records.

## 3. The interpolation structure and the pre-registered predictions

Define the **web-churn supply** `W(L, inc, carrier, birth)`: fleet of `M` live slots; committer uniform per event (F1); increment per F2 (`3-atom` menu or matched-mean continuum control); after each commit the lineage dies with probability `1/L` and its slot is reborn (fresh id; `χ = 0` under *reset*, `χ` kept under *inherit*; mode redrawn under lineage-carried).

- **P1 (endpoint identity).** `W(L = 1, continuum, reset)` is exactly the singleton-chain faithful realization (iid `χ` per seal + uniform race = the Winkler product = paper 13's positive control): **in-band by construction**. `W(L → ∞)` is family A: **measured `6.2×`**. So for continuum increments a crossing `L*` exists and `D*(L)` should rise monotonically from the band to A's value. The record question stops being existence and becomes **location**: where is `L*`, and does the record-native corner sit below it?
- **P2 (the reset/inherit fork separates).** Under *inherit*, births do not create low-`χ` seals at late `b` — O1 survives — so `D*` stays `≳ 2×` at every `L`. If inherit *also* crossed into the band, the O1 mechanism story would be wrong; this is the design's built-in refutation arm.
- **P3 (the quantization tension — T3's new prediction).** With three atoms, `χ` values live on sums `n₁C₁ + n₂C₂ + n₃C₃`: at small `L` few terms → near-lattice structure → the statistical layer (dispersion) should degrade as atom count falls (`continuum → 3 → 2 → 1` atoms), *even where T1/T2 pass* — at `1` atom, `L = 1` is a pure tie degeneracy. Consequence if confirmed: **records need mode diversity to be spacetime** — a new, named constraint hooking the manifoldlikeness gate to paper 1's mode ledger. (Quantization also cuts the other way: fewer atoms mean fewer slope classes under lineage-carried modes, weakening O2 — the tension is measured, not assumed.)
- **P4 (the carrier fork is second-order at short `L`).** Mode persistence can act only over a lineage's lifetime; churn bounds the mixing time by `L`. Prediction: at `L ≲ 8`, commit-local vs lineage-carried differ mildly; the carrier fork matters mainly through P3's atom mixing, not through O2 rays. (Design note 2's M3 — bare mean-reversion — collapses into the Markov-τ arm and is no longer a separate mechanism.)

## 4. The correction to design note 2

Design note 2 §3 defined the "exchangeable ceiling" (τ = 0, identity-blind) as the decisive control and its failure as closing the 2D route. **That control is family A in disguise** — exchangeable increments over a common-birth immortal fleet — and family A is already measured dead at `6.2×`. The ceiling as designed was refuted before being run; what its failure actually indicates is O1, not unreachability. Corrected kill-switch: the **churn ceiling** — `W(L, continuum, commit-local, reset)` minimized over the `L` grid (excluding the trivial `L = 1` endpoint, which passes by construction and serves as the instrument check instead). If *no* `L > 1` row reaches T2+T3, turnover cannot rescue the frame at physical chain lengths and the closure argument fires with the same force design note 2 intended — now aimed at the right mechanism. M1 (capacity feedback) is demoted: rate feedback cannot cure O1 (it equalizes slopes but every odometer still co-grows); it returns only if a feedback→death reading emerges. M2 (topology churn) is promoted from mechanism-candidate to **derived structure** (F3).

## 6. What each outcome means

- **A record-native corner (3-atom, reset, either carrier) reaches T1+T2+T3 at some `L`:** the cell is reachable by the *derived* process — paper 13's kinematic route reopens with the supply class no longer free but forced-up-to-`(L, carrier)`; the remaining physics input is the web's branching statistics (`L_eff`), a named measurable, and design note 1's L3 unblocks with this supply.
- **Continuum crosses but every atomic corner fails T3:** P3's constraint is real — the mode menu is too coarse for Poisson statistics; the named next question becomes whether the corpus admits richer mode families (the §4.4 trichotomy's open flank), a sharp, honest wall with a stated location.
- **Only `L ≲ 2` crosses:** turnover must be near-total per commit — physically implausible chain lengths; the frame survives only in a corner that strains the web reading, reported as such.
- **Inherit crosses too (P2 fails):** the O1 mechanism story is wrong; back to measurement without the derivation's guidance (and this note's §2 gets a correction entry).
- **Nothing but `L = 1` crosses even for continuum:** the churn ceiling fails — the corrected closure argument fires: the two-clock route is dead at these regimes and the program's manifoldlikeness weight moves to `d ≥ 3` tomography.
