## Abstract

Paper 12 turned 2D manifoldlikeness into a measurement; this paper points the instrument at the first **record-native** candidate configurations and reports what it reads. The verdict is a **double negative with structure** — and one organizing theorem that converts the manifoldlikeness gate into a named corpus open. **(1) The two-clock frame [POSITED model; THEOREM for its scope].** Seals carry two record-native scalars: the commit index `b` and the chain's accumulated content `χ` (paper 1's odometer). Positing precedence = dominance in both clocks (two posits named: a global commit sequencing; cross-chain `χ`-comparability) gives orders of `dim ≤ 2` *by construction* — and **Theorem 2.1 (universality, trivial but organizing): every 2D order is a two-clock configuration** (`b` = rank in `L₁`, a linear extension, hence a legal commit sequence; `χ` = rank in `L₂`, chain-monotone, hence a legal supply). So the frame costs nothing beyond `dim ≤ 2`, and *all* the physics sits in the joint (commit, supply) statistics: **the 2D manifoldlikeness question for records is thereby relocated — exactly and without loss — into paper 1 §7's supply item — the [PHYSICAL] configuration data (an input, not an open law, as paper 1 tags it)**, hooking the manifoldlikeness gate directly to the supply/spacing ledger (§4.4 there): one gate, one physical input, no longer two unrelated frontiers. The class trivially contains manifoldlike members (positive control: a sprinkling *is* a two-clock configuration and passes the pipeline at the faithful band — the instrument is not rigged). **(2) The natural-supply sweep [DEMONSTRATED negative].** Every physically natural supply family fails the volume layer against the pooled faithful band (`0.0263 ± 0.0047`, 20 replicates, consistent with paper 12's independent `0.0270`): homogeneous renewal `6.2×`, static heterogeneous rates `3.4×` (the best), wandering log-rates `4.7×/4.2×/4.0×`, and the Lévy/chain-count pair — an `M = 128` homogeneous control `3.9×` vs Lévy `3.7×`, run precisely to *deconfound* the two (most of the Lévy family's apparent advantage is the mechanical interleaving of more, shorter chains; the increment law adds little). The best family also fails the ensemble-calibrated dispersion layer at `z = 12`. The obstruction is **localized and structural**: both clocks grow with time, so natural supplies carry strong rank–rank dependence (Spearman `+0.42` to `+0.78` against the faithful `≈ 0.04`) — within-chain diagonality plus rate persistence produce ray/staircase chain geometry where sprinkling chains are diffusive and interleaving. The relocation theorem then makes the necessary direction precise: **records measure positive only if their supply statistics erase the clock correlation** (necessary, not sufficient — the full condition is membership of the joint statistics in the cell) — a sharp, named target in place of a vague gate. **(3) The Gibbs reconnaissance [DEMONSTRATED, arm (b)].** Does paper 11's misfit-action candidate *select* manifoldlike orders? At recon scale (`n = 64`) the landscape is **link-dominated** (the action's own disclosed crossover: `S_KR/S_sprinkling = 1.7×` only — the discrimination onset is not reached, so the recon probes small-`n` minimizers, not asymptotic selection). The driven search reaches **sprinkling-level action** (`295 → 178.4` and `197 → 175.7` vs the ensemble `175.6 ± 6.2` — `S` alone does *not* discriminate at this scale, and the finding rests on classification, not on `S`) while landing in a **third class**: globally dense orders (`r ≈ 0.78` vs the sprinkling `0.5`, heights `16–19`, both endpoints `dim ≤ 2`: NO) whose interiors are *near-balanced* (`mean r_I ≈ 0.54` — they pay `2–3×` **more** r-term than sprinklings and only slightly less link term; the measured mechanism, replacing this paper's own first-draft story, which had it backwards). The **β = 0 control** completes the attribution: the proposal kernel alone over-densifies to a near-chain (`r = 0.99`, `S = 443`) — **the action *resists* the kernel's densification** (a near-chain pays a huge r-term) but parks the system at the third-class compromise instead of pushing toward the sprinkling cell. The selection gap is *measured*, with the failure mode identified — and the abundance-term extension (named in paper 11) is now the concrete next rung, with a measured attractor to beat. **What survives every negative here:** the instrument worked exactly as built (positive control passed; every rejection quantified with a localized cause), the existence question is *not* closed (universality guarantees positive configurations exist in the class — what failed is every *natural* supply tried, at the swept regimes and scales), and the program's next questions are now named measurables: which physical mechanism produces decorrelated supply statistics, and does the abundance-augmented action move the minimizers off the dense-chain attractor.

## 1. The question, and its honest shape

Paper 12 measures a *given* order; nothing in the corpus yet derives which orders records realize (paper 1 explicitly does not deliver seal placement, the dense-vs-sparse realization, or a measure over webs). The next rung is therefore an **existence** question: is there a record-native configuration class — built from corpus primitives, no held-out geometry anywhere — that measures positive? Two candidate routes are tested, each set up as a fork with both arms stated in advance: a *kinematic* route (§2: the two-clock frame — what the records' own two scalars give) and a *dynamic* route (§3: what the one surviving dynamics candidate actually selects). Both come back negative at the swept regimes; the value is in the structure of the negatives — one relocation theorem, one localized obstruction, one measured attractor — and in the fact that the instrument, on its first field deployment, did precisely its job.

## 2. The two-clock frame

### 2.1 The model, its posits, and the universality theorem

Each seal carries a global commit index `b` (posit 1: cross-chain commit sequencing) and its chain's accumulated content `χ` (paper 1's odometer; posit 2: cross-chain `χ`-comparability as a causal criterion). Define `x ≺ y` iff `b_x < b_y` **and** `χ_x < χ_y`. Same-chain seals are automatically chains (the odometer is monotone along its own chain — receipt-checked), and every two-clock order has `dim ≤ 2` by construction.

**Theorem 2.1 (universality).** Every 2D order is a two-clock configuration: given a realizer `(L₁, L₂)`, take `b` = rank in `L₁` (a linear extension of the order — hence a legal commit sequence) and `χ` = rank in `L₂` (strictly increasing along every chain of the order — hence a legal supply assignment for *any* chain decomposition; the model's chain machinery is order-irrelevant, only the pair `(b, χ)` enters, and singleton chains suffice). ∎

Trivial, but it reorganizes the problem: the two-clock *posit* costs nothing beyond `dim ≤ 2` — the frame is not a mechanism but a **coordinate system on the dim ≤ 2 class** — so the entire question "do records realize 2D-manifoldlike orders?" becomes, exactly and without loss: **"do the records' joint (commit, supply) statistics land in the manifoldlike cell?"** That is paper 1 §7's supply item — [PHYSICAL] configuration data, an input the corpus reads rather than a law it derives — and the spacing ledger (§4.4) is its home. One gate, hooked to one named physical input. Corollary: manifoldlike members exist in the class trivially — the receipt's positive control builds a sprinkling *as* a two-clock configuration (`b` = `u`-rank, `χ` = `v`-coordinate), gets the bit-identical order, and passes the pipeline at the faithful band (`D* = 0.023` vs the pooled band `0.026`): the instrument is not rigged against the frame.

### 2.2 The natural-supply sweep [DEMONSTRATED negative]

The tested question: do *physically natural* supply families land in the cell? Families (all with `M = 32` chains, `N = 512`, `6` replicate draws from the one seeded stream; `M = 128` for the Lévy family and its homogeneous control): **A** homogeneous renewal (iid exponential increments); **B** static heterogeneous rates (log-uniform over two decades); **C** wandering log-rates (per-commit Gaussian steps, `σ ∈ {0.15, 0.6, 1.2}`); **F** Lévy supply (Pareto `α = 0.5`, infinite mean — single jumps dominate, the strongest natural decorrelator). Scored by the paper-12 pipeline against the pooled same-`N` faithful band (`0.0263 ± 0.0047`):

| family | `D*` (rank) | ratio | Spearman(`b`, `χ`) |
|---|---|---|---|
| A homogeneous (M = 32) | 0.163 | 6.2× | +0.78 |
| B heterogeneous | 0.090 | **3.4×** | +0.43 |
| C wander 0.15 (+ static decade spread) | 0.124 | 4.7× | +0.58 |
| C wander 0.6 (+ spread) | 0.112 | 4.2× | +0.53 |
| C wander 1.2 (+ spread) | 0.106 | 4.0× | +0.46 |
| A128 homogeneous control (M = 128) | 0.102 | 3.9× | +0.48 |
| F Lévy α = 0.5 (M = 128) | 0.097 | 3.7× | +0.42 |

*(Band: `0.0263 ± 0.0047`, pooled over 20 replicates — paper 12's independent `0.0270` sits inside the spread; each family row is 6 replicate draws from the one seeded stream. The C rows carry static heterogeneous initial rates over one decade in addition to the wander — both effects are in those rows, disclosed.)*

**Every natural family fails the volume layer** (best: heterogeneous at `3.4×` the band), and the best family fails the ensemble-calibrated dispersion layer at `z = 12`. The `A128` control deconfounds the Lévy row: more, shorter chains alone drop homogeneous `D*` from `0.163` to `0.102` (mechanical interleaving); the infinite-mean increment law adds only `0.102 → 0.097` on top — the decorrelation leverage is mostly in chain count, not in jump statistics. The obstruction is localized, not a tuning accident: both clocks grow with time, so every natural supply carries strong built-in rank–rank dependence (Spearman `+0.42…+0.78`; faithful coordinates: `0.04`). Geometrically: within-chain diagonality (chain members increase in *both* ranks — forced, correct, sprinklings share it) *plus rate persistence* make the chains rays and staircases in rank–rank space, where sprinkling chains are diffusive and mutually interleaving.

**What the sweep does and does not establish.** It does *not* close existence — universality guarantees positive supply realizations exist (the control is one). It establishes that the manifoldlike cell is **not reached by generic/renewal-type supply statistics**: to measure positive, the physical supply must (necessarily, not sufficiently) *erase the clock correlation* — produce chain content histories that cross and interleave like diffusive paths rather than persist like rays; the full requirement remains cell-membership of the joint statistics. The named, falsifiable target on the kinematic route: a physical mechanism for decorrelated supply — or the measured conclusion that records do not support 2D manifoldlikeness through this frame (which would redirect to `d ≥ 3`, where the corpus currently has no characterization — the honest stakes).

## 3. The Gibbs reconnaissance [DEMONSTRATED, arm (b)]

**Setup.** Paper 11's combined r+link action, `β = 20`, driven local search (not an equilibrium sampler — same disclosure as paper 11 §5) at `n = 64`, `4×10³` proposals, from a KR start and from a random-order start; endpoints classified with the instrument (capped `dim ≤ 2` oracle carrying an honest UNDECIDED verdict when the work cap binds; ordering fraction; height), against a 12-replicate sprinkling ensemble, a term-by-term action decomposition, and a **β = 0 control of the same proposal kernel** (post-review — a static baseline cannot separate action from kernel bias). Both fork arms were written down before running, and the CHECK-3 gate branches on the measured arm.

**The scale disclosure first.** At `n = 64` the landscape is **link-dominated**: `S_KR/S_sprinkling = 1.7×` only (`295` vs `176`) — the action's discrimination onset (h1's slow link-term crossover, disclosed in paper 11) is not reached, so the recon probes the action's *small-`n` minimizers*, not its asymptotic selection. Consequence, stated as a check: the endpoints reach **sprinkling-level** action (`295 → 178.4`, `197 → 175.7`, vs the ensemble `175.6 ± 6.2`) — `S` alone does not discriminate here, and everything below rests on classification data, not on `S`. *(This paper's first draft claimed the search "digs below the sprinkling band"; against the ensemble spread that claim was false and is retracted — round-1 referee.)*

**The finding — arm (b), with the mechanism measured.** Both endpoints classify as a **third class**: globally dense (`r = 0.79/0.76` against the sprinkling `0.5` and KR's `0.59`), tall (`H = 19/16` against KR's `3`), and `dim ≤ 2`: NO — neither KR-like nor sprinkling-like. The decomposition says *how* they sit at sprinkling-level `S`: their interiors are **near-balanced** (`mean r_I ≈ 0.54` — not chain-heavy), they pay `2–3×` *more* r-term than sprinklings (`S_r = 15.2/10.7` vs `4.4`) and slightly *less* link term (`163–165` vs `174`) — global densification buys the link savings at an r-term cost that nets out to the sprinkling level. *(The first draft's mechanism — "chain-heavy interiors dodge the r-term" — was backwards on both counts and is replaced by the measured one.)*

**Attribution — the β = 0 control.** The proposal kernel alone (add-relation-plus-closure vs delete-cover) over-densifies to a near-chain (`r = 0.99`, `H = 50`, `S = 443` — trivially `dim ≤ 2` as a near-chain, and just as trivially volume-rejected: its rank embedding is the diagonal). The driven runs sit far *below* that: **the action resists the kernel's densification** — a near-chain pays an enormous r-term — but parks the system at the third-class compromise (`r ≈ 0.78`) rather than pushing on toward the sprinkling cell. So the honest attribution is neither "the action prefers density" (first-draft wording, retracted) nor "kernel artifact": the density pressure is the kernel's, the action moderates it, and the *selection failure* — stopping at the compromise instead of the geometric cell — is the action's measured shortfall.

**What this buys paper 11's program.** The abundance-term extension (named there as the next axis) now has a *measured attractor to beat*: any improved action must make the dense-compromise class expensive — which the interval-abundance ledger does naturally (dense orders have wildly non-Poisson abundance profiles) — and the test is rerunnable verbatim, including the β = 0 control. The recon also sharpens expectations: selection claims for *any* candidate action at `n ≲ 10²` are suspect until the link-term crossover is passed; asymptotic selection remains untested territory, honestly beyond current reach.

## 4. What stands after two negatives

**The relocation is the durable positive.** Theorem 2.1 converts the 2D manifoldlikeness gate from "an open property of unknown record dynamics" into "a stated condition on the supply statistics" — a corpus-internal, physical, falsifiable question hooked directly to paper 1's supply/spacing ledger. **The obstruction and the attractor are reusable.** Clock correlation (kinematic route) and the dense-chain attractor (dynamic route) are the two measured failure modes any future proposal must clear — both quantified, both receipt-rerunnable. **The instrument is field-validated.** On its first deployment against unknowns it passed its positive control, rejected six families with a localized cause, priced the best-case gap (3.4×), and carried an honest UNDECIDED verdict option throughout (exercised in the draft-stream run; the final-stream endpoints both classified decisively). **Existence stays open in both directions**: no natural configuration measured positive; no argument shows none can. And the `d ≥ 3` stakes are now sharp: if the decorrelated-supply target proves physically unreachable, the 2D route closes and the program inherits the harder cage-only regime — a real, stated risk, not a footnote.

## 5. Honest scope

The two-clock model rides two [POSITED] bridges (global commit sequencing; cross-chain `χ`-comparability), and the sweep covers six named supply families at `N = 512`, `M ∈ {32, 128}` — the negative is *regime-scoped*: other supply classes (branching/merging chains, supply–commit feedback, non-renewal correlated increments) are untested. The Gibbs recon is a driven search at `n = 64` — below the action's own discrimination onset (disclosed), far below any asymptotic claim; its UNDECIDED verdict option is a work-cap convention, not a mathematical one (unexercised in the final run — both endpoints classified NO). Neither negative bounds what records *can* do — only what these natural realizations *measured*. The instrument's own limits carry over from paper 12 (2D, flat, volume-faithfulness + statistical layer separately). Nothing here touches `d ≥ 3`, curvature, or the derived-measure problem, which remains the true gate behind all of this.
