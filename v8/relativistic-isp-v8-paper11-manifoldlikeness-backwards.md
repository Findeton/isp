## Abstract

The forwards problem — *which* record orders are manifoldlike — is the field-shared gate (papers 4 §5, 7). This paper runs it **backwards**: assume faithful embeddability, derive what it forces on the order, and use the forced properties themselves as instrument and dynamics. **(1) The heredity theorem [THEOREM].** Every order interval of a faithfully embedded order is itself faithfully embedded (Alexandrov sets are causally convex; a Poisson sprinkling restricts to a Poisson sprinkling) — manifoldlikeness is *hereditary*, so it can be tested inside intervals, recursively, at every scale. **(2) The per-interval law [THEOREM, exact constants].** Faithfulness in `M²` forces every interval interior to be a rectangle sprinkling, whose internal ordering fraction obeys the exact U-statistic law `E[(r_k − ½)²] = (2/(k(k−1)))·[2(k−2)ζ₁ + ζ₂]` with `ζ₁ = 1/36`, `ζ₂ = 1/4` — i.e. `≈ 1/(9k) → 0` (receipt: fitted slope `−1.097`; the backwards-derived action carries the demanded super-extensive structure and, at the **finite coupling `β > β* = 16 ln 2 = 11.09`**, suppresses the 3-layer KR ensemble relative even to a single reference faithful order (`β·n²/64 > (n²/4)ln 2`). Selection of manifoldlikeness is not claimed — the paper exhibits its own counterexample: **(5) the cluster adversary [HONEST FINDING]**: a blow-up order (tight jittered clusters on geometric centers) *survives* the `r`/height heredity axes (`97%/100%` pass) and hence carries low `r`-misfit while being non-manifoldlike — the `r`-ledger is necessary-not-sufficient, the next axis (internal abundance profiles, more `n²` constraints) is named, and the iterative structure of the backwards program (each new necessary axis = a new super-extensive action term) is the point. ## 1. The backwards move

The forwards question ("which orders are manifoldlike?") has consumed the field; the backwards question — *given* faithfulness, what does the order provably carry? — is cheap, and the corpus has already run it once with real yield: in `M²`, backwards gives dim ≤ 2 (an *iff* for embeddability) plus the one-directional faithfulness bound low discrepancy ⇒ correct ordering fraction (paper 7 §3 — with its own scope note that the discrepancy is realizer-dependent and the canonical-realizer question is [OPEN]; no full faithfulness characterization is claimed there or here). This paper runs the same move at the level that survives in every dimension: **interval structure**. Three steps: derive the forced properties (§2–§3, theorems); turn the property list into an instrument (§4, the heredity axis) and into a dynamics (§5, the misfit action as a Gibbs weight); and say exactly where the move stalls (§6). Throughout, "faithful" means: the order is that of a Poisson sprinkling of a causal diamond at density `ρ` (fixed-`N` binomial in the receipts, papers 7/8's convention), and all instruments are order-only with coordinates held out.

## 2. What faithfulness forces: heredity and the per-interval law

**Theorem 2.1 (heredity).** Let `P` be the order of a faithful sprinkling of a causally convex region. Then for every related pair `x ≺ y`, the interval `I(x,y)` (with its induced order) is the order of a faithful sprinkling of the Alexandrov set `A(x,y)`, at the same density.

*Proof.* `A(x,y) = J⁺(x) ∩ J⁻(y)` is causally convex, so the induced order on the sprinkled points inside it is the restriction of the ambient order — no relations enter or leave through the boundary; and the restriction of a Poisson (or fixed-`N` binomial, conditionally) process to a subregion is a Poisson process at the same density. ∎

So manifoldlikeness is **hereditary**: every interval, of every interval, is again manifoldlike. This is the property the layered bulk fails maximally — a KR order's intervals are antichains at every scale — and it converts the certificate from a global test into a *recursive* one (§4).

**Theorem 2.2 (the per-interval law in `M²`, exact constants).** In `M²`, the interior of `I(x,y)` is a sprinkling of the lightcone rectangle `[x_u, y_u] × [x_v, y_v]` under dominance order. Its internal ordering fraction `r_k` (over `k` interior points) satisfies `E[r_k] = ½` exactly and

> `E[(r_k − ½)²] = Var(r_k) = (2/(k(k−1)))·[2(k−2)ζ₁ + ζ₂]`,  `ζ₁ = 1/36`, `ζ₂ = 1/4`,

so `E[(r_k − ½)²] = 1/(9k)·(1 + O(1/k))`.

*Proof.* Two iid uniform points in a rectangle are comparable with probability `½` (dominance either way), giving `E[r_k] = ½` for every `k`. `r_k` is a U-statistic with kernel `X_ij = 𝟙[comparable]`: `ζ₂ = Var(X) = ¼`; the projection `h(u,v) = P[(u,v) comparable to an independent uniform] = uv + (1−u)(1−v)` has `E[h²] = 1/9 + 1/18 + 1/9 = 5/18`, so `ζ₁ = 5/18 − ¼ = 1/36`; the displayed formula is the standard exact U-statistic variance. ∎

Receipt check: the measured decay slope over `k = 8…512` is `−1.097`, and at `k = 8` the measured `0.0201` sits against the formula's `0.0208`. The same backwards move forces internal height (`2√k`, BDJ-corrected) and internal abundance laws — quoted as further necessary axes (the abundance one becomes load-bearing in §6), not re-derived here.

## 3. The misfit action

### 3.1 The definition and the two scaling regimes

The ledger of §2 has one constraint per related pair. Its natural aggregate, **order-only by construction**:

> `S_misfit(P) = Σ_{x≺y, |I(x,y)| ≥ m₀} (r_I(x,y) − ½)²`,  `m₀ = 8` (disclosed; the action is blind to *everything* below `n ≈ 64`, where no intervals clear the cutoff — disclosed).

**Proposition 3.1 (KR misfit, exact).** For the 3-layer KR order at `p = ½` (layers `n/4, n/2, n/4`): with high probability every `L₁×L₃` pair is related with interior an antichain of mean size `n/8` (clearing `m₀` for `n ≳ 64`), each contributing misfit exactly `¼`; hence `S_misfit = (1 + o(1))·n²/64`. *Measured: `S = n²/64` to four digits at `n = 192, 384` (`c_KR = 0.015625`) — the `o(1)` is already invisible.* The cross-layer pairs `L₁×L₂`, `L₂×L₃` have near-empty interiors and do not enter. ∎ *(One-line core: an antichain interior has `r_I = 0`, misfit `(0 − ½)² = ¼`.)*

**Theorem 3.2 (faithful misfit is sub-quadratic).** For faithful `M²` orders, `E[S_misfit] = Θ(N log² N)`: by Theorem 2.2 each pair contributes `E[(r_I − ½)²] ≈ 1/(9k)` given interior size `k`; for a comparable pair the lightcone-gap fractions `(s, t)` carry density `4(1−s)(1−t)` with `k ≈ Nst`, so `E[1/(9k); k ≥ m₀] = (4/9N)∬_{st ≥ m₀/N}(1−s)(1−t)/(st) = (2/9N)·ln²N·(1 + o(1))`, and summing over the `∼N²/4` related pairs gives `E[S] = (N/18)·ln²N·(1 + o(1))` — sub-quadratic, one log heavier than the naive count *(the log² is the round-1 referee's correction of this paper's own first `O(N log N)` claim)*. *Finite-range honesty:* in the measured window (`N ≤ 384`) the `m₀` cutoff produces a transient plateau `c_geo = S/N² ≈ 10⁻³` (naive exponent fits are inflated by it); the *gap* is nonetheless present at every measured size — `c_KR > 10·c_geo` for all `N ≥ 96` — and the asymptotic vanishing of `c_geo` is this theorem's, proved not measured. ∎

### 3.2 Threading paper 7's no-go [COROLLARY, explicit constant]

Paper 7 Thm 4.1: over a counting base measure, *extensive* actions cannot suppress the `2^{⌊n²/4⌋}` bulk; its Corollary 4.2 is the `Ω(n²)` log-weight demand. The backwards-derived action has exactly that:

**Corollary 3.3 (ensemble-scoped).** Under the Gibbs weight `e^{−βS_misfit}` over the labeled counting measure, the weight of the **`p = ½` 3-layer KR ensemble** (whp, per Prop 3.1 — the corollary is scoped to the ensemble; quantifying over *all* 3-layer orders requires the entropy-vs-action tradeoff across density slices `p`, stated not carried out, and the labeled class count carries an unabsorbed `e^{O(n)}` multinomial factor, harmless) relative to a *single* reference faithful order is at most `2^{n²/4}·e^{−βn²/64}·e^{βc′n log² n} → 0` iff `β·(1/64) > (¼)ln 2`, i.e.

> `β > β* = 16 ln 2 = 11.0904…`

— a **finite, explicit coupling**. *(Receipt: the measured `(ln 2/4)/c_KR = 11.09`.)* The comparison against a single reference order makes the statement airtight without estimating the count of faithful orders; adding that count only helps. ∎

### 3.3 The height-2 blindness, the link term, and what the action family actually achieves

**Honest finding (in-receipt).** The `r`-action alone is **blind to height-2 orders**: every interval of a 2-layer order is empty, so `S_r ≡ 0` identically on the free-bipartite class — which is paper 7 §4.2's own `2^{⌊n²/4⌋}` bulk witness (receipt: `S = 0.0` at `N = 96/192/384` on the `g1` A3 population). Worse than blind: it ranks that class *below* faithful orders (`0 < S_geo`) — the same wrong-direction failure paper 7 §4.3 flagged for the BD action. The first draft of this paper claimed the `r`-action alone "threads the no-go"; the round-1 referee's counterexample stands, and the repair is the ledger's own next line:

**The link term.** Faithfulness also forces the interval-*size* statistics: the fraction of related pairs with interior `< m₀` tends to `0` (`∼(m₀/N)ln N`), while a height-2 order has that fraction `= 1` identically. The combined action `S = S_r + ¼·#{related pairs, |I| < m₀}` is still order-only and still `Θ(N log² N)` on faithful orders (the undersized-pair count is `O(N log N)`). Measured: geometric fraction `0.366 → 0.119 → 0.041` at `N = 192/1024/4096` vs `1.000` identically for 2-layer — asymptotic separation with a slow, disclosed crossover (comparable magnitudes below `N ∼ 10³`).

**Corollary 3.4 (the arithmetic of the aggregate, both directions).** Under `β·S`: every *dense-violation* class (3-layer KR-type; dense 2-layer; chains — each with `Ω(n²)` charged pairs) has log-weight gap `Ω(n²)` at fixed `β > β*(class)`. But the free-bipartite **aggregate** `Σ_m C(n²/4, m)e^{−βm/4} = (1 + e^{−β/4})^{n²/4}` keeps log-weight `(n²/4)ln(1 + e^{−β/4}) = Ω(n²)` at every fixed `β` — a fixed coupling does **not** kill the two-layer aggregate (its sparse tail hides at low action) — while a **log-growing coupling** `β_n = 8 ln n` bounds it by `¼` (receipt arithmetic). ∎

**What is actually achieved, stated once:** the backwards-derived action family **reduces the measure problem from the quadratic scale to the sub-quadratic arena** — every class that violates the ledger on `Ω(n²)` pairs is suppressed (fixed `β` for dense violators, `β_n = 8 ln n` for the full bulk aggregate), leaving a residual competition at the `e^{O(n log² n)}` scale where faithful orders themselves live. Inside that arena the contest (sparse-relation orders: low action *and* low entropy; the cluster class of §4) is **[OPEN]** — that is the frontier this paper hands forward, and it is a different, smaller problem than the one paper 7's no-go closed. The `Ω(n²)` structure came *from* the backwards ledger, not from tuning; that remains the point.

## 4. The heredity axis as an instrument [DEMONSTRATED]

Implementing Theorem 2.1 as a certificate axis: take the largest intervals (interior `≥ 24`), score each interior's `r_I` (band `|r − ½| ≤ 0.15`) and height ratio `H/(2√k)` (band `(0.55, 1.35)` — wide, BDJ-deficit-inclusive; bands disclosed), and recurse one level into each interval's own largest sub-interval. Four populations at `N = 384`, five seeds (receipt `h2`):

| population | level-1 pass | level-2 pass |
|---|---|---|
| geometric | **100%** | **100%** |
| KR | **0%** | 0% |
| KR+spine (paper 7's designed adversary) | **0%** | 0% |
| cluster blow-up | 97% | 100% |

*(KR/KR+spine level-2 entries are vacuous, not measured: their level-1 interiors are antichains containing no qualifying sub-intervals, and the axis returns the default fail — disclosed.)*

Two headlines. First, the **designed adversary is annihilated**: the KR+spine order that paper 7 tuned to fool the *global* Myrheim–Meyer and height axes has nowhere to hide inside its own intervals — its top intervals are the KR block's antichain interiors, scoring `r_I = 0`, maximally far from `½` (its spine's chain intervals would score `r_I = 1`, equally fatal, though at `N = 384` they never reach the top-6 against the larger KR-block interiors — stated for completeness, not exercised in the receipt). Heredity converts "fool four global numbers" into "be manifoldlike at every scale simultaneously," a qualitatively harder forgery problem. Second, the honest finding:

**The cluster adversary survives.** A blow-up order — `N/6` geometric centers, six tightly-jittered points each — passes the `r`/height heredity axes at `97%/100%`: within a tight cluster, jittered points are dominance-comparable with probability exactly `½` (Theorem 2.2's own arithmetic!), so blow-ups mimic the `r`-law at every scale. This is *reported as the finding it is* (the cousin of the v7 linear-jitter adversary that survived the recursive-interval campaigns of the frozen line): the `r`-ledger is **necessary, not sufficient**, and the discriminating axis it names is the internal **abundance profile** (blow-ups distort `N₀/N₁/N₂` counts — cluster-mates produce anomalous small-interval statistics), which is the next `∼n²`-constraint family the backwards program would add to the action. Each evasion buys the forger a narrower cell; each new derived axis is another super-extensive action term. That iterative structure — adversary ↔ derived axis — is the program.

## 5. The drift toy [DEMONSTRATED, toy-scale, all caveats in the label]

A driven local search on labeled posets at `n = 28` under `e^{−βS_misfit}` (`m₀ = 6` at this size, disclosed): moves add a random non-cycle relation (+ transitive closure) or delete a random cover (transitivity-safe); Metropolis acceptance on `ΔS`. **This is not an equilibrium sampler** (the closure step breaks proposal symmetry — disclosed); the claim is *drift*, against a control. From a KR(`p = 0.9`) start: at `β = 15 > β*`, `S: 12.25 → 1.21` within `4×10³` proposals; the `β = 0` control (pure move-set, no action) instead *rises* to `33.3` — the drift is the action's, not the move set's; and the largest interval's internal `r` moves from the antichain `0.000` to `0.705` (toward, and past, `½` — direction at toy scale, nothing more). No convergence, mixing, or thermodynamic-limit claim; `n = 28` cannot exhibit manifoldlikeness. What the toy establishes is the minimum viable positive: **the derived action, at the derived coupling, moves record structure off the layered bulk by local order moves** — the first positive dynamics exhibit in the program, where previously there was only the no-go.

## 6. The wall, and what this paper does not claim

**Sufficiency is the Hauptvermutung.** "Ledger-complete ⇒ faithfully embeddable" is not proved here or anywhere; the cluster adversary is this paper's own in-house proof that the current ledger is incomplete, and no finite ledger is known to close in `d ≥ 3` (recognition hardness stands as paper 7 §3 left it: dim ≤ 3 NP-complete as proxy, the 2D door the only one known open). The backwards program produces a *monotone cage* — every derived axis shrinks the forger's cell and adds an action term — with no claim the cage closes at any finite stage. **The dynamics candidate is a candidate.** Corollary 3.3 proves 3-layer-ensemble suppression at finite `β` and Corollary 3.4 prices the rest (dense violators at fixed `β`; the two-layer aggregate only at `β_n = 8 ln n`); neither proves the Gibbs measure concentrates on faithful orders — the sub-quadratic arena's residual contest (sparse-relation orders, the cluster class, unknown others) is open, and the toy shows drift at `n = 28`, not emergence. **Record-nativity:** `S_misfit` is a function of the order alone (relabeling-invariant, receipt-checked), so it is a record-native action over paper 7 §4.1's counting measures (the action slot of its §4.2/Cor 4.2); whether any *physical* record dynamics realizes it is untouched and is the program's next real question, adjacent to paper 1 §4.4's spacing ledger. **Dimension:** everything quantitative here is 2D (the `M^d` per-interval laws exist and generalize the ledger; not run). The manifoldlikeness gate (papers 4 §5, 7) remains **[OPEN]** — this paper changes what stands on the dynamics side of it: one derived, explicit, receipt-backed candidate with a proved suppression threshold, plus a strictly stronger instrument, plus a named next axis.
