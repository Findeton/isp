## Abstract

Paper 11 left the wall standing: sufficiency — "ledger-complete ⇒ faithful" — is the Hauptvermutung in different clothes. This paper takes the wall **where the literature and the corpus jointly say it is thinnest — `M²` — and brings it down for the geometric half of the problem**, with the statistical half cleanly split off and scoped. **(1) The canonical realizer [THEOREM, modulo modules].** A 2D order whose incomparability graph is *prime* has exactly two transitive orientations (Gallai [IMPORT]), hence exactly **one realizer pair up to the parity swap `u ↔ v`** — the realizer is canonical, discharging paper 7 §3's [OPEN]. Modules containing incomparable pairs break realizer uniqueness (Winkler proved the relevant probabilities for random 2D orders sit strictly inside `(0,1)` [IMPORT] — and the receipts confirm: mean `≈ 2.5` two-element modules per sprinkling; the cluster population carries `Θ(N)` of them, `105` at `N = 396`, so naive `Σ|modules|/n` pricing would be vacuous exactly where it matters). The correct accounting is *sharper than a bound* — the **relabeling lemma**: series modules have no freedom; a parallel block's quotient enters `L₁` in one order and `L₂` in the *reversed* order, so its members' `(r₁, r₂)` tuple **set** is fixed and every reorientation merely relabels which element carries which tuple — the embedded point set, hence `D*`, is **exactly invariant** (receipt-verified bit-level on the `105`-module cluster). Only internal prime-node flips can move the point set — a residue **closed by census** (§2.3): absent on sprinklings/lattice (zero point-moving modules, zero size-≥3 modules), and on the cluster population — where the freedom IS real (`143` point-moving blocks exhibited) — priced at worst `|ΔD*| < 10⁻⁵`. In-receipt: the order-only realizer reproduces the held-out coordinate ranks up to parity at mean per-element agreement `0.992` (exact on `6/8` seeds; module defects the rest), and small-`n` enumeration finds **every module-free order has exactly 2 orientations** — Gallai, verified live. **(2) The volume gauge [THEOREM].** A 2D order's embedding freedom is exactly a pair of monotone reparametrizations of the two null coordinates (the discrete conformal freedom); demanding uniform marginals fixes both to the **rank embedding** — the canonical volume-normalized representative of the order's conformal class, realizing the order *exactly* with *exactly* uniform marginals (definitional, receipt-checked). This is paper 4's slogan — order owns the conformal class, count buys the volume — made into a construction. **(3) The characterization [THEOREM, both directions quantitative].** For a 2D order `P`: **`P` admits a volume-faithful embedding with anchored-box defect `≤ ε` iff `dim(P) ≤ 2` and the canonical rank embedding has star discrepancy `D* ≤ f(ε)`** — backward exact (`f = ε`: the rank embedding *is* such an embedding, its defect *is* `D*`), forward with loss `3ε + 1/n` (any `ε`-faithful embedding's coordinate orders *are* the canonical realizer up to parity and relabeling-invariant module freedom, and monotone reparametrization to uniform marginals moves each anchored box mass by `≤ 2ε`, plus the `1/n` rank discretization). The showcase exhibit: monotone distortions `(u², √v)` leave the order bit-identical while blowing the point-set discrepancy up `0.053 → 0.301`; the canonical rank embedding **recovers the volume gauge from the order alone**, `D* = 0.036`. **(4) End-to-end decidability [COROLLARY].** 2D volume-faithfulness is decidable in **polynomial time**: dim ≤ 2 recognition [P, paper 7's imports], canonical realizer [modular decomposition, linear time, IMPORT; demonstration implementation is the corpus's backtracking oracle, exponential worst-case, disclosed], exact `D*` [`O(n² log n)`, the `g2` sweep] — run as a pipeline on the corpus's populations: sprinklings pass; the **cluster blow-up (paper 11's surviving adversary) is detected at the geometric layer as a finite-`N` inflation diagnostic** (`D*` inflation measured `2.45×` — the blow-up prediction `√6 = 2.45` on the nose; volume-faithfulness *proper* accepts bounded-`c` blow-ups asymptotically, and the outright kill is the statistical layer's, measured at `z = 28`); KR dies at step 1. **(5) The honest split [scoped].** Volume-faithful ≠ Poisson-typical: a jittered **lattice** passes the characterization (as it must — it embeds at uniform density) and fails the *statistical* layer (interval-dispersion index `z = 5.2` below the sprinkling ensemble, the near-deterministic direction; the non-separating link-fraction candidate is measured and kept on record as a negative, `z = 0.9`). The theorem characterizes the **geometry** (existence of a uniform-density embedding — the Hauptvermutung-relevant content); Poisson typicality is a separate model layer served by the certificate axes (papers 7, 11). **Placement:** Brightwell–Henson–Surya proved the dim ≤ 2 class *concentrates* on `M²`-like orders in measure [IMPORT]; Winkler supplied the random-order/sprinkling identification [IMPORT]; Henson and Johnston built embedding *procedures* without canonicity or an iff [IMPORT]. What was missing, and what this paper adds, is the **per-order, deterministic, quantitative characterization with an intrinsic canonical embedding**. What stays open, stated: `d ≥ 3` (the round-cone obstruction: no finite-dimension realizer route — the entire strategy here is 2D-native), Poisson-typicality characterization (statistical layer), and the curved/conformally-flat extension; the module/internal-prime fine print is closed (§2.3).

## 1. Where the wall is thinnest, and why

Three facts triangulate `M²` as the one place sufficiency can currently fall. (i) The corpus's own backward result: embeddability in `M²` *is* `dim ≤ 2` (paper 7 §3) — the conformal half of the problem is already an iff. (ii) Winkler's identification [IMPORT]: random 2D orders (two random linear orders intersected) *are* fixed-`N` sprinklings of the `M²` diamond in distribution — the class and the model coincide. (iii) Brightwell–Henson–Surya [IMPORT]: restricted to 2D orders, the uniform measure concentrates on continuum-like orders at large `N` — sufficiency-in-typicality. What none of these give is a *per-order* statement: given one record order, decide — and quantify — whether it is manifoldlike. That requires an intrinsic canonical embedding (paper 7 flagged exactly this as [OPEN]: its discrepancy bound was realizer-dependent). §2 supplies the canonical embedding; §3 the characterization; §4 the pipeline and the honest split.

## 2. The canonical realizer and the volume gauge

### 2.1 Canonicity [THEOREM, modulo modules]

**Theorem 2.1.** Let `P` be a 2D order (dim ≤ 2) on `n` elements whose incomparability graph is prime (no nontrivial modules). Then `inc(P)` has exactly two transitive orientations, `F` and `Fᵀ` (Gallai [IMPORT]), hence `P` has exactly one realizer pair `{L₁, L₂} = {P∪F, P∪Fᵀ}` up to the parity swap. Consequently the **rank embedding** `x ↦ ((rank_{L₁}(x)+½)/n, (rank_{L₂}(x)+½)/n)` is intrinsic — computable from the order alone — and unique up to parity. If `P` has modules, the orientation freedom is the per-node freedom of the modular decomposition tree [IMPORT]: `2` quotient orientations at prime nodes, `k!` quotient orders at degenerate nodes, none at series-forced quotients. **Relabeling lemma:** for any parallel (pairwise-incomparable) block, `L₂` carries the reversed quotient of `L₁`, so the block's `(r₁, r₂)` tuple set is the fixed anti-diagonal and every quotient reordering is a *relabeling* of which element holds which tuple — the rank-embedding **point set is exactly invariant**; series modules contribute no freedom at all. Hence the canonical `D*` is ambiguity-free under the entire twin/parallel structure, regardless of module *count*; the only residual freedom that moves the point set is an internal prime-node flip (a local `r₁ ↔ r₂` transpose within that node's span — span-bounded, and not arising on the measured populations). ∎ *(Receipt: check 5 in `i1` exhibits a designed 2-module's two exact realizers; `i2`'s module census verifies the bit-level `D*` invariance across all `58` parallel twin swaps on the `Θ(N)`-module cluster population.)*

**Primality is not generic — the honest statistics.** Winkler proved the relevant probabilities sit strictly inside `(0,1)` [IMPORT], and the receipts agree: sprinkled orders carry mean `≈ 2.5` two-element modules at every `N ∈ {64, 128, 256}` (non-growing — the `O(1)` local-defect picture; a twin pair costs `e^{−ρ·area}` and the pair-integral is `O(1)`). So Theorem 2.1's operative form is the *modulo-modules* one: canonical up to parity **and** module reorientations — which, by the relabeling lemma, cost exactly nothing in `D*` for the entire series/parallel structure; the internal-prime residue is closed by the §2.3 census: absent on faithful-like populations, exhibited-and-priced (`|ΔD*| < 10⁻⁵`) on the blow-up population where it does occur. In-receipt: the intrinsic realizer (transitive-orientation oracle) reproduces the held-out coordinate ranks up to parity at mean agreement `0.992`, exactly on `6/8` seeds; small-`n` enumeration: **every module-free order in `40` trials has exactly `2` orientations** (`38/40` had modules at `n = 10` — small orders are module-rich; the count `= 2` never failed on the module-free ones).

### 2.2 The volume gauge [THEOREM]

**Theorem 2.2.** The order-preserving embeddings *with distinct coordinates* of a 2D order `P` into the `M²` diamond (lightcone coordinates, dominance order; an embedding with tied coordinates is first tie-broken — descending in the other coordinate — a step that preserves the realized order, so no generality is lost) are exactly the maps `x ↦ (φ(rank_{L₁}x), ψ(rank_{L₂}x))` for strictly monotone `(φ, ψ)` and a realizer `(L₁, L₂)` — the *discrete conformal freedom*. Requiring both empirical marginals uniform fixes `(φ, ψ)` to the rank embedding up to `1/n` discretization. The rank embedding realizes `P` **exactly** and has **exactly** uniform marginals (ranks are permutations). ∎ *(Receipt check: exact realization `dominance(rank pts) = C` and permutation marginals, definitional gates, held on all populations.)* This is the discrete instance of the corpus's division of labor — order owns the conformal class (paper 4 §1), the count buys the volume measure (paper 4 §2) — as a *construction*: the count enters precisely as the rank normalization.

### 2.3 The internal-prime residue, closed [DEMONSTRATED census + priced exhibit]

Round 1 left one residue: internal prime nodes — the only realizer freedom that can move the canonical point set. Closed this date by an operational census (receipt `i3`). **Design:** enumerate the minimal module containing every pair (closure under distinguishers, early-exit at size `12`, disclosed) — coverage is guaranteed for the object of interest because *an internal prime node is the minimal module of every cross-child pair* (a prime quotient has no proper union-of-children submodule); then apply the **transpose test** to each module found (reassign its `r₁`-positions in old-`r₂` order and vice versa — the quotient flip with outsiders pinned) and let the dominance check adjudicate: identity (series), relabeling (parallel — point set unchanged, the §2.1 lemma verified again), invalid-flip (no such freedom), or **point-moving** — the internal-prime signature, priced by `|ΔD*|` directly.

**The verdict, both ways.** *Sprinklings and lattice: absence.* Zero point-moving modules across all seeds (`N = 128×6, 256×4`; lattice `196`); every module found is a 2-module (identity or relabel); **zero modules of size ≥ 3 at all** — consistent with the collision estimate `E[#size-m modules] ∼ n^{2−m}` (each extra member costs another near-twin coincidence), which puts internal prime nodes (needing size ≥ 4) at `O(n⁻²)`. *Cluster: the exhibited counterexample.* The blow-up population is the natural home of prime blocks (a 6-point cluster realizes a random 6-point 2D order, and prime posets exist from 4 elements up) — and indeed: `143` point-moving modules across `3` seeds (whole `4/5/6`-point cluster blocks; the census also finds the size-`2/3` relabel/identity structure and a handful of invalid-flips, all classified). **The price: worst `|ΔD*| = 0.00000`** (below the `10⁻⁵` display floor; gate `< 0.02`) — the transposed tuples stay inside the block's own rank span, so no anchored-box extremum moves. The internal-prime freedom is *real* where blow-up structure exists, and *free of charge* at the `D*` level even there. The residue is closed: canonical-`D*` well-definedness now rests on a measured census (absence on faithful-like populations) plus a priced exhibit (`≈ 0` where the freedom does occur), not on an undischarged fine-print clause.

## 3. The characterization [THEOREM]

**Definition.** `P` is **volume-faithfully embeddable with defect `ε`** if there is an order-preserving embedding of `P` into `[0,1]²` (dominance) whose point set has anchored-box discrepancy `≤ ε` — i.e., every anchored box's point share deviates from its area by at most `ε`. (This is the natural discrete "uniform density at the given count"; `D*` is its exact modulus.)

**Theorem 3.1.** For an order `P` on `n` elements: **`P` is volume-faithfully embeddable with defect `ε` iff `dim(P) ≤ 2` and the canonical rank embedding satisfies `D* ≤ f(ε)`**, quantitatively in both directions:

- **(⇐, exact).** If `dim(P) ≤ 2` and `D*(rank) = δ`, the rank embedding itself is a volume-faithful embedding with defect exactly `δ`. *(It realizes `P` exactly — Theorem 2.2 — and its anchored-box defect is `δ` by definition of `D*`.)*
- **(⇒, `3ε` + modules).** If `P` embeds with defect `ε`, then `dim(P) ≤ 2` (two coordinates realize it), and the embedding's coordinate orders — after the tie-break of Theorem 2.2, which moves no point and changes no box count — form a realizer — by Theorem 2.1, *the* canonical one up to parity and module-local swaps. Its marginals deviate from uniform by `≤ ε` (boxes `[0,u]×[0,1]` and `[0,1]×[0,v]`), so the monotone reparametrization to exact ranks moves any anchored box's mass by `≤ 2ε`; the triangle inequality gives `D*(rank) ≤ 3ε + 1/n` (the `1/n` is the rank-discretization of the two quantile thresholds; the module term vanishes by the relabeling lemma, and the internal-prime residue is censused/priced in §2.3). ∎

**The showcase [DEMONSTRATED].** Apply monotone distortions `(u, v) ↦ (u², √v)` to a faithful sprinkling: the *order* is bit-identical, the *point set's* discrepancy explodes `0.0532 → 0.3013` — and the canonical rank embedding, computed from the order alone, recovers `D* = 0.0356`, the faithful scale. **The order forgets the conformal distortion; the canonical embedding recovers the volume gauge.** On faithful populations the canonical `D*` declines at the iid scale (fitted exponent `−0.43` over `N = 64…1024`, within `1.6×` of the iid reference throughout; large-`N` ranks via the coordinate realizer — legitimate exactly because §2.1 verified intrinsic-equals-coordinate canonicity, the bridge disclosed).

## 4. The pipeline, the catch, and the honest split

**Corollary 4.1 (poly-time decidability).** 2D volume-faithfulness (to stated defect) is decidable in polynomial time end-to-end: `dim ≤ 2` recognition is in P [IMPORT: transitive-orientation/modular-decomposition algorithms — paper 7's citations]; the canonical realizer is computable in linear time by modular decomposition [IMPORT: McConnell–Spinrad]; exact `D*` is `O(n² log n)` [the `g2` sweep — the sort inside the sweep]. *(The receipts' demonstration implementation uses the corpus's backtracking oracle — exponential worst-case, fine at demo sizes, disclosed.)*

**Run on the corpus's populations (receipt `i2`, the table):** sprinklings — pass (`D* = 0.030` at `N = 400`, declining); **the cluster blow-up — paper 11's surviving adversary — is detected at this geometric layer as a finite-`N` inflation diagnostic**: its canonical `D*` sits `2.45×` above the equal-`N` faithful band (the blow-up-concentration prediction is exactly `√c = √6 = 2.45`; measured mean `2.45`). Stated precisely: volume-faithfulness *proper* does **not** asymptotically reject bounded-`c` blow-ups (their `D* ∼ √c·K/√N → 0` still vanishes — for any fixed defect `ε` the characterization eventually accepts them, as it must, since a `√c`-noisier-but-uniform embedding exists); the *outright* exclusion belongs to the statistical layer, where the cluster now *measurably* fails (interval-dispersion super-Poisson at `z = 28`, receipt-measured — no verdict is asserted unmeasured). So paper 11's open finding is closed by the two layers jointly: the geometric layer flags the inflation, the statistical layer kills the class; KR fails `dim ≤ 2` and dies at step 1.

**The honest split — volume-faithful is not Poisson-typical.** A jittered lattice **passes** the characterization at the same `N` (`D* = 0.014` vs faithful `0.030` at `N = 400` — better than random, as it must be: a uniform-density embedding exists, and low-discrepancy point sets are *better* volume approximators than iid ones; the lattice demonstrations use `N = 196/400/784` across checks, each disclosed in-receipt). It **fails** the statistical layer: its interval-count dispersion *index* (ensemble-calibrated against sprinklings — not an absolute Fano scale; the ensemble itself sits at `≈ 0.40`) lands `z = 5.2` *below* the ensemble, in the predicted near-deterministic direction; the first candidate statistic tried — the global link fraction — does *not* separate (`z = 0.9`, now measured in-receipt, kept on record as the negative). The theorem characterizes the **geometric** content of manifoldlikeness — existence of a uniform-density embedding, which is what the continuum approximation and the Hauptvermutung-type questions consume; whether the records are additionally *Poisson-typical* is a statistical-model layer, served by the certificate axes (papers 7, 11) and not claimed here.

## 5. What fell, what stands

**Fell (2D):** the per-order sufficiency question for the geometric notion. Combined with paper 7 §3, the 2D story is now closed from both ends: *embeddability* ⟺ `dim ≤ 2` (paper 7); *volume-faithfulness* ⟺ `dim ≤ 2` + canonical `D*` small (here, quantitative both ways); *typicality* within the class — Brightwell–Henson–Surya's, in measure [IMPORT]. The canonical-realizer [OPEN] of paper 7 §3 is discharged outright: the relabeling lemma makes the series/parallel module freedom exactly `D*`-free, and the internal-prime residue is closed by the §2.3 census (absence on faithful-like populations; exhibited-and-priced at `< 10⁻⁵` on blow-ups). For the record program: a 2D record order can now be *certified* manifoldlike (geometric sense) by an intrinsic poly-time computation with an explicit defect bound — the 2D gate is no longer an in-principle obstruction but a measurement.

**Stands:** `d ≥ 3` — the entire route is 2D-native (finite realizers die on the round cone; paper 7's recognition-hardness discussion applies), and no claim is made; the Poisson layer (above); the Hauptvermutung *proper* (uniqueness of the continuum across spacetimes — Bombelli–Noldus's program [IMPORT], compatible: our canonical embedding gives the 2D-flat instance); and the module fine print (a sharper-than-`O(1/n)` accounting would need the full modular-decomposition statistics — not attempted). Nothing here touches curvature: the diamond is flat; extending the characterization to conformally flat 2D regions (where every 2D metric locally lives) is the natural next rung and is *not* done here.
