## 2. Certificate v2 and the ghost budget

**Certificate v2.** To paper 14's pilot certificate — (i) every antipodal shadow within the max-calibrated same-law floor, (ii) harmonic residual `< 0.05` — add the condition round 1 exposed as missing: **(iii) the harmonic reconstruction realizes the input order** (agreement `≥ 0.98`). Verified jointly on one faithful order (`N = 1200`: `max wD* 0.035` vs `0.045`; residual `0.010`; agreement `0.998`). Condition (iii) is what makes soundness discussable at all: the certificate now asserts *this order embeds as this point set*, not merely *some projections look right*.

**The clarifying negative first — as a dichotomy, not a dismissal.** Discrete tomography's ghosts — switching components à la Ryser/Gardner–Gritzmann [IMPORT] — rearrange points while preserving the tested marginals. Two regimes: *small-support* rearrangements (`O(1)` points moved) leave the configuration faithful up to negligible defect — the certificate rightly still passes, and the input is simply another faithful order (note the certificate tests 2D `(t, n·x⃗)` copulas, which classical 1D-X-ray-preserving switches need not even preserve — passage is not automatic); *extended* switching patterns, by contrast, are exactly density modulations — the paper's own spectral adversary — and fall under the ghost budget below, not under this paragraph. The taxonomy is complete: every marginal-preserving rearrangement is either negligible or spectral.

**The true adversary and the closed loop.** The genuine threat is a density modulation `1 + a·cos(q⃗·x⃗)`. Two elementary facts pin its fate: *(a)* its `k`-th shadow marginal is fiber-averaged, leaving relative leakage `∼ a/(q·sin∠(q̂, n_k)·ℓ)` — so invisibility to all `K` directions forces `q ≳ 1/sin(π/2K)`: **Logan's finite-angle uncertainty principle** [IMPORT: Logan, Duke Math. J. **42**, 661 (1975)], in our setting; *(b)* any mode's contribution to *any* anchored-box count — in a shadow or in 3D — is `≤ ∼a/q` (the CDF of an oscillation is flat to `1/q`). Together: **certificate-invisible ghosts cannot move the discrepancy metric anywhere**; what they can move is pointwise/spectral density below wavelength `2π/q`. Measured, all four legs (receipt `l1`): the `q = 120` bisector ghost is really there (matched filter `z = 15.3`, `â = 0.625`); it passes the *full* v2 certificate at `K = 8` and its shadows at `K = 16` (`0.032` against floors `0.047/0.040` — CDF-blindness is direction-independent, so more directions do *not* catch it, exactly as (b) predicts); its 3D box discrepancy stays within the disclosed `1.5×` gate (`0.039` vs clean `0.027` — a real but bounded increase, not "unchanged"); and the *same amplitude at* `q = 6` — where (a)'s leakage is large — is caught at `> 2×` floor. **The guarantee, final form:** certificate v2 ⇒ an order-realizing embedding whose anchored-box volume defect is controlled at all scales; spectral non-uniformity above frequency `∼ K` *can be invisible* — it is not certifiable by this or any discrepancy-type certificate whenever its amplitude sits below the CDF floor (`a/q ≲` the statistical floor; the demonstrated invisibility used `q = 120 ≫ K = 8`, and a grid-aligned mode at `q ∼ 2K` with the same amplitude *would* be caught) — the budget is metric-intrinsic (Logan), amplitude-conditioned, and not a defect of the construction. Callers who need spectral uniformity must add a spectral axis (matched filters over a frequency band — the natural cage extension, priced at one check per band); the *geometric* content — embeddability with volume control — is closed.
