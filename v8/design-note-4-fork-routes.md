## 2. The three readings, tagged

**Route A — sparse reading kept, richness from the menu [DERIVED menu; mixture convention open].** Seals are indivisible clicks; per-click content is exactly `C(h_K)` for the committing ledger's rung. Randomness in `χ` = rung mixture only. Unpinned and disclosed: the mixture weights over rungs (uniform over available rungs is the receipt's convention; one skewed sensitivity arm) and how many rungs physical webs host. *Mechanism analysis, pre-registered:* exact ties require identical rung-multisets, so tie-mass should collapse combinatorially as `(L, rungs)` grow (multiset space `≫ N`); the residual risk is **multi-scale near-lattice dispersion** — sums over a lacunary menu cluster at the big atoms' lattice with fine splittings, and the dispersion layer may see that structure even at low tie-mass. Counter-pressure: growing `L` re-awakens O1 co-growth (`j3`: `D*` rises with `L`). **The route-A question is whether an `(L, rungs)` window passes all three layers — genuinely open in both directions.** Prediction (falsifiable): if a window exists it requires `L ≥ ~8` *and* `≥ ~6` rungs; at `L = 2` no menu depth rescues it.

**Route B — dense reading [POSITED reading; statistically already measured].** In the divisible regime, content accrues continuously between division events; division events fire on the evidence clock `I ~ Exp(1)`; evidence converts to content through `κ`. Sketch: `Δχ(event) = κ·ΔI ⇒` iid **exponential** increments. Since the entire two-clock pipeline is rank-based, the scale `κ` drops out — route B's supply is *rank-identical to `j3`'s continuum arm*, which passed all three layers at `N = 2048`. So under this reading **the cell-membership result is already the derived result**. What `j4` adds is the tagged identity arm and the named upgrade obligations: (i) does the `κ`-conversion hold at event granularity (not just in aggregate)? (ii) are division events the right two-clock atoms in the dense regime? (iii) which regime is *realized* is exactly paper 1 §7's deferred dense-vs-sparse computation on the decoherence functional — **promoted from phenomenology backlog to wall-deciding**.

**Route C — sparse reading + configuration-magnitude spread [POSITED; tension made explicit].** Paper 1 carries both "`Var[content | mode] = 0`" (§4.3, the arena's fixed point) and "the supply's magnitude is physical configuration data, constrained only differentially" (§4.1, the coboundary clause). If the second clause modulates realized per-seal content around the mode root with relative width `w`, then exact ties vanish for *any* `w > 0` and the question becomes the threshold `w*` at which the dispersion layer passes. Prediction (from `j3`'s 128-atom pass at `~0.7%` spacing): `w*` is small — order `1%` — so even slight configuration spread suffices. If measured so, the wall is *fragile to the C-reading*: it stands only if the arena's exact determinism survives in physical webs to sub-percent precision.
