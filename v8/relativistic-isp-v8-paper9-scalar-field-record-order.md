## 3. The discrete d'Alembertian, honestly priced [IMPORT + DEMONSTRATED]

**The operator [IMPORT: Sorkin; Benincasa–Dowker].** `Bf(x) = (4/ℓ²)[−½f(x) + (S₁ − 2S₂ + S₃)(f)]`, the `S_k` sums over the first three past layers, `ℓ² = 1/ρ`. Its Poisson-expectation kernel is `K(z) = e^{−z}(1 − 2z + z²/2)`, and the limit works because of three moment identities, verified sympy-exact in the receipt: `∫₀^∞K = 0`, `∫₀^∞Kz = 0` (killing the down-the-lightcone and deep-past divergences), and `−∫₀^∞K ln z = ½` (cancelling the local `ρf(x)` term exactly).

**Exact expectation, converging [DEMONSTRATED, semi-analytic].** The receipt evaluates the exact finite-`ρ` expectation `E_ρ[Bf]` (nested quadrature over the Poisson kernel — no matrices, no sampling) on a held-out Gaussian test function: it converges to `□f` in the **mostly-plus** convention (`□f = −2∂_u∂_v f` in lightcone coordinates), entering the `1%` band by `ρ = 2048` and staying (rel. errors `0.048 → 0.004 → 0.003 → 0.003` at `ρ = 512…32768`); the `∼0.3%` plateau is the finite-diamond boundary residual, which does not vanish with `ρ` and is disclosed as such.

**The fluctuation problem, exhibited [DEMONSTRATED].** Point evaluations of `B` do *not* self-average: the per-sprinkling std of `Bf(x)` **grows** with `N` (`1448 → 5854` from `N = 128 → 512` on our test function) — the known BDG fluctuation pathology, reproduced on the record order rather than hidden. The standard cure is smearing **[IMPORT]**: the damped operator `B_ε` with layer weights `w(n) = (1−ε)ⁿ[1 − 2εn/(1−ε) + ε²n(n−1)/(2(1−ε)²)]` and prefactor `4ερ[−½f + εΣw(n)f]`, whose expectation equals the unsmeared operator at `ρ_eff = ερ` (the generating-function identity `Σ_n w(n)·Poisson(n; μ) = e^{−εμ}(1 − 2εμ + (εμ)²/2)` is receipt-checked sympy-exact). Scored the self-averaging way — as a **volume pairing** `⟨g, B_ε f⟩/ρ` against the continuum `⟨g, □f⟩` — the smeared operator at `N = 2048`, `ε = 0.125` lands at `−0.577 ± 0.089` against `−0.611` (`6%`, sign at `> 3` SEM; receipt check 5). Point-value physics from `B` on records at these densities is *not* licensed; paired/action-level statements are.

## 4. The Sorkin–Johnston vacuum on the record order [IMPORT + DEMONSTRATED]

**Machine-precision structure.** On the record order (`N = 400`): `iΔ` Hermitian to `10⁻¹⁴`; the nonzero spectrum comes in `197` exactly-paired `±` modes (residual `10⁻¹⁴`); `W` is PSD; the defining identities hold at machine precision — `W − W̄ = iΔ` (`3×10⁻¹⁴`), the ground-state condition `WW̄ = 0` (`7×10⁻¹⁴`), and `Im W = Δ/2` (the commutator is state-independent, `2×10⁻¹⁴`). The matrix is `ρ ×` the continuum integral operator: `λ_max` doubles with `N` (ratios `1.963`, `2.028` over `N = 200 → 400 → 800`).

**Held-out validation — the center log law.** The continuum massless Wightman function behaves as `−(1/4π)ln(ΔuΔv) + const` on timelike pairs, and in the diamond's center region the SJ state approaches the Minkowski form [IMPORT: AAS].
