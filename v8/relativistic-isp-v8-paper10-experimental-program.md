## 1. What paper 6 leaves winnable

Paper 6's structure: passive reduced-coherence spectroscopy is mechanism-blind to all orders (Channel B, structural); the swerve is testable but scale-gated with a [POSITED] tie and an [OPEN] σ-consistency flag (Channel A); the quarter law is a theorem whose physical application rides one [POSITED] bridge; and the empirical bottom line is one narrow neutrino-sector face. Three moves respect all of that and still buy ground: **(i)** develop the blindness theorem's *own stated boundary* (active probes, reversibility axis) into a designed protocol with sample complexities — §2; **(ii)** validate the quarter-law *structure* in an engineered system where the bridge holds by construction — §3; **(iii)** protect the one empirical face with a third-party timestamp — §4. What is deliberately *not* here: new swerve designs (blocked on the [OPEN] σ-consistency flag, which is upstream physics, not experiment design), and anything claiming seal-vs-QM discrimination through the CP-divisibility wall (§2's honest endpoint stops exactly at it).

## 2. The echo-floor protocol [DESIGNED + DEMONSTRATED]

### 2.1 The model, disclosed

A qubit under an `n`-pulse CPMG sequence of total time `T`:

> `−ln W(T, n) = χ_noise(T, n) + Λ_s·T`

with `χ_noise` the Gaussian-dephasing functional of the sequence's toggling function against the environmental autocorrelation (`C_n(t) = σ²e^{−|t|/τ_c}`, Ornstein–Uhlenbeck, in the receipt), and `Λ_s T` the seal survival term, **pulse-invariant by model**. The pulse-invariance encodes the corpus claim — a committed seal is not a unitary rotation; no π-pulse undoes a commit (paper 1 §3.5's definitional no-revival; paper 6 §3's boundary exhibit) — it is *the hypothesis under test*, not a derivation. The protocol's logic needs only the dichotomy: refocusable vs not.

### 2.2 The designed ladder and its numbers

Sweep `n` at fixed `T`. The receipt's integrator is validated against the OU closed forms (free-induction and Hahn-echo `χ` to `< 0.2%`), and then: `χ_n` falls strictly down the ladder (`0.311 → 0.0086 → 0.0001` at `n = 1/8/64`) with the known `n^{−2}` law in the `τ_p ≪ τ_c` regime (fitted exponent `1.994`), while the total `−ln W` **saturates at the floor**: with an injected `Λ_s T = 0.05`, `−ln W(64) = 0.0501`. The printed ladder (receipt check 6) shows the two-hypothesis split directly: at `n = 64` the seal+noise curve sits `368×` above the pure-noise curve.

**The estimator.** The protocol estimator is the raw plateau value `−ln Ŵ` at large `n`; its bias *is* the residual refocusable `χ_n` (disclosed; `∝ n^{−2}`, so it is driven under any target by adding pulses), and its shot noise at `M = 2×10⁴` is `0.0024` — the injected floor `0.05` is recovered as `0.0502 ± 0.0024`. **Sample complexity:** 5σ discrimination of `Λ_s T = 0.05` against `0` needs `M ≈ 1,004` shots at `n = 64`. These are trapped-ion/superconducting-qubit-trivial numbers; the protocol is hardware-cheap.

### 2.3 The honest boundary, in-receipt

White (exactly Markovian) dephasing has `χ_n = γT` for **every** `n` — analytic, the toggling function squares to one; the receipt verifies the mechanism on the grid (`±1`-valuedness, `∫y²dt = T` at each `n`) rather than restating the identity (check 5). The protocol therefore ends where paper 6 §3 proved it must: the residual mimic class after the full ladder is **precisely the CP-divisibility line, in the multi-time/interventional sense** — memoryless (delta-correlated) dephasing survives; correlated noises are excluded even though their reduced free-induction maps are themselves CP-divisible. What has changed is operational, not structural: *before*, the seal was one member of an undifferentiated decoherence background; *after* the ladder, every refocusable (correlated-noise) explanation is excluded at a stated confidence and the floor rate `Λ_s` is a measured number — the seal's rate budget, an input the corpus can consume (e.g., any future reading of the physical `σ` supply, paper 1 §7.1) even though the floor's *mechanism* remains underdetermined within the CP-divisible class. No seal-vs-QM claim is made; none is possible on this axis (paper 6 §3, structural).

### 2.4 What would have to be true for the floor to be a seal bound

Three steps, each named: (i) run the ladder on a maximally-isolated qubit (the floor then bounds *intrinsic* dephasing + seal); (ii) subtract the certified intrinsic-Markovian budget of the platform (`T₂`-limit engineering, the hard experimental part — this is where the real cost lives); (iii) the remainder is an upper bound on `Λ_s` for that system's record coupling. Step (ii)'s difficulty is not hidden: state-of-the-art qubits attribute their residual `T₂` to identified noise sources only within factors, so near-term the protocol yields *bounds*, not detections — which is still the first direct experimental number on the no-revival channel, a channel paper 6 otherwise proves passively invisible.

## 3. The quarter-law analog test [DESIGNED + DEMONSTRATED]

### 3.1 The bridge, instantiated by construction

Paper 6 §1: `−ln BC = σ/4 + (ε²/6)σ + O(σ³)` is a theorem *within* the which-path pair; the [POSITED] seal-is-record postulate is what identifies the arrow pair `(P_AB, P_BA)` with the which-path pair `(P₀, P₁)` in a physical seal. An **engineered** open system inverts the epistemics: build the recorder so the identification holds *by construction* — a driven qubit + binary pointer where the forward-protocol and reverse-protocol pointer distributions are the two which-path laws (`P₀ = (½+ε, ½−ε)`, `P₁` swapped). Then: visibility `V = BC` from the interference fringe; `σ = D(P₀‖P₁)` from forward/reverse sampling of the pointer — **two independent measurements of the two sides of the law**.

### 3.2 The numbers

Receipt `e2`, all closed forms re-derived sympy-exact against paper 6's anchors (including the running series `¼ + ε²/6 + 14ε⁴/45 + 724ε⁶/945` and the Gaussian all-strength `¼`). At `ε = 0.1` (`V = 0.9798`, `σ = 0.0811`, running value `κ = 0.2517`): the finite-shot estimator `κ̂ = −ln V̂ / σ̂` over `800` protocol replicas at `10⁵` shots per leg recovers `0.2518 ± 0.0095` — an unbiased, percent-level measurement of the quarter. **Separating `¼` from `1`** — the dissolved equality's value, equivalently the mutual-information/complementarity measure paper 6 §1.2 identifies — needs `M_{5σ} = 301` fringe shots under the detection-resolution convention (per-shot variance under the quarter-law hypothesis, `V(¼) = 0.9798` vs `V(1) = 0.9221`), or `1,125` under the strict convention that takes the `κ = 1` null's variance; both conventions are disclosed and quoted in the receipt. **Systematics, priced exactly:** any extraneous visibility loss `η` biases `κ̂ = κ(ε) + η/σ` (identity, in-receipt); the `10%`-bias budget is `η ≤ 0.025σ ≈ 0.002` at this `ε` — the design driver is calibrating away non-recorder dephasing to a few `×10⁻³` in `−ln V`, standard interferometric practice. **Bridge-failure detection:** if the engineered arrow pair is *not* the which-path pair (receipt: `ε′ = 2ε` mismatch), the deterministic ratio `D_B(ε)/D_KL(2ε) = 0.060` sits far off the running curve — a `∼20`-shot-σ offset at the stated `M`, so the protocol carries its own consistency check (the receipt exhibits the closed-form value; it is not a shot-noise simulation).

### 3.3 The falsification logic, stated once

In the engineered system, `κ̂` off the running curve indicts the engineering match — the theorem is unconditional given the match — so the analog test is a *validation instrument*: it demonstrates the seal-decoherence structure (the Rényi-½-over-Rényi-1 ratio, the running, the Gaussian flatness) in a real open system, cheaply and near-term, which is exactly the kind of falsifiable-structure exhibit that buys external credibility. In a *natural* seal system the same measurement would test the [POSITED] bridge itself — a deviation there falsifies the postulate, not the theorem. The test discriminates **nothing between SM and SHARD** (both predict the same open-system arithmetic once the recorder is specified) and is not claimed to; its value is validating the corpus's one forced dimensionless number where an equality was once hoped for, and doing so at `≈ 300`-shot cost.
