## 3. The washout theorems

**Theorem A (clock-correlation washout).** *For the churn supply `W(L, reset)` — `M` slots, uniform race, geometric lifetimes of mean `L`, exchangeable positive continuous increments — Kendall's `τ` between the two clocks satisfies:*

*(i) (exact decomposition) `τ·C(N,2) = SL + CROSS`, where `SL` counts same-lineage pairs, every one of which is concordant (both clocks strictly increase along a lineage — the odometer is a positive sum);*

*(ii) (stationary cross-structure, two parts) — (ii-a) cross pairs on **distinct slots** have mean sign exactly zero: the slots' renewal processes are independent of each other and of the race, so the committing ages are iid. (ii-b) same-slot different-lineage pairs are **not** symmetric: conditioning on "different lineages" forces an intervening death, and ages are functions of the death history, so the later age is truncated (stochastically below stationary) while the earlier is not — a negative sign bias, `−(L−1)/(L+1)` at lag one in closed form for exponential increments (`P(X > Γ(m,1)) = 2^{−m}` summed over the geometric age; the sign statement for general positive increment laws follows by stochastic dominance). The bias is confined to the renewal window, so its aggregate is negative and `O(L/N)`-class: `E[τ | stationary] = E[SL]/C(N,2) − c_ss(L, M)/N + o(1/N) ≤ E[SL]/C(N,2)`. With `E[g(g−1)]/2 = L(L−1)` per completed geometric lineage and renewal-reward accounting, `E[SL] = N(L−1)·(1 − O(LM/N) edge)`, so `E[τ] → [2(L−1) − c_ss]/(N−1)` — the `1/N` rate is the theorem; the sharp constant is named-measured. An earlier draft asserted (ii-b)'s pairs exactly symmetric "by regeneration"; that clause was false — refuted in closed form and at `z = −4.6` in the receipt's own configuration — and is corrected here;*

*(iii) (transient) a fresh-start (common-birth) process differs from the stationary value by at most `O(L·M/N)` — couple the fresh process to a stationary copy: they differ only on slots not yet reborn, the expected number of pre-renewal commits is `O(LM)`, and `τ` has bounded influence `≤ 4k/N` under `k` point changes (each changed point touches `N−1` pairs, each pair's sign moving by at most `2`); the sign of the difference — an excess — is measured, receipt-gated nonnegative;*

*(iv) (fluctuation) `SD(τ) ∝ √(L/N)` — same-lineage clustering sets the effective dependence range.*

Proof: (i) structural; (ii-a) slot independence; (ii-b) the truncation argument with the exponential closed form; (iii) the coupling sketch above; (iv) heuristic, verified as a measured collapse. Receipt `j5` (8/8) carries every face: same-lineage concordance exact; the iid pair-budget engine at `L(L−1)` with the *windowed census's length bias measured and disclosed* (the first draft gated the census on the ideal value and failed — the receipt caught the straddle-truncation bias, which is itself the `(ii)` `E[SL]` edge term); the pooled cross-sign zero within 4 SEM at every `L` — a bound on the aggregate, *disclosed as underpowered* against the `O(L/N)` same-slot term; the term itself **resolved** by the two repaired checks: the lag-1 closed form matched within 4 SEM at every `L` (`−0.333/−0.603/−0.779` vs `−0.3333/−0.6000/−0.7778` at `L = 2/4/8`), and the aggregate detected directionally at 800 replicas (mean cross-sign `−0.0070 ± 0.0017`, `z = −4.1`); the stationary mean equal to the exact `SL/C(N,2)` value within 4 SEM at every grid point with the asymptotic form printed beside it; the scaled transient `Δτ·N/(LM)` collapsing to `0.3–0.7` across a `4×` grid; and `SD(τ)/√(L/N)` within a factor `1.8`.

**Theorem B (volume washout, weak form).** *For the same process, the canonical rank embedding's star discrepancy `D* → 0` almost surely at fixed `(L, M)`: the churn supply is asymptotically volume-faithful.* Proof sketch, two steps (the rank embedding is self-normalizing, so the box indicators are not stationary observables and the ergodic theorem cannot be applied to them directly): (1) for a *fixed* value level and time fraction, the block average of the level indicators converges by the ergodic theorem (regenerative ⇒ ergodic); (2) the empirical quantile function converges uniformly (Glivenko–Cantelli along the regenerative structure), so the self-normalized boxes inherit the limit by plug-in; uniformity over the two-parameter box family then follows from monotonicity on a grid — no empirical-process machinery needed. The *rate* — hence the ratio to the same-`N` faithful band — is not claimed; measured: the ratio stays bounded and band-adjacent (`1.35× → 1.01×` from `N = 1024 → 4096` at `L = 2` in `j5`; the `j3`/`j4` plateau `1.1–1.5×` is the finite-`N` face). The sharp constant is a named open.
