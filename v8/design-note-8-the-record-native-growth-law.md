## 1. What the corpus already forces, and where the freedom actually lives

Every prior placement-shaped object in the corpus was a *configuration* (a supply family laid down, an order scored, an equilibrium landscape searched). A growth law is a different kind of object: a map from the committed history to the next move. The corpus constrains that map more tightly than a naive reading suggests, and the note's first job is the enumeration — the f7 discipline (design note 7): pin each clause to its source, so the freedom that remains is *named*, not assumed.

**C1 — the clock is not free [DERIVED — paper 1 §4.3; paper 16 §2 F1].** The seal fires on the per-lineage evidence clock `I ~ Exp(1)` with `λ = 1` fixed by exact self-accounting (`−log S(I) = I`; shape closed at Poisson, `r = 1`, v6 Paper 4 §71). There is no room for per-chain rate heterogeneity, and under the independence refinement of paper 13's posit 1 the committer is uniform on the live fleet. **Any growth law whose history-dependence enters through the firing rates is corpus-illegal.** This kills the most obvious design (a "preferential firing" law) before it is written.

**C2 — the content is fixed up to the spread [DERIVED + f7].** Per mode the committed content is the §4.3 atom; the realized spread `w` is [PHYSICAL], generically nonzero (the magnitude no-go, ledger #35: no corpus principle pins `w = 0`; the precision map `w = 4.570·δp`). The growth law does not get to choose increment statistics beyond the Route B (dense, continuous exponential) / Route C (sparse + spread) readings already priced in paper 16 §5 — both in-cell statically.

**C3 — birth is a `χ`-reset [DEFINITIONAL + measured — paper 16 §2 F3, receipt j3].** The odometer is per-chain by definition; the inherit fork is refuted (`6.3–6.6×` flat at every `L`). Not revisited here.

**C4 — the freedom: the web-building moves [OPEN — paper 1 §7 item 3, verbatim].** The corpus is silent on branching statistics: *which lineages die, when, how many are born, and where they attach*. In the churn model this silence was parameterized by one exogenous number (the mean lifetime `L`); paper 16 §9 lists `L` as [PHYSICAL] input. **The growth law's entire history-dependence budget lives here.** A law in the class is a rule

> `Φ: (committed web `W_t`) → (move distribution over {kill lineage ℓ, spawn lineage at parent p, no-op})`

evaluated at seal events only — moves are defined seal-to-seal, never between commits. This is the indivisibility discipline stated at the record grain (f6's Ω): between-commit structure is regime data, not law data, and the law class is written regime-agnostically so that Routes B and C both instantiate it. Under a growth law, `L` stops being an input and becomes an *output* — the realized lifetime distribution is a measurement (§4, gate G3).

**C5 — Bell causality is not imposed [normative guardrail — paper 1 §7 item 2].** *"Locality must never be imposed on the Tier-A correlation structure — only the emergent Tier-B dynamics is local, and only as no-signaling."* This is the structural divergence from Rideout–Sorkin classical sequential growth, and it should be stated as loudly as the constraints above: RS derive their law class from discrete general covariance **+ Bell causality**, and that class (transitive percolation and its generalizations) is exactly the one that has never reached manifoldlike orders. The corpus *forbids* importing the second axiom. The record-native growth class is therefore strictly larger than the CSG class by construction, and the extra room is not a liberty taken — it is a Tier-A guardrail obeyed. Whether the extra room is *where manifoldlikeness lives* is the measured question of gate G1.

**C6 — covariance is a fork, not an axiom [posit-level].** RS's other axiom, label invariance, is also not obviously corpus-forced: the commit index `b` is physical in the record ontology (it *is* the evidence-clock sequencing, posit 1), so a `Φ` that reads commit ranks is not automatically illegitimate. But the induced measure on unlabeled orders is the object the manifoldlikeness gate scores, and label-dependent laws carry `e(P)`-class multiplicity factors with a known sting: paper 7's census measured the *history measure anti-selecting* (KR-type share `0.2405 → 0.3424` at `n = 7`). Pre-registered fork: **V-cov** (Φ order-invariant) vs **V-lab** (Φ reads labels), both run, the induced-measure accounting disclosed per arm.

**C7 — the measure position [structural, the licensed path].** Paper 7 Thm 4.1/Cor 4.2 closes counting base measures and extensive actions (super-extensive `Ω(n²)` log-weight demanded); paper 11 reduced the arena to `e^{O(n log² n)}` at explicit couplings (`β* = 16 ln 2` for dense violators, `β_n = 8 ln n` for the two-layer aggregate) with the arena's interior [OPEN]. **A growth measure is a dynamical measure — explicitly outside Cor 4.2's hypothesis class (paper 7's own scoping clause).** The program has so far fought the measure problem only inside the arena where its own no-go applies. This note relocates it: the measure over orders is *induced* by the growth law, suppression of the two-layer bulk is to be *checked* rather than engineered (growth builds height monotonically; the free-bipartite bulk is plausibly unreachable at any β — a theorem-shaped target, §5), and the residual question becomes growth-law selection, not weight design.

## 2. The β-family: one class, both versions

Fix a **move functional** `Φ(W_t, m)` scoring candidate moves `m` on the committed web, and define the placement law as Gibbs over moves:

> `P(m | W_t) ∝ exp(−β·Φ(W_t, m))`.

- **β = 0** — the structureless endpoint: uniform moves recover the churn class `W(L, reset)` with exogenous rates; paper 16's in-cell corner (`L = 2`, quasi-continuous content) is this endpoint's positive control.
- **0 < β < ∞** — the **probabilistic version**: the next click's placement is distributed according to the full history through `Φ`. Non-Markovian in general (Φ may read the whole web, the odometers, and any `χ`-decorations); Markov sub-class = Φ reads only the live frontier — a pre-registered ablation, since RS-style growth is frontier-Markov and its manifoldlikeness failure may be *tied* to that.
- **β = ∞ (D1)** — the **finite/deterministic-placement version**: next move = argmax Φ; the placement is fully determined by the committed history; residual randomness only in the race (C1) and the spread `w` (C2).

**D2 — the fully deterministic click law (a posit swap, flagged).** "Next click fully determined by full history" in the strongest sense also determines *which lineage commits*, replacing the independence refinement of posit 1 with a history-functional committer. This is not automatically corpus-legal: C1's derivation fixes the per-lineage evidence marginal at `Exp(1)`, and a deterministic committer-given-history must still reproduce that marginal along every lineage. Whether any D2 candidate can is itself a question this note elevates: **if the self-accounting derivation already excludes deterministic sequencing, the corpus's click law is irreducibly stochastic by its own arithmetic** — a mini-theorem candidate worth a proof attempt before any D2 code (the KS gate in §4 is the measured fallback). Either outcome is content: a proof is a foundations result (the Barandes-aligned law *cannot* be secretly deterministic at the sequencing layer); a passing D2 candidate is the strongest possible input to the randomness question (G2).

## 3. Candidate functionals, ranked

**(a) The misfit increment [primary — corpus-derived, order-only].** `Φ(W, m) = ΔS` under paper 11's combined action `S = S_r + ¼·#{related pairs, |I| < m₀}` (`m₀ = 8`), computed on the two-clock order after the candidate move. Rationale: the action came *from* the backwards ledger (faithfulness ⇒ interval balance + interval abundance), not from tuning; its equilibrium failure is measured and specific (the dense third-class attractor, `r ≈ 0.78`, j2/k4 — an action-value success and a landscape/kernel failure), which makes irreversible growth the sharpest available probe of whether the failure was the *sampling mode's*. The attractor is the pre-registered benchmark to beat.

**(b) The abundance-extended increment [secondary].** (a) plus the interval-abundance term paper 13 §3 named as the next axis — priced statically at `2.7×` against the attractor (k4) but "pricing without steering"; growth is the steering-mode retest.

**(c) Trivial Φ [control].** The β-independent arm = the churn baseline; must reproduce paper 16's cell membership or the pipeline is broken.

The `Φ` grid is deliberately small — the note's bet is on the *sampling mode* (irreversible growth vs equilibrium), not on functional search.

## 4. The falsifiable ladder (gates set now, measured later)

All geometric verdicts obey the onset discipline (paper 8): nothing below `N* = 128` (M², dimension axes), primary runs at `N ∈ {512, 2048}`; the link-term crossover (comparable magnitudes below `n ~ 10³`, paper 11 §3.3) disclosed beside every action-selection claim. Every arm carries the **β = 0 same-kernel control** (the j2 lesson: the kernel's bias must be attributed before the law's selection is claimed).

- **G0 — positive control.** Trivial-Φ churn corner at `N = 2048` reproduces paper 16 §4's membership (`1.2–1.5×` band-adjacent, three layers). Fails ⇒ stop.
- **G1 — growth vs equilibrium [the central bet].** Φ = (a), β-grid `{0, β*/4, β*, 4β*, ∞}`, V-cov arm primary. Gate, two-sided: the growth endpoints' classification against the measured attractor — **escape** = in-cell at `N = 2048` (all three layers) with endpoint `r` inside the sprinkling class, **capture** = third-class endpoints (`r ≥ 0.7`, `dim ≤ 2` NO) reproducing j2's compromise, **third outcome** = an RS-type pathology (originary/dust screens: height and width trajectories logged per run, gates pinned at first execution from the β = 0 control's own trajectory — disclosed then, not fitted later). Any of the three is a finding; "escape" flips the placement sector's default the way paper 16 flipped the supply's.
- **G2 — the determinism axis.** D1 (β = ∞) × `w ∈ {0, 0.5%, 10%}`; D2 if any candidate passes the corpus-legality KS gate (per-lineage inter-commit evidence vs `Exp(1)`; pinned `p ≥ 0.01` at `10⁴` commits, power analysis attached per the #36 M1 lesson — an exactness-adjacent claim never rides an unpowered gate). Pre-registered conjecture, both directions live: **deterministic placement with `w = 0` fails the dispersion layer** (bounded away from Poisson — the lattice precedent: volume-pass, statistical-fail `z = 5.2` sub-Poisson, paper 12), i.e. irreducible click randomness is *necessary* for statistical spacetime; the refutation arm is a chaotic argmax reaching `z ≤ 2` at `N = 2048`, which would demote the Born layer's randomness to geometrically invisible and hand the statistical work to `w` (consistent with Route C's fragility). Either verdict is a headline; neither is assumed.
- **G3 — endogenous lifetime.** Under Φ-driven death, measure the realized lifetime law and its `L_eff`; gate: does the law *select* the fleet-renewal region (`L·M/N` small) that paper 16 identified as the cell's neighborhood, or must it be imposed? Selection here would discharge one of the two [PHYSICAL] inputs paper 16 §9 left standing.
- **G4 — the finite census.** Exact small-`n` accounting of the induced growth measure (`n ≤ 7`, riding g3/pE's enumeration): growth-reachable orders, their weights under each arm, the two-layer bulk's induced weight, and the KR-type share — the direct successor of paper 7's history-measure census, now for a *law* instead of a counting convention. The theorem-shaped target: two-layer bulk weight `→ 0` under any `β ≥ 0` growth in the class (height monotonicity), turning C7's "plausibly unreachable" into a statement.

## 5. Kill conditions and known risks

1. **Kernel confound (the j2 inversion).** The β = 0 growth kernel may itself densify or thin; all selection claims are relative to the same-kernel control, and the kernel's move menu (kill/spawn/no-op proportions) is disclosed as a design choice, ablated once.
2. **Onset mirage.** In-cell verdicts below the link-term crossover probe small-`n` minimizers, not asymptotic selection (paper 13 §3's scale disclosure, kept verbatim). No asymptotic language below `N = 2048`.
3. **Induced-measure bookkeeping.** V-lab arms carry linear-extension multiplicities; the g3 identity (`Σ e(P)·n!/|Aut| = n!·A006455(n)`) is the census's consistency check. A V-lab in-cell verdict that a V-cov arm refuses localizes the effect to labeling — reported as such, not averaged away.
4. **Deterministic pseudo-randomness.** A chaotic D1/D2 arm passing dispersion refutes the G2 conjecture; that is the design working, not failing. The conjecture's proof attempt (if made) must carry hypotheses that exclude the chaotic route explicitly (finite generative state, equivariance) or it is not the measured statement.
5. **The honest failure mode.** If G1 returns "capture" at every β and Φ, the equilibrium attractor is a landscape property that irreversibility does not cure — the placement wall then stands on the functional, the redirect is the abundance axis (b) and, past it, the retirement of the action route with the coupling route (design note 1's L3, now on a grown web) as the surviving line.

## 6. What this note does not claim

No growth law is derived here — the class is *bounded* (C1–C7) and the freedom named (C4); which law nature runs is placement-sector physics, exactly as open after this note as before. The 3+1 face is untouched by design: every gate above is 2D, where paper 12 makes the verdict instrument theorem-grade; grown webs feed the coupling program (note 1, receipts n1–n3) only after G0–G1 report. The regime realized (dense/sparse) remains f6's open; the class is written so both readings instantiate it.
