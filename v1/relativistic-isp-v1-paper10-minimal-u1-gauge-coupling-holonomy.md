## Abstract

Phase 9 is the first genuine gauge benchmark for the relativistic ISP program. From the Barandes point of view, it should begin at the level of exact primitive kernels, not with a covariant continuum slogan detached from the transition law. We therefore study static lattice $U(1)$ minimal coupling in the localized finite-deformation framework already developed for the free one-particle model.

The exact results are fivefold. First, primitive slab kernels, localized comparison maps, and exchange defects are exactly gauge invariant under local lattice $U(1)$ transformations. Second, on a periodic ring the entire exact kernel family depends only on the gauge holonomy $W=e^{-iq\Phi}$; any two static link fields with the same holonomy are gauge equivalent, and every gauge class has a one-bond twisted representative. Third, the first localized exchange coefficients can already be computed exactly for arbitrary static link field: the singleton $d=1$ and $d=2$ floor coefficients are identical to the free ones, so nonzero flux does not yet enter at that local $\Delta^4$ order. Fourth, on the even ring and in the one-bond twisted representative, the first order at which holonomy enters can also be located exactly: for the adjacent singleton pair that straddles the twisted bond, the first holonomy-dependent exchange contribution appears at order $\Delta^{L+2}$, and its adjacent opposite-spin entry is generated by interference between the direct cross-twist path and the complementary once-around path. Fifth, the gauge-dressed bond-centered strip density built from Peierls-dressed bond-flip and two-step strips has the exact continuum action
$$
K_\parallel^A[\beta]=\Bigl(\beta D_x+\frac12\partial_x\beta\Bigr)(I-\alpha),
\qquad
D_x=\partial_x-iqA_x,
$$
on smooth sampled profiles. This does not yet close the full higher-order gauge-coupled exchange algebra. But at the exact static lattice $U(1)$ scope proved here, it does close phase 9 as the first genuine gauge benchmark in the relativistic ISP sequence. What remains for later work is the rest of the wrapped order-$\Delta^{L+2}$ block and its bond-centered reduction, together with the broader higher-order gauge-dressed strip algebra.

Scope note. The exact theorem-level content of this paper is the $\Gamma$-level gauge invariance of primitive slab data, the exact reduction of static ring gauge classes to holonomy, the exact singleton $d=1$ and $d=2$ floor coefficients at arbitrary static flux, the exact first even-ring twist-straddling holonomy-sensitive adjacent entry at order $\Delta^{L+2}$ together with its onset order, and the exact continuum action of the displayed gauge-dressed bond-centered density. The wrapped $\Delta^{L+2}$ term is finite-ring data and should not be read as a volume-uniform large-ring theorem. At that exact static lattice $U(1)$ scope, phase 9 is complete as a genuine gauge benchmark. The still-uncomputed part is the rest of the wrapped higher-order block and its bond-centered reduction, together with the broader higher-order gauge-dressed strip algebra.

## Introduction

The earlier free-model phase papers deliberately postponed gauge coupling. That delay was methodologically necessary. Before one asks how a gauge field enters the relativistic ISP exchange law, one must know what the free exchange law itself is, which continuum object survives the thin-slab limit, and which microscopic differences are genuine rather than regulator artifacts. The present stack now answers those one-particle questions through the first explicit non-leading regulator comparison, and phase 8 has lifted the conservative number-preserving part to finite Fock truncations.

So phase 9 can finally be formulated sharply. The right first question is not yet non-Abelian structure or gravity. It is the static lattice $U(1)$ benchmark. In particular: what is exactly gauge invariant at the primitive $\Gamma$ level, what is the first global gauge datum that cannot be removed by local rephasing, and what bond-centered continuum operator should the gauge-coupled exchange algebra reduce to if the explicit coefficient calculation succeeds?

This question has to be handled with care because two distinct gauge notions are in play. Barandes’ foundational papers identify a broad Schur–Hadamard gauge freedom of a chosen time-evolution lift $\Theta$ with fixed $\Gamma=|\Theta|^2$. The present paper concerns something different: physical lattice $U(1)$ gauge transformations acting on minimally coupled Hamiltonians and therefore on the exact primitive slab kernels built from them. The two notions are compatible, but they live at different levels of the formalism.

The same methodological caution also fixes the present paper’s upper scope. No non-Abelian extension is claimed here. At the exact level proved below, the gauge data reduce to commuting Peierls phases and to the single ring-holonomy invariant $W=e^{-iq\Phi}$. A genuine non-Abelian analogue would require new primitive-kernel control of path-ordered Wilson-line data, together with the corresponding matrix-valued wrapped exchange coefficients. None of that structure is derived in this paper, so from the Barandes point of view it should not be smuggled into phase 9 by extrapolation.

**Main results (informal).**

1. *Exact primitive gauge invariance.* Static lattice $U(1)$ gauge transformations leave primitive slab kernels, localized comparison maps, and exchange defects exactly unchanged.
2. *Exact holonomy reduction on the ring.* On a periodic ring, two static link fields define the same primitive kernel family if and only if they have the same holonomy; every gauge class has a one-bond twisted representative.
3. *Exact local nonzero-flux floor coefficients.* The singleton $d=1$ and $d=2$ $\Delta^4$ exchange coefficients are computed exactly for arbitrary static link field and are holonomy-blind.
4. *Exact first wrapped holonomy-sensitive singleton entry on the even ring.* For the adjacent singleton pair that straddles the twisted bond, the first holonomy-dependent exchange contribution occurs at order $\Delta^{L+2}$ and its adjacent opposite-spin entry is computed exactly.
5. *Exact covariant bond-centered benchmark.* The gauge-dressed bond-centered strip density has continuum action $(\beta D_x+\tfrac12\partial_x\beta)(I-\alpha)$ on smooth sampled profiles, identifying the correct gauge-covariant thin-slab target.

Strategic note. The paper does not claim the full holonomy-sensitive higher-order gauge-coupled algebra. It isolates the exact gauge-invariant kernel structure first, computes the local floor terms and the first wrapped even-ring coefficient that really can be computed now, and states plainly what remains.

## Static lattice U(1) minimal coupling at the primitive-kernel level

**Definition 1**

(Static lattice minimal coupling).

Let $A_{n+1/2}\in\mathbb R$ be a static link field on the one-dimensional periodic lattice, and let $q$ be a charge. Define the Peierls link variable
$$
U_{n+1/2}:=e^{-iqaA_{n+1/2}}.
$$
The minimally coupled lattice Dirac Hamiltonian is
$$
H_D[A]
=
m\sum_n \beta_n
-\frac{i}{2a}\sum_{n,s}
\Bigl(
U_{n+1/2}\,|n+1,\bar s\rangle\langle n,s|
-U_{n+1/2}^{-1}\,|n,s\rangle\langle n+1,\bar s|
\Bigr).
$$
For a support region $R$, define the coupled collar Hamiltonian $C_R[A]$ by replacing the free kinetic bonds in the collar term with their minimally coupled versions, and set
$$
B_R[A]:=H_D[A]-C_R[A].
$$
The primitive slab kernels are
$$
\Gamma_0[A](\Delta):=\Gamma(e^{-i\Delta H_D[A]}),
\qquad
\Gamma_R[A](\Delta):=\Gamma(e^{-i\Delta B_R[A]}),
$$
with comparison map
$$
J_R[A](\Delta):=\Gamma_R[A](\Delta)\Gamma_0[A](\Delta)^{-1}
$$
and exchange defect
$$
E_{R,S}[A](\Delta):=
J_R[A](\Delta)J_S[A](\Delta)J_R[A](\Delta)^{-1}J_S[A](\Delta)^{-1}.
$$

**Definition 2**

(Local lattice gauge transformation).

For a site phase function $\chi_n$, let
$$
G_\chi|n,s\rangle:=e^{iq\chi_n}|n,s\rangle.
$$
The corresponding local gauge transformation of the link field is
$$
A_{n+1/2}\longmapsto A_{n+1/2}^\chi
:=
A_{n+1/2}+\frac{\chi_{n+1}-\chi_n}{a},
$$
or equivalently
$$
U_{n+1/2}\longmapsto U_{n+1/2}^\chi
=
e^{-iq\chi_{n+1}}U_{n+1/2}e^{iq\chi_n}.
$$

**Proposition 1**

($\Gamma$-level gauge invariance of the primitive slab data).

Under the gauge transformation of Definition 2 one has
$$
H_D[A^\chi]=G_\chi H_D[A]G_\chi^{-1},
\qquad
B_R[A^\chi]=G_\chi B_R[A]G_\chi^{-1}.
$$
Consequently,
$$
\Gamma_0[A^\chi](\Delta)=\Gamma_0[A](\Delta),
\qquad
\Gamma_R[A^\chi](\Delta)=\Gamma_R[A](\Delta),
$$
and therefore
$$
J_R[A^\chi](\Delta)=J_R[A](\Delta),
\qquad
E_{R,S}[A^\chi](\Delta)=E_{R,S}[A](\Delta).
$$

Proof.

The Peierls-substitution transformation law gives
$$
H_D[A^\chi]=G_\chi H_D[A]G_\chi^{-1}.
$$
Because $C_R[A]$ is obtained from the local kinetic bonds and mass terms by the same minimal-coupling replacement, the same conjugation law holds for $C_R[A]$ and hence for $B_R[A]$. Exponentiating yields
$$
e^{-i\Delta H_D[A^\chi]}
=
G_\chi e^{-i\Delta H_D[A]}G_\chi^{-1},
\qquad
e^{-i\Delta B_R[A^\chi]}
=
G_\chi e^{-i\Delta B_R[A]}G_\chi^{-1}.
$$
In the configuration basis,
$$
\bigl(G_\chi U G_\chi^{-1}\bigr)_{AB}
=
e^{iq(\chi_A-\chi_B)}U_{AB},
$$
so entrywise moduli are unchanged:
$$
\bigl|\bigl(G_\chi U G_\chi^{-1}\bigr)_{AB}\bigr|^2=|U_{AB}|^2.
$$
Hence the primitive kernels are invariant. The comparison maps and exchange defects are built algebraically from those kernels, so they are invariant as well.

$ \square $

Remark.

This physical lattice $U(1)$ gauge invariance is distinct from the Schur–Hadamard gauge freedom of a chosen time-evolution lift $\Theta$ in Barandes’ correspondence papers. The former acts on the Hamiltonian input and therefore on the primitive slab kernels; the latter acts on a chosen lift with fixed $\Gamma$. They are different symmetries at different levels.

## Exact ring-holonomy reduction

On a periodic ring the first genuine global gauge datum is the holonomy. The exact phase-9 benchmark should therefore reduce the full static link-field freedom to that single invariant quantity.

**Definition 3**

(Ring holonomy).

For a periodic lattice of length $L$, define the static gauge holonomy
$$
W[A]
:=
\prod_{n=0}^{L-1}U_{n+1/2}
=
\exp(-iq\Phi[A]),
$$
where
$$
\Phi[A]:=a\sum_{n=0}^{L-1}A_{n+1/2}
$$
is the lattice flux around the ring.

**Theorem 1**

(Exact classification of static ring gauge fields by holonomy).

Let $A$ and $A'$ be two static link fields on the same periodic lattice ring. Then the following are equivalent:

1. $W[A]=W[A']$;
2. there exists a site phase function $\chi_n$ such that
  $$
  U'_{n+1/2}
  =
  e^{-iq\chi_{n+1}}U_{n+1/2}e^{iq\chi_n}
  \qquad\text{for every }n.
  $$

Consequently every exact primitive kernel, comparison map, and exchange defect constructed from the static link field depends only on the holonomy $W[A]$.

Proof.

If $A'$ is obtained from $A$ by a gauge transformation, then multiplying the transformed links around the full ring telescopes the site phases and gives
$$
W[A']=W[A].
$$
Conversely, assume $W[A]=W[A']$. Set $\chi_0=0$ and define $\chi_{n+1}$ recursively by
$$
e^{iq\chi_{n+1}}
:=
U_{n+1/2}e^{iq\chi_n}\bigl(U'_{n+1/2}\bigr)^{-1}.
$$
Then by construction
$$
U'_{n+1/2}=e^{-iq\chi_{n+1}}U_{n+1/2}e^{iq\chi_n}
$$
for each link. Because the ring is periodic,
$$
e^{iq\chi_L}
=
\prod_{n=0}^{L-1}U_{n+1/2}\bigl(U'_{n+1/2}\bigr)^{-1}
=
W[A]W[A']^{-1}
=
1,
$$
so the recursive definition is consistent with $\chi_L=\chi_0$. Thus the two link fields are gauge equivalent. The final sentence now follows from Proposition 1.

$ \square $

**Corollary 1**

(One-bond twisted representative of every ring gauge class).

Every static ring gauge class has a representative with
$$
U_{n+1/2}^{\mathrm{tw}}=1
\qquad (n=0,\dots,L-2),
\qquad
U_{L-1/2}^{\mathrm{tw}}=W[A].
$$
Hence the finite-slab Aharonov–Bohm benchmark reduces exactly to a one-bond twist carrying the entire holonomy.

Remark.

This is the exact global content of the static gauge problem. Once the one-bond twisted representative is fixed, every remaining gauge-invariant statement about the primitive slab kernels is a statement about holonomy, not about the local distribution of the link phases.

## The gauge-dressed bond-centered strip density

The free-model phase papers showed that the correct continuum object is not a site-centered matrix coefficient but a bond-centered strip density acting on smooth sampled profiles. The gauge benchmark should therefore identify the gauge-dressed version of that density directly.

**Definition 4**

(Gauge-dressed strip operators and bond-centered density).

Define the gauge-dressed bond-flip and two-step strip operators by
$$
F_{n+1/2}^A
:=
\sum_{s}
\Bigl(
U_{n+1/2}|n+1,\bar s\rangle\langle n,s|
-U_{n+1/2}^{-1}|n,s\rangle\langle n+1,\bar s|
\Bigr),
$$
$$
S_{n,n+2}^A
:=
\sum_s
\Bigl(
U_{n+1/2}U_{n+3/2}|n+2,s\rangle\langle n,s|
-U_{n+1/2}^{-1}U_{n+3/2}^{-1}|n,s\rangle\langle n+2,s|
\Bigr).
$$
Set
$$
\tau_{n+1/2}^{A,(a)}
:=
\frac{1}{2a}F_{n+1/2}^A
+\frac{1}{8a}\bigl(S_{n-1,n+1}^A+S_{n,n+2}^A\bigr),
$$
and for a smooth compactly supported profile $\beta$ define the smeared bond-centered density
$$
\mathcal T_a^A[\beta]
:=
\sum_n \beta_{n+1/2}\,\tau_{n+1/2}^{A,(a)}.
$$

**Proposition 2**

(Exact covariant continuum action of the gauge-dressed bond-centered density).

Let $\phi=(u,d)\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R,\mathbb C^2)$ and sample the continuum gauge potential at bond midpoints:
$$
A_{n+1/2}=A_x(x_{n+1/2}).
$$
Then
$$
\mathcal T_a^A[\beta]\iota_a\phi
=
\iota_a\bigl(K_\parallel^A[\beta]\phi\bigr)+O(a^2)
$$
uniformly on compact sets, where
$$
K_\parallel^A[\beta]
:=
\Bigl(\beta D_x+\frac12\partial_x\beta\Bigr)(I-\alpha),
\qquad
D_x:=\partial_x-iqA_x.
$$

Proof.

Expand the link phases at bond midpoints:
$$
U_{n+1/2}=e^{-iqaA_{n+1/2}}=1-iqaA_{n+1/2}+O(a^2).
$$
The bond-flip part of $\mathcal T_a^A[\beta]$ produces one-step covariant differences. For example, in the lower component one obtains terms of the form
$$
\frac{\beta_{n-1/2}}{2a}U_{n-1/2}^{-1}d_{n-1}
-\frac{\beta_{n+1/2}}{2a}U_{n+1/2}d_{n+1},
$$
which are the midpoint discretization of
$$
-(\beta D_x+\tfrac12\partial_x\beta)d(x_n)+O(a^2).
$$
The two-step strip terms similarly yield the same-spin centered covariant difference
$$
\frac{\beta_{n+1/2}+\beta_{n+3/2}}{8a}U_{n+1/2}U_{n+3/2}u_{n+2}
-\frac{\beta_{n-3/2}+\beta_{n-1/2}}{8a}U_{n-3/2}^{-1}U_{n-1/2}^{-1}u_{n-2},
$$
which is the midpoint discretization of
$$
(\beta D_x+\tfrac12\partial_x\beta)u(x_n)+O(a^2).
$$
Combining the same-spin and opposite-spin pieces gives exactly the matrix action of $(I-\alpha)$, now with $D_x$ in place of $\partial_x$.

$ \square $

**Corollary 2**

(Continuum gauge covariance of the bond-centered benchmark).

Under the continuum gauge transformation
$$
\phi\longmapsto e^{iq\chi}\phi,
\qquad
A_x\longmapsto A_x+\partial_x\chi,
$$
one has
$$
K_\parallel^{A+\partial_x\chi}[\beta](e^{iq\chi}\phi)
=
e^{iq\chi}K_\parallel^A[\beta]\phi.
$$

Proof.

This is the standard covariance of the gauge-covariant derivative
$$
D_x=\partial_x-iqA_x.
$$
Since $\beta$ is a scalar profile built from the deformation smearing functions rather than a charged matter field, the prefactor $\beta\partial_x+\tfrac12\partial_x\beta$ transforms exactly as displayed.

$ \square $

### Exact leading localized coefficients at nonzero flux

Before one asks for the full gauge-dressed bond-centered reduction of the exchange algebra, one can already compute the first localized exchange coefficients themselves. The key point is that the leading one-region coefficient depends only on the moduli of the minimally coupled nearest-neighbor matrix elements. So there is a real gauge-coupled calculation to do even at nonzero flux, but the answer at the local $\Delta^4$ floor order is flux-blind.

**Proposition 3**

(Exact leading one-region coefficient under static $U(1)$ coupling).

For singleton support $R=\{n_0\}$, write
$$
J_R[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^2A_R^{(1)}[A]+O(\Delta^4).
$$
Then $A_R^{(1)}[A]$ is independent of the static link field $A$, the charge $q$, and the holonomy $W[A]$. Its nonzero entries are exactly
$$
[A_R^{(1)}[A]]_{(n_0,s),(n_0,s)}=+\frac{1}{2a^2},
\qquad
[A_R^{(1)}[A]]_{(n_0\pm1,\bar s),(n_0,s)}=-\frac{1}{4a^2},
$$
$$
[A_R^{(1)}[A]]_{(n_0,s),(n_0\pm1,\bar s)}=-\frac{1}{4a^2},
\qquad
[A_R^{(1)}[A]]_{(n_0\pm1,s),(n_0\pm1,s)}=+\frac{1}{4a^2},
$$
with all other entries zero. Hence the exact leading localized comparison coefficient is identical to the free collar-excision coefficient.

Proof.

Write
$$
\Gamma_0[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^2L_0[A]+O(\Delta^4),
\qquad
\Gamma_R[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^2L_R[A]+O(\Delta^4).
$$
Then
$$
A_R^{(1)}[A]=L_R[A]-L_0[A].
$$
For any Hermitian Hamiltonian $H$, the order-$\Delta^2$ kernel coefficient of $\Gamma(e^{-i\Delta H})$ is $|H_{AB}|^2$ off diagonal, with diagonal entries given by minus the corresponding column sum. In the minimally coupled lattice Dirac Hamiltonian every present nearest-neighbor spin-flip bond has modulus $1/(2a)$ and every absent collar bond has modulus $0$, regardless of the Peierls phase. The mass term is diagonal and therefore does not enter this coefficient. So $L_0[A]$ and $L_R[A]$ depend only on which bonds are present, not on the link phases themselves. Evaluating the resulting entries gives exactly the displayed free-model values.

$ \square $

**Proposition 4**

(Exact leading gauge-coupled singleton exchange coefficients at nonzero flux).

Let $R=\{n\}$ and let $S=\{n+1\}$ or $S=\{n+2\}$ on the periodic lattice ring. For every static link field $A$ one has
$$
E_{R,S}[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^4C_{R,S}^{(2)}[A]+O(\Delta^6),
\qquad
C_{R,S}^{(2)}[A]=[A_R^{(1)}[A],A_S^{(1)}[A]].
$$
Moreover:

1. If $S=\{n+1\}$, then
  $$
  [C_{n,n+1}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n,\uparrow),(n+1,\downarrow)}=-\frac{1}{8a^4},
  \qquad
  [C_{n,n+1}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n+1,\downarrow),(n,\uparrow)}=+\frac{1}{8a^4},
  $$
  together with the spin-flipped partner
  $$
  [C_{n,n+1}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n,\downarrow),(n+1,\uparrow)}=-\frac{1}{8a^4},
  \qquad
  [C_{n,n+1}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n+1,\uparrow),(n,\downarrow)}=+\frac{1}{8a^4}.
  $$
  All diagonal entries vanish, same-spin nearest-neighbor entries vanish, and the support is confined to $\{n-1,n,n+1,n+2\}$.
2. If $S=\{n+2\}$, then
  $$
  [C_{n,n+2}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n,s),(n+2,s)}=+\frac{1}{16a^4},
  \qquad
  [C_{n,n+2}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n+2,s),(n,s)}=-\frac{1}{16a^4}
  $$
  for $s\in\{\uparrow,\downarrow\}$. Opposite-spin entries vanish, and the support is confined to $\{n,n+1,n+2\}$.

These formulas hold unchanged even when the ring holonomy is nontrivial, $W[A]\neq 1$.

Proof.

From
$$
J_R[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^2A_R^{(1)}[A]+O(\Delta^4),
\qquad
J_S[A](\Delta)=I+\Delta^2A_S^{(1)}[A]+O(\Delta^4),
$$
the group-commutator expansion gives
$$
E_{R,S}[A](\Delta)=J_R[A]J_S[A]J_R[A]^{-1}J_S[A]^{-1}
=I+\Delta^4[A_R^{(1)}[A],A_S^{(1)}[A]]+O(\Delta^6).
$$
By Proposition 3 the coefficients $A_R^{(1)}[A]$ and $A_S^{(1)}[A]$ are exactly the free ones, so the leading exchange coefficient is computed by the same finite commutator algebra as in the free collar-excision case.

For $S=\{n+1\}$, the representative entry is
$$
[C_{n,n+1}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n,\uparrow),(n+1,\downarrow)}
=
\Bigl(\frac{1}{2a^2}\Bigr)\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)
+\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{1}{2a^2}\Bigr)
-\Bigl(\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)
-\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)\Bigl(\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)
=-\frac{1}{8a^4},
$$
and the transposed and spin-flipped entries follow by antisymmetry and spin symmetry. Same-spin nearest-neighbor entries vanish because no allowed product of the displayed first-order singleton coefficients reaches them.

For $S=\{n+2\}$, the representative same-spin entry is
$$
[C_{n,n+2}^{(2)}[A]]_{(n,\uparrow),(n+2,\uparrow)}
=
\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)\Bigl(-\frac{1}{4a^2}\Bigr)
=\frac{1}{16a^4},
$$
with the reverse entry given by antisymmetry. The opposite-spin distance-two entries vanish because the first-order singleton coefficients only connect opposite spins across one bond. The support bounds follow from the one-step support of $A_R^{(1)}[A]$ and $A_S^{(1)}[A]$. Since Proposition 3 is exact for every static link field, the displayed coefficients hold unchanged at nonzero flux.

$ \square $

Remark.

Proposition 4 is the first explicit gauge-coupled localized exchange-coefficient calculation in the phase-9 paper. It is exact, gauge invariant, and valid at arbitrary static flux, but it does not yet detect holonomy. The reason is local: at the $\Delta^4$ floor order every contributing term depends on the Peierls factors only through moduli or through bond/inverse-bond cancellation. The next question is therefore where the first genuinely wrapped ring contribution enters.

### The first wrapped holonomy-sensitive coefficient on the even ring

To see the first place where holonomy really enters the localized exchange algebra, one should pass to the one-bond twisted representative of Corollary 1 and place the two singleton supports across the twisted bond itself. Then the local region Hamiltonians cut the ring open, so the only surviving holonomy dependence comes from the wrapped part of the reference kernel $\Gamma_0[W]$.

**Proposition 5**

(First holonomy-sensitive adjacent singleton coefficient on the even twisted ring).

Assume that the ring length $L$ is even and work in the one-bond twisted representative
$$
U_{n+1/2}=1 \qquad (n=0,\dots,L-2),
\qquad
U_{L-1/2}=W.
$$
Let
$$
R=\{L-1\},
\qquad
S=\{0\}.
$$
Then no coefficient of $E_{R,S}[W](\Delta)$ below order $\Delta^{L+2}$ depends on $W$. More precisely,
$$
E_{R,S}[W](\Delta)
=
I+\Delta^4C_{R,S}^{(2)}
+\Delta^{L+2}\Bigl(C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{loc}}+C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]\Bigr)
+O(\Delta^{L+4}),
$$
where $C_{R,S}^{(2)}$ and $C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{loc}}$ are independent of $W$. The holonomy-dependent part satisfies
$$
\bigl[C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{(L-1,\uparrow),(0,\downarrow)}
=
\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^{L+1}a^{L+2}}
\left(
\frac{1}{(L-1)!}+\frac{1}{2(L-2)!}
\right),
$$
$$
\bigl[C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{(0,\downarrow),(L-1,\uparrow)}
=
-\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^{L+1}a^{L+2}}
\left(
\frac{1}{(L-1)!}+\frac{1}{2(L-2)!}
\right),
$$
with the spin-flipped partner
$$
\bigl[C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{(L-1,\downarrow),(0,\uparrow)}
=
\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^{L+1}a^{L+2}}
\left(
\frac{1}{(L-1)!}+\frac{1}{2(L-2)!}
\right)
$$
and its transpose negative.

Proof.

By Corollary 1 we may work in the displayed one-bond twisted gauge. For $R=\{L-1\}$ and $S=\{0\}$, the collar excision removes the twisted bond itself. Hence $B_R[W]$ and $B_S[W]$ live on open chains, so all remaining link phases can be gauged away completely; therefore $\Gamma_R[W]$ and $\Gamma_S[W]$ are independent of $W$ to every order. The first possible holonomy dependence in $J_R[W]$ and $J_S[W]$ must therefore come from the wrapped part of $\Gamma_0[W]^{-1}$.

Let
$$
X:=(L-1,\uparrow),
\qquad
Y:=(0,\downarrow),
\qquad
Z:=(L-2,\downarrow),
\qquad
T:=(1,\uparrow).
$$
Because $L$ is even, the first pair of amplitudes with different winding number that reaches the same channel $X\to Y$ is the direct one-step path across the twisted bond and the complementary once-around path of length $L-1$. Their contributions to $e^{-i\Delta H_D[W]}$ are
$$
[e^{-i\Delta H_D[W]}]_{Y,X}
=
-\frac{\Delta}{2a}W
+\frac{\Delta^{L-1}}{(L-1)!(2a)^{L-1}}
+O(\Delta^{L+1}),
$$
so the order-$\Delta^L$ holonomy-sensitive kernel coefficient is
$$
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{X,Y}
=
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{Y,X}
=
-\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^L(L-1)!a^L}.
$$
Likewise, in the distance-two same-spin channels $X\to T$ and $Z\to Y$, the short two-step path crossing the twisted bond interferes with the complementary path of length $L-2$. This gives
$$
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{X,T}
=
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{T,X}
=
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{Z,Y}
=
\bigl[(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{Y,Z}
=
\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2!(L-2)!(2a)^L}.
$$
No lower-order coefficient of $\Gamma_0[W]$ can depend on $W$, because before order $L$ no two amplitudes with different winding number can reach the same configuration channel.

Write
$$
M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]
:=
-\,(\Gamma_0^{(L)})_{\mathrm{hol}}[W].
$$
Then
$$
J_R[W](\Delta)
=
I+\Delta^2A_R^{(1)}+\cdots+\Delta^LM_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]+O(\Delta^{L+2}),
$$
$$
J_S[W](\Delta)
=
I+\Delta^2A_S^{(1)}+\cdots+\Delta^LM_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]+O(\Delta^{L+2}),
$$
where every omitted coefficient is $W$-independent. The group-commutator expansion therefore gives
$$
C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]
=
[A_R^{(1)},M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]
+[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W],A_S^{(1)}].
$$
Equivalently,
$$
C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]
=
[A_R^{(1)}-A_S^{(1)},M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]].
$$

From Proposition 3 one reads off the only relevant entries:
$$
[A_R^{(1)}-A_S^{(1)}]_{X,X}=+\frac{1}{4a^2},
\qquad
[A_R^{(1)}-A_S^{(1)}]_{Y,Y}=-\frac{1}{4a^2},
$$
$$
[A_R^{(1)}-A_S^{(1)}]_{X,Z}=-\frac{1}{4a^2},
\qquad
[A_R^{(1)}-A_S^{(1)}]_{T,Y}=+\frac{1}{4a^2},
$$
while
$$
[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{X,Y}
=
\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^L(L-1)!a^L},
$$
$$
[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{X,T}
=
[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{Z,Y}
=
-\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2!(L-2)!(2a)^L}.
$$
Therefore
$$
\bigl[C_{R,S}^{((L+2)/2),\mathrm{hol}}[W]\bigr]_{X,Y}
=
\frac{1}{2a^2}[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{X,Y}
-\frac{1}{4a^2}[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{Z,Y}
-\frac{1}{4a^2}[M_{\mathrm{wrap}}^{(L/2)}[W]]_{X,T},
$$
which is exactly
$$
\frac{W+W^{-1}}{2^{L+1}a^{L+2}}
\left(
\frac{1}{(L-1)!}+\frac{1}{2(L-2)!}
\right).
$$
The transposed entry is negative because the commutator is antisymmetric, and the spin-flipped partner follows from the spin symmetry of the minimally coupled lattice Dirac Hamiltonian. Since no $W$-dependent term appears in $J_R[W]$ or $J_S[W]$ before order $\Delta^L$, no exchange coefficient below order $\Delta^{L+2}$ can depend on holonomy.

$ \square $

Remark.

The displayed wrapped term depends on the ring only through the gauge invariant combination
$$
W+W^{-1}=2\cos(q\Phi).
$$
Relative to zero flux, the shift is proportional to
$$
W+W^{-1}-2=-4\sin^2\!\Bigl(\frac{q\Phi}{2}\Bigr),
$$
so Proposition 5 is the first place where the localized exchange algebra can actually distinguish nonzero flux from zero flux.

**Even-$L$ proviso.**

The proof uses interference between a direct path and its complementary once-around path in the same spin channel. That parity match holds when $L$ is even. For odd $L$, the first wrapped contribution is pushed to higher order by the Dirac parity constraint, and that odd-ring case is not computed here.
