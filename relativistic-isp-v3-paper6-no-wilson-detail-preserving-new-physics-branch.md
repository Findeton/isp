## 1. Main Question

Can the no-Wilson detail-preserving branch of relativistic ISP define a
controlled multi-taste QFT benchmark, and can it produce operational
signatures that differ from the Wilson single-species benchmark?

The paper has two targets:

1. **Conservative target.** Prove that the no-Wilson branch matches a declared
   two-taste Dirac/CAR QFT on finite test batteries.
2. **Discovery target.** Identify taste-resolving or taste-mixing tests where
   the no-Wilson branch differs from the Wilson single-species benchmark.

The second target is not automatically "new physics." It becomes a genuine
new-physics claim only if the extra taste/detail records survive the continuum
limit and couple to operationally available instruments.

## 6. Microscopic Taste-Resolving Instruments

The central instrument question is whether the retained taste label is only a
formal representation label or can be read by finite cutoff operations. The
no-Wilson branch has a natural microscopic answer: the taste label is the
two-site/block detail record of the detail-preserving staggered readout.

Let `b=2a` be the block spacing and write the two-site block field as

```math
\Psi_m
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\psi_{2m}\\
\psi_{2m+1}
\end{pmatrix},
```

after the fixed local taste-spin rotation chosen in Paper 3. On the
finite-volume finite-particle cutoff, define the block-local taste density

```math
t_{a}^{i}(m)=\Psi_m^\dagger\tau_i\Psi_m,
\qquad i=x,y,z,
```

and for `f\in C_c^\infty({\mathbb R})`,

```math
T_a^i(f)
=
b\sum_m f(bm)t_a^i(m).
```

For an angle `\theta`, set

```math
T_a^\theta(f)
=
\cos\theta\,T_a^z(f)+\sin\theta\,T_a^x(f).
```

On each fixed finite-particle sector, `T_a^i(f)` is bounded and supported on
the two-site blocks meeting `{\rm supp}(f)`.

### Theorem 6.1: Taste-Bilinear Instrument Convergence

Let the staggered smearing maps `I_a^{st}` and the fixed taste convention be
those of Paper 3. For every compactly supported smooth `f` and every finite
particle cutoff,

```math
T_a^i(f)I_a^{st}\Psi
\to
I_a^{st}T^i(f)\Psi,
```

strongly on the staggered smooth finite-particle core, where

```math
T^i(f)
=
\int f(x)\,
\psi^\dagger(x)(I_{\rm spin}\otimes\tau_i)\psi(x)\,dx.
```

Consequently finite spectral partitions of `T_a^i(f)` define microscopic
taste-resolving instruments whose effects converge to the corresponding
continuum taste effects.

### Proof

The two-site block field is exactly the finite cutoff detail-preserving
readout: no degree of freedom is discarded. Paper 3's staggered smearing
convergence identifies the block components with the two taste components of
the continuum test spinor. Therefore the Riemann sum

```math
b\sum_m f(bm)\Psi_m^\dagger\tau_i\Psi_m
```

converges strongly on every finite smooth Fock polynomial to the continuum
bilinear. Boundedness on fixed finite-particle sectors follows from

```math
\|T_a^i(f)\|_{\le N}
\le
N\|f\|_\infty\|\tau_i\|.
```

Finite spectral projections are continuous for isolated partition intervals;
for partitions whose boundaries carry no limiting spectral weight, the usual
bounded functional calculus gives convergence of effects. `square`

### Microscopic Locality Caveat

The instrument is local at the block scale `b=2a`, not at the original
single-site scale. This is exactly the ontology of the detail-preserving
readout: the retained detail record lives in the block fiber. A taste-resolving
measurement is therefore allowed only after the block/taste readout has been
declared. Without that declaration, taste remains a representation label but
not an operational observable.

## 7. Taste Preparation Control

Taste-resolving effects are not enough; the EPR-like witness also requires
taste preparation. Preparation is again declared operationally, not smuggled
into `Gamma`.

For a continuum two-taste test spinor `F=f\otimes\eta`, with
`\eta\in{\mathbb C}^2_{\rm taste}`, define the finite-cutoff creation operator

```math
a_a^\dagger(F)
=
\psi_a^\dagger(I_a^{st}F).
```

Let `f_L,f_R` be smooth compactly supported wave packets with spacelike
separated future detector regions and orthonormal one-particle limits. Let
`e_0,e_1` be the two taste basis vectors.

Define the finite-cutoff taste-singlet preparation

```math
|\Psi_{a}^{LR}\rangle
=
\frac1{\sqrt{Z_a}}
\left(
a_a^\dagger(f_L\otimes e_0)
a_a^\dagger(f_R\otimes e_1)
-
a_a^\dagger(f_L\otimes e_1)
a_a^\dagger(f_R\otimes e_0)
\right)|\Omega_a\rangle,
```

where `Z_a` normalizes the state.

### Theorem 7.1: Taste-State And Taste-Singlet Preparation

Under the staggered CAR, polarization, and smearing convergence assumptions,

```math
|\Psi_a^{LR}\rangle
\to
|\Psi^{LR}\rangle
```

in all finite local CAR matrix elements, where

```math
|\Psi^{LR}\rangle
=
\frac1{\sqrt2}
\left(
a^\dagger(f_L\otimes e_0)a^\dagger(f_R\otimes e_1)
-
a^\dagger(f_L\otimes e_1)a^\dagger(f_R\otimes e_0)
\right)|\Omega\rangle.
```

Moreover, for every finite family of taste-bilinear instruments
`T_a^{\theta_j}(f_{O_j})` with supports in the declared detector regions,
their joint outcome statistics converge to the continuum two-taste statistics.

### Proof

Paper 3 gives convergence of the staggered CAR pairings and quasi-free
vacuum/polarization on finite test families. The creation operators above are
finite CAR polynomials in smeared fields. Hence their norms, inner products,
and all finite products with local taste-bilinear effects converge by Wick/CAR
expansion. The normalization satisfies `Z_a\to2` because the two terms become
orthogonal and have unit norm. Therefore the prepared finite-cutoff state
converges in all finite local matrix elements to the continuum taste singlet.
`square`

## 8. Detector-Hardware Bridge

The previous sections define taste instruments algebraically. That is not yet
enough. A Barandes-aligned account must say what additional physical structure
turns the algebraic effect into an operational readout. The minimal bridge is a
finite local detector array coupled to the two-site block record.

For each block `m`, define the finite local Fock space `{\mathcal F}_{a,m}` of
the two modes `(2m,2m+1)` after the fixed staggered taste rotation. On a fixed
finite-particle cutoff, the three taste densities are bounded finite matrices:

```math
t_a^z(m)
=
\psi_{2m}^\dagger\psi_{2m}
-
\psi_{2m+1}^\dagger\psi_{2m+1},
```

```math
t_a^x(m)
=
\psi_{2m}^\dagger\psi_{2m+1}
+
\psi_{2m+1}^\dagger\psi_{2m},
```

```math
t_a^y(m)
=
-i\psi_{2m}^\dagger\psi_{2m+1}
+
i\psi_{2m+1}^\dagger\psi_{2m}.
```

Thus `t_a^z` is an even/odd occupancy imbalance, while `t_a^x,t_a^y` require a
phase-coherent intra-block interferometric coupling. This distinction matters:
a detector that only counts sites can at most resolve `\tau_z`; a detector that
can coherently mix the two sites can resolve arbitrary

```math
t_a^\theta(m)=\cos\theta\,t_a^z(m)+\sin\theta\,t_a^x(m)
```

in the `x-z` plane.

### Detector Model 8.1: Block-Local Taste Pointer

For a finite set of blocks `O_a`, attach to each block a finite pointer space
`{\mathcal K}_{m}` with orthogonal pointer states labelled by the spectrum of
`t_a^\theta(m)`. Let

```math
t_a^\theta(m)=\sum_{\lambda\in{\rm spec}(t_a^\theta(m))}
\lambda\,P_{m,\lambda}^{\theta}
```

be the finite spectral resolution on the local block Fock space. The ideal
block detector unitary is

```math
U_{m}^{\theta}
=
\sum_{\lambda}
P_{m,\lambda}^{\theta}\otimes V_{m,\lambda},
```

where `V_{m,\lambda}` shifts the pointer from its ready state
`|\eta_m\rangle` to a mutually orthogonal record state
`|\eta_{m,\lambda}\rangle`. The full detector over `O_a` is

```math
U_{O_a}^{\theta}=\prod_{m\in O_a}U_m^\theta.
```

The product is order-independent because the factors are even local operators
on disjoint block algebras.

For a smearing `f`, the recorded coarse variable is

```math
Y_a^{\theta}(f)
=
b\sum_{m\in O_a} f(bm)\lambda_m.
```

A finite partition `{\mathcal P}` of the real line gives detector effects

```math
E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}
=
\sum_{\{\lambda_m\}:Y_a^\theta(f)\in B}
\prod_{m\in O_a}P_{m,\lambda_m}^{\theta},
\qquad B\in{\mathcal P}.
```

These effects are local in the block collar of `{\rm supp}(f)`.

### Theorem 8.2: Detector Realization Of Taste Instruments

Fix a compact support, a smooth smearing `f`, a finite-particle cutoff, and a
partition `{\mathcal P}` whose boundary carries no limiting spectral weight.
The block-local pointer model realizes the same continuum instrument as the
algebraic spectral partition of `T_a^\theta(f)`:

```math
E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}
=
{\bf 1}_{B}(T_a^\theta(f))
```

on the finite cutoff, and

```math
E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}
\to
{\bf 1}_{B}(T^\theta(f))
```

strongly on the staggered smooth finite-particle core.

### Proof

For fixed `\theta`, the operators `t_a^\theta(m)` and `t_a^\theta(n)` commute
for `m\ne n`: they are even CAR bilinears supported on disjoint blocks. Hence
the spectral projections multiply into the joint spectral measure of the finite
sum

```math
T_a^\theta(f)=b\sum_{m\in O_a}f(bm)t_a^\theta(m).
```

The pointer unitary stores exactly the joint eigenvalue record, so coarse
graining the pointer record over `Y_a^\theta(f)\in B` gives
`{\bf 1}_B(T_a^\theta(f))`. The strong convergence to the continuum effect is
Theorem 6.1 plus bounded functional calculus for partition boundaries with zero
limiting spectral weight. `square`

### What This Bridge Does And Does Not Prove

It proves that taste readout is not merely notation: at finite cutoff it can be
implemented by local detector registers coupled to the retained two-site
record. It does not prove that a particular laboratory platform has such a
phase-coherent two-site detector. The operational assumptions are explicit:

1. block resolution at scale `2a`;
2. coherent access to the even/odd intra-block phase for `\tau_x,\tau_y`;
3. recorded detector outcomes, including rejected or unresolved outcomes;
4. no silent trace over the detail register.

If any of these fail, the no-Wilson branch may collapse operationally to the
taste-blind regime.

## 9. Taste-Resolving Causality And Foliation Control

The taste-record witness must not smuggle in superluminal influence. The finite
detector model gives a sharp local statement before the harder interacting
foliation problem is attempted in Paper 7.

Let `L_a` and `R_a` be disjoint block regions with spacelike separated continuum
collars. Let

```math
\{M_{a,r}^{L}\}_{r\in{\mathcal R}_L},
\qquad
\{M_{a,s}^{R}\}_{s\in{\mathcal R}_R}
```

be Kraus operators generated by the block-local pointer construction for
taste observables in `L_a` and `R_a`.

### Theorem 9.1: Taste-Instrument No-Signaling

For disjoint block supports,

```math
[M_{a,r}^{L},M_{a,s}^{R}]=0,
\qquad
[M_{a,r}^{L},(M_{a,s}^{R})^\dagger]=0.
```

Consequently the joint probabilities are independent of detector ordering:

```math
p_{a}(r,s)
=
{\rm tr}\!\left(
M_{a,s}^{R}M_{a,r}^{L}\rho_a
(M_{a,r}^{L})^\dagger(M_{a,s}^{R})^\dagger
\right)
```

equals the expression with `L` and `R` interchanged. The local marginal in
`L` is independent of the choice of taste angle and partition in `R`:

```math
\sum_s p_a(r,s)
=
{\rm tr}\!\left(
M_{a,r}^{L}\rho_a(M_{a,r}^{L})^\dagger
\right).
```

The same holds with `L` and `R` reversed.

### Proof

Each detector Kraus operator is an even local function of the block bilinears in
its region. Even CAR operators with disjoint support commute. Therefore the
Kraus operators and their adjoints commute across `L_a` and `R_a`, giving
order-independence of the joint probability. For the marginal,

```math
\sum_s (M_{a,s}^{R})^\dagger M_{a,s}^{R}=I,
```

and this identity can be moved through the `L` Kraus operators by the same
commutation. Thus the remote detector setting cannot change the local outcome
statistics. `square`

### Theorem 9.2: Restricted Detector-Foliation Independence

Consider two regulated hypersurface descriptions that differ only by the order
in which spacelike separated taste detector collars `L_a` and `R_a` are crossed,
with the same initial and final hypersurfaces and the same local dynamical
kernel outside those collars. Then the detector part of the endpoint
instrument is exactly independent of the ordering. If the intervening
interacting kernel satisfies the Paper-3/Paper-4 exchange-collar estimate

```math
\|{\mathcal E}_{a}(L_a,R_a)\|
\le
C e^{-\mu d(L_a,R_a)}
+\varepsilon_a,
\qquad
\varepsilon_a\to0,
```

then the full endpoint probabilities differ by at most

```math
O\!\left(e^{-\mu d(L_a,R_a)}+\varepsilon_a\right)
```

on the fixed finite test battery.

### Proof

The detector-order part follows from Theorem 9.1. The only remaining possible
dependence is dynamical: the two hypersurface paths may insert the same local
interacting kernels in a different order across the corridor between the
collars. By definition, that difference is the exchange-collar defect
`{\mathcal E}_a(L_a,R_a)`. Composing bounded finite-battery instruments with an
operator difference of norm `\delta_a` changes each probability by at most a
constant times `\delta_a`; here
`\delta_a=C e^{-\mu d(L_a,R_a)}+\varepsilon_a`. `square`

This is intentionally weaker than a full Lorentz-covariant interacting ISP
theorem. It proves that the taste detectors themselves are causal, and it
identifies the remaining dynamical foliation problem as the exchange-collar
estimate that Paper 7 must control.

## 10. Finite Discriminator Battery

The comparison with Wilson must be operationally finite. The following battery
uses only finitely many preparations and detector settings, and includes the
taste-blind controls needed to prevent a fake mismatch.

### Battery 10.1: Taste-Record CHSH Discriminator

Prepare the two-region taste singlet from Theorem 7.1. Use four local detector
settings

```math
\theta_0=0,\quad
\theta_1=\frac{\pi}{2},
\qquad
\phi_0=\frac{\pi}{4},\quad
\phi_1=-\frac{\pi}{4}.
```

For each pair `(i,j)`, measure the sign-binned local effects of
`T_L(\theta_i)` and `T_R(\phi_j)`, producing outcomes `A_i,B_j\in\{\pm1\}`.
Include three taste-blind controls:

1. the one-particle localization/click rates in `L` and `R`;
2. the total number and energy-window acceptance rates;
3. the same CHSH schedule with taste-blind detectors replacing the
   taste-resolving detectors.

The discriminator is

```math
S_{\rm taste}
=
E_{00}+E_{01}+E_{10}-E_{11},
\qquad
E_{ij}={\mathbb E}(A_iB_j).
```

### Theorem 10.2: Finite Wilson/No-Wilson Separation

Assume the detector visibility tends to `V\in[0,1]`, meaning

```math
E_{ij}^{st}
=
-V\cos(\theta_i-\phi_j)+o(1)
```

for the no-Wilson taste-singlet battery. Then

```math
S_{\rm taste}^{st}
=
-2\sqrt2\,V+o(1).
```

For the Wilson single-species comparison class in which all accepted
instruments are generated by the Wilson local algebra and all taste-blind
controls are matched, the corresponding taste-channel operator is absent. Hence
the calibrated Wilson prediction for the taste-channel contrast is

```math
S_{\rm taste}^{W}=0
```

unless an additional internal two-level degree of freedom, a nonlocal
postprocessing rule, or a declared projection/selection channel is imported.
Therefore the finite contrast gap is

```math
\left|S_{\rm taste}^{st}-S_{\rm taste}^{W}\right|
=
2\sqrt2\,V+o(1).
```

If one allows arbitrary shared classical detector randomness while still
forbidding an extra quantum taste degree of freedom, the Wilson-side CHSH
contrast obeys the local bound `|S|\le2`, so a loophole-resistant separation
requires

```math
2\sqrt2\,V>2,
\qquad
V>\frac1{\sqrt2}.
```

### Proof

The no-Wilson value is the singlet calculation on the retained taste factor,
with imperfect detector visibility multiplying the ideal correlations. The
taste-blind controls ensure that both branches are compared on the same
spatial, number, and energy acceptance window.

In the Wilson single-species benchmark there is no operator corresponding to
`\tau_x` or `\tau_z`. Under the declared comparison map, taste-blind operators
map to Wilson local operators, while taste-Pauli contrasts map to zero because
their taste trace vanishes and no Wilson internal factor remains. Hence the
calibrated taste-channel contrast is zero. Any nonzero reproduction of the four
taste correlations requires adding precisely the data that the comparison class
forbids: an internal two-level system, nonlocal classical instructions, or a
selection rule that changes the battery. The final inequality is the standard
CHSH local bound for a purely classical hidden detector variable. `square`

### Interpretation Of The Battery

This battery does not say that ordinary QFT cannot violate Bell inequalities.
Ordinary QFT can violate Bell inequalities with spin, polarization, flavor, or
other declared degrees of freedom. The claim is narrower and sharper: the
single-species Wilson benchmark cannot reproduce a taste-channel Bell contrast
while also saying that the taste/detail record was removed and no replacement
degree of freedom was imported.

Thus a positive result would not merely be "Bell violation"; it would be Bell
violation in the retained detail/taste channel after taste-blind controls have
already matched the Wilson benchmark.

## 11. Four Empirical Regimes

The no-Wilson branch is not a single prediction until the instrument class is
specified. There are four sharply different regimes.

### Theorem 11.1: Regime Separation

Fix a finite no-Wilson test battery `{\mathcal T}` with compact spacetime
support. Exactly one of the following descriptions applies after passing to
the continuum limit.

1. **Taste-inaccessible regime.** If all effects in `{\mathcal T}` are
   taste-blind, all prepared states are taste-unpolarized or taste-blind in
   the tested sector, and all interaction coefficients commute with the taste
   projectors up to vanishing tested error, then `{\mathcal T}` is equivalent
   to a taste-traced Wilson battery after normalization.

2. **Ordinary multi-species regime.** If taste-resolving instruments exist and
   the target is declared to be two-taste Dirac/CAR QFT, then the predictions
   are those of ordinary multi-species QFT. This is a valid no-Wilson ISP
   branch, but not by itself new physics.

3. **Selected-taste Wilson-reduction regime.** If a declared projection selects
   one taste sector, records the rejected outcomes, and the accepted sector
   satisfies the Wilson-reduction assumptions of Section 13, then accepted
   conditional statistics match the Wilson benchmark. This is not erasure,
   because the reject statistics remain part of the operational record.

4. **ISP-retained-record discovery regime.** If the intended comparison target
   is single-species QFT and `{\mathcal T}` contains a taste-resolving effect
   or taste-mixing curvature probe with nonzero continuum expectation, then
   the no-Wilson branch predicts a finite operational difference from the
   single-species Wilson benchmark.

### Proof

In regime 1, every effect has the form `E_0\otimes I_{\rm taste}` and the
state/interactions produce no tested taste coherence. Taking the partial trace
over taste reduces all probabilities to the taste-blind sector, with only the
declared normalization/multiplicity factor. This is the Wilson comparison
after the accepted normalization.

In regime 2, the taste Pauli algebra is part of the declared local algebra.
The convergence theorem in Section 6 then gives the two-taste CAR/QFT
predictions. Since ordinary QFT can also be formulated with two species, this
is not yet a beyond-QFT claim.

In regime 3, the selected accepted algebra is isomorphic to the Wilson algebra
only after the declared projection. Since rejected outcomes are recorded, no
retained record has been silently erased.

In regime 4, the finite test battery contains an effect or curvature probe
whose continuum operator has a nonzero component along `\tau_x`, `\tau_y`, or
`\tau_z` not represented in the single-species Wilson algebra. No
single-species Wilson observable can reproduce all taste-blind controls and
that nonzero taste-resolving statistic without adding a hidden two-level
degree of freedom or conditioning away the test. `square`

This theorem is the operational version of the Barandes rule. The retained
record either remains inaccessible, becomes an ordinary declared species, is
selected with rejects recorded, or becomes a genuine difference relative to the
single-species benchmark. It is never silently erased.

## 12. Theorem: No-Wilson Multi-Taste QFT Benchmark

Assume:

1. the staggered smearing, polarization, and propagator convergence from
   Paper 3;
2. the no-Wilson smooth-core interacting curvature limits from Paper 4;
3. compactly supported finite test families;
4. declared taste-resolving or taste-blind instruments.

Then for every finite family of compactly supported two-taste test spinors
`F_1,\ldots,F_n`, every finite local CAR polynomial `A`, and every fixed
finite spacetime test window,

```math
\omega_{a,st}^{\rm ISP}
\left(A_a(I_a^{st}F_1,\ldots,I_a^{st}F_n)\right)
\to
\omega_{st}
\left(A(F_1,\ldots,F_n)\right),
```

where `\omega_{st}` is the declared two-taste Dirac/CAR target state.

Moreover the interacting exchange-defect coefficients converge on the tested
smooth core:

```math
{\mathfrak C}_{a,st}[N,M;g]
\to
K_{\parallel,st}[N\partial_xM-M\partial_xN]
+
g{\mathfrak C}_{st,{\rm preserve}}^{(1)}[N,M]
+
g{\mathfrak C}_{st,{\rm mix}}^{(1)}[N,M]
+
O(g^2)
```

for off-diagonal or alternating interactions, and diagonal density-density
interactions first contribute through the delayed two-taste coefficient at
order `gDelta^6`.

### Proof

The local CAR convergence is Paper 3's staggered two-taste reconstruction. The
interaction coefficient convergence is Paper 4 restricted to the no-Wilson
branch. Since there is no Wilson gap, the taste label remains in the target
space. Folding the Brillouin zone decomposes each local coefficient into
taste-preserving, taste-mixing, and taste-splitting components. Taste-blind
tests see only the components that survive their declared instrument; taste
resolving tests can see the full two-taste coefficient. `square`

## 13. Theorem: When No-Wilson Reduces To The Wilson Benchmark

The no-Wilson branch agrees with the Wilson single-species benchmark on a
finite test battery only under the following declared restrictions:

1. the tested initial states lie in a selected taste sector or are
   taste-blind mixtures whose statistics match the Wilson target;
2. the tested instruments are taste-blind or taste-projected with recorded
   reject outcomes;
3. the interactions are taste-preserving on the selected sector;
4. taste-mixing and taste-splitting coefficients either vanish in the
   continuum limit or are annihilated by the declared instruments.

Under these assumptions, for every finite Wilson-compatible test `T`,

```math
P_{a,st}^{\rm ISP}(T\mid{\rm accepted})
-
P_{a,W}^{\rm ISP}(T)
\to0.
```

### Proof

The accepted no-Wilson test algebra is isomorphic to the selected Wilson
physical algebra only after the stated projection or taste-blind restriction.
Taste-preserving dynamics stays inside that accepted algebra. Taste-mixing
terms have zero matrix elements by assumption, and rejected taste outcomes are
kept as recorded statistics. The Wilson benchmark theorem from Paper 5 then
applies to the accepted sector. `square`

## 14. Theorem: Difference Criterion

Let a finite no-Wilson test battery contain at least one taste-resolving effect
or one exchange-defect probe whose continuum coefficient has

```math
{\mathfrak C}_{st,{\rm mix}}^{(1)}[N,M]\ne0
```

or delayed density-density taste-mixing coefficient

```math
{\mathfrak C}_{st,{\rm mix}}^{(dd,del)}[N,M]\ne0.
```

Then no single-species Wilson benchmark can match the full no-Wilson test
battery unless it imports an additional hidden two-level internal degree of
freedom or conditions on a declared projection.

More concretely, for a weak exchange-defect effect

```math
E_{\pm}^{\rm mix}
=
\frac12(I\pm\epsilon X_{\rm mix}),
```

with `X_{\rm mix}` the normalized taste-mixing curvature probe, the outcome
difference has leading behavior

```math
P_{st}(+)-P_W(+)
=
\frac{\epsilon}{2}
\langle X_{\rm mix}\rangle_{st}
+
o(\epsilon),
```

where the Wilson term is zero because `X_{\rm mix}` is not an operator in the
single-species Wilson local algebra.

### Proof

A single-species Wilson local algebra has no nontrivial taste Pauli matrices.
Any Wilson observable corresponding to a taste-mixing operator must therefore
be represented by zero, by a scalar, or by an imported extra internal degree of
freedom. If the no-Wilson expectation of the taste-mixing probe is nonzero,
zero/scalar matching fails for a finite test battery that also contains
normalization and taste-blind controls. Matching requires adding the missing
two-level structure or conditioning it away by a declared instrument. `square`

## 15. EPR-Like Taste-Record Witness

This section is the possible "EPR moment" of the no-Wilson route. It is not a
superluminal-signaling claim. It is an operational witness that the retained
detail/taste label behaves as a real local record in a way absent from the
single-species Wilson benchmark.

Consider two spacelike separated regions `L` and `R`. On the two-taste
two-excitation sector, prepare the taste-entangled state

```math
|\Psi_{\rm taste}\rangle
=
\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\left(
|0\rangle_L|1\rangle_R
-
|1\rangle_L|0\rangle_R
\right),
```

where `0,1` denote taste labels, not spatial locations.

For angles `\theta,\phi`, define local taste observables

```math
T_L(\theta)
=
\cos\theta\,\tau_z^L+\sin\theta\,\tau_x^L,
\qquad
T_R(\phi)
=
\cos\phi\,\tau_z^R+\sin\phi\,\tau_x^R.
```

### Theorem 15.1: Taste-Record CHSH Witness

If the no-Wilson branch admits taste-resolving local instruments for
`T_L(\theta)` and `T_R(\phi)`, then

```math
\langle\Psi_{\rm taste}|
T_L(\theta)T_R(\phi)
|\Psi_{\rm taste}\rangle
=
-\cos(\theta-\phi).
```

For the standard CHSH choices

```math
\theta_0=0,\quad
\theta_1=\frac{\pi}{2},\quad
\phi_0=\frac{\pi}{4},\quad
\phi_1=-\frac{\pi}{4},
```

the no-Wilson branch gives

```math
|S|=2\sqrt2.
```

The Wilson single-species benchmark has no corresponding taste-resolving
observable. Therefore it cannot reproduce this test battery unless it imports
an additional two-level internal degree of freedom or declares an operational
projection that removes the test.

### Proof

The calculation is the standard two-qubit singlet calculation on the taste
factor. The spacelike separation belongs to the spatial support of the two
local excitations; the nonclassical correlation belongs to the retained taste
record. The finite operational no-signaling test is respected because the
local marginal of each taste measurement is independent of the remote angle.
This is not a Haag-Kastler AQFT causality theorem. The mismatch with the
Wilson benchmark is algebraic: the Wilson single-species algebra has no
operators `\tau_x,\tau_z`. `square`

### Interpretation

If such taste-resolving instruments are physically available, the no-Wilson
branch predicts an operational sector absent from the Wilson single-species
benchmark. If all real instruments are taste-blind, or if taste states cannot
be prepared and read out, the witness is not testable and the no-Wilson branch
may be empirically equivalent to Wilson on the tested battery.

Thus this is a possible discovery channel, not yet a prediction of ordinary
laboratory physics.

## 16. Taste-Blind Versus Taste-Resolving Instruments

A taste-blind local instrument has effects of the form

```math
E_{\rm blind}=E_0\otimes I_{\rm taste}.
```

A taste-resolving local instrument has at least one effect with nonzero
component along `\tau_x`, `\tau_y`, or `\tau_z`.

### Proposition 16.1: Taste-Blind Suppression

If the state is taste-unpolarized,

```math
\rho=\rho_0\otimes\frac{I_{\rm taste}}2,
```

and all tested effects are taste-blind, then all taste-mixing one-point
signals vanish:

```math
{\rm tr}(\rho\,E_0\otimes\tau_i)=0,
\qquad i=x,y,z.
```

Taste-mixing curvature can still affect higher-order or conditional
statistics only if the dynamics creates taste correlations before the
taste-blind readout.

### Proof

The trace over taste of each Pauli matrix is zero. The final statement follows
because taste-blind effects can see taste only through its backreaction on
taste-blind reduced statistics. `square`

## 17. Operational Decision Tree

The no-Wilson branch has four possible empirical statuses.

```text
1. Taste inaccessible:
   all realistic instruments are taste-blind and taste-mixing backreaction
   vanishes on tested states. No-Wilson is empirically equivalent to Wilson on
   the tested battery.

2. Taste is ordinary multi-species QFT:
   taste-resolving instruments exist, but the result is just a known
   multi-species QFT. This is not new physics; it is a different declared
   target theory.

3. Taste is selected and recorded:
   a declared projection selects one taste sector and keeps reject outcomes as
   records. Conditional accepted statistics may reduce to Wilson.

4. Taste is an ISP-retained record with unexpected access:
   taste-resolving or taste-mixing curvature probes are available even though
   the corresponding sector is absent from the intended single-species QFT
   target. This is the possible new-physics branch.
```

The paper's job is not to choose among these by wishful thinking. Its job is
to state the exact tests that separate them.

## 18. Concrete Hardware Protocols

The pointer model in Section 8 can be tied to ordinary finite hardware
primitives. The important point is not a specific engineering platform; it is
the operational pattern:

```text
two retained local modes
-> optional coherent two-mode rotation
-> number/occupation readout
-> recorded classical pointer.
```

This is the same mathematical structure in a double-well cold-atom block, a
double quantum dot, a superconducting two-mode circuit, a trapped-ion pair, or a
dual-rail photonic block. The platforms differ in errors and practical scaling,
not in the finite operator identity.

For each block `m`, define the coherent taste rotation

```math
R_m(\theta)
=
\exp\!\left(-\frac{i\theta}{2}t_a^y(m)\right).
```

Since

```math
R_m(\theta)^\dagger t_a^z(m)R_m(\theta)
=
\cos\theta\,t_a^z(m)+\sin\theta\,t_a^x(m),
```

an instrument for `t_a^\theta(m)` can be implemented by:

1. applying the local two-mode rotation `R_m(\theta)`;
2. measuring the site imbalance `t_a^z(m)`;
3. recording the pointer outcome.

Thus the hardware burden is sharply located. A platform that can count the two
sites but cannot coherently rotate them gives only `\tau_z` readout. A platform
with a calibrated beam-splitter/Rabi/Josephson/tunneling pulse between the two
local modes gives `x-z` taste-plane readout. Adding a phase shift before the
rotation gives `\tau_y` readout.

### Protocol 18.1: Two-Mode Taste Analyzer

For each block and each requested angle `\theta`:

```text
prepare block modes;
apply local mixing pulse R_m(theta);
couple occupation imbalance to a pointer register;
read and record the pointer;
keep all reject, no-click, and unresolved outcomes as records.
```

At the ideal finite cutoff this realizes the Section 8 detector. With
imperfections, write the implemented effect as

```math
\widetilde E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}
=
E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}+\delta E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}.
```

Let

```math
\epsilon_a^{\rm hw}
=
\sup_{\theta,f,B}
\|\delta E_{a,B}^{\theta,f}\|
```

over the finite test battery.

### Theorem 18.2: Hardware-Robust Taste Statistics

For every finite taste detector battery with `N_{\rm det}` detector effects, if

```math
\epsilon_a^{\rm hw}\to0,
```

then the implemented statistics converge to the ideal no-Wilson taste
statistics. More explicitly, for any event `B` in the battery,

```math
|P_a^{\rm hw}(B)-P_a^{\rm ideal}(B)|
\le
C_{\mathcal T}N_{\rm det}\epsilon_a^{\rm hw}
+o_a(1),
```

where `C_{\mathcal T}` depends only on the fixed finite battery and finite
particle cutoff.

### Proof

Each probability is a trace of a finite product of bounded effects and bounded
source/dynamical maps. Replacing one ideal effect by its implemented effect
changes the trace by at most the operator-norm error times a battery-dependent
bound. A telescoping expansion over the finite product gives the factor
`N_{\rm det}`. The ideal effects converge by Theorem 8.2, so the implemented
statistics converge whenever the hardware error vanishes. `square`

### Hardware Status

This completes the bridge at the theorem level:

```text
finite block/taste operator
-> finite pointer detector
-> explicit two-mode hardware primitive
-> stable finite-battery statistics under calibrated errors.
```

It still does not claim that Nature supplies the no-Wilson lattice block as a
literal laboratory double well. It says that if the retained detail degree of
freedom is physically instantiated by a two-mode local block, the taste
instrument has a standard hardware realization.

## 19. Full Finite-Battery Foliation Theorem

Section 9 proved no-signaling for the taste detectors themselves and a
two-collar conditional estimate. The complete Paper-6 foliation statement is
the finite-battery extension. It is conditional on the same exchange-collar
control that Paper 7 must later derive for broader interacting kernels.

### Hypothesis EC: Exchange-Collar Summability

For a fixed finite detector/instrument battery and a pair of regulated
hypersurface paths `\pi,\pi'` with the same endpoints, suppose `\pi` can be
changed into `\pi'` by finitely many adjacent swaps of spacelike separated
collars `C_k,D_k`. Let `{\mathcal E}_a(C_k,D_k)` be the exchange-collar defect
for the `k`th swap. Assume

```math
\sum_k\|{\mathcal E}_a(C_k,D_k)\|
\to0
```

as the regulator is refined on the fixed continuum test window.

### Theorem 19.1: Finite-Battery Foliation Independence

Under Hypothesis EC, every finite no-Wilson taste battery from this paper has a
foliation-independent continuum limit:

```math
\lim_{a\to0}P_{a,\pi}(B)
=
\lim_{a\to0}P_{a,\pi'}(B)
```

for every recorded battery event `B`, including source records, taste detector
records, taste-blind controls, and reject/no-click records.

More quantitatively,

```math
|P_{a,\pi}(B)-P_{a,\pi'}(B)|
\le
C_{\mathcal T}
\sum_k\|{\mathcal E}_a(C_k,D_k)\|
+o_a(1).
```

### Proof

It is enough to compare paths that differ by one adjacent spacelike swap. The
local detector Kraus operators commute by Theorem 9.1. Therefore the detector
part contributes no ordering error. The dynamical part contributes precisely
the exchange-collar defect for that adjacent swap. Composing the defect with
the bounded finite-battery source, detector, and endpoint effects changes the
event probability by at most a constant times its operator norm. Summing the
telescoping bound over the finite chain of adjacent swaps gives the displayed
estimate. Hypothesis EC sends the right-hand side to zero. `square`

### Boundary Of The Result

This is the strongest honest Paper-6 foliation result. It proves foliation
independence for the finite taste batteries once the exchange-collar defects
are summable. It does not prove the universal interacting exchange-collar
estimate for all future kernels; that is the job of Paper 7. But Paper 6 no
longer leaves the taste witness with an unresolved detector-order ambiguity.

## 20. Gauge And Non-Abelian Compatibility

Taste must also be checked against gauge constraints. The safe statement is not
"full non-Abelian continuum gauge theory." The safe statement is finite and
local: if the two taste components transform in the same gauge representation,
the taste bilinears can be made gauge invariant.

Let `\alpha` denote a finite gauge index in a representation `\rho` of a compact
group or finite representation cutoff. A local gauge transformation acts by

```math
\psi_{j,\alpha}
\mapsto
\rho(g_j)_{\alpha\beta}\psi_{j,\beta}.
```

If the two sites in a block are compared at different gauge points, insert the
link parallel transporter `U_{2m,2m+1}`. Define the gauge-covariant block field

```math
\widehat\Psi_m
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\psi_{2m}\\
U_{2m,2m+1}\psi_{2m+1}
\end{pmatrix}.
```

Then define the gauge-compatible taste density

```math
t_{a,{\rm gauge}}^i(m)
=
\widehat\Psi_m^\dagger
(I_{\rm gauge}\otimes\tau_i)
\widehat\Psi_m.
```

### Theorem 20.1: Gauge-Invariant Taste Readout

For finite gauge groups, compact groups with representation cutoff, or any
finite link Hilbert-space truncation in which `U_{2m,2m+1}` transforms as a
parallel transporter, the operators `t_{a,{\rm gauge}}^i(m)` commute with the
local gauge action at the block endpoints. Therefore their smeared versions
define gauge-invariant local detector effects on the finite Gauss sector.

### Proof

Under a gauge transformation,

```math
U_{2m,2m+1}
\mapsto
\rho(g_{2m})U_{2m,2m+1}\rho(g_{2m+1})^{-1}.
```

Hence both components of `\widehat\Psi_m` transform at the same endpoint:

```math
\widehat\Psi_m\mapsto \rho(g_{2m})\widehat\Psi_m.
```

The taste Pauli matrix acts only on the two-component taste/block index and
commutes with `\rho(g_{2m})`. Contracting the gauge index in
`\widehat\Psi_m^\dagger(I_{\rm gauge}\otimes\tau_i)\widehat\Psi_m` therefore
cancels the gauge action. The spectral projections of a gauge-invariant
self-adjoint operator preserve the Gauss sector, so the detector effects are
gauge-invariant. `square`

### Non-Abelian Boundary

This section proves compatibility of taste readout with finite gauge
constraints. It does not construct the full non-Abelian exchange-defect
coefficient or representation-cutoff continuum limit. Those remain Papers 9
and 10. The important Paper-6 result is that taste readout is not automatically
destroyed by gauge invariance; it only needs the standard local parallel
transporter when the two block sites carry independent gauge frames.

## 21. Final Taste-Sector Verdict

The no-Wilson detail branch is now classified. There are only four honest
statuses.

### Theorem 21.1: Taste-Sector Classification

For a fixed finite no-Wilson test battery with declared source, detector,
interaction, and comparison data, exactly one of the following operational
descriptions applies:

1. **Taste-blind regulator sector.** All accessible effects are taste-blind and
   all taste-mixing backreaction vanishes on the battery. The branch is
   empirically equivalent to Wilson on that battery.
2. **Ordinary multi-taste QFT.** Taste-resolving effects are accessible and the
   target theory declares the taste factor as an ordinary species/taste degree
   of freedom. The branch is standard multi-species QFT, not new physics.
3. **Selected-taste Wilson reduction.** A declared projection selects one taste
   sector and records the rejected outcomes. Conditional accepted statistics
   match the Wilson benchmark under the assumptions of Section 13.
4. **ISP-retained-record channel.** The intended comparison target is
   single-species Wilson QFT, yet a taste-resolving or taste-mixing probe has
   nonzero continuum expectation after taste-blind controls have matched. The
   branch differs operationally from the single-species benchmark.

### Proof

The local algebra generated by the declared instruments is either contained in
the taste-blind subalgebra, contains a declared taste algebra, is restricted by
a recorded selected-taste projection, or contains a nonzero taste operator while
the comparison target lacks one. The first case gives taste trace equivalence,
the second is ordinary multi-species QFT, the third is conditional Wilson
reduction, and the fourth is the discriminator/difference theorem of Sections
10 and 14. The cases exhaust the possible relation between the declared
instrument algebra and the taste factor. `square`

### Final Verdict

Paper 6 does not prove that ISP is beyond QFT. It proves something narrower and
cleaner:

```text
No-Wilson retained detail is either invisible, ordinary multi-taste QFT,
recorded selected-taste Wilson physics, or a finite retained-record channel
absent from the single-species Wilson benchmark.
```

That is the Barandes-aligned endpoint: nothing is silently erased, and nothing
is promoted to new physics unless the instrument algebra actually measures it.
