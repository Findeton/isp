## Abstract

The classic black-hole puzzles — information loss, the singularity, and the
firewall/complementarity tension — are treated here as three faces of a single error:
over-extending, past the horizon, one of three idealizations that the indivisible-stochastic (ISP)
ontology drops. ISP drops (A) a foliation-independent global quantum state, (B) divisible
(Markovian) evolution, and (C) the continuum field-value-at-a-point as an element of reality. The
**information** puzzle is the over-extension of (B): it dissolves at the level of *plain,
non-relativistic* ISP, where the indivisible (non-Markovian) globally-unitary process preserves
information and the "loss" is the artifact of reading it through a divisible exterior map; the late
coherence follows the Page curve, agreeing with the gravitational-entropy (island) account. The
**singularity** is the over-extension of (C): in ISP it is the boundary of the finite-record
descent — where the continuum ceases to descend from finite records — not a curvature infinity;
again plain ISP. The **firewall/complementarity** tension is the over-extension of (A) and is the
one genuinely *relativistic*-ISP item: the foliation-relative beable of Paper 3 realizes
complementarity at the ontological level (the smooth-horizon infaller and the membrane-seeing
asymptotic observer are two foliation-relative realities) and removes the single-global-state
premise the firewall's monogamy argument requires. We show, from Result 12, that this RISP
difference is *predicted to be unobservable*, because the event horizon is a global object and
foliation-relativity is confined to unobservable global structure. We connect the black hole to the
program's record-stabilization machinery (Paper 2) and to the eternal-black-hole / thermofield-double
arena, read the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy as a record count (a reinterpretation, not a derivation
of the coefficient), and keep the two layers strictly separated throughout. We claim no resolution
of any paradox and no new measurable physics; the contribution is the unified diagnosis and the
ISP/RISP separation.

## 1. Introduction

Four puzzles cluster around the black hole. **Information**: Hawking's thermal emission
[Hawking1975] appears to take a pure infalling state to a mixed final state, conflicting with
unitarity. **The singularity**: Penrose's theorem [Penrose1965] forces geodesic incompleteness and
the classical curvature diverges. **Entropy**: the Bekenstein–Hawking law `S=A/4`
[Bekenstein1973, Hawking1975] assigns a vast entropy to a featureless horizon. **The firewall**:
the AMPS argument [AMPS2013] shows that unitarity, a smooth horizon, and standard field theory
cannot all hold, sharpening the older black-hole complementarity [Susskind1993]. Decades of work —
the membrane paradigm, holography, complementarity, ER=EPR [MaldacenaSusskind2013], and the
gravitational replica/island program [Islands2021] — have reframed these without a consensus
ontology of *what is real* at and inside a black hole.

The indivisible-stochastic-process (ISP) program [Barandes2023a, Barandes2023b] offers an ontology:
configurations are definite finite records at all times, the transition law is non-divisible
(encoding interference and decoherence), and the wavefunction is derived. Its discipline is to
*reproduce* the observables of quantum theory and *reframe* their ontology. Paper 1 [Paper1] began
applying this to black holes (the horizon as record compression); Paper 2 [Paper2] derived the
program's gravitational decoherence; Paper 3 [Paper3] isolated the *foliation-relative beable*
(Result 12) and the open problem of its covariant construction (§10).

This paper asks what the *whole* black-hole puzzle cluster looks like in this ontology, and insists
on a clean separation: which conclusions need only *plain, non-relativistic* ISP (and so stand on
the program's ordinary footing), and which need the *relativistic*-ISP foliation-relative beable
(and so are vivid but rest on Paper 3's open construction and are unobservable). The organizing
thesis is a single diagnosis (§2): each paradox is the over-extension of one idealization ISP drops.

```math
\text{(C)} & \text{the continuum field value at a point as a beable} & \text{finite records; no field-at-a-point beable (ISP)}

```

The thesis: each canonical black-hole paradox is the *same mistake* — pushing one of (A)–(C) past

```math
\boxed{
\begin{array}{l|c|l}
\text{puzzle} & \text{over-extended} & \text{ISP status (and layer)}\\
\hline
\text{information ``loss''} & \text{(B)} & \text{dissolves at plain-ISP level (Part I, §4)}\\
```

This table is also the **ISP/RISP map**: (B) and (C) are Barandes' non-relativistic features, so

that one structural error, in three guises, generates the cluster. **[SYN]**

## 3. The ontology and the ISP/RISP boundary

- **Plain ISP (Barandes).** An indivisible stochastic process over configuration space:
  configurations are definite (beables) at each time; the transition law is *non-divisible* (no
  Chapman–Kolmogorov), encoding interference and decoherence; the wavefunction is derived
  [Barandes2023a, Barandes2023b]. Formulated with a single time parameter (one foliation /
  absolute-time level). Its black-hole-relevant content is *indivisibility* and the *finite-record*
  reading of configurations.
- **Relativistic ISP (RISP).** The extension in which the beable lives on spacelike hypersurfaces
  with *no preferred foliation* — the **foliation-relative beable** argued tentatively coherent in
  Paper 3 (Result 12), whose covariant interacting-field construction is the open problem of Paper 3
  §10. Foliations are physically meaningful only relativistically, so this layer is intrinsically
  RISP.

**Boundary rule (used throughout).** A claim using only indivisibility or finite records, with GR
entering only as a fixed curved background for an otherwise-reconstructed QFT, is **[ISP]**; a claim
using the foliation-relative beable is **[RISP]**. We will not blur them.

# Part I — ISP content (plain ISP, GR as backdrop)

## 4. Information: the "loss" is an over-extension of divisibility [ISP]

**The standard paradox.** On a fixed collapse background, Hawking's calculation [Hawking1975] yields
a steady thermal flux; tracing out the interior modes, the exterior reduced state is thermal and
mixed. Treating the exterior evolution as an autonomous, *divisible* completely-positive map
`Φ_t` from initial to final exterior data, a pure initial state is sent to a mixed final state,
and information is lost — in tension with global unitarity.

**The ISP diagnosis (type-B over-extension).** In ISP the fundamental process is *indivisible*: the
transition law does not factorize, `Γ(t₂,t₀) ≠ Γ(t₂,t₁)Γ(t₁,t₀)`. "The exterior
density matrix evolves by a divisible semigroup" *is* precisely the Chapman–Kolmogorov factorization
ISP denies. The information "loss" is manufactured by that factorization:

```math
\boxed{
\text{``information loss''}\;=\;\text{(indivisible, globally unitary process) read as (divisible exterior Markov map)}.
}
```

Concretely, indivisibility means the multi-time / multi-region correlations of the radiation are
*not* those of any Markov process; the purifying correlations between the late radiation and the
early radiation — exactly what a divisible exterior map cannot carry forward — are retained by the
non-Markovian memory. The coarse-grained-exterior mixedness at intermediate times is the ordinary
record-correlation structure (entanglement with not-yet-emitted/interior records), not a loss. The
resulting coherence of the radiation rises and falls along the **Page curve** [Page1993]; the rapid
return of information after the Page time is the Hayden–Preskill mirror behaviour [HaydenPreskill2007];
and the whole picture matches the gravitational-entropy / island account [Islands2021]. **[ISP;
reproduces STD]**

**Honest status.** Plain ISP: it uses indivisibility (a non-relativistic Barandes feature) and GR
only as the backdrop for the reconstructed Hawking process. The *conclusion* (information preserved,
Page curve) coincides with mainstream unitarity, so it is not a new result; the *diagnosis* — that
"loss" is specifically the over-extension of divisibility — is the synthesis. **[REINT + SYN]**

## 5. The singularity: a finite-record descent boundary, not a curvature infinity [ISP]

**The standard picture.** Penrose's theorem [Penrose1965] guarantees geodesic incompleteness inside
the horizon; in the classical continuum the curvature scalars diverge at `r=0`.

**The ISP diagnosis (type-C over-extension).** ISP takes the smooth continuum metric to be a
*coarse-grained descent* from finite records — licensed only where the records support a smooth
description. The continuum field-value-at-a-point is not itself a beable; "the field at `x`" is a
derived, smeared quantity. Approaching `r=0`, the curvature grows until the finite-record structure
can no longer support a continuum: the descent map (finite records → smooth geometry) ceases to be
defined. So the singularity is **the boundary of the finite-record descent**, not a physical locus
of infinite curvature:

```math
\boxed{
\text{singularity}\;=\;\text{boundary of the finite-record descent}\;\neq\;\big(\mathcal R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}\to\infty \text{ as an ontic fact}\big).
}
```

The "infinity" is the continuum extrapolated past its domain of descent — structurally like reading
hydrodynamics below the mean free path, or a lattice theory below its spacing. This dissolves the
*ontological* puzzle of an actual infinity (there is none to resolve; the question becomes "what are
the finite records in that regime," i.e. the quantum-gravity question) while changing no exterior
observable. It is the position sketched in Paper 1 [Paper1], placed here under heading (C). **[ISP;
REINT]**

**Honest status.** Plain ISP (finite records); GR enters only as the failing-continuum backdrop. It
is a reinterpretation of the singularity's *status*, overlapping the general "singularity =
breakdown of the effective description" view; not a new result, not a resolution of the
quantum-gravity regime, and unobservable (singularities are interior).

# Part II — RISP content (foliation-relative beable; open and unobservable)

## 6. The horizon and complementarity: foliation-relative beables [RISP]

The firewall/complementarity tension is the over-extension of (A): a single, foliation-independent
global state spanning interior *and* exterior radiation. Here plain ISP is insufficient and the
relativistic-ISP object is required.

The RISP beable is foliation-relative (Paper 3, Result 12): there is no foliation-independent
"configuration of everything," only configurations on hypersurfaces, with all *local* facts
foliation-independent (by microcausality) and only the *global* structure foliation-relative. At a
black hole the two natural foliations diverge maximally:

- the **infalling** foliation's slices cross the horizon smoothly → a beable with a smooth horizon
  (no drama at crossing);
- the **asymptotic** foliation's slices pile up at the horizon → a beable that *is* the
  record-compression / stretched-horizon membrane of Paper 1 [Paper1].

In RISP these are **two foliation-relative realities, neither privileged** — which is exactly
**black-hole complementarity** [Susskind1993], now realized at the level of the beable rather than
posited as an interpretive rule. Where Susskind–Thorlacius–Uglum asserted "no single observer sees
both descriptions," RISP supplies the ontological reason: there is no single beable in which both are
facts. Paper 1's "many-to-one record compression at the horizon" is the asymptotic-foliation beable;
the smooth-horizon infaller is the other. **[RISP; SYN realization of a known stance]**

## 7. Firewall dissolution: no single global state to run monogamy on [RISP]

**The AMPS argument, precisely** [AMPS2013]. Take a late Hawking mode `b`. (i) *Unitarity/Page*: after
the Page time `b` is nearly maximally entangled with the early radiation `R`. (ii) *Smooth horizon*
(equivalence principle): `b` is nearly maximally entangled with its interior partner mode `ã`.
(iii) *Monogamy of entanglement*, applied within one global quantum state, forbids `b` from being
maximally entangled with both `R` and `ã`. Contradiction ⇒ drop (i) (information loss) or drop (ii)
(a firewall — the horizon is not smooth).

**The RISP dissolution (denying the type-A premise).** Monogamy needs `R`, `b`, and `ã` to be
co-present in *one global state*. In the foliation-relative ontology they are not: the `b`–`R`
entanglement is a record-fact of the **asymptotic** foliation (both are exterior, on asymptotic
slices); the `b`–`ã` entanglement is a record-fact of the **infalling** foliation (`ã` is interior,
present only on slices that cross the horizon). No foliation has `{R,b,ã}` simultaneously as definite
beables with a joint state, so the monogamy inequality is never instantiated:

```math
\boxed{
\text{AMPS needs } \{R,\,b,\,\tilde a\}\ \text{in one global state};\quad
\text{RISP: } \{R,b\}\in\text{asymptotic beable},\ \{b,\tilde a\}\in\text{infalling beable}\ \Rightarrow\ \text{monogamy not posable}.
}
```

This is the ontological content of complementarity, and it is the beable version of the
"no global Hilbert space" family of firewall responses. **[RISP; SYN]**

**Honest status / known-address caveat.** The *resolution stance* — that complementarity / the
absence of a single global state defuses AMPS — is not new (the Susskind–Bousso line, and relatives
such as state-dependence and `A=R_B`). RISP's contribution is to supply a *beable-level ontology* for
it, grounded in Result 12, rather than an interpretive postulate. It rests on Paper 3's **open**
construction (§10) and on the *tentative* coherence of Result 12. **[RISP; OPEN dependency]**

## 8. The interior and ER=EPR: foliation-relative records linked by entanglement [RISP, SPEC]

In RISP the interior is real *relative to the infalling foliation*; the radiation is real relative to
the asymptotic foliation. The ER=EPR proposal [MaldacenaSusskind2013] holds that the entanglement
between a black hole and its radiation *is* an Einstein–Rosen bridge — "the interior is built from the
entanglement." This aligns naturally with the foliation-relative beable: the interior records (read
by the infalling beable) and the radiation records (read by the asymptotic beable) are *the same
information in two foliation-relative forms*, and the entanglement that ER=EPR geometrizes is exactly
the record-link between them. Complementarity (§6) and ER=EPR then become two descriptions of one
fact: there is no foliation-independent answer to "where the interior information *is*," only the
foliation-relative records and the entanglement bridging them.

**Honest status.** This is a *heuristic alignment*, not a derivation: RISP does not derive the ER
bridge geometry, and ER=EPR is itself a conjecture. Marked speculative. **[RISP; SPEC/OPEN]**

## 9. Why the RISP difference is unobservable — predicted, not patched [RISP]

A natural objection: if RISP differs from QFT+GR at the horizon, why is there no experiment to catch
it? The answer is structural and follows from Result 12 rather than being added by hand.

- The **event horizon is a globally / teleologically defined object** (it depends on the entire
  causal future), so the difference between the infalling and asymptotic beables lives in the
  *global* structure.
- Result 12 (Paper 3) shows RISP's foliation-relativity is **confined to the unobservable global
  structure**: all *local* facts — records, measurement outcomes, two-region correlations — are
  foliation-independent by microcausality (the reduced state of a region is fixed by its local
  algebra, independent of the slice through it).
- **Complementarity is itself the statement that the two foliations cannot be jointly observed**: no
  observer can be both the infaller and the asymptotic observer; the infaller cannot report out.

Hence RISP's distinctive horizon content and the impossibility of measuring it are **the same fact.**
RISP does not merely fail to predict a measurable horizon difference; via Result 12 it *predicts that
the difference must be unobservable*, because the difference *is* the foliation-relativity, and
foliation-relativity is unobservable. The black hole is the cleanest illustration in the program of
why ISP's value is ontological, not empirical. **[RISP; SYN]**

# Cross-cutting

## 10. The black hole as a record-stabilizer: link to the decoherence channel [ISP, connects Paper 2]

In the ISP ontology, *division events* / record stabilization are where definiteness and decoherence
occur. A black hole is, in this language, a maximal record-stabilizer: matter falling in carries
superpositions that decohere through the gravitational record-stabilization channel of Paper 2
[Paper2], and the Hawking radiation is the exterior record stream. The horizon (Paper 1's
many-to-one record compression) is the boundary across which records become exterior-inaccessible.

Quantitatively, the Paper 2 decoherence time is `τ_G=ℏ/E_G` with `E_G` the gravitational self-energy
of the mass-density difference between branches; near a black hole `E_G` is large, so a spatial
superposition decoheres rapidly — the black hole is the extreme of the program's
quantum-to-classical transition. With the indivisible (non-Markovian) survival law of Paper 2 the
onset is Gaussian rather than exponential, but at horizon-scale `E_G` the distinction is academic
(decoherence is essentially immediate). **[ISP, connects Paper 2; reproduces the expected
"horizons decohere" intuition]**

**Honest status.** Conceptual: it ties the BH to the program's machinery (division events, Paper 2
decoherence — all Barandes-level / non-relativistic, GR a backdrop). Observationally inaccessible
(one cannot watch infalling superpositions), and the decoherence channel is the Paper 2 one (a known
address, not BH-specific).

## 11. A concrete arena: the eternal black hole / thermofield double [RISP]

The eternal AdS–Schwarzschild black hole is the thermofield double (TFD): two boundary CFTs in a
maximally entangled state, whose bulk dual is a two-sided black hole connected by an Einstein–Rosen
bridge [MaldacenaSusskind2013]. This is the cleanest concrete setting for the foliation-relative
beable, for three reasons: (i) the entanglement (§8) is explicit and tunable; (ii) the left/right
boundary records and the bulk interior provide a sharp instance of "the same information in two
foliation-relative forms"; (iii) the local-algebra structure that makes the RISP difference
unobservable (§9) is well understood there (boundary-region algebras and their bulk duals).

The TFD is therefore the natural target for any *rigorous* RISP construction: a covariant interacting
beable process (Paper 3 §10) would have to reproduce the known TFD correlations as foliation-relative
records and recover the bulk ER bridge as the record-link. We do not carry this out — it depends on
the open §10 construction — but we flag the TFD as where the RISP claims of §6–§8 could be made
precise and checked against an established structure. **[RISP; target, OPEN]**

## 12. Entropy: a record-counting interpretation of `S=A/4`, with the gap flagged

The Bekenstein–Hawking entropy [Bekenstein1973, Hawking1975]

```math
S_{\rm BH}=\frac{A}{4}\quad(\text{natural units})
```

reads, in the finite-record ontology, as the logarithm of the number of distinguishable
exterior-accessible finite records — the record-accessibility structure of Paper 1 [Paper1]. The
record-*counting* reading is plain-ISP (finite records); the *area-law form* and the coefficient
`1/4` involve the horizon (a RISP/global object) and are the content of holography.

**Honest status — reinterpretation, not derivation.** Deriving the area scaling and the `1/4` from

theory, loop quantum gravity, and the gravitational replica/island program [Islands2021]; ISP/RISP
offers a *reading* of what the entropy counts, not a count. We claim only the interpretation and flag
the derivation as outside the program's reach. **[REINT; OPEN for the coefficient]**

## 13. The endpoint of evaporation: discrete record-shedding, no runaway, and the final burst

The same finite-record logic that recast the singularity (§5) recasts the *endpoint of Hawking
evaporation*. Semiclassically `T_H ∝ 1/M`, `dM/dt ∝ −M⁻²`, and as `M→0` the temperature runs away
to infinity — a continuum singularity in the evaporation itself. In the finite-record ontology the
record count is

```math
S = \frac{A}{4\ell_P^2} \sim \Big(\frac{M}{M_P}\Big)^2 ,
```

so discreteness is invisible while `S≫1` (a solar-mass hole has `S ∼ 10⁷⁷`) and becomes controlling
only when `S∼O(1)`, i.e. `M∼M_P` — the final Planck-time. There:

- **The bulk of evaporation reproduces standard Hawking** (large `S`, quasi-continuous). **[ISP;
  reproduces STD]**
- **The endpoint is discrete record-shedding, not a continuous thermal runaway.** The hole sheds whole
  records, emitting at discrete frequencies — a line spectrum (the Bekenstein–Mukhanov picture
  [BekensteinMukhanov1995], here grounded in the record count) rather than a smooth thermal tail.
- **No `T→∞` runaway.** The final stage is a *finite* sequence of `∼O(1)` record-emissions ending at
  the minimal-record configuration; the divergence of `dM/dt` as `M→0` is exposed as a continuum-descent
  artifact, exactly parallel to the singularity's curvature infinity (§5). **[ISP; SYN]**
- **Unitary / record-conserving.** With no curvature singularity to destroy records (§5), the
  information leaves with the radiation (Page, §3); the endpoint is a record-bookkept transition, not an
  information sink.
- **End-state: minimal-record configuration.** RISP does not force "complete evaporation vs. remnant,"
  but a surviving remnant would be a *finite*-record object, which **avoids the standard remnant
  objection**: the usual worry (remnants need unbounded internal degeneracy to store the information)
  does not apply to a bounded-record remnant. **[SYN; OPEN which]**

The one plausibly RISP-*specific* feature is the **emission statistics**: because the underlying process
is indivisible, the late record-emissions are **correlated (non-Markovian)**, not independent thermal
quanta — which is precisely what Page-curve unitarity requires (late quanta entangled with early ones),
now with the program's non-Markovian (Gaussian-onset) character. **[RISP; SYN]**

```math
\boxed{\;\text{RISP endpoint: discrete record-shedding}\to\text{line spectrum, no }T\to\infty,\ \text{unitary, correlated final-burst quanta.}\;}
```

**In-principle observable.** Primordial black holes of `M ∼ 10¹¹`–`10¹² kg` are evaporating
now and produce a **final burst**; RISP predicts that burst is a discrete, correlated line spectrum
terminating without a `T→∞` divergence, distinguishable in principle from a continuous thermal flash
[Page1993]. (Not observed; the discreteness is at the Planck-approach tail.)

**Honest ceiling.** The *discreteness* is a **known address** — quantized late-stage emission / line
spectra are generic to any quantum-gravity discreteness (Bekenstein–Mukhanov [BekensteinMukhanov1995],
loop quantum gravity, fuzzballs); RISP's finite-record count is one realization, not a unique
fingerprint. The RISP-flavored part is the *correlated emission statistics*, which also overlaps the
general unitarity requirement. It rests on the finite-record substrate being operative at small scales
(§5, the descent boundary, `[OPEN]` — the Paper 3 §10 construction would have to deliver it), and it
reproduces standard Hawking for the entire bulk of evaporation. **[ISP/RISP; known-address discreteness,
RISP-flavored correlations, OPEN substrate]**

## 14. Testable content (thin, and honest)

- The only deviation channel is the strong-field limit of the Paper 2 gravitational decoherence (the
  non-Markovian Gaussian onset). Near a horizon `E_G` is large, so decoherence is fast — but real
  horizons are inaccessible to controlled superpositions, and the channel is shared with collapse
  models (a known address), not BH-specific.
- The ISP layer (Part I) reproduces all observables (Hawking, Page, QNMs) by construction → nothing
  to distinguish.
- The RISP layer (Part II) is unobservable *by* Result 12 (§9).
- The one *in-principle* observable is the evaporation endpoint (§13): a discrete, correlated final-burst
  line spectrum from a primordial black hole, vs. a continuous thermal flash. But the discreteness is a
  known address (any quantum-gravity discreteness predicts it), no such burst has been observed, and the
  effect sits at the Planck-approach tail.

So this paper makes **no new *uniquely-distinguishing* black-hole prediction.** Its content is diagnostic
and ontological, with the evaporation endpoint as the lone in-principle (but known-address) observable.

## 16. Conclusion

The black-hole paradox cluster is, on this account, three over-extensions of the idealizations the
indivisible-stochastic ontology drops. Information "loss" over-extends divisibility (B): the
indivisible globally-unitary process preserves information and yields the Page curve, with the "loss"
exposed as a divisible-map artifact — *plain ISP*, GR a backdrop. The singularity over-extends the
continuum field-at-a-point (C): it is a finite-record descent boundary, not an ontic infinity —
again *plain ISP*. The firewall over-extends the single global state (A): once the beable is
foliation-relative (Paper 3, Result 12), complementarity is the default, the firewall's monogamy
premise is denied, and — by the *same* result — the distinctive difference is provably unobservable,
because it lives in the global structure no observer can survey. The interior, ER=EPR, and the
eternal-black-hole / thermofield-double arena fit the same foliation-relative-record picture, and the
Bekenstein–Hawking entropy reads as a record count whose coefficient the program cannot derive.

We claim no resolution of any paradox, no derivation of `S=A/4`, and no new measurable black-hole
physics. What the finite-record indivisible ontology offers the black hole is a unified diagnosis, a
beable-level realization of complementarity, and — its sharpest methodological point — a clean
separation of what it can say *solidly* (the plain-ISP layer: information, singularity, decoherence)
from what it can say only *vividly and conjecturally* (the RISP layer: horizon, firewall, interior),
together with a structural reason why the latter is hidden from experiment.
