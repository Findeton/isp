To the Editors, *Foundations of Physics*

Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the enclosed manuscript, "Non-Markovian Gravitational Decoherence: A
Gaussian-Onset Alternative to the Diósi–Penrose Exponential," for consideration as a research
article in *Foundations of Physics*.

The paper makes a single, focused point about gravitationally induced decoherence. The
Diósi–Penrose (DP) coherence-decay law is exponential, and I show that this exponential form is not
a prediction of gravity but a consequence of an implicit Markov (white-noise) assumption about the
underlying stochastic dynamics. Motivated by Barandes' indivisible-stochastic-processes
reformulation of quantum mechanics — in which the dynamics is non-divisible, hence non-Markovian —
I replace that white-noise assumption with a finite-memory one, and show that the coherence then
develops a non-exponential, quadratic (Gaussian) short-time onset at the same gravitational energy
scale. The resulting prediction is experimentally distinguishable from strict Markovian DP/CSL by
the *shape* of the residual log-coherence rather than by an absolute rate, with a second axis (the
gravitational E_G-scaling) separating it from non-gravitational collapse models, and a third (a
non-Gaussian fingerprint — a kurtosis term, up to coherence revivals) separating the discrete-record
channel from generic Gaussian colored noise.

I believe the manuscript fits *Foundations of Physics* because it sits at the interface the journal
serves: it connects a foundational reformulation of quantum mechanics to a concrete, falsifiable
consequence for an active experimental program — matter-wave and levitated-optomechanical tests of
gravitational decoherence and collapse models.

I have tried to be scrupulous about scope, and the manuscript states its limitations plainly:
indivisibility *motivates* the finite-memory channel, with an explicit division-event model grounding
the exponential kernel and pinning the memory time τ_c to the gravitational time τ_G — forced there by
a self-consistency fixed point and by record information-gating, not fitted, though resting on one
stated ansatz rather than a first-principles derivation; the quadratic onset is the generic signature
of non-Markovianity and is therefore shared with other coloured-noise collapse models (so the onset
alone confirms a *class*, though a non-Gaussian fingerprint narrows it toward discrete-record channels
— not uniquely ISP); the
energy scale is inherited from DP; and the spontaneous-radiation and heating bounds constrain only the
model's short-distance regularization length, which decouples from the regularization-independent
decoherence the interferometer measures (so they do not threaten the prediction). The contribution is
the falsifiable onset-shape discriminator and a clear separation of what is forced, what is inherited,
and what is assumed.

The manuscript (~11 pages, 1 figure, 4 tables) is original, has not been published previously, and
is not under consideration at any other journal. I am the sole author and declare no competing
interests. The work was carried out without institutional support.
