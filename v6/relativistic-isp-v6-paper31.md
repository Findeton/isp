## 3. The Equivalence Theorem

### 3.1 Determinant charges and the linkage

Irreducible representations of $U(d)$ are labeled by decreasing
integer tuples; the **determinant charge** is the total box count
($a$ for $U(3)$, $b$ for $U(2)$), and the center linkage is a
theorem of $U(d)$ representation theory:

$$
a \equiv \tau \pmod 3, \qquad b \equiv \sigma \pmod 2 ,
$$

with $\det_3 = (1,1,1)$ at $a = 3$ and $\det_2$ at $b = 2$.  These
linkages are *not* inputs: they come with the fibers.

### 3.2 The theorem

**Theorem 3.1.**  Define hypercharge as a determinant-character
combination $Y_6 = \alpha a + \beta b$.  Then the P17 congruence
$2\tau + 3\sigma + Y_6 \equiv 0 \pmod 6$ holds **identically** (for
every representation, i.e. every $(a,b)$) if and only if
$(\alpha, \beta) \equiv (-2, 3) \pmod 6$ — the class of the relative
determinant character

$$
Y_6 \;=\; 3b - 2a .
$$

In that case the congruence is the identity
$2\tau + 3\sigma + Y_6 \equiv 2a + 3b + (3b - 2a) = 6b \equiv 0$,
and every Z6-allowed triple $(\tau, \sigma, Y_6)$ is realized by
some $(a,b)$.

*Proof.*  Using the linkages, $2\tau \equiv 2a$ and
$3\sigma \equiv 3b \pmod 6$; the congruence reads
$(2+\alpha)a + (3+\beta)b \equiv 0 \pmod 6$ for all integers $a, b$,
which holds iff $6 \mid (2+\alpha)$ and $6 \mid (3+\beta)$.
Surjectivity: given a Z6-allowed triple, solve
$3b - 2a = Y_6$ with $a \equiv \tau\ (3)$, $b \equiv \sigma\ (2)$;
the obstruction is $Y_6 - 3\sigma + 2\tau \equiv 0 \pmod 6$, which
is the Z6 condition itself.  $\blacksquare$

Receipts: zero violations on $[-9,9]^2$; zero unrealized triples
($|Y_6| \le 12$); the 36-class scan returns exactly $\{(4,3)\}$.
The minimal $(a,b)$ representatives reproduce all six multiplets —
$Q(1,1) \mapsto +1$, $L(0,-1) \mapsto -3$, $u^c(2,0) \mapsto -4$,
$d^c(-1,0) \mapsto +2$, $e^c(0,2) \mapsto +6$, $\nu^c(0,0) \mapsto
