## 2. Campaign C3: Wick rotation without a manifold

**Palindrome Lemma.**  Symmetric transfer operators with
$T_{m+k} = T_{m+1-k}$: the OS pairing operator is
$(T_1\cdots T_m)(T_m\cdots T_1) = BB^{\mathsf T} \succeq 0$.

**Two-Mirror Lemma.**  Mirrors at $k_0 < k_1$ compose to
translation by $2(k_1-k_0)$; mirrors at every plane ⟺ static.

**The Gaussian-chain instance.**  Static and palindromic
mass histories pass Gram-RP ($-1.3\times 10^{-17}$,
$-3.3\times 10^{-16}$); a scrambled history violates it (worst

continuation — without a mirror** (v2 wording: the Euclidean
measure itself exists fine; what fails is reconstruction).  The
review validated this Gram as *equivalent* to RP for Gaussian
measures and confirmed the violation by Monte Carlo.

**v1's corollary is dead; the conjecture that survives it.**
v1 stated "RP about every plane ⟺ static" as a corollary.  Two
receipted counterexamples kill it: a *static* free-boundary
chain fails Gram-RP at off-center planes ($-4.0\times 10^{-3}$,

split under the boundary pairing ($+1.3\times 10^{-7}$, so ⟹
fails there).  What survives, **registered as a conjecture**

passes at every plane ($-3.6\times 10^{-17}$), a scrambled ring
