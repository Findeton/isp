## 0. The conjecture

**Object.**  The lowest entanglement-Hamiltonian level
$\varepsilon_1$ of a screen — general definition; for Gaussian
bosonic states $\varepsilon_1 = \min\log\frac{\nu+1/2}{\nu-1/2}$,
for fermionic Gaussian states $\varepsilon_1 = \min|\log\frac{1-n}{n}|$,
for tower cuts under the **declared** class-function/electric-
center algebra choice, the coefficient-ladder gap.  Why
$\varepsilon_1$ and not the low-end spacing: interior two-cut
screens carry exact level degeneracy ($\varepsilon_2 -
\varepsilon_1 = 2\times10^{-14}$, receipted), so spacing is the
wrong object; $\varepsilon_1$ itself is receipted cut-position
invariant (gapped, $4\times10^{-15}$ over four cut classes).

**Screen class.**  Boundary-anchored single cuts AND
two-component cuts (torus/cylinder bipartitions).  The
dichotomy is receipted on single cuts; topological content
lives exactly in the two-component class — which is where the
p+ip receipt operates and why H3 exists.  The gapless bosonic
interior two-cut class (log zero-mode branch) is registered
separately.

**Hypotheses.**  H1 unique vacuum — necessity *cited*
(symmetry-broken degenerate vacua give cat-state entanglement
degeneracy); H2 exponential clustering — *structural* (implied
by gap + H1 on one side, Hastings–Koma; carried for the gapless
side); H3 trivial topological content — necessity *receipted*
(**p+ip**, §3; toric code and Li–Haldane cited as
interacting/chiral analogues).  H3's precise definition is
**declared definitional debt**: the intended bulk-side class is
invertible/short-range-entangled with trivial invariants in the
relevant symmetry class; operationally (at a circularity risk
the declared debt covers), no protected entanglement zero modes
on two-component cuts.  A two-chain construction (coupled
Kitaev wires) delimits further: the plateau can be made
arbitrarily small at fixed bulk gap — so **C46 claims no
quantitative gap-to-plateau bound** — and the decoupled point
is componentwise topological (H3 fails there).

**C46 (positive-plateau saturation form).**  Under H1–H3, the
tower has a mass gap in physical units **iff**
$\varepsilon_1(B)$ — *evaluated in the infinite-volume state at
each tower level* — saturates at a finite block scale $B^*$
(physical size $B^*a$ uniform along the tower) **to a positive
plateau at each fixed tower level**.  Rationale for each
clause: *positivity* is per-level because exact zero modes
satisfy bare saturation trivially (§3) — without it the
criterion is vacuous; the *saturation-scale* form (not a value
bound) because the plateau value decays logarithmically along
any continuum tower (CTM class; consistent with the continuum
BW continuous spectrum) — absolute cross-level value-uniformity
is false in any continuum limit; the *infinite-volume* clause
because finite-volume complement symmetry produces trivial
flattening at $N/2$ (the contamination receipt, §2).  The $B^*$
detector is defined on positive plateaus only.

**Two bridges.**  C46a: modular gap ↔ correlation length
(free-level receipts, known physics).  C46b: tower/transfer gap
↔ physical mass gap (P39's object).  Their identification is
receipted only on the 2d abelian solvable tower (an identity
there); generally **registered**.  C46(⟹) — uniform positive
saturation ⟹ mass gap — *is* P39's uniform tower gap: the wall,
open, **no Clay progress claimed**.  C46(⟸) holds only under
H1–H3 and is also open; neither direction is import-completable
from known theorems: leg 1 (gap ⟹ clustering; Hastings–Koma /
Nachtergaele–Sims, unique vacuum required) is a theorem, but
leg 2 (clustering ⟹ modular-gap bound) has **no known theorem**
and is false in general (p+ip clusters).
