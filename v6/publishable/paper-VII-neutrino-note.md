**Author:** Felix Robles Elvira (ORCID: 0009-0009-2017-4394; independent researcher)

**Status:** preprint, not peer reviewed, version 2026-06-11

## Abstract

We document, with falsifiers and an honest trials accounting, a
comparison between a dimensionless constant arising in a
stochastic-process program for fundamental physics and the neutrino
mass-squared splitting ratio.  The constant
$\varepsilon = 3\kappa - 1 = 0.031768636446582$ derives, with no
Standard-Model
input, from the unique real root of $\theta^3 + \theta^2 + \theta =
1$ (the reciprocal tribonacci constant ‚Äî an *algebraic* number whose
identities $\tanh\eta = e^{-\eta}$, $\theta = e^{-\eta}$, and
$\tanh(\eta/2) = \theta^2$ are elementary and verified) through the
binding analysis now fully self-contained in the submitted
companion [C0]; $\kappa$ is defined in Section 1.
**What is registered ‚Äî rebuilt after two independent hostile
reviews found the previous version's registered claim incoherent,
and we agree.**  The earlier version registered "the exponent
$x_\nu = 1/2$" while simultaneously asserting the program does not
determine the $O(1)$ coefficient $C$ in $m_2/m_3 =
C\varepsilon^{1/2}$.  Those cannot coexist: with one observable,
coefficient and exponent are exactly degenerate ($x = 1/2 + \ln
C/\ln\varepsilon$), and the program's own $68\%$ texture spread
$C \in [0.22, 1.26]$ covers an exponent interval of width $0.506$
‚Äî more than the rung spacing $0.5$, i.e. *a lattice with free
$O(1)$ coefficients covers the entire exponent axis and has zero
discriminating power*.  The registered claim is therefore the
strictly stronger statement the program motivates but does not
derive: the **undressed spectrum point** $m_1 : m_2 : m_3 =
\varepsilon : \sqrt\varepsilon : 1$ exactly (no texture dressing in
the neutrino sector), giving the self-consistent prediction
$\sqrt{\Delta m^2_{21}/\Delta m^2_{31}} =
\sqrt{\varepsilon/(1+\varepsilon)} = 0.175472$ (exact
$\varepsilon$; see the Section 2 precision correction) against the
registration-snapshot fit $0.1718 \pm 0.0026$: a $1.4\sigma$
tension at registration.  **The expected outcome is stated against
our own interest, at both reference centrals**: at the snapshot
central, JUNO-class precision ($\sigma_S \approx 0.00067$)
excludes the registered point at $\approx 5.5\sigma$ ‚Äî the kill
zone; at JUNO's own first measurement (Appendix B: $S = 0.17283$),
the expectation is $\approx 4.0\sigma$ ‚Äî inside the pre-assigned
$(2\sigma, 5\sigma]$ *failed* band rather than the kill zone.
Either way the maximum-likelihood extrapolation is that this claim
*fails*; we register it anyway because the program asserts it, both
outcomes are informative, and a registration that hides its own
expected failure is theater.  The trials accounting is given in
full and corrected (the earlier version misstated its own record:
under the band-grade criterion the charged ratio $m_e/m_\mu$, at
exponent $1.546$, *is* a hit of rung $1.5$).  The spectrum
consequence $\Sigma m_\nu \approx 0.0607$ eV differs from the
normal-ordering floor ($0.0587$ eV) by only $\sim 1.9$ meV, below
projected cosmological sensitivity ‚Äî a consistency statement, not a
discriminating test.  Voiding scenarios, including inverted
ordering, are spelled out, and the running escape hatch is closed
with a computed bound.  Full disclosure up front: the program
separately holds a mechanism-derived *successor* claim set (a
dressed four-member menu with two members live, one of them at
$-0.05\sigma$ against the JUNO first data) ‚Äî cross-declared in the body, with the burden
transfer stated, so this note's expected failure cannot be read as
costless.

## 1. Provenance and definition of the constant

The constant arises in a research program deriving quantum and
gauge-theoretic structure from stochastic processes with sealed
records (the program papers, including the mathematical companions
with full proofs, accompany this note as a citable batch).  The
chain, with its load-bearing identifications named:

1. $\theta = 0.5436890\ldots$ is the unique real root of
   $\theta^3 + \theta^2 + \theta = 1$: an **algebraic** number (the
   reciprocal of the tribonacci constant), produced in the program
   as the fixed point of projective consistency under binary
   refinement of records.  The companion identities
   ($\tanh\eta = e^{-\eta}$ with $\theta = e^{-\eta}$;
   $\tanh(\eta/2) = \theta^2$) are elementary algebra, stated
   because all three forms occur in the program's renormalization
   analysis.
2. $\kappa$ is the **single-relation first-order marginality
   coefficient** of the binding theory ‚Äî the coefficient in the
   exact law $\theta^w(1 - w\kappa)$ ‚Äî computed (not fitted) in
   closed form, $\kappa = \eta(1-\theta^2)/(1-\theta^2+\theta)
   = 0.343922878815527$ (the submitted companion [C0], with exact
   algebraic relations), giving $\varepsilon = 3\kappa - 1 =
   0.031768636446582$; all comparisons below use the exact value
   (Section 2's precision correction records the earlier 3-digit
   rounding).
3. The provenance predates any neutrino comparison by a long,
   internally dated sequence of program papers.  *Honesty note*:
   the numerical proximity to the neutrino ratio was noticed before
   the internal registration and motivated it.  What this note
   adds: precise claim forms, tolerances, falsifiers, the trials
   accounting, and the correction of Appendix A.  We make no
   priority-of-prediction claim for the central-value agreement ‚Äî
   only the forward tests below count.

## 2. The observable and the self-consistent prediction

Under normal ordering with the program's spectrum
$m_1 : m_2 : m_3 = \varepsilon : \sqrt\varepsilon : 1$:

$$
\sqrt{\frac{\Delta m^2_{21}}{\Delta m^2_{31}}}
= \sqrt{\frac{m_2^2 - m_1^2}{m_3^2 - m_1^2}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon - \varepsilon^2}{1 - \varepsilon^2}}
= \sqrt{\frac{\varepsilon}{1+\varepsilon}}
= 0.175472 .
$$

**Precision correction (found in review).**  The registration
snapshot computed this value at 3-digit $\varepsilon = 0.0318$,
printing $0.17556$; at the exact constant ($\varepsilon =
0.031768636446582$, companion [C0]) the registered point is
$\mathbf{0.175472}$ ‚Äî an $8.4\times 10^{-5}$ shift that is
$0.13\sigma$ at this note's JUNO-class precision
($\sigma_S = 0.00067$; it is $0.23\sigma$ at the $\sim 0.00037$
ultimate-precision convention used elsewhere in the program ‚Äî
both stated to avoid a units trap).  The registered *claim* (the
undressed spectrum point) is unchanged; its exact evaluation is
$0.175472$, used everywhere below; the rounded print survives only
in this paragraph as the correction record.

The naive hierarchical formula $\sqrt\varepsilon = 0.17824$ ignores
$m_1$ and errs by $O(\varepsilon) \approx 1.6\%$ ‚Äî comparable to the
experimental precision, so the self-consistent form is mandatory.
Current global-fit inputs (NuFIT 5.x-class, normal ordering,
without SK-atmospheric: $\Delta m^2_{21} = (7.41 \pm 0.21)\times
10^{-5}\,\mathrm{eV}^2$, $\Delta m^2_{31} = (2.511 \pm 0.027)\times
10^{-3}\,\mathrm{eV}^2$; the mild fit correlation between the two
splittings is neglected, which slightly *overstates* our error bar):

$$
S_\nu = 0.1718 \pm 0.0026, \qquad
\frac{S_\nu}{0.175472} = 0.979, \qquad
\text{deviation} = 1.4\sigma .
$$

## 3. The claims, with the trials accounting first

**Trials accounting (read before the claims; corrected twice over
in this version).**  The program's
half-integer exponent lattice spaces rungs $0.5$ apart in
$x = \ln S/\ln\varepsilon$.  All distances are measured to the
*self-consistent* prediction (exponent $x_{\mathrm{pred}} =
0.5045$, not the bare rung $0.5$ ‚Äî an earlier version mixed the two
targets; using one target consistently *strengthens* the
agreement: $|x_\nu - x_{\mathrm{pred}}| = 0.0062$).  (i) The
probability that a random exponent lands within the observed
distance of the prediction is $2(0.0062)/0.5 = 2.5\%$ per trial
(fragile: the distance $0.0062$ carries its own measurement
uncertainty $\sigma_x \approx 0.0044$, so this per-trial figure
is indicative, not sharp).
(ii) A band-tolerant criterion ($|x - \mathrm{rung}| \le 0.065$,
the withdrawn band's width) costs $25.9\%$ per trial.  (iii) The
available RG-clean lepton-sector
ratios number at least three ($m_e/m_\mu$, $m_\mu/m_\tau$ ‚Äî pole
masses, QCD-free ‚Äî and $S_\nu$); if the lattice is read as
sector-wide (the charged-quark steps motivated it), the
quark-sector ratios belong in the band-grade denominator too,
which would further dilute the central-value evidence ‚Äî counted
against us either way.  **Correction of our own record**:
the earlier version stated the charged ratios "do not land on
rungs" (exponents $1.546$ and $0.819$) ‚Äî but under the band-grade
criterion of the same paragraph, $m_e/m_\mu$ at $1.546$ *is* a hit
of rung $1.5$ ($|\Delta x| = 0.046 \le 0.065$).  So the honest
record is: band-grade, $2$ of $3$ trials hit (chance of $\ge 2$
accidental band-grade hits in $3$ trials: $\approx 16.6\%$);
observed-grade, $1$ of $3$ ($\ge 1$ accident: $\approx 7.3\%$).
(iv) *Constant-selection freedom, counted against ourselves*: the
program's small-constant menu ($\theta = 0.544$, $\theta^2 =
0.296$, $\theta^3 = 0.161$ ‚Äî itself within $7\%$ of $S_\nu$ ‚Äî
$\kappa$, $\eta$, $\varepsilon$, $\sqrt\varepsilon$) offers
post-hoc matching freedom; the defense is that the base
$\varepsilon$ and the half-integer lattice were fixed by the
*charged* sector years before the neutrino comparison, but a
skeptic discounts post-hoc readings for menu freedom, and we agree.
(v$'$) *Registered-claim multiplicity, counted*: program-wide
there are three live registered points (this note's, plus the two
live members of [IX]'s four-member menu); a skeptic should treat the ensemble as three
trials on one observable, which further dilutes any
central-value credit ‚Äî counted against us, like everything else
in this section.  (v) *The dressing problem, stated as the
structural lesson*: the
same $O(1)$ Yukawa dressing invoked for the charged misses would,
if available in the neutrino sector, absorb any outcome ‚Äî which is
why the only registrable claim is the undressed point below, and
why the central-value agreement today carries **little evidential
weight**: everything of value is forward-looking.

**Claim S (the registered content: the undressed spectrum point).**
The program asserts the texture-free spectrum
$m_1 : m_2 : m_3 = \varepsilon : \sqrt\varepsilon : 1$ exactly,
hence $S_\nu = \sqrt{\varepsilon/(1+\varepsilon)} = 0.175472$
(exact $\varepsilon$; Section 2) up to
the experimentally-shrinking error.  This is a *point* claim: it is
the program's spectrum, it is falsifiable, and it is strictly
stronger than the exponent-lattice statement (which clause (v)
shows is unfalsifiable while $C$ is free).  **Expected outcome,
computed and pre-committed**: JUNO
($\sigma_{21} \approx 0.6\%$, $\sigma_{31} \approx 0.5\%$, hence
$\sigma_S \approx 0.00067$) yields a deviation of $\approx
5.5\sigma$ at the registration-snapshot central ($\approx
4.0\sigma$ at the JUNO-first central, Appendix B) ‚Äî the claim is
*expected to fail* on either reading.  Pre-committed
readings at JUNO-final precision: within $2\sigma$ = survives
(requires the central values to drift); in $(2\sigma, 5\sigma]$ =
the registration **fails** its survival criterion and we report it
as failed (revivable only by subsequent central-value drift, and we
will not relabel failure as "tension"); beyond $5\sigma$ = the
undressed spectrum point is dead, we say so in an update, and what
dies with it is the program's *no-dressing assertion for the
neutrino sector* ‚Äî the exponent lattice itself survives only in
the unfalsifiable free-$C$ form, which we do not register.

**Consequence (consistency, not test).**  $\Sigma m_\nu =
m_3(1 + \sqrt\varepsilon + \varepsilon) \approx 0.0607$ eV ‚Äî only
$\sim 1.9$ meV above the massless-$m_1$ normal-ordering floor
($0.0587$ eV), below projected
cosmological sensitivity.  Recorded for completeness; we claim no
discriminating power.

**What we do not register.**  (a) No coefficient band: the
program's
own analysis proves the $O(1)$ coefficient is texture, not
structure (it spreads over $[0.22, 1.26]$ at $68\%$ under
unconstrained textures), so any band narrower than that spread
would be drawn around the known value, not derived ‚Äî the earlier
internal version's $[0.80, 1.25]$ band is withdrawn for exactly
this reason.  (b) No exponent-lattice claim with free coefficient:
zero discriminating power, per the abstract's computation.

## 4. Premises and voiding/falsifying scenarios

- **(h) Hierarchy/ordering premise.**  Normal ordering with the
  program spectrum.  *Voided by*: established inverted ordering
  (JUNO/atmospheric ‚Äî listed explicitly as a voiding outcome);
  established quasi-degeneracy from cosmology or endpoint
  measurements.  Voiding is recorded as premise failure, not as a
  pass.
- **F-S**: JUNO-era exclusion of $S_\nu =
  \sqrt{\varepsilon/(1+\varepsilon)}$ at $> 5\sigma$ kills the
  undressed spectrum point; the $(2\sigma, 5\sigma]$ band is
  pre-assigned as *failed* (Section 3) ‚Äî no outcome is left
  unassigned.
- **Running, bounded now (closing the escape hatch an earlier
  version left open).**  The comparison is made at the low-energy
  oscillation scale.  Under Standard-Model running (no extended
  Higgs sector), the flavor-dependent part of the neutrino
  mass-ratio evolution is governed by the $\tau$ Yukawa:
  $|\Delta(m_2/m_3)|/(m_2/m_3) \lesssim
  (y_\tau^2/16\pi^2)\ln(\Lambda/m_Z)\cdot O(1) \approx 1.04\times
  10^{-4}/158 \times 23 \times O(1) \approx 5\times 10^{-5}$ for
  matching scales up to $\Lambda = 10^{12}$ GeV ‚Äî four orders below
  JUNO precision.  The registration is pinned to this: SM running,
  $\Lambda \le 10^{12}$ GeV, declared now.  A future exclusion
  cannot be attributed to running within these premises; invoking
  an extended sector (e.g. large-$\tan\beta$ two-doublet running,
  which can reach percent level) would itself be a premise change
  and is pre-declared as such, not as a rescue.
