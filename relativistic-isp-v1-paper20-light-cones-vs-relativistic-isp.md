## Abstract

The reason the earlier pictures can still feel unclear is that the difference is not merely geometric. The ordinary lightcone picture answers an *event question*: what can influence a given event, and what unfolds event by event? The relativistic ISP picture answers a different question: what exact Γ[Σ₁←Σ₀] connects the configuration spaces of two whole Cauchy hypersurfaces, how does that kernel change when only a bounded support region is deformed, and what is left when two such deformations are exchanged in opposite orders? The guided animation below is built around that replacement of the basic story unit.

**Difference in one line.** The lightcone picture is centered on an event and its causal future/past. The relativistic ISP picture is centered on an exact finite kernel between whole hypersurfaces, then on localized deformation maps J_R(Δ), and finally on the exchange defect E_R,S(Δ).

That is why the animations below show two different kinds of story. On the left, events happen one after another. On the right, the primitive object is a whole-surface slab comparison, and only afterward do localized deformations and order comparisons enter.

**Scope of the comparison.** Standard relativistic physics also has hypersurface, regional-algebra, and Cauchy-evolution formulations. The contrast here is not that ordinary relativity lacks global or algebraic language; it is that the ISP program chooses the exact stochastic kernel between whole configuration spaces as the primitive object, and only then asks how localized supported deformations exchange.

### The decisive test changes

**single local update** → **compare** R →right S *versus* S →right R and inspect the resulting exchange defect

## What the animations are trying to teach

### The left side is event-centered.

You watch one event produce a causal future, or a chain of later events. That is the right picture when the question is “what can influence this event?”

### The right side is whole-surface-centered.

You begin from two hypersurfaces and an exact Γ-level kernel between their ordinary configuration spaces. The story is not built event by event first.

### Temporal indivisibility is visualized by a failed split.

In the ISP story, you can try to cut the slab at an intermediate hypersurface, but the algebraic factor through that cut need not remain an honest stochastic map.

### Locality lives in supported deformations.

“Local” does not mean “a single pointlike kick detached from the whole slab.” It means a bounded support region on the hypersurface whose deformation is compared against the reference slab.

### The decisive integrability datum is the exchange defect.

The essential question is not simply whether there is a local generator, but what happens when the exact finite deformations of two separated regions are exchanged.

### The leading free-model data are boundary-first.

The explorer fades from support volumes toward gap-facing boundaries and bonds because that is where the first nontrivial exchange data naturally live in the sharpened free-model picture.

**Compressed moral.**

The lightcone picture asks for the causal domain of an event. The relativistic ISP picture asks how an exact whole-surface Γ-kernel changes under localized supported deformations, whether the resulting comparison remains decomposable through an intermediate slice, and whether two separated deformations exchange trivially or leave a gap-localized, boundary-controlled defect.
