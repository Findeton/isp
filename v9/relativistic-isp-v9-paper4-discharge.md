## 3. Pillar two — volume [THEOREM-track; the write-out]

**Lemma W1 (geometric ergodicity by coupling; PROOF for the geometric corner; hypothesis split disclosed).** *Geometric corner (hazard 1/L at every age — the canonical member; all receipts).* Couple two copies of the fleet process with shared innovations (the same slot choices, increments, and lifetime coins). Because the hazard is age-free, the shared coin ends slot i's life **in both copies simultaneously**; on that first joint regeneration the copies agree on slot i thereafter (the reset wipes content; shared increments preserve equality), and a matched slot is never unmatched (the victim is the committing slot — innovation-determined). Slot i regenerates jointly with probability exactly 1/(LM) per commit, so the coupling time T = max over the M slots' first-regeneration times (these share the choice sequence and are negatively dependent; the union bound needs no independence) obeys `P(T > t) ≤ M·e^{−t/(LM)}` and `E[T] ≤ LM(ln M + 1)` (coupon collector). The coupling inequality bounds sup-TV; for a stationary Markov chain β(t) = E_{x∼π}‖P^t(x,·) − π‖_TV ≤ sup-TV, giving `β(t) ≤ M·e^{−t/(LM)}` and `τ_mix = O(LM·log M)`; the derived pair sequence (t/N, χ_t) is a measurable functional of (state, innovation), so its β is bounded by the chain's. ∎ *Hazard-floor laws* (hazard ≥ h₀ > 0 at every age): the same proof with 1/(LM) → h₀/M. *Aperiodic non-lattice laws*: the joint-regeneration event needs the copies' age offsets to align; single-sided resets randomize the offset and span-1 support makes the offset walk hit 0 — the standard renewal offset-randomization argument, **not written here** [obligation, carried; expected cost a constant O(L²)]. *Lattice laws (deterministic-L)*: the fleet chain is **periodic with period L** — the total-age mod L is a deterministic clock, so `β(t) ≥ 1 − 1/L` for all t and geometric β-mixing of the commit-time chain is **unrescuable** (the round-14 audit's counterexample, adopted). W1 then holds for the **L-skeleton** (the phase-decomposed chain, which is aperiodic and couples as above), and W2–W4 consume only phase-averaged empirical statements over windows ≫ L, which the skeleton supplies. ## 4. Pillar three — statistics [THEOREM-track; the write-out]

**Lemma P0 (ranking measurability).** A band interval count is a function of the pair (commit order, value ranking) alone — counts are order-functionals — so the race model (note-u4 Addendum; validated exactly by u4a) governs their joint law: conditional on the life partition, distinct lives' value sets are independent Poisson arrival prefixes and the ranking follows the sequential race. ∎ (near-definitional; stated for the record.)

**Lemma P1 (the dependency graph — value space) [PROOF-SKETCH; re-homed per the round-14 audit F2].** For *value-space* intervals (thresholds on (t, χ) directly), conditional on the life partition two point-indicators are dependent only if their points share a life (distinct lives' value sets are independent under the race model), and the dependency neighborhoods are the lives, with the per-point partner count the **size-biased** quantity E[S(S−1)]/E[S] (geometric-L: 2(L−1); deterministic-L: L−1) — finite exactly because sub-geometric tails give E[S²] < ∞ (audit F10; this hinge is what P3's divergence tests). For *rank-space* intervals — the instrument's — the indicator depends on all lives through the global rank map: the extra dependence is precisely the margins conditioning of Lemma P4 (at L = 1 the rank-space counts are hypergeometric, not Poisson — P4 is the counterexample to a naive rank-space P1). The rank-space statement is therefore a *corollary of the value-space theorem plus the P4 margins term*, not a separate independence claim. ∎(sketch)

**Lemma P2 (Chen–Stein; the two regimes) [value space: PROOF-SKETCH; capped rank space: rate corrected per audit F3].** *Value space (thin bands, p_i = λ/N over ≤ N points):* by Arratia–Goldstein–Gordon conditional on the life partition, b₁ (including its diagonal Σp_i² = λ²/N) = O(λ²L/N) with the size-biased L-factor of P1, and b₂ (same-life joint landings) = O(λ²L/N) by the one-exponential-step two-sided estimate [the constants of that estimate are the carried sketch obligation]; de-conditioning costs a second-order mixture term O(Var λ(Π)/λ) = O(λ²L/N) (per-life CLT over ~N/L lives) — **net TV = O(λ²L/N), the dependency-density rate, in value space** [obligations: the b₂ constants; the de-conditioning lemma's write-out]. *Margin-capped rank space (the instrument's regime, sides Θ(√N) forced by λ ∈ [10, 40] and the √(40N) cap):* the value→rank transfer imports the P4 margins term, and the Barbour–Hall lower bound (TV ≥ (1/32)(1∧1/λ)Σp_i², with Σp_i² = Θ(λ^(3/2)/√N) under the cap) shows **no O(1/N)-type rate is attainable — the true rate is Θ_λ(√(λ/N))**, achieved: the margins deficit ≈ (a+b)/N = Θ(√(λ/N)) and the dependence excess ≈ c_L·b/N are the two opposite-signed vanishing terms, with the L-dependence living in the **signed control-relative excess** (law − box) — exactly the functional the t23 review migrated the receipts to (V3‴).
