## 3. Pillar two — volume [THEOREM-track; the write-out]

**Lemma W1 (geometric ergodicity by coupling; proof).** Couple two copies of the fleet process with shared innovations (the same slot choices, increments, and lifetime coins). On the first regeneration of slot i (the shared coin ends its life in both copies), the copies agree on slot i thereafter — the reset wipes the content and subsequent shared increments preserve equality. Slot i regenerates with probability ≥ 1/(c·LM) per commit (touch × hazard; c absorbs the sub-geometric tail constant), so the coupling time T = max over M slots of independent geometric-tailed times obeys `E[T] = O(L·M·log M)` (coupon collector) and `P(T > t) ≤ M·e^{−t/(cLM)}`. Hence the chain has a unique stationary law and is geometrically ergodic with `β(t) ≤ M·e^{−t/(cLM)}` — the β-mixing coefficient W4 needs, and `τ_mix = O(LM·log M)`. ∎ [Measured joints: the fleet-total e-folding lag 67 → 121/123 under L- and M-doubling — ratios 1.80/1.84 vs the ×2 predictions; receipt w1-V2.]

## 4. Pillar three — statistics [THEOREM-track; the write-out]

**Lemma P0 (ranking measurability).** A band interval count is a function of the pair (commit order, value ranking) alone — counts are order-functionals — so the race model (note-u4 Addendum; validated exactly by u4a) governs their joint law: conditional on the life partition, distinct lives' value sets are independent Poisson arrival prefixes and the ranking follows the sequential race. ∎ (near-definitional; stated for the record.)

**Lemma P1 (the dependency graph).** Under the race-model conditional law, two point-indicators for a fixed interval are dependent only if their points share a life. The dependency neighborhoods are the lives: sizes with sub-geometric law, mean L. ∎

**Lemma P2 (Chen–Stein; the b₁/b₂ arithmetic).** For the count W of points in a margin-capped band interval with E[W] = λ ∈ [10, 40]: by Arratia–Goldstein–Gordon, `TV(W, Poisson(λ)) ≤ b₁ + b₂` with b₁ = Σ_{i~j} p_i p_j and b₂ = Σ_{i~j} p_{ij} over same-life pairs. Per point p_i = O(λ/N); each point has O(L) same-life partners (sub-geometric mean); summing over the ≤ N points contributing: b₁ = O(λ²L/N); the joint term b₂ — two same-life points both landing in the interval — is controlled by the life's comonotone geometry: consecutive same-life values are one exponential step apart, so the pair lands in a side-√(40N)-capped interval with probability O(λL/N)·per-pair, giving b₂ = O(λL/N)·(the same-life pair density N-normalization) [the density itself measured exactly: 2(L−1)/(N−1)-form within 9%/3% at N = 1024/4096; t23-V4]. Net: `TV = O(λ²L/N)` per interval — the dependency-density rate. [PROOF-SKETCH grade: the b₂ geometric step needs the two-sided landing estimate written with constants; obligation carried to the ledger, not hidden.] [Measured: the capped churn-L2 band statistic at N = 4096 sits at +0.0197 ± 0.0220 from exact Poisson — within 1 SE of the ideal; the uncapped band gap decays 0.381 → 0.191 (churn) and 0.476 → 0.287 (box); t23 runs 3–4.]
