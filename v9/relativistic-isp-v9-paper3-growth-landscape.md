# The growth landscape of the derived action: the grader does not build

**Series:** Relativistic ISP, v9 paper 3. **Status:** WRITTEN 2026-07-05, REVISED same date after review round 1 (two referees) + the two receipts that review demanded (v9 rounds 6–9; receipts `s5_faithful_guided.py`, `s6_repricing_test.py`, `s6b_mechanism_family.py`, `s7_stage_priced.py`, `s8_paper3_repairs.py`; design notes note-s5 through note-s8 — every gate below was pinned in its note before its receipt ran). **Grades:** [DEMONSTRATED] at n = 256–512 (one kernel/menu class) unless marked; one [THEOREM-level] integral and two exact per-step oracle bounds are marked in place; review-measured quantities are attributed in place.

## Abstract

The derived action of this series — Row A per-pair charges (each qualifying pair, relata count k ≥ m₀ = 8, pays `(r − ½)²`; other pairs pay the diffuse ¼) plus Row C, the global comparability block `(n(n−1)/2)(r − ½)²` — is a proven **grader**: faithful webs are cheap (`Θ(N log²N)` at the measured H-var exponent, paper 1), forged webs pay `Ω(n²)` walls (papers 1–2), and nothing in this paper touches those results. This paper answers the remaining question: can the same action **build** — does stepwise increment-minimization grow faithful webs? The answer is no, in five receipted parts — and the builder that does work is certified in part five.

**(1) The landscape verdict.** Along forced-faithful trajectories with the true ancestor set inserted into every menu (14 candidates: truth + 12 kernel decoys + the empty set), the increment-argmin selects the true move at **3.9%** of steps (pinned rejection line 30%; exchangeable null 7.1–7.7%, directionally below chance at rep-level t = −2.76, 4 reps). Free-run endpoints under the same guided menus stay fully forged (interior heredity r_I 0.80–0.92 against the faithful band 0.35–0.65; interior abundance misfit ≈ 15× faithful).

**(2) The mechanism [committed-stream-receipted].** The rejection is carried by Row C's **transient**: the faithful prefix of a 2D box order under the volume (u+v) arrival order runs at comparability **exactly ⅓** in the sub-diagonal regime ([THEOREM-level] one-line integral, §3.1) against Row C's target ½ — an *equilibrium* value attained only at completion (measured profile 0.327 → 0.504). The block increment therefore carries a per-pair gradient `2(r_t − ½) ≈ −⅓` that pays the argmin to take the *largest* candidate at every step: at 93.1% of truth-losses the winner is larger than truth, the true move is Row-A-*cheaper* at 93.0%, and |Row C| exceeds |Row A| at **93.8%** of loss decisions. Increments of an endpoint functional are not a growth law.

**(3) The single-lever re-pricings are closed [exact oracle, raw base].** Centering the pair term by its exact finite-sample variance leaves the rate at chance (6.1%, t = −1.42 vs the null); the allowance form `ΔS̃ = ΔS − c·|D|` on the raw base is *monotone worse* in c (3.9% → 0.4% across c = 0…0.24, paired on one committed stream); **90.4%** of steps carry a decoy both larger and raw-cheaper than truth, so truth loses there at every c ≥ 0; and the raw-base per-step oracle over all admissible profiles c(t) ∈ [0, ¼) caps at **7.7% ≈ chance**. The stage-priced global form (Row C's ½ replaced by the derived faithful stage profile ρ(t); the endpoint functional — the grader — returned to at completion up to the printed calibration error) is REJECTED at **1.3%** with its registered mechanism signature confirmed: the transient collapses (Row-C dominance 93.8% → 5.7%) and the selection flips to Row A — **the argmin's preferred response to a faithful arrival becomes the empty set** (97.0% of losses, paying Row-A median 0.000 against truth's 6.401; exact-centering control 0.26% [review-measured]).

**(4) The pincer — and the seam.** Two receipted forces jointly squeeze the true move: endpoint-anchored density targets reward *over-builders* (§3.2); neutralized targets expose per-pair charges rewarding *do-nothings* (§4.1). The one member sitting in neither jaw — the **fully-centered joint arm**, both terms charged as excess over their faithful expectations — was run at the review's demand (§4.3): it *triples* the raw rate and still lands **REJECTED at 12.9%** at the derived point c̄ (reps 0.0–22.4%; chance-consistent, t = +1.23), with a **humped** family curve peaking exactly at c̄. The two-term family's per-step oracle caps at **46.4%** [exact oracle, staged base]: above the 30% rejection line — the jaws do not quite meet; a per-step window exists — but **below the 60% preference line: no admissible increment profile in the two-term class, fixed, derived, or per-step-tuned, can make the true move *preferred***. The best constant member reaches 12.9% against the 46.4% ceiling (the window demands per-step coefficient variation no single profile provides — the profile question is a named open, not a rescue: the free-run bench stays forged at every tested point, m_ab ≈ 10× at the joint optimum). Within the single-lever arms the squeeze strengthens with n (truth's Row-A bill grows without bound — Θ(polylog m): the ¼-payers thin as Θ(log m), qualifying pairs pay Var(r_k) → 0; measured 0.080/element — while the skimpy penalty stays Θ(1) ≈ 2ρ² [review-derived structure; receipt owed]); for the joint member the required coefficient *falls* with n, so no scale claim is made class-wide.

**(5) The closure, and the certificate.** Increment-optimization is closed as a production route at **five actions** — raw, variance-centered, allowance, stage-priced, joint — with a receipted mechanism at each step, and the entire two-term family is closed *against preference* by the oracle bound. Within the attachment-kernel move class the division of labor stands: **the action grades; churn builds** — and the second half now carries its own receipt: the churn corner's webs, put under this paper's interior instruments for the first time, come out **indistinguishable from sprinklings** (r_I 0.496–0.573, 8/8 reps in the faithful band across N = 256 and 512; m_ab 0.8–0.9× the faithful base — where every action arm measured 10–20×) [DEMONSTRATED, n ≤ 512, j3 corner; uniform-victim variant INFO]. Path-level (non-Markovian) pricing would be a C4 class extension requiring its own corpus-legality argument — named, out of scope.

## 1. The question and the frame

Papers 1–2 established the action's grader credentials and left one fork open (paper 2 §6): is the forgery of grown webs a property of the *kernel* (fixable by menu enrichment) or of the *action's marginal landscape* (fatal to increment-growth as such)? The faithful-guided control decides it. Expressibility is trivial — the attachment class rebuilds a sprinkling bit-exactly from its own down-sets (receipt anchor G-O) — so the fork is **action-preference**: with the truth on the menu, does the argmin take it? Note the direction of robustness: a rejection measured with the truth *on* the menu is a fortiori robust to menu enrichment — more candidates can only lower an argmin share.

**Design (note-s5, pinned).** Substrate: 2D box-order sprinklings, n = 256, arrival order u+v (a linear extension of the true order). Forced-faithful trajectories: the web is kept exactly faithful; at each step t ≥ 64 the true ancestor set competes against 12 kernel decoys (log-uniform seed fraction up to 0.6, closed downward through the current web) and the empty set — 14 candidates, mean 0.60 duplicate-empties, exchangeable null in [1/14, 1/13] = [7.1%, 7.7%]. Win condition tie-generous (`ΔS_truth ≤ min`). Branches pinned: rate ≥ 0.6 TRUTH-PREFERRED / ≤ 0.3 TRUTH-REJECTED / else MIXED. Four reps; every cross-arm rate comparison in this paper is unpaired and seed-confounded — [directional] — and no mechanism claim rides one: mechanisms ride within-stream decompositions and paired family curves only.

## 2. The landscape verdict

**G1 = TRUTH-REJECTED at 3.9%** (reps 2.1/3.6/7.3/2.6%; rep-level t vs 1/14 = −2.76 — directionally below chance, the anti-faithful direction). **G2 refused:** free-run growth under guided menus (truth always offered) takes the true move at only 5.1–7.5% of steps — itself chance-level — and lands forged endpoints: r_I 0.804–0.916 (0/4 in the faithful band), interior abundance misfit 1.283 ≈ 15× the faithful base. The offer gap (Jaccard distance of the best decoy from truth) is 0.553 pooled, roughly declining 0.65 → 0.38 across arrival-deciles before snapping to 0.64 at the endpoint decile (the printed decile vector is the record) — the menus are not starved of truth-like options; the action declines them.

## 3. The mechanism

### 3.1 The transient, exact

For two iid uniform points in the triangle T = {u, v ≥ 0, u + v ≤ s} (the sub-diagonal prefix region, scale-free): `P(u₁<u₂ ∧ v₁<v₂) = ∫_T (1−u−v)²/2 · du dv ÷ |T|² = (½·B(2,3))/¼ = 1/6`, so the prefix comparability is **ρ = ⅓ exactly**, against the completed box order's ½. The calibrated profile reproduces it (0.3434 flat-window mean; 0.4904 at completion; s6b point values 0.327/0.327/0.383/0.504). Row C's target is right at the endpoint and wrong along the entire path: the block increment's per-pair gradient `2(r_t − ½)` sits at ≈ −⅓ early and reaches 0 only at the final arrival. The closed form for the rising segment (s > 1) is an open integral — deferred, calibration covers it.

### 3.2 The decomposition, on the committed stream

Receipt s6b replays the s5 stream exactly (proven: the replay reproduces 3.9% and the rep vector bit-for-bit) and decomposes every loss: the winner is **larger** than truth at 93.1% (menu-maximum at 73.7%); truth is **Row-A-cheaper** at 93.0% (mean gap −6.2 in truth's favor); the Row-C gap runs +16.7 against truth; |Row C| > |Row A| at **93.8%**. The winners are not undercutting truth's per-pair prices — they are harvesting the transient gradient with sheer size. Retro-consistently: the small-n growth successes of paper 1's bench lived below m₀ (no qualifying interiors to forge), and the forgery onset tracked the first quadratic interiors.

### 3.3 The single-lever closures (raw base)

On the same committed stream, paired on one evaluation per candidate: the allowance family `ΔS̃ = ΔS − c·|D|` scores 3.9 → 2.7 → 1.2 → 0.5 → 0.4 → 0.4% across c = 0, 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20, 0.24 — **monotone against truth** on the raw base (the subsidy pays exactly the oversized decoys Row C already favors). **Dominance:** 90.4% of steps carry a decoy both larger and raw-cheaper; at those steps truth loses at every c ≥ 0 (0/768 dominated steps are tie-only [review-verified]). **Oracle:** the per-step feasibility windows over c(t) ∈ [0, ¼) — ¼ being the wall bound above which the link wall dies — admit a truth-win at only **7.7%** of steps. Any fixed or derived profile assigns one c per step, so the oracle upper-bounds the entire raw-base family: closed. The variance-centered arm (subtracting the exact U-statistic `Var(r_k) = (2/(k(k−1)))(2(k−2)/36 + ¼)` from qualifying charges) is a near-null perturbation by construction — it removes ~4% of the faithful floor (4.15% under the receipt's per-arrival profile convention [review-verified]; the balance is sub-m₀ ¼ charges it does not touch) — and measures 6.1%, statistically indistinguishable from chance (t = −1.42): licensed reading "no preference", not "anti-preference". The arrival-level allowance at the calibrated faithful mean (c̄: mean 0.1038, max 0.1781, sd 0.0410 — all < ¼, walls preserved) measures 3.9% (t = −3.06, directionally below chance).

## 4. The stage-priced form, the pincer, and the seam

### 4.1 Arm C (note-s7, pinned)

`S̃_comp(nrel, n; t) = (n(n−1)/2)(r − ρ(t))²` with ρ(t) the calibrated faithful stage profile — the derivation-shaped repair aimed at the binding term. The endpoint functional (the grader) is returned to at completion up to the printed calibration error (0.4904 vs ½; walls robust). Consistency gate C-ρ held (the calibration reproduces the derived ⅓ as the pinned window mean; the gate is an integrity check, measured weak — ~21% population false-refusal — not a sharp instrument).

**Result: G1 = REJECTED at 1.3%** (reps 0.5/3.1/1.0/0.5%; t = −9.42, df 3 — p ≈ 1.3×10⁻³, the strongest below-chance reading of the campaign). **D1, the registered mechanism signature, confirmed:** Row-C dominance collapses 93.8% → **5.7%**; winner-larger collapses 93.1% → **2.1%**; the Row-A gap flips sign, −6.2 → **+6.2 against truth**. The residual Row-C gap (−2.1, mildly *for* truth) is the calibration-residual imprint (per-rep r_t − ρ RMS 0.033 [review-measured]) and favors truth — the rejection is conservative. **The winner anatomy (in-receipt):** the empty set wins **97.0%** of losses; A_winner mean 0.164, median 0.000, |D| median 0 — against truth's A = 6.401 over |D| = 80.5; the Row-A gap is positive at 98.2% of losses. Under the review's exact-centering control (target = each rep's own realized path; calibration residue removed identically) the rate falls to **0.26%** with dominance 0.0% [review-measured]. Free-run endpoints under arm C: r 0.434–0.442 (in the guard window — the profile steers the global rate by construction), r_I 0.866–0.895, m_ab 1.590 = 15.0× faithful — forged as at every arm.

### 4.2 The pincer

Two real forces, each receipted in its own regime: **(i)** any endpoint-anchored global density target mis-prices the transient and rewards *maximal* candidates (§3.2); **(ii)** with the transient neutralized, the per-pair charges reward *minimal* candidates — the true move pays Θ(polylog m) in Row A (the ¼-paying sub-m₀ pairs thin as Θ(log m); qualifying pairs pay the vanishing variance floor; measured 0.080 per charged element) while the empty move's stage penalty stays Θ(1) ≈ 2ρ² [review-derived structure; receipt owed]. The raw action's 3.9%, the centered arm's chance-level 6.1%, the raw-base allowance's monotone decline, and arm C's 1.3% are one landscape seen from four angles. Within these single-lever arms the squeeze widens with n; the class-wide scale question is cut off, not settled, by §4.3's member (its required coefficient falls with n) — no claim in either direction.

### 4.3 The joint arm — the seam measured (note-s8, pinned at the review's demand)

The one member sitting in neither jaw: **ΔS̃ = [ΔS_A − c·|D|] + the stage-priced block increment** — both terms charged as excess over their faithful expectations, the derivation shape of note-s6 §1 *completed*. The review's registered prediction — the allowance rescues truth from the empty set at c̄ — is borne out by the hump (the empty set's score is c-invariant while truth's falls by c̄·|D|); the residual losses fall to the large decoys [review-arithmetic; a winner-anatomy print at c̄ is not receipted]. The large-decoy question was pinned as the open one; the measurement decided it. Measured paired on one evaluation per candidate (fresh seed; calibrations in-run, ρ flat 0.3159 held vs ⅓, c̄ = 0.1115):

- **The joint family curve is humped** — 0.1% (c = 0, arm C's anchor) → 8.1% → **12.9% at c̄** → 10.7% → 7.3% → 9.6%: the two centerings interact; the derived point is the constant-c optimum. The joint arm **triples the raw rate and stays REJECTED** (12.9% vs the 30% line; reps 0.0–22.4% — large heterogeneity, disclosed; t = +1.23 vs chance, n.s.).
- **Staged-base dominance nearly vanishes**: 7.4% of steps (vs 90.4% on the raw base) — the jaws, separately near-total, do not close on this member.
- **The two-term oracle [exact]: 46.4%** of steps admit some c ∈ [0, ¼) rescuing truth. Above 30%: the family is **not** blanket-rejected — a live per-step window exists (the pinned surviving-arm branch, recorded as such). Below 60%: **the family is closed against preference** — no admissible profile, constant, derived, or per-step-tuned, can make the true move preferred, since the oracle dominates every profile. The gap between the best constant member (12.9%) and the ceiling (46.4%) measures how much per-step coefficient variation the window demands; whether any *derivable* profile lands in the MIXED band (0.3–0.6) is a named open — not a production rescue: **the free-run bench refuses at the joint optimum too** (r centers at 0.491–0.503; r_I 0.751–0.900, 0/4 in-band; m_ab 10.1× faithful).

## 5. What stands

- **The grader, untouched.** Every wall, floor, and pricing result of papers 1–2 concerns the endpoint functional, which no arm modified (arm C and the joint arm return to it at completion by construction). The action's standing after this paper is *exactly* its proven one: it separates faithful from forged webs; it does not manufacture them.
- **The division of labor [DEMONSTRATED — three selection rules on the raw action (paper 2: uniform, argmin, rate; n = 512) plus the argmin under four re-priced forms (variance-centered, allowance, stage-priced, joint; n = 256), one kernel/menu class; independence across move classes untested].** Global comparability is selection-controllable (the rate rule centers r at 0.500–0.501; the joint arm at 0.491–0.503; argmin variants land 0.43–0.48); interior fine structure is move-class-inherited (r_I at the kernel floor — pooled 0.73–0.91 under pure-kernel menus, to 0.916 under guided menus — across every selection rule and action form; m_ab ≈ 10–20× faithful across n = 256–512).
- **The builder, certified at the arc's own instruments.** The churn corner (paper 16's j3: fleet M = 32, lifetime L = 2, trivial placement; the causal order = the dominance order on its (b, χ) stream), which never consults increments, was put under this paper's interior instruments for the first time: **r_I 0.500–0.573 (N = 256) and 0.496–0.511 (N = 512), 8/8 reps in the faithful band; m_ab 0.9× and 0.8× the matching-N faithful base** — indistinguishable from sprinklings at the instruments that measured every action arm at 10–20×. The uniform-victim kernel variant concurs (INFO rows, r_I 0.526–0.557). With its three-layer cell membership (paper 16) this makes churn the certified builder at n ≤ 512; its ensemble-level test (the exact induced-measure census against the 0.2405/0.3424 goalposts) is the standing next step (note-u4).
- **Named opens.** The joint-family profile question (does any *derivable* c(t) approach the 46.4% ceiling into the MIXED band?); the pincer's n-dependence as a measurement (the Θ(polylog)-vs-Θ(1) structure is review-derived; its receipt is owed; the joint member's threshold falls with n, so no class-wide scale claim is available in either direction); the closed form of ρ(t) on the rising segment; path-level pricing as a C4 class extension (own design note + corpus-legality argument required — the click law's C1 discipline forbids history-dependent *rates*, and any path functional must thread that needle).
