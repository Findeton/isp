## 1. The gap restated

## 2. Candidate routes (in rising ambition)

- **R1 — the light-cone-lattice transplant.** The census theorem (v9 paper 4 corner: dim ≤ 2) means the web's order embeds in 2 dimensions; the two-clock coordinates (rank_b, rank_χ) give a light-cone-like lattice whose causal edges approximate the Hasse relation. Build paper 5's Wilson→overlap operator on that induced lattice (links = Hasse covers where present; defects where the lattice is irregular). **Risk (must be controlled by design):** the (rank_b, rank_χ) embedding is supply-derived — the round-16 lesson applies verbatim; any ml4 claim must carry the supply-surrogate must-fail control from day one. **Cost:** near paper-5 (the operator is sparse; fleets of ~10³–10⁴ commits are the natural scale).
- **R2 — the direct Hasse-Dirac.** A staggered-type operator on the DAG itself (incidence-based, no embedding). Unknowns: fermion doubling on irregular graphs; whether ANY γ₅-analog exists such that a GW relation can hold exactly (the machinery's non-negotiable). Research-grade risk; no known template in the corpus.
- **R3 — the resampling bridge.** Poisson-resample the web's (b, χ) volume onto a regular lattice, run paper 5 verbatim, pull back per-thread. Cheapest — but it imports the sprinkling assumption the discreteness line explicitly does not own for grown webs (the scout's Gap-2), and the pullback re-introduces supply alignment. Fallback only.
