# T3.1 — the irreducibility theorem: the click law's randomness is irreducible at the process level, relocatable between channels, and unfalsifiable-by-marginals

**Status:** theorem note, 2026-07-05 (PLAN.md T3.1; round 2; target relocated per note-q3). Receipt: `v9/code/t31_irreducibility_exhibits.py` (the boundary exhibits; gates in docstring, pinned pre-run). `u5` is redesigned by §4 and stays owed.

## 1. What the law is (the q3 split, one level deeper)

Note-q3 fixed the law-level object: the per-lineage `Exp(1)` *evidence* marginal. The §71 derivation pins more than the marginal: eventless gluing `S(I+J) = S(I)·S(J)` is a statement about the *conditional* survival — having survived evidence `I`, the residual waiting law is fresh. **The click law is a process-level (conditional-law) claim:** given the committed past, the next inter-commit evidence gap is `Exp(1)` — memoryless, atomless, history-blind on the clock layer (history lives in the odometers and the web, never the clock — C1). Everything below follows from taking that seriously.

## 2. The theorem (three legs)

**Theorem (irreducibility at the process level).** Let the stack's next gap be `g_t = T(H_t, ξ_t)` with `H_t` the committed past, `T` deterministic, and `ξ_t` the fresh randomness available at step `t` (the content channel's spread draw; `ξ_t ≡ const` if `w = 0` and the menu is deterministic). The conditional law of `g_t` given `H_t` is the push-forward of `ξ_t` through `T(H_t, ·)`. Then:

- **(i) Zero-randomness stacks cannot realize the law [THEOREM, one line].** If the stack is deterministic given the committed past (`ξ` degenerate), the conditional law is a point mass — and `Exp(1)` has no atoms. No hypothesis on state size, equivariance, or chaos is needed: *at the process level, determinism-given-the-record is excluded unconditionally.*
- **(ii) Finite-alphabet randomness cannot realize the law exactly [THEOREM, quantitative].** If `ξ_t` takes at most `m` values (atomic content, `w = 0`, random mode choice), the conditional law of `g_t` has at most `m` atoms; its largest atom has mass `≥ 1/m`, so its Kolmogorov–Smirnov distance from `Exp(1)` is `≥ 1/(2m)` — bounded away, uniformly over histories and horizons. Finite menus cannot make a continuum.
- **(iii) Continuous spread can realize the law — by relocation, not removal [THEOREM + exhibit].** With `w > 0` continuous content draws `U_t`, the stack `g_t = F_exp^{−1}(Φ_{H_t}(U_t))` (any history-indexed measurable map pushing `U_t` to uniform, then inverse-CDF) realizes the conditional `Exp(1)` *exactly* — a "deterministic committer" that is stochastic given the record because it consumes the content channel's randomness. What is conserved: the process `(contents, gaps)` is never deterministic given the past — the law's stochasticity is **irreducible in total and interchangeable between the clock and any continuous physical channel**. The corpus's own magnitude no-go (#35: `w` generically nonzero, [PHYSICAL]) says nature holds such a channel.

**Corollary (the Barandes reading, earned).** Division-event stochasticity is irreducible *by the corpus's own arithmetic* at the process level — note 8 §2's mini-theorem, resolved in the strong form for legs (i)–(ii) and in the conservation form for leg (iii). Combined with the D-axis measurements (placement determinism beneficial — `u3`/`y1`; committer determinism churn-laundered — `u3`/`u3b`): every layer of the stack may be determinized *except one continuous channel*, and which channel carries the randomness (clock vs content) is not fixed by the law. The G2-strict conjecture becomes: the statistical layer needs exactly this one channel.

## 3. The boundary, honestly: marginals cannot close leg (i)

The theorem lives at the conditional level. The **marginal** reading — the empirical distribution of gaps along a lineage — is *not closable against determinism*: an infinite-state deterministic stack can equidistribute. **Exhibit (the Weyl stack):** `g_t = F_exp^{−1}(frac(t·φ))`, `φ` the golden ratio — deterministic, zero randomness, and by Weyl equidistribution its empirical gap law converges to `Exp(1)`: it passes any single-run marginal KS gate asymptotically. So a marginal-only gate (as `u5` was first sketched, and as `u3`'s D2 gate measured) can catch fixed-menu finite stacks (leg (ii)'s floor transfers to empirical marginals only for history-independent atom locations) but **cannot in principle** decide the process-level question. This is the corpus's undecidability boundary (paper X / f6) reappearing at the clock layer: passive single-time statistics are regime-blind; the discriminator must be *conditional*.

## 4. The operational face — u5's gate, redesigned

What distinguishes the *exhibited* Weyl stack from the law is the joint structure: under the law successive gaps are independent; under a **gap-autonomous** zero-randomness stack (`g_{t+1} = ψ(g_t)` — the linear-Weyl/fixed-menu class) the pair `(g_t, g_{t+1})` is supported on a curve — singular, detectable. **Scoping correction (review round, counterexample executed): this is NOT universal** — the quadratic Weyl stack `g_t = F⁻¹(frac(t²φ))` is zero-randomness and passes *both* gates (the pair `(t²φ, (t+1)²φ)` jointly equidistributes), and degree-`k` Weyl stacks defeat `k`-time batteries: **the undecidability of §3 persists for every fixed finite test battery** — the two-time gate extends the marginal boundary exactly one order, no further. `u5` therefore gains a pre-registered **two-time gate** (serial rank correlation + binned pair-χ²) alongside the marginal KS — with its power honestly scoped: the pair decides the **named corpus candidate stacks** (round-robin/argmin committers, fixed menus, gap-autonomous laundering), *not* the zero-randomness class as such (E4). Measured expectations, all four exhibits in the receipt: the linear Weyl stack passes marginal/fails two-time; the atomic stack fails marginal at the `1/(2m)` floor; the relocation stack passes both (it *is* the law); the quadratic Weyl stack passes both (the battery's boundary). `u5` runs against this gate pair with the boundary stated.

## 5. What this does and does not settle

Settled: the process-level no-go (i), the quantitative atomic floor (ii), the relocation construction (iii), and the in-principle limit of marginal gates (§3). Not settled: which channel nature uses (clock vs content — a physical question, adjacent to f6's regime decider, not a mathematical one); whether the *statistical layer of the web* requires the surviving channel (G2-strict — `u5`'s question, now properly gated); and the equivariant-finite-state refinement of (ii) with the constant sharpened.
