## Abstract

The u-line (v8 paper 17) left the placement program with a two-sided demand: the bare r+link action's growth endpoints fall into the sparse degeneracy (`S = 0` iff antichain) while its equilibrium search parks at the dense compromise (`r ≈ 0.78`) — any action that is to select geometry must charge under-comparability enough to leave the sparse class without entering the dense one. This paper derives that charge from the backwards ledger, prices it exactly, and runs it. **(1) The comparability block [THEOREM, elementary].** Faithfulness forces `P(u ∼ v) = ½` per pair (the `k = n` instance of paper 11's per-interval law); the block charge `S_comp = (n(n−1)/2)·(r_P − ½)²` therefore has faithful floor **exactly** `(n−2)/18 + ¼ = O(n)` (the U-statistic law with `ζ₁ = 1/36, ζ₂ = ¼`) — and **both measured walls are Ω(n²) from this one row**: the antichain pays `n(n−1)/8`, the u-line's growth endpoints (`r = 0.059/0.017/0.000`) pay `≥ 0.097·n(n−1)`, and the dense-compromise class (`r ≥ 0.7`) pays `≥ 0.02·n(n−1)`. One derived row, both walls, an `O(n)`-vs-`Ω(n²)` separation. The multiplicity convention is the ledger's own (one constraint per pair, exchangeable rows entering through their block mean) and is *disclosed as sufficient-condition engineering* — paper 7 Cor 4.2's `Ω(n²)` demand is scoped to counting measures and neither requires nor licenses it for a growth measure. **(2) The exact interval-abundance law [THEOREM].** For a `k`-point rectangle sprinkling the small-interval abundance counts obey a closed form: conditioning on the pair's gap rectangle (area `s`, density `g(s) = (1+s)·ln(1/s) − 2(1−s)`, total mass exactly `¼`),

> `E[A_j(k)] = k(k−1)·C(k−2, j)·{B(j+1, k−1−j)[ψ(k) − ψ(j+1)] + B(j+2, k−1−j)[ψ(k+1) − ψ(j+2)] − 2B(j+1, k−j)}`,

exact at every finite `k` (receipt-confirmed against Monte Carlo within the pre-registered 4-SEM gate at every `(k, j)`, max deviation `1.24` SEM; independently re-derived and confirmed to `10⁻⁴¹` in hostile review). The exactness is load-bearing, with the failure orders now stated correctly (round-1 review): a **one-term reference `k ln k`** leaves per-pair bias² `= Σ_j c_j² + o(1) = Θ(1)` (`c_j = −ψ(j+1) − 2 ≠ 0`) — a **fully quadratic `Θ(N²)` floor**, a dead action; a correct **two-term reference** `k(ln k + c_j)` leaves only `Θ(log²k/k²)` bias — *no asymptotic trap* — but at bench scale (`k ≈ 64`) it still roughly doubles the per-pair misfit (bias² `0.080` vs variance `0.096`), so the exact reference is what keeps the growth increments faithful at small `k`. The collapsed form makes exactness transparent: `E[A_j(k)] = k[ψ(k) − ψ(j+1)] + (j+1)[ψ(k+1) − ψ(j+2)] − 2(k−1−j)`. **(3) The abundance floor [THEOREM conditional on H-var; H-var measured].** With the exact reference, the per-pair abundance misfit `m_ab = Σ_{j≤2}(A_j/k − E[A_j(k)]/k)²` is pure estimator variance; under H-var (`Var(A_j) = O(E[A_j]·polylog k)` — measured Poisson-flat, `Var/E ≈ 0.81–1.13` with fitted `k`-exponent `0.054`) the faithful total obeys `E[S_ab] = O(N log^{2+a} N)` by paper 11 Thm 3.2's own size-density integral. The measured totals track the exact-law finite-`N` prediction at a flat ratio (`0.94/1.03/0.90` over `N = 256–1024`, post the review-round estimator fix) — the floor stands on the law, not on window-flatness. **(4) The cluster is priced [DEMONSTRATED].** Paper 11's surviving adversary — the blow-up class that passes `S_r`, the heredity axes, and Row C alike — pays `7.4×` the faithful abundance misfit on the common k-range (`[8, 288]`; bin-wise `2.3–13.1×` per the review's independent probe): Row A is the discriminating axis the backwards program named, now measured per-interval. `S_ab(antichain) = 0` identically — the division of labor (sparse wall = Row C's) is stated, not hidden. **(5) The growth bench [DEMONSTRATED; directional, 3 replicates].** Under sequential irreversible growth with the u-line's kernel and exact increments (the telescope for the interval-local terms; exact global updates for `S_comp`), with trajectory screens pinned from the β = 0 control as originally registered: **A1 = r+link+`S_comp` breaks the degeneracy** (argmin endpoints `r = 0.43–0.45` against A0's reproduced `r = 0.000`) and lands **MIDDLE-sprinkling-band on 6/6 runs** at `β ∈ {β*, ∞}` — ordering fraction in `[0.35, 0.65]` *and* height inside the `2√n` band — replicated 4/4 at `n = 128`. **The control's own class, disclosed (review round):** the β = 0 kernel control itself classifies MIDDLE-sprinkling-band (`r = 0.555/0.649`, height-band `1.21/1.49` — the `n = 128` values boundary-grazing the class edges): the kernel reaches the band with random picks, so the action's specific contributions are **(i)** the argmin escape from A0's antichain (the degeneracy result proper) and **(ii)** centering and holding `r ≈ 0.44` against the control's DENSE-ward drift with scale (`0.555 → 0.649`). **A2 = A1+`S_ab` steers**: its endpoints' top-interval abundance misfit is `3.3×` smaller than A1's (`0.284` vs `0.929`) — under the growth mode, the abundance term does exactly what paper 14 measured it failing to do in drift mode ("pricing without steering" reads as mode-specific, mode-favored-not-isolated; ## 2. Row C — the comparability block [THEOREM]

**Derivation.** Heredity (paper 11 Thm 2.1) makes every interval of a faithful order a rectangle sprinkling; the global order is itself the top interval (the diamond). Thm 2.2's kernel at `k = n` forces `P(u ∼ v) = ½` for every unordered pair (the diamond/rectangle value — the corpus's population convention; a general causally convex region carries its own constant) and the exact block law `E[(r_P − ½)²] = Var(r_n) = (2/(n(n−1)))[2(n−2)ζ₁ + ζ₂]`, `ζ₁ = 1/36`, `ζ₂ = ¼`. The ledger convention — one constraint per pair, exchangeable rows entering through their mean — gives the block charge with pair multiplicity:

> `S_comp(P) = (n(n−1)/2)·(r_P − ½)²`.

**Theorem 2.1 (floor and walls).** (a) *Faithful floor, exact:* `E[S_comp] = 2(n−2)ζ₁ + ζ₂ = (n−2)/18 + ¼` — `O(n)` (receipt: measured within `4` SEM at `N ∈ {256, 512, 1024}`). (b) *Sparse wall:* any order with `r_P ≤ ½ − δ` pays `≥ (n(n−1)/2)δ²`; the antichain pays `n(n−1)/8`; the u-line endpoints pay `≥ 0.097·n(n−1)`. (c) *Dense wall:* the measured compromise class (`r ≥ 0.7`) pays `≥ 0.02·n(n−1)`; the `j2` near-chain (`r = 0.99`) pays `≥ 0.12·n(n−1)`. ∎ *(Arithmetic; the content is that both walls come from one derived row with an `O(n)` floor between them.)*

**The disclosed convention.** The pair multiplicity is `S_r`'s own convention applied to the comparability rows, and the walls are *sufficient-condition engineering* for a growth measure: paper 7 Cor 4.2's `Ω(n²)` demand governs counting base measures and is scoped out for dynamical measures by its own clause. What Row C cannot do is *sufficiency* — the jittered lattice and any `r = ½` forgery pass it at zero charge (the standing cage logic); that is Row A's job.

## 3. Row A — the exact abundance law and the conditional floor

**Theorem 3.1 (the exact law).** For `k` iid uniform points in a rectangle under dominance, with `A_j` the number of related pairs with interior exactly `j`: the gap-area density of a related pair is `g(s) = (1+s)ln(1/s) − 2(1−s)` (total mass `¼` — `P(pair related)`), interior counts are `Binomial(k−2, s)` given the gap, and the Beta/digamma reduction of `∫₀¹ g(s)s^j(1−s)^{k−2−j}ds` gives the closed form of the abstract, which collapses (the prefactors cancel) to `E[A_j(k)] = k[ψ(k) − ψ(j+1)] + (j+1)[ψ(k+1) − ψ(j+2)] − 2(k−1−j)` — exact at every finite `k`, leading behavior `k(ln k + c_j + o(1))`, `c_j = −ψ(j+1) − 2`. ∎ *(Receipt: MC agreement within the 4-SEM gate at every `(k, j) ∈ {64, 128} × {0, 1, 2}`; the identity `∫g = ¼` at `dps = 60`.)*

**The bias ledger, corrected in review.** In the `A_j/k` normalization: a one-term reference `k ln k` leaves per-pair bias² `= Σ_j c_j² + o(1) = Θ(1)` with `c_j = −ψ(j+1) − 2` — the total is **`Θ(N²)`, fully quadratic**: the one-term action is dead. A correct two-term reference leaves `Θ(log² k/k²)` per pair — total `O(N log N)`, *subdominant to the variance floor*: asymptotically harmless. The practical point is finite-`k`: at `k ≈ 64` (the bench's interval scale) the two-term bias² (`≈ 0.080`) is comparable to the variance term (`≈ 0.096`) — the exact reference is what keeps the bench's increments faithful where they are actually evaluated. (The growth bench therefore carries the exact reference, cached; the collapsed closed form above is the cheap way to have it.)

**Theorem 3.2 (the floor, conditional).** Define `m_ab(I) = Σ_{j≤2}(A_j/k − E[A_j(k)]/k)²` and `S_ab = Σ_{|I| ≥ m₀} m_ab`. Under **H-var** (`Var(A_j(k)) = O(k·polylog k)`): `E[m_ab | k] ≤ C·polylog(k)/k`, and paper 11 Thm 3.2's size-density integral gives `E[S_ab] = O(N log^{2+a} N)` on faithful orders. ∎ **H-var measured:** `Var/E ≈ 0.81–1.13` over `k = 64–256`, fitted exponent `0.054` — Poisson-flat; its proof (a second-moment/dependency-graph integral) is the named refinement. **The floor observable:** sampled totals over `N = 256–1024` track the exact-law finite-`N` prediction at ratio `0.94/1.03/0.90` — flat; the rising `S_ab/(N ln² N)` normalization is the law's own transient, not a violation.

**The cluster priced [DEMONSTRATED].** On the common k-range (`k ∈ [8, 288]`; bin-wise 2.3–13.1×), the blow-up population pays mean `m_ab` `7.4×` the faithful value — the adversary that survives `S_r`, the heredity axes, and Row C is caught by the profile misfit, per-interval, exactly as paper 11 §4 predicted of the abundance family. `S_ab(antichain) = 0` (no related pairs): the sparse wall is Row C's, disclosed.

## 4. The sandwich, assembled

**Corollary 4.1 (the two-sided cage).** For `S_aug = S_r + link + S_comp [+ λ_ab S_ab]` with unit weights, every named wall class pays `Ω(n²)`: the 3-layer KR ensemble (paper 11 Prop 3.1), dense violators (Cor 3.4), the antichain/sparse class and the dense compromise (Theorem 2.1) — **and the entire height-2 class, at fixed coupling**: a two-layer order with ordering fraction `r` pays `S_comp + link ≥ (n(n−1)/2)·[(r−½)² + r/4]`, minimized at `r = 3/8` with value **`7n(n−1)/128`** — so the free-bipartite *aggregate* (`2^{n²/4}` states) dies at any fixed `β > (32/7)ln 2 ≈ 3.17`, **upgrading paper 11 Cor 3.4's log-growing coupling `β_n = 8 ln n` to a fixed one** (review-round contribution, adopted). Faithful orders pay `Θ(N log² N)` for H-var exponent `a = 0` (`O(N log^{2+a} N)` generally). The low-action region at large `n` therefore excludes both u-line walls and every class the v8 cage priced, and contains the faithful class (in expectation, with Markov giving the high-probability form across the `n²`-vs-polylog gap). ∎

What the sandwich does *not* say: that the low-action region contains *only* faithful-class orders (`r ≈ ½` forgeries with in-band heights and non-Poisson fine structure are not excluded by Row C, only priced by Row A where their interiors deviate — the forger's cell shrinks, the cage logic, no closure claim); nor anything about the induced *measure* under a growth law (entropy-vs-action inside the arena is the standing [OPEN] of paper 11 §6). The upgrade path to a selection theorem is exactly: (i) an anti-forgery bound (which `r ≈ ½` classes escape Row A, at what cost), and (ii) the growth-measure census (`u4`) over the assembled action.
