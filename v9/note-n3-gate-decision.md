# The n3 gate decision — why an L1 pass on synthetic webs cannot carry the identification, and the two options

**Status:** decision note, 2026-07-05 (round 2; the ladder item). No code — the round-1 bundle review's M6 finding, taken to its structural conclusion.

## The finding

Design note 1's L0–L2 test the `w ↔ χ_AB` dictionary on *synthetic* webs, where the note itself specifies the record side be "computed from the synthetic web's own seal moments." But a synthetic web (a sprinkling with chains) commits **only order data** — every constructible "record-side" functional (the merged-sequence kernel, cross-count hops, any monotone reweighting styled as an entangling content) is a functional of the same cross-order structure `w` is. The bundle review's M6 ("the L2 closure is an order-side result; the χ-attribution rides paper 2 §5's untested bridge") is therefore **intrinsic to the rung, not to the instantiation**: no synthetic-web construction can carry the record-native content of the identification — an order-functional candidate could well PASS L1 numerically, and that pass would be gate-theater. (Separately from this structural point: the executed routes also fell numerically short of the 0.9 pin, leveling at ~0.85 — two independent facts, not one.) The identification's record-native face is only testable at **L3**, where a Tier-A `χ`-field is *laid down* (legitimately free, per paper 2's no-go) to source connectivity, and the pipeline runs with no geometry anywhere.

What L0–L2 *did* establish (n1/n2, corrected verdicts): the coupling's **geometry transport works** — hop-composed connectivity closes the transverse plane at Procrustes 0.92–0.97 against the 0.99 benchmark — and the per-pair rank dictionary levels at ~0.85 (< the 0.9 pin) because MDS aggregation, not pairwise rank fidelity, is what geometry consumes.
