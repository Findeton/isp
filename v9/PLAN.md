## 0. The state, and the target theorem

**The target.** Paper 7 §5 already wrote the sentence that would discharge the 2D gate:

> *"A proof that click-law-weighted sequential growth concentrates on `dim ≤ 2` orders with vanishing realizer discrepancy would discharge the gate in 2D — every ingredient of that sentence is now defined, and receipt-testable."*

Post-u-line, the honest decomposition of what is missing from that sentence is exactly five pieces, and they are this campaign's tiers:

1. an action whose walls are **proved** on both measured attractors — the abundance term (Tier 1);
2. the statistical layer (Poisson-typicality) as **theorems** instead of ensemble-calibrated `z` readings (Tier 2);
3. the growth-measure theorems — bulk unreachability, representation, the covariance fork, the class itself (Tier 4);
4. the randomness theorem — sequencing irreducibility (Tier 3);
5. discharge of the posits the whole slate rides on — (b2) stationarity and the two two-clock posits (Tier 5).

The 3+1 face (Tier 6) runs as an independent parallel track on its own machinery. Tier 0 is the set of cheap gates that must run before any new certification, because two of them can move existing flagship numbers.

**What the u-line changed.** The external post-paper-16 plan's Moves 0–2 are executed (design note 8; `u1`/`u2`/`u3`), and its central bet is *resolved*: irreversible growth does not escape the equilibrium attractor — it falls into the opposite pathology, the literal antichain (`S = 0.000, r = 0.000, H = 1`), the bare action's own degenerate global minimum. Equilibrium parks dense (`r ≈ 0.78`, j2); growth parks sparse. The missing comparability/abundance term is therefore demanded **from both sides**, which converts it from a search into a derivation target. Second: placement determinism (D1) is *beneficial* (kill-the-oldest reaches `0.96×`, `z = −1.3` at `N = 1024`, directional), sequencing determinism (D2) is marginal-visible but churn-laundered, and the strict G2 conjecture is untested. Third: the telescope lemma makes growth the exact, cheap bench for all action-family work.

## 2. Tier 1 — the abundance campaign (the program's decision point)

### T1.1 The abundance term derived backwards, and the faithful-floor go/no-go

**Task.** Derive the interval-abundance action term in paper 11's discipline — from faithfulness, not tuning: for each related pair with interior size `≥ m₀`, the interior's small-interval abundance counts (`N₀/N₁/N₂/…` per paper 7's axis-4 profile) scored against the exact Poisson-rectangle expectations that Theorem 2.1 (heredity) + the `M²` rectangle law force. Then prove the **faithful floor**: `E[S_ab] = O(N log² N)` on sprinklings — the analogue of paper 11 Thm 3.2, per-pair contribution decaying like the U-statistic variance of the abundance profile at interior size `k`, integrated over the pair-size density.

**Honesty clause (verification-forced).** The adversarial check flagged this precisely: paper 11 does **not** re-derive the abundance laws (it quotes them as "further necessary axes"), so the floor lemma is genuinely new mathematics with **no in-corpus template**. It is the single point where the whole dynamics program can die, which is why it runs first and alone.

**Kill condition (the campaign's master fork).** If the faithful abundance floor is *quadratic* (faithful orders pay `Ω(n²)` in any backwards-derived abundance misfit), the action route is dead at its root — no augmented action can separate faithful orders from the walls at the measure level. The redirect is note 8 §5.5's: the coupling route (Tier 6) becomes the surviving line, and Tiers 1b/1c, plus Tier 4's weight legs, are cancelled as stated. **Grade ceiling.** THEOREM (floor, exact constants where the U-statistic machinery gives them).

### T1.2 The two-sided sandwich theorem — the augmented action's walls

**Task.** Two parts. **Part I (one page, currently only a receipt observation):** state the bare action's degeneracy as a theorem — `S_{r+link}(P) = 0 ⟺ P` is an antichain (both terms charge only related pairs); corollary: under any β-family growth law whose move menu supports incomparable placement, the `β = ∞` endpoint is the antichain (the `u2` finding, made arithmetic). **Part II:** for `S_aug = S_r + link + λ_ab·S_ab`, prove the **two-sided `Ω(n²)` walls**: (sparse wall) any order with comparability deficit `Θ(n²)` — including the antichain and the free-bipartite sparse tail — pays `Ω(n²)` in the link+abundance terms; (dense wall) the measured dense-compromise class (`r ≥ 0.7`, paper 13's `r = 0.79/0.76` endpoints — use the paper's numbers) has interval-abundance profiles bounded away from Poisson on `Ω(n²)` pairs, hence pays `Ω(n²)` in `S_ab`. With paper 11 Cor 3.4 this cages the arena from all four sides: layered bulk, two-layer aggregate, dense compromise, antichain — leaving the sub-quadratic interior as the only low-action region.

**Scope correction (verification-forced).** Paper 7 Cor 4.2's `Ω(n²)` demand is scoped to **counting base measures**; it neither requires nor licenses quadratic walls for a growth measure. The walls are *sufficient-condition engineering* for measure-level separation, not corpus-mandated structure — say so in the paper. **Grade ceiling.** THEOREM (Part I; Part II sparse wall) + THEOREM or ensemble-scoped COROLLARY (dense wall, scoped to the measured class the way Prop 3.1 is scoped to the KR ensemble).

## 3. Tier 2 — the statistical layer as theorems

### T2.1 The staircase / variance-ledger law — receipt `y1` (= memo queue item 2, upgraded)

**Task.** Derive the law behind everything `u3` saw: for churn-class supplies at small renewal fraction `L·M/N`, the mean-level interval-count dispersion excess decomposes at leading order as `z_excess ∝ (cv²[lifetime] − cv²_ref) × (same-lineage pair density)`, with closed forms at the exponential/geometric and deterministic lifetime laws. Corollaries, all currently measured-only: why D1 (kill-the-oldest) helps — it concentrates the lifetime law (`sd 2.13 → 1.41`); why the `L = 2` staircase residual (`z_comb = +2.3`, paper 16's named open) exists and what lifetime tail cures it; the pre-registered kill branch — a proved *nonzero* offset `c(L) > 0` would mean the residual is structural, not tail-curable. Then the memo's queue-item-2 certification retest (D1-type laws, `N = 2048`, ≥ 12 seeds, both content readings) runs **as this law's receipt**, under T0.2's powered semantics — a strictly better use of the same run.

**Prereq.** T0.1 (the D1+atoms population is tie-rich; audit first). **Grade ceiling.** DERIVED (leading-order closed form on the churn class) + CERTIFIED/REFUSED verdicts under powered semantics.

### T2.2 The dispersion-budget theorem (the forward half of G2-strict)

**Task.** Prove: within the two-clock class, a stack that is deterministic with finite generative state in *every* layer (sequencing, content, placement, event timing) produces an eventually periodic web; its two-clock point set is a finite union of arithmetic-staircase translates (a generalized lattice); and generalized lattices have interval-count dispersion bounded away from Poisson from below (the sub-Poisson direction — the jittered lattice's `z = 5.2` is the measured precedent; the proof is number-variance rigidity of lattice-class point sets against the Poisson reference at matched density).

**Why it matters.** This is the first theorem-grade instance of the G2 conjecture — "irreducible click randomness is necessary for statistical spacetime" — scoped to the class where it is true, with the chaotic/infinite-state escape excluded **by hypothesis, not by silence** (note 8 §5.4's own demand). **Grade ceiling.** THEOREM (scoped, constants explicit).

### T2.3 Poissonization for concentrated-lifetime laws

**Task.** The converse direction: a Chen–Stein / dependency-graph argument that churn-class supplies with sub-geometric lifetime tails and quasi-continuous content reach Poisson interval-count statistics at rate (total-variation bound in the renewal fraction and the tail weight). With T2.1 and T2.2 this brackets the statistical layer from both directions — the analogue of what paper 12 did for the geometric layer. **Grade ceiling.** THEOREM (scoped to the churn class; the rate constant named-measured).
