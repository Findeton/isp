# note-s6 — the centered action: re-pricing steps by excess over the faithful expectation

**Status:** design note + pre-registration, 2026-07-05 (round 7). Gates pinned pre-run. Receipt: `v9/code/s6_repricing_test.py`.

**The derivation shape.** s5 measured that faithful steps are locally dear because their many new pairs each pay the raw charge — but paper 1's own theorems say faithful pairs are *expected* to pay a floor (the estimator variance: `E[(r_I−½)²|k] = Var(r_k)` exactly; the link fraction likewise). The floor is not misfit. The principled re-pricing: **charge each row its excess over the faithful expectation.** Two implementable forms:
- **Arm A (Var-centered):** qualifying pairs pay `(r_I−½)² − Var(r_k)` (exact closed form); links/S_comp unchanged. Conservative.
- **Arm B (the allowance form):** `ΔS̃ = ΔS − c̄_t·|D|` — every new pair carries a subsidy at the *arrival-level faithful mean per-pair charge* `c̄_t` (this round: empirical, from 4 oracle-trajectory charge profiles, disclosed as CALIBRATED-not-derived; the closed form of `c̄_t` from the exact size-density mixture is the theory task accompanying). Equivalently: argmin of (cost minus a faithful-rate per-pair allowance) — a linear re-pricing.

**Walls, pre-checked (arithmetic):** centering per *arrival-level mean* (not per-pair-conditional-on-size) preserves the walls — a link-heavy/two-layer class pays `(¼ − c̄)·Ω(n²)` (with `c̄ < ¼` strictly, since faithful growth's pair mix is not all-links); the antichain still pays `S_comp = n(n−1)/8`; the dense class pays the r-term excess `≈ (r_I−½)² − O(1/k)` on `Ω(n²)` pairs. *(Per-pair-conditional centering would zero the link wall — the named trap, avoided by construction.)*

**Pinned gates (both directions live).** G1: the s5 marginal-preference rate under each arm (same instrumentation, same 4-sprinkling faithful trajectories, `t ≥ 64`): **≥ 0.6 = REPRICED-PREFERRED** for that arm; ≤ 0.3 rejected; else mixed. Expected ordering: B ≥ A (A corrects only the r-term's floor). G2 (the bench, best arm): free-run growth (guided menus NOT provided — pure kernel menus, the production setting): endpoints `r ∈ [0.35, 0.60]` (the subsidy-overshoot guard: subsidizing pairs must not resurrect the dense wall), heredity `r_I ∈ [0.35, 0.65]` in ≥ 3/4 reps, `m_ab ≤ 3×` faithful. **G2 passing = the production mechanism reborn under derived-shaped pricing** (grade: DEMONSTRATED-calibrated until `c̄_t`'s closed form lands); G1-pass/G2-fail = the landscape is fixed but the kernel's menus still can't realize it (the enrichment question returns, now meaningfully); G1-fail = the centering family is insufficient — the inverse problem needs path-level information, and the churn-synthesis reading hardens.

Seed `default_rng(20260713)`; float64; s5 machinery verbatim; `n = 256`, 4 reps per measurement.

## Addendum — results (2026-07-05, run of `s6_repricing_test.py`, seed 20260713, n = 256)

**Calibration.** `c̄_t` (arrival-level faithful mean per-pair charge, 4 oracle profiles, t ≥ 64): mean **0.1034** — well below the diffuse-pair charge ¼, so the link wall survives the allowance at coefficient (¼ − c̄)·Ω(n²) exactly as §3 pre-checked. Implementation disclosure: the raw profile was smoothed with a centered moving average (window 15) before use; this is a stability choice made before the G1 run, not a tuned parameter.

**G1 — both arms REJECTED.** Marginal-preference rates against the pinned 0.6/0.3 lines:
- **Arm A (Var-centered): 6.1%** (reps 0.052 / 0.047 / 0.068 / 0.078) — REJECTED.
- **Arm B (allowance): 3.6%** (reps 0.016 / 0.016 / 0.062 / 0.052) — REJECTED.

Arm A sits directionally above the raw action's 3.9% (s5) [directional, 4 reps, overlapping ranges]; arm B is indistinguishable from raw. Neither approaches preference. **The s5 landscape verdict is robust to the centering family:** the marginal anti-faithfulness of the action is not an artifact of the uncentered r-term or of the per-pair ¼ charge — charging *excess over the faithful expectation* still prices the true move above kernel decoys at ~94% of steps.

**G2 (arm A, pure kernel menus) — REFUSED, with a named split.** Endpoints over 4 reps: r = 0.453–0.468, r_I = 0.811–0.882, m_ab mean 1.380 (≈ 5.0× the faithful 0.276 ceiling at 3×).
- The **overshoot guard held**: global r landed inside [0.35, 0.60] in 4/4 reps. The centered action does regulate the *global* comparability rate in free-run — as Φ did in s4.
- The **interiors stayed forged**: r_I in the kernel-floor band (0.73–0.91) in 4/4 reps, m_ab 5× — the fine structure is inherited from the move class, untouched by the re-pricing.

This is the **third independent confirmation of the division-of-labor result** (after s4's charge-rate Φ and the round-5 kernel-floor analysis), now under a centered action: global comparability is selection-controllable; interior fine structure is move-class-inherited.

**Verdict per §5's branches:** the re-pricing family is **rejected as a production rescue**. The increment-optimization route to growth is now dead at three actions (raw A1, Var-centered, allowance). The production mechanism remains the churn class; the action's standing is as grader (walls/floors/prices), per paper 2. The closed-form derivation of c̄_t is *withdrawn as a priority* — with G1 rejected at the calibrated profile, a derived profile cannot change the verdict (any derived c̄_t is a fixed arrival-level profile; G1's rejection is uniform in that class up to the measured spread).
