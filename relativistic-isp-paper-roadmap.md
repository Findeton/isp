# Relativistic ISP Paper Roadmap

Preprint, not peer reviewed, version 2026-05-28.

Author: Felix Robles Elvira

## Purpose
This document maps the relativistic ISP program into a sequence of phases and papers aimed at reaching theorem-level scope without overclaiming.

The central strategic lesson is now sharper than in the earlier roadmap drafts: each phase should close a real proof burden at the exact scope currently justified, and broader ontology-expanding phases should come only after the exchange-defect core has been extended one exact layer at a time.

## Recommended paper count
### Recommended
Five more core papers, plus one optional final synthesis paper.

Status after the current implementation: the five core post-Paper-11 theorem papers are represented by Papers 12 through 16, and the optional final synthesis is now represented by Paper 17.

### Compressed minimum
Four more papers, if the Einstein-causality bridge is merged aggressively into the operational phase after the dynamical gauge-field enlargement is done and the final synthesis is kept optional.

### Why the recommended count is better
The dynamical Abelian gauge-field enlargement is already a distinct proof burden beyond the static background-field paper, detector/control dynamics is distinct again, the Einstein-causality bridge to Haag-Kastler locality is a separate conceptual theorem burden, the first genuinely interacting gauge-matter benchmark is distinct yet again, and the QED-adjacent truncated compact-$U(1)$ continuation beyond the $Z_2$ benchmark is distinct once more. Keeping all five separate from the final synthesis makes it easier to avoid presenting either locality or interaction as if it were already a finished theorem package before the relevant exact benchmark has been earned.

## Dependency order
The dependency order should stay strict:

1. exact free one-particle localized kernels;
2. exact leading support-level boundary law;
3. leading-order restricted universality;
4. exact higher-order overlap structure and strip-moment criterion;
5. normalized strip-moment regulator-stability criterion;
6. explicit higher coefficients and first nontrivial singleton strip moments;
7. explicit first non-leading regulator comparison;
8. variable particle number;
9. minimal static `U(1)` benchmark;
10. dynamical Abelian matter-link gauge field;
11. detector/control dynamics;
12. Einstein-causality bridge theorem;
13. boundary-flux-resolved exact physical-sector locality;
14. minimal genuinely interacting `Z_2` gauge-matter benchmark;
15. truncated compact-`U(1)` gauge-matter benchmark with Coulomb-string reduced interaction;
16. optional synthesis.

The strategic lesson is simple:

close the structural higher-order and regulator-stability criteria before asking explicit coefficient computations or ontology-expanding phases to carry unresolved algebraic burdens.

Possible future generalization route. Lieb-Robinson bounds for finite-range lattice Hamiltonians strongly suggest that some quasi-local propagation mechanism should survive in interacting lattice extensions of the program. But the present exchange-defect theorems should not be rephrased as automatic corollaries of Lieb-Robinson. Those bounds control Heisenberg commutators of local observables, whereas the ISP exchange defect is a group commutator of comparison maps
`J_R = \Gamma_R \Gamma_0^{-1}`
built from primitive kernels and their inverses. So an additional bridge theorem would be needed before operator-level Lieb-Robinson control could be transferred to `J_R` and `E_{R,S}`. That interacting extension is a later task, not a reason to collapse the current exact free-model phase ordering.

- six more core papers beyond Paper 10 are now the recommended route;
